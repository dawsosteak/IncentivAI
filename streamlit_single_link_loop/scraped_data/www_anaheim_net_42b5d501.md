#  Electrical System Inspections 
Anaheim Public Utilities (APU) relies on inspections of electrical infrastructure to ensure the safety and reliability of service and for consistency with state guidelines as industry best practice. Inspections are critical for assessing the physical condition of infrastructure and for allowing APU to prioritize capital projects. Inspections also proactively identify equipment that may need immediate attention to avoid prolonged unplanned outages.
## OVERHEAD INSPECTION
![Overhead Inspection](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=63334)
![Overhead Inspection](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=63333)
![Powerlines](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=63332)
### What to Expect:
  * Overhead inspections include utility poles, power lines, transformers, switches, capacitors, automation devices, and other utility equipment.
  * The city’s contractor, Intec Services Inc., will schedule an inspection time with customers.
  * Overhead inspections will last approximately 15-20 minutes, focusing on identifying any damage or signs of deterioration.
  * Access to private property may be necessary where utility poles are located along rear property lines.
  * Some minor excavation and drilling may take place around the utility poles.
  * There will be temporary noise associated with these activities.  
  



### How to Prepare:
  * Kindly ensure that gates are unlocked and pathways are clear for field personnel to access the utility pole.
  * Please secure any pets during the inspection period.
  * APU understands this may disrupt your normal activities. We appreciate your patience and cooperation.

## UNDERGROUND INSPECTION
![UG-Inspection 1](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=63452)
![UG-Inspection 2](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=63453)
![UG-Inspection 3](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=63454)
### What to Expect:
  * Underground inspections are conducted on electrical equipment such as transformers, cables and switches, and include an assessment of the structural integrity of underground structures.
  * Underground inspections will typically last approximately 30-45 minutes, depending on traffic control and access requirements to underground vaults.
  * Access to private properties, including apartments and commercial businesses, may be required where pad-mounted equipment is located. 
  * The city’s contractor, Henkels & McCoy West, LLC, will schedule an inspection time with affected customers.
  * There will be temporary parking restrictions, and “No Parking” signs will be posted 72 hours prior to inspection.  
  



### How to Prepare:
  * Kindly ensure a clear pathway for field personnel to access pad-mounted equipment and underground vaults.
  * Please adhere to the temporary “No Parking” signs and relocate vehicles as necessary. 
  * APU understands this may disrupt your normal activities. We appreciate your patience and cooperation.
