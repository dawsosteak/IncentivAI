#  Save Energy and Money with CORE's Rebates  

##  From planning energy-saving projects to providing funding for home improvements, we have you covered.  

[ Apply for a Rebate ](https://energysmartcolorado.formstack.com/forms/residential_rebate_application)
![CORE staff member conducting a home energy assessment in Aspen, demonstrating their hands-on approach to climate solutions.](https://lirp.cdn-website.com/06c12080/dms3rep/multi/opt/DSC01358--281-29-c0294170-1920w.JPG)
###  Our region's residential building sector emitted 247,800 MT CO2 in 2020. **That’s equivalent to consuming nearly 28 million gallons of gasoline.** Reducing energy consumption in the built environment can have a significant impact on our work to address climate change.  

###  Residential Building Rebates  

Funding for residential building electrification projects is available to community members living in Pitkin County, Garfield County, and the City of Basalt.
#### Energy Efficiency Projects
**Rebate Amounts**
  * 50% of the project cost up to $2,500 for standard participants
  * 50% of the project cost up to $5,000 for [Community Priority Participants](https://www.aspencore.org/community-priority-participants)


[ Eligible Energy Efficiency Projects ](https://www.aspencore.org/residential-rebates#ResEE)
#### Fuel Switching Projects
**Rebate Amounts**
  * 50% of the project cost up to $7,500 for standard participants
  * 50% of the project cost up to $10,000 for [Community Priority Participants](https://www.aspencore.org/community-priority-participants)


[ Eligible Fuel Switching Projects ](https://www.aspencore.org/residential-rebates#ResFS)
#### Not sure where to start?
Not a problem; we're here to help![Contact our Energy Concierg](https://www.aspencore.org/energy-concierge)[e](https://www.aspencore.org/energy-concierge)at any stage of your building project. Find answers to other frequently asked questions about rebates on our [FAQ webpage](https://www.aspencore.org/faq).
#### Is your property in Eagle County?
Properties located in Eagle County but outside of Basalt town limits qualify for [special incentives.](https://www.aspencore.org/eagle-rebates) Rebates are available for existing single-family homes or individual units in a multifamily building. 
#### Looking for more funding?
You may be eligible for even more funding! Local community members, from teachers and childcare workers to veterans and government staff, may qualify for savings of up to $10,000 on a single residential project, and up to $50,000 for a commercial or multi-family building project. Check out our [Community Priority Participants](https://www.aspencore.org/community-priority-participants) criteria or our [Savings Calculator](https://www.aspencore.org/federal-state-and-local-funding-programs) to see if you qualify for more incentives.
#### After I complete my project, how long do I have to submit my rebate form?
Your application must be submitted within 90 days of completing a project. Invoices must be dated within 90 days of applying for a rebate.
#### Ready to apply for a residential rebate?
Fill out the residential rebate application and our Energy Concierge team will contact you.
[ Apply for a Rebate ](https://energysmartcolorado.formstack.com/forms/residential_rebate_application)

##### Eligible Fuel Switching Projects
### Space Heating, Water Heating, Snowmelt, and Ventilation
  

Replacing a fossil fuel system with:
  * A heat pump for space heating, water heating, or snowmelt
  * A heat-recovery or heat pump ventilation system


Design costs and electrical capacity upgrade costs associated with a completed heat pump project will be included in the project cost used to determine the rebate.
[ FAQs ](https://www.aspencore.org/faq)
### New Construction
  * All-electric individual homes without natural gas lines, which use heat pump technology for space and water heating and heat recovery technology for fresh air ventilation


Rebates can be requested once final construction design documents ("100% CD's") are provided. Rebate for Community Priority Participants is $20,000 and $10,000 for others.
  

If the building code prohibits natural gas, then no rebates will be provided.
[ Button ](https://www.aspencore.org/residential-rebates)
### Custom Rebates
  

Custom rebates are considered for projects that do not fall within the other project types but produce significant energy savings.
