#  Temporary Power 
### Step 1: Temporary Service Evaluation
  * Present your request for temporary power to Electrical Engineering at Anaheim West Tower or [online](https://www.anaheim.net/FormCenter/Public-Utilities-6/Temporary-Power-Form-50).
  * You'll need to provide the voltage and ampacity you are requesting as part of your service. An electrical engineering representative will determine the service point for your temporary power request and provide you with an Electric Service Plan within 4-8 weeks, depending on the complexity of the service requirements. You will also receive a bill for fees associated with your Electric Service Plan, which can be paid by mail or at the cashier located on the 1st Floor of Anaheim West Tower.


### Step 2: Electrical Permit
  * Once your meter location has been approved, you may apply for an electrical permit over the counter at the Anaheim Building Division, located on the 1st Floor of City Hall (200 S. Anaheim Blvd). Please note that there will be a fee for the permit. You may pay for your permit over the counter by check or debit/credit card; if you choose to pay by cash, you will be directed to the cashier at Anaheim West Tower.
    * For larger projects, the Anaheim Building Department may require a formal plan check rather than an over-the-counter review. A finalized permit will be required before an electric service plan can be issued for construction. Please visit the following [link ](https://www.anaheim.net/5252/Building-Division)or call 714-765-5153 for more information and fee requirements


### Step 3: Application for Temporary Power
  * After receiving your permits, contact Anaheim Public Utilities Customer Service by telephone at 714-765-3300 to apply for temporary power service and meter installation. If you prefer, you may also apply in person on the 1st Floor of Anaheim West Tower.


### Step 4: Inspections & Meter Installation
  * After installing your electrical panel, you will need to receive three inspections: 
    1. A building inspection to check your panel and other related construction items
    2. A utility inspection to check between the power source to the panel
    3. A meter shop inspection
  * First, call the Building Inspection Line at 714-765-5153 to request an electrical service inspection from the Building Division - you'll be asked to provide your Meter Spot Report during the time of your scheduled inspection. 
  * Once you have passed the building inspection, contact our electrical utility inspector, which is provided to you on the Electric Service Plan, and arrange for the final utility and meter shop inspection. 
  * Upon passing the utility and meter shop inspections, and when all required fees have been paid, a service crew will automatically be scheduled to energize your panel and install a temporary power meter for you within 3-5 business days.
  * If you prefer to schedule installation for a specific time/date, please call 714-765-6867.
  * When your construction project is completed and you no longer require temporary power, please contact customer service at 714-765-3300 to request termination.
