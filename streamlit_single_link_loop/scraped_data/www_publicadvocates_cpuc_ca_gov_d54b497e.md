#  Distribution Grid Electrification Model Findings
Technical Report, August 01, 2024 - 
_  
__(Updated on August 1, 2024: We recently completed a study on EV charging locations and potential costs impacts to supplement our Distribution Grid Electrification Model Study and Report (August 2023). You can find the supplemental study below.)_
**Overview**
The Public Advocates Office at the California Public Utilities Commission has undertaken a study of the costs of upgrading the distribution grids of the three largest investor-owned electric utilities (IOUs) to meet California’s transportation electrification goals. Our results indicate that the total cost of upgrading the IOUs’ distribution grids by 2035 will be approximately $26 billion. This is about half of the cost identified by a similar recent study, the _Electrification Impacts Study Part 1_ (EIS), conducted by Kevala, a consultant engaged by the California Public Utilities Commission. 
California’s goal that all new light-duty vehicles sold be electric by 2035 drives the need to plan for distribution system upgrades and their attendant costs in a manner that is thoughtful, careful, and comprehensive.electrification could put downward pressure on electric rates by increasing electricity sales. As the cost of providing electric service – including the costs to upgrade the system – are recovered across more units of electricity sold, electrification may cause downward pressure on electricity rates across California.
We look forward to the continuing public discourse on how to best plan for and implement the state’s transportation electrification goal.
