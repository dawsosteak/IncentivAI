#  Resources for Solar Customers
## Installing a System
If you are interested in installing a system on your home or property that utilizes the net billing tariff, also called the Solar Billing Plan, please visit the following links:
  * Pacific Gas & Electric Company customers: [PG&E customer-sited generation webpage](https://www.pge.com/en/clean-energy.html)


  * Southern California Edison Company customers: 
    * [SCE’s residential customer-sited generation webpage](https://www.sce.com/residential/generating-your-own-power)
    * [SCE’s non-residential customer-sited generation webpage](https://www.sce.com/business/generating-your-own-power)


  * San Diego Gas & Electric Company customers: [SDG&E’s customer-sited generation webpage](https://www.sdge.com/solar)


**Federal Tax Credits**
The U.S. Department of Energy has published information on federal tax credits for solar systems at the following links.
  * [Homeowner’s Guide to the Federal Tax Credit for Solar Photovoltaics](https://www.energy.gov/eere/solar/homeowners-guide-federal-tax-credit-solar-photovoltaics)
  * [Federal Solar Tax Credits for Businesses](https://www.energy.gov/eere/solar/federal-solar-tax-credits-businesses)


**Federal Resources**
The U.S. Department of the Treasury (Treasury), the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB), and the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) have published resources on good practices to follow before installing a solar system, and protecting yourself from scams. Links to resources published by each entity can be found at the following link.
  * [U.S. Department of the Treasury, Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, and Federal Trade Commission Announce Steps to Protect Residential Solar Consumers, Ensure Access to Credits | U.S. Department of the Treasury](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/jy2522)

Complaints
If you are having difficulties with your solar installation or contractor, we recommend these resources.
  * For all complaints against solar contractors, whether licensed or unlicensed, please contact: [Contractors State License Board](http://www.cslb.ca.gov/Consumers/Filing_A_Complaint/ "Contractors State License Board")
  * The Attorney General’s office addresses all types of complaints. Contact the AG Public Inquiry Unit: 
    * By phone (916) 322-3360 or toll-free at (800) 952-5225 
    * [Online Complaint Form](http://oag.ca.gov/contact/consumer-complaint-against-business-or-company "Online Complaint Form")
    * AG Office’s [FAQs](http://oag.ca.gov/consumers/morefaqs "FAQs")
  * The trade association California Solar + Storage Association (CALSSA) is available to resolve complaints: [CALSSA Complaint Investigation](https://calssa.org/contractor-investigation/ "CALSSA Complaint Investigation")
  * For unwanted telemarking, file a claim with the Federal Trade Commission (FTC): [FTC Complaint Assistant](https://www.ftccomplaintassistant.gov/Information#crnt&panel1-1 "FTC Complaint Assistant")


If you have any additional questions, please contact energy@cpuc.ca.gov.
