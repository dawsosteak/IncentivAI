After I submit my application, what happens next?
Your application will be reviewed by the Primary recruiter to see if your qualifications meet those posted for that position. If you meet the minimum qualifications, your information will be referred to the appropriate hiring authority/department.
What if I make a mistake on my application and need to correct it but I have already submitted it?
You may be able to resubmit your application if the position is still currently posted by contacting the primary recruiter, and requesting your current application for that position be marked as "incomplete", and denied accordingly (to allow you to reapply to the same position).   
  
Please allow time (more than 1 business day) for the primary recruiter to respond to your request to mark your application as incomplete.   
  
If you still desire to be considered for that postion, the updated application must be completed and submitted before the job posting closing date.
How do I check the status of my application?
To view the status of an application, review the “My Applications” section in the Candidate Home in Workday.
How long does it take to process my application?
It takes approximately 7 to 10 business days for processing. Positions that require passing an exam to qualify will necessitate additional processing time (scheduling and grading of the exam).   
  
(After submitting your application) Please allow a minimum of 3-5 business days once a job posting closes for your application to be reviewed and referred to the corresponding hiring authority within the sponsoring Department.
How will I be notified of my application status?
You will be notified by an email sent to the email address provided on your job application.
How long is my application active in Workday?
Your application will remain on file until the position is filled. One the position is filled; you will be notified on whether you were selected or not.
Do I have to fill out an application for every position?
If you have already applied for a position in Workday, you can use the “Use My Last Application” feature after you have clicked the apply button. This will utilize your saved application as the basis for each posting in which you are interested.
What steps can I take if I am notified that I do not qualify for a position and I would like to discuss the decision further?
You are welcome to contact the recruiter to follow up or contact: For Talent Acquisition: HR Talent at HRTalent@dallas.gov For Civil Service: civilserviceinfo@dallas.gov
​  

What types of positions are there?
There are Civil Service Positions, both classified and unclassified, as well as Non-Civil Service Positions. Full-time, Part-time, Seasonal, and internship positions are available.
What is a Classified Civil Service Position?
Positions that are regulated by the Code of Rules and Regulations of the Civil Service Board. They require certain established qualifications, and may include competitive exams.
What is an unclassified Civil Service Position?
Positions like directors, assistant directors, and other executive-level personnel, who may have different hiring or promotional standards.
What is a non-Civil Service Position?
These are roles that are not regulated by the Code of Rules and Regulations of the Civil Service Board, and include employees of departments listed in Chapter XVI of the City Charter. They may have different hiring, promotion, and administrative employment standards.
Can I withdraw my application after submitting it?
Yes, you can login to your candidate profile (on the City of Dallas Careers website) to view the applications for positions you have applied for (since February 15, 2024).   
  
For any active applications, you should log in to the Workday portal, navigate to your "Candidate Home" (external applicants) or "Jobs Hub" section (internal applicants), find the application you wish to withdraw, and then select the "Withdraw Application" option.
How do I print my application for my records?
  1. **Log in to Workday** : Go to the Workday login page for the organization you applied to.
​ 
  2. **Access Candidate Home** : Find and click on the "Candidate Home" page or your candidate profile link.
  3. **Go to Submitted Applications** : On your Candidate Home page, find the section for "My Submitted Applications".
  4. **Select the Application** : Click on the specific job application you want to print.
  5. **Find the PDF/Review Option:** Look for a "Review Document," "View PDF," or similar option to open a PDF version of your application. This is often located under your "Tasks" or in a blue menu.
  6. **Download and Print** : Once the PDF opens, you can then select the "Print" option from your web browser to print the application.  



Can I view positions I have previously applied for?
Yes, you can login to your candidate profile (on the City of Dallas Careers website) to view the applications for positions you have applied for (only since February 15, 2024, when the current Workday system became active).  
  
For current City of Dallas employees: You will access your internal applications by logging into Workday with your current City of Dallas username and password. Within Workday, you will access the menu (top, left-hand side) under Organization sub-heading, then click the jobs Hub. On the menu under jobs hub (left-hand side) select my applications and view the active and inactive job applications.
What if I am not ready to submit my application yet? Is my information saved for later?
If you are unready to submit your current application, please select the "Save For Later" option (near the bottom of the screen) to save your current progress for that application.   
  
Keep in mind, the application must be submitted before the job posting closes to be considered for the position.
Can I update my contact information after submitting an application?
To change your contact information in Workday, click on your email address in the top right corner and click “account settings”. After this you can choose to change your email or request to delete your information.
What happens if I lose my application information while applying?
Information can be saved during the application process on Workday. Navigation buttons like "back" or "refresh" can remove progress, so be sure to save periodically.
How do I know if my application was received by the City?
After you have submitted your online application, you will receive a confirmation email. You can also log into your Candidate Home account in Workday to check the status of your application.
What should I do if I need accommodations for a disability in the application process?
For potential applicants needing disability related assistance or with ADA Compliance concerns, please reach out to the ADA Manager (within the office of Equity & Inclusion) at 214-670-3247, or you may access the ADA manager through visiting the Office of Equity & Inclusion at Dallas City Hall located at the following address:
  
Dallas City Hall  
1500 Marilla St.  
Room 1B North  
Dallas, Texas 75201
If I qualify for one department, does that mean I am qualified for similar positions in other departments?
No. Departments may have special requirements for their specific position (e.g., language skills, special software, etc.). If no special requirements exist, all candidates meeting the posted requirements will be forwarded to the hiring authority.
Can I contact the hiring manager directly to follow up on my application?
Yes, a courtesy follow up email can be sent (to the Department directly), or the hiring manager in reference to submitted application.  
  
For a listing of current City of Dallas Departments and Offices, please reference the following website link: https://dallascityhall.com/departments/Pages/default.aspx
Are there resources available for public access to computers or internet for completing my application?
Free internet access is available for you to apply for City positions. Locations include the City of Dallas public library branches (contact the library for hours of operation) and the employment centers located at City Hall, 1500 Marilla Street (Room 1CS), Dallas (open Monday – Friday from 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.).
What should I do if I forgot my account login or password?
For external applicants-You can click on the forgot your password link, from the login page on the City of Dallas Careers site.   
  
For internal applicants (current City of Dallas employees) you will need to use your current City of Dallas login credentials (username and password), and reset your password through the [password reset and enrollment website](https://dallascityhall.com/passwordreset/Pages/default.aspx), or contact the Help desk at (214) 670-1234.
How do I apply for a position?
Depending on the position, you will apply using Workday or NeoGov.   
  
Note: Tested positions (entailing employment-related tests), and Uniform positions (Police & Fire positions are listed on NeoGov).  
  
The links on the City of Dallas - [Employment Opportunities website](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/humanresources/employment/Pages/default.aspx) will direct you to the appropriate place to apply.
Where do I apply for City of Dallas jobs?
Most positions are posted in our Workday system. You'll find the application link on each job posting on our Employment Opportunities page. Some specialized positions (like those requiring written exams or uniformed roles) use a different system called NEOGOV—the job posting will direct you to the right place.
I don't have access to a computer or internet. How can I apply?
Visit any City of Dallas public library or stop by our Employment Center at Dallas City Hall, Room 1CS (open Monday–Friday, 8am–5pm). Our staff can help you access the online application system.
Can I reuse my information when applying for multiple jobs?
Yes! In Workday, click "Use My Last Application" when applying for a new position, then update any details specific to that role before submitting.
How do I check my application status?
Sign into Workday and go to:  
  
External applicants: Candidate Home → My Applications  
Current City employees: Jobs Hub → My Applications  
  
For positions requiring exams, you may receive status updates via email or the NEOGOV system.
What happens after I submit my application?
Our recruiting team reviews applications to ensure candidates meet the minimum qualifications. Qualified applicants are then referred to the hiring department for interviews and selection.
How long does the initial review take?
Plan for about 7–10 business days after the posting closes for the initial screening. If the position requires an exam, it may take a bit longer.
How will I be notified about next steps?
We'll email you at the address you provided on your application. For positions requiring exams, you'll receive separate instructions from our Civil Service team.
Will I be notified if I'm not selected?
Yes. You'll receive an email when the position is filled or when you're no longer being considered.
I made a mistake after submitting. Can I fix it?
Workday doesn't allow you to edit a submitted application. If the posting is still open, you can withdraw your application and resubmit with the correct information. Go to My Applications and select "Withdraw Application."
The posting has closed—can I still make changes?
No. Once a posting closes, you cannot submit a new application or edit an existing one. Please review your application carefully before the deadline.
What types of positions require exams?
Some roles require you to pass a competitive written, skills, or physical exam. These include certain clerical positions (like Office Assistant), some Code Enforcement and 911 roles, and public safety trainee positions. Job postings will clearly indicate if an exam is required.
How do I know if a job requires an exam before I apply?
Exam requirements are noted on the job posting. These positions typically route you to the NEOGOV system for application and testing.
How will I be scheduled for an exam?
After your application is reviewed, our Civil Service team will email you with exam instructions and scheduling details.
What should I bring on exam day?
Arrive early with a valid photo ID. Your exam notice will tell you what to bring and what items are prohibited. Exams are given in a proctored environment.
What happens after I pass an exam?
Passing the exam typically places you on an eligibility list. Hiring departments use these lists to select candidates, often in rank order based on exam scores.
How long does the full hiring process take?
The timeline varies by position. Generally:  
  
• Initial screening: 7–10 business days after the posting closes  
• Department interviews: Timing varies by department  
• Background checks and onboarding: Additional time after an offer is made  
  
Positions requiring exams typically take longer due to test scheduling, administration, and scoring.
Why is my application taking longer than expected?
Positions requiring exams have additional steps. Hiring timelines also vary by department based on their interview and selection processes.
Who do I contact for accommodations during the application or testing process?
​If you need accommodations such as alternate formats, interpreters, or other assistance, please visit the City’s ADA Compliance page, now managed by the Office of Housing & Community Empowerment:  
  

[https://dallascityhall.com/departments/fairhousing/Pages/ADA-Compliance.aspx](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/fairhousing/Pages/ADA-Compliance.aspx)  

  

I have other questions about my application. Who can help?
For specific questions about your application status or the hiring process, please refer to the contact information provided in your application confirmation email or job posting.
