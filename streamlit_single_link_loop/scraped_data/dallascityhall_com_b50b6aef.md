# Changes to Fee Structure
As of October 2025, the fee payment process has returned to an early-fee collection approach. This reduces the number of payment transactions and reduces time in the overall permitting process.   
  

  
**Why is the fee structure process being changed?  
** Prior to the launch of _DallasNow_ , all plan review and permit fees were collected at the beginning of the process. Since the launch of our new software, fees begin being collected at three separate stages. We have received developer feedback indicating a strong preference for fewer collection points, as each payment adds additional time to the overall process. PDD will implement a change to return to an early-fee collection approach similar to the pre-launch process, reducing the number of transactions.
**What does this fee structure change mean?  
** This fee update will move the collection of the most the common commercial permit fees to end of the pre-screen phase, reducing the overall number of payment transactions customers will need to make during the review process.
**What record types are affected by this update?  
** This update will be applied to the following record types:  
- Commercial New Construction Permit  
- Commercial Alteration Addition Permit  
- Commercial Drive Approach and Sidewalk Permit  
- Commercial Pool/Spa Permit  
- Commercial Solar/PV Permit  
- Commercial Accessory Structure Permit  
- Commercial Electrical Permit  
- Commercial Mechanical Permit  
- Commercial Plumbing Permit  
- Commercial Foundation Repair  
- Commercial Roofing Permit  
- Commercial Demolition Permit
**Who does this affect?**  
The commercial development community will be affected by this update directly, but the benefits of this update will also be felt by those not directly involved in the process to obtain a commercial building permit.
**What are the benefits of this fee structure change?  
** This fee streamlining update will result in the following benefits for customers:  
- Reduced number of payment collection points  
- Improved record issuance time  
- Improved fee transparency
**Will this also affect Residential records?  
** At this time, this affects a select number of Commercial records to start. Residential permits will be streamlined in the future.
