  1. Online Courses 
Gale Courses offers a wide range of highly interactive, instructor led courses that you can take entirely online.
[Link to page](http://education.gale.com/l-anaheim_main/)
  2. Research Databases 
Search the library's many magazine, journal, and news sources for free and easy access to full-text articles for research or homework. Try the power search feature to search several databases at once. 
[Link to page](https://www.anaheim.net/1105/Research-and-Learning)
  3. Language Learning 
The library offers online language learning resources for all ages.
[Link to page](https://www.anaheim.net/2682/Language-Learning)
  4. What to Read 
Use various lists, guides, and resources to find that next great read.
[Link to page](https://www.anaheim.net/991/What-to-Read)
  5. Early Literacy 
Early Literacy programs help to build a foundation for reading through a set of positive interactions, giving children the building blocks for language, reading and writing development.
[Link to page](https://www.anaheim.net/1045/Early-Literacy)
  6. Local History 
The Heritage Center has an inventory of almost a million items organized and cataloged for use by scholars, students and researchers in the Disney Resort Reading Room. The center is located at Carnegie Plaza in the Anaheim Muzeo complex. Heritage Services also oversees the historic Mother Colony House and Woelke-Stoffel Victorian on the Founders' Park site. 
[Link to page](https://www.anaheim.net/2474/Heritage-Services)
  7. About the Library 
There are ten Library branches serving the City of Anaheim and surrounding communities, including the Anaheim Heritage Center which houses our local history collections, and a new self-service branch at ARTIC transportation center. We also have a Bookmobile that serves the Community Development Block Grant neighborhoods in the city.
[Link to page](https://www.anaheim.net/1222/About-the-Library)
