#  Nohl Canyon Water Storage Tank 
Project Summary  
Anaheim Public Utilities continually works with local and regional water agencies to make sure that we all have reliable water supplies today and for future generations.  

![View from above Nohl Canyon water storage tank](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=2493)
The Nohl Canyon Water Storage Tank - a planned 10-million gallon, above-ground drinking water storage tank constructed on the site of the existing Olive Hills Reservoir - is one critically important project that improves water quality and service reliability to our community.  
  
The storage tank helps meet customer demand for water, particularly during the typically hot, dry, high-demand summer months. It also: 
  * Delivers water by gravity flow to a wide area in the event of power outages
  * Meets the high water flows required to fight fires
  * Provides emergency storage to help supply water during short-term service interruptions from Metropolitan Water District of Southern California

### Quick Facts
  
  
For more than three decades, this site housed a 60-million gallon open reservoir at adjacent Olive Hills.  
  
It has since been replaced with a 10-million gallon, partially-buried, drinking water storage tank which improves water quality and service reliability to our community.  
  
A water storage tank at this elevation helps maintain adequate water pressure to deliver water to central Anaheim by gravity. It provides critical short-term redundancy that ensures continued service when our imported water from MWD’s supply source is interrupted.  
  
This facility includes a leak detection system and can withstand a major earthquake.  
  
Dedicated in 2011.
