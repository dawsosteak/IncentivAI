#  Veterans Tribute 
The city of Anaheim is proud to recognize the brave men and women of the armed forces through active service recognition and honoring of Anaheim veterans.
  
Anaheim Veterans Day Celebration
Veterans from all branches are honored in Anaheim on a special Veterans Day celebration hosted by the city of Anaheim. The ceremony is held at the city's Veterans Monument in downtown Anaheim. The public is invited to join us nd pay tribute to the brave men and women who willingly served our country.
For more information on city events please visit our [events calendar](https://www.anaheim.net/Calendar.aspx).

## Anaheim Community Events
  

Each year, Anaheim honors our veterans and active military personnel at special events including:
  * Memorial Day
  * Flag Day parade
  * Fourth of July parade
  * Veterans Day celebration
  * Military banner ceremony
  * Yellow ribbon experience


For more on city events please visit our [events calendar](https://www.anaheim.net/Calendar.aspx).
  

## Anaheim Veterans Gallery
  

The Anaheim Veterans Gallery honors veterans who submit a personal photo and a brief story of their service to our country. To be part of the Anaheim Veterans Gallery please fill out our online [Veterans Gallery application form](https://www.anaheim.net/FormCenter/City-Administration-10/Veterans-Gallery-Application-Form-75).
  

## Anaheim Veterans Monument
  

The Anaheim Veterans Monument is next to the Muzeo in downtown Anaheim at 241 S. Anaheim Blvd. Anaheim's 9-foot high Veterans Monument was dedicated in 1999 and designed by Anaheim artist Richard Turner. The monument, made from bronze and concrete, depicts all branches of the military from wars of the 20th century. Turner's research in the Anaheim Library led him to include images depicting actual Anaheim residents. The monument is flanked by fountains, representative of water washing away years of conflict.
## Military Banner Program
  

The [Military Banner Program](https://www.anaheim.net/1681/Military-Banner-Program) is designed to honor Anaheim residents that are currently deployed in our military. Active military personnel are honored and recognized through a city dedicated webpage, and with a banner displayed on a streetlight pole in various locations throughout Anaheim to show the city's public expression of gratitude. The city has proudly recognized 72 honorees since the inception of the program.
  

## Honor Fallen Heroes
  

The city honors Anaheim residents who served in the military that gave the ultimate sacrifice to protect our country. In respect for our [fallen heroes](https://www.anaheim.net/1703/Fallen-Heroes), Anaheim pays special tribute at the Military Banner Ceremony and honors them with a military banner in each of three designated banner locations. In addition, the city honors these fallen heroes by recognizing them on a dedicated City webpage.
  

## Purple Heart City
  

February 2014, the Anaheim City Council proclaimed Anaheim a Purple Heart City. The Purple Heart is given to military members who are wounded or killed in combat. Cities are eligible for recognition under the national Military Order of the Purple Heart. California became the nation’s first Purple Heart State in 2012, and Orange County is poised to become a Purple Heart County in 2013.
