  * I have a problem or question about a tree on my property. Can someone help me?
You can email CODForestry@dallas.gov or find a certified arborist at [**Trees are Good**](https://www.treesaregood.org/findanarborist).  

  * When is planting season?
The best time to plant a tree in our area is from November 1- March 1. This gives the tree root system enough time to grow and establish before our intense heat hits!  

  * Does the City of Dallas give away free trees?
Yes, the City of Dallas has several programs. Branch Out Dallas is an annual event that provides residents with a free tree for their front, back or side yard. The Neighborhood Reforestation program provides trees for streets, parkways and medians. For more information, visit [**Programs**](https://dallascityhall.com/projects/forestry/Pages/tree-plantings.aspx).  

  * What do I do after a storm?
A well-cared-for, healthy tree can survive winter or summer storms and minor damage. Surprisingly, “well-cared-for” does not mean constant pruning. In fact, pruning causes stress for the tree and the wound left behind is a wound that never heals. What do trees need more than anything else in the world? Water!   

As always, call a certified arborist to assess your tree and assist in making an informed decision on next steps. To find an arborist certified by the International Society of Arboriculture, visit [**Trees are Good**](https://www.treesaregood.org/findanarborist), click “Find a Professional” and search by state, city or zip code.
  
  

  

  * Natural Disasters-Can I get a tree for my yard?
​With threat of tree loss due to Natural Deforestation Events, such as storms or Emerald Ash borer, private property affected by these events may qualify for a free tree if:
    * Located within a declared federal, state, or local disaster area
    * Declared eligible for reforestation by to city council: or
    * Confirmed by the director to have contained vulnerable or threatened tree species on the property, as specified by the Texas Department of Agriculture, and was in imminent threat of infestation.
Contact Planning and Development Department, Arborist Office at 214-948-4205 for more information!  

  * Do I need a permit to remove a tree in my yard?
​For single-family homes under 2 acres, a tree removal permit is not required unless the tree is in the right-of-way (ROW). If the tree is within the ROW, please contact the District Arborists to assess the tree conditions and potential for removal.  

  

  * What trees are not protected?
​Any tree that is less than 8-inches caliper and is designated as "Unprotected" including Callery pear (all cultivars), Chinaberry, Chinese tallow, Ilex species (except for yaupon or Possumhaw), Tree-Of-Heaven or Ailanthus, other trees listed as invasive plants, trees with a diameter of less than 10-inches at the point on the trunk and 4.5 feet above the ground, located on a lot with an existing single-family or duplex use that is occupied at the time of removal.  

  * Do I need a permit to remove a tree?
​If the diameter of the tree is 8-inches or greater and the property does not have a single-family or duplex use on less than 2 acres, then a building permit or tree removal permit is required.
  

  * Can I remove or have trees removed from my parkway?
​Trees in the parkway are considered city property. However, maintenance and removal of the tree is the adjacent property owner's responsibility. Please contact your District Arborist to determine if the tree can be removed.  

  * What is needed on a tree survey and does the survey need to be prepared by an official surveyor?
​The tree survey does not need to be prepared by an official surveyor or licensed individual. It must include the location, diameter, and species name (common and scientific) of all trees 8-inches or greater in diameter, regardless of whether the tree is proposed to be removed or to remain. The survey should also include the date, scale, north arrow, the name(s), address(es), and phone number of the property owner. It should also include the name of the person preparing the survey, the location of existing boundary or property lines, a vicinity map, and the location of existing and proposed streets, alleys and utility easements. Additionally, including the condition of the trees on the survey will help the District Arborists to better assess if mitigation is required for the removal.  

  * Do I need to plant a tree the same size as the tree I removed?
#### ​You do not need to plant the same size tree that is removed. However, mitigation is on an inch-for-inch basis. Replacement trees must be a minimum of 2-inch caliper. For example, if you remove a 10-inch protected tree, then you must replace 10-inches, and you could do it with five 2-inch replacement trees. 
  * Can street trees count as site trees?
​Street trees can count as site trees if they are located on the property.   

  * When does required landscaping have to be completed?
​Prior to the issuance of a final certificate of occupancy.
  

  * Does a demolition permit allow me to remove trees?
​No. Trees can only be removed with a building permit or tree removal permit. A tree removal permit must be applied for in conjunction with a grading only or grading & paving permit. 
  

  * Do I need to have my landscape inspected?
​Yes, if the landscape is required as part of a building permit, planned development, specific use permit, certificate of occupancy, or board of adjustment case, it must be inspected.
If the landscape work you are doing is completely voluntary and not a part of any city requirement, it does not need to be inspected.
  

  * How can I find out more about wildlife in the City of Dallas?
​Check out this website for information on coyotes, bobcats, racoons and much more!  

[**Urban Wildlife in Dallas**](https://bedallas90.org/wildlife/)  

  * I want to volunteer!
​You can either check out our Events page, or email us at CODForestry@dallas.gov with a short description of what you want to do, and we'll send you information!  

  

  * Who is responsible for trees in my alleyway?
​The adjacent property owner is responsible for any vegetation in an alleyway, to the middle of that alleyway.  

Any vegetation over 12-inches in height is prohibited in an alleyway, which includes trees.  

  * Who is responsible fo trees on streets or parkways?
​Tree inbetween the sidewalk and back of curb, or along the street, are the responsibility of the adjacent property owner.  

  * What's that blue tag?
​If you see a small blue round metal tag on a public tree, that means that this significant ash tree is being treated to protect from the Emerald Ash Borer (EAB). EAB beetle can kill ash trees, so the City of Dallas is treating significant specimens to preserve the species.  

![ash tree Tagging.png](https://dallascityhall.com/projects/forestry/PublishingImages/Lists/DCH%20Faq/AllItems/ash%20tree%20Tagging.png)  

  * Coyotes
​Report a sighting to 311.  

If you live near an active area, here are a few things to do to keep coyotes away:  

    * Remove all outdoor pet food
    * Keep outdoor trash bins secure and shut  

    * Keep pets inside or on a leash or harness whenever they're outside  

    * Haze coyotes away from homes and businesses  

  

  * How do I report a suspected illegal tree removal?
​Report an illegal tree removal by calling 311 or using the Dallas 311 mobile app.
