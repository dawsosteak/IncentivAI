  * How often does the City Plan and Zoning Commission meet?
The City Plan and Zoning Commission meets Bi-Weekly.  

  * What does a rezoning sign mean?
Rezoning signs are posted on the subject property of a site that has applied for rezoning. Scan the QR code on the sign for more information, or enter the case number (e.g., Z-25-000___) in the search field in [_DallasNow_](https://aca-prod.accela.com/DALLASTX/Default.aspx) (no registration is required).​​  

  * What does a letter in the mail mean?
The map in your letter shows the subject property in the center with diagonal crosshatching. If your property isn't crosshatched, you received notice because you own property within the required notification buffer. A brief description is included in the notice. For more information, enter the case number (e.g., Z-25-000___) in the search field on [_DallasNow_](https://aca-prod.accela.com/DALLASTX/Default.aspx) (no registration is required). Most zoning cases are submitted by private property owners to change the permitted uses or development standards (height, density, setbacks) on their own property.​  
​  

  * Who receives letters for zoning cases?
In accordance with City Code and State Law, the City mails notices to all property owners in the surrounding area. The area notified varies from 200' to 500' depending on the size of the area to be rezoned and the type of request. The mailing addresses of owners are based on the current year mailing address from the County tax office. Anyone not receiving letters at their proper address may need to contact their County tax office to update their mailing info.​  

  * How should I respond to a letter about a zoning case?
If you received a letter in a mail, is to return that letter in the mail with the enclosed instructions. Written comments will be counted and made available to decision makers. Please return the original copy of the paper form.​  

  * When is the CPC Briefing and Meeting?
The CPC Briefing usually begins at **9:00 a.m,** the public meeting usually begins at **12:30 p.m.**
**Important:** These times are subject to change. You should refer to the agenda HERE (https://cityofdallas.legistar.com/Calendar.aspx) to confirm the start time.​  

  

  * What happens at the briefing?
During the CPC Briefing, the public can observe while City staff provide information about the afternoon's CPC cases and answer questions from the Commissioners. Only staff and CPC speak at this briefing, and the subdivision docket is not briefed.​  

  * What happens at the public hearing?
  

    * The Chair will announce zoning items on the consent agenda and items that have been removed from it.
    * Consent agenda items are read, and a final call for speakers is made. If there are no speakers, these items are acted upon in one motion. If speakers are identified, those items are moved off the consent agenda and addressed individually later.
    * Remaining agenda items are read individually, typically in numerical order. The Chair will ask for speakers and call the names of those who registered online.
    * The published agenda order may be reordered during the meeting, so it's advised to follow along closely.
    * After speakers, a motion to approve, deny, or hold under advisement will be made, seconded, and discussed by Commissioners
    * During the discussion portion after a motion, additional questions for the staff and the speakers may occur.
    * When the discussion and questions are complete, the motion is voted on. If the vote fails, a new motion will be made, and the process repeats until a final recommendation is established. ​  

  

  * I am interested in a case on the agenda and I might want to speak. How do I participate?
**Register as a speaker** before the deadline using the form [HERE](https://www.google.com/search?q=https://dallascityhall.com/government/citysecretary/Pages/register-to-speak.aspx). Registration is required for online participation, but not for in-person participation (though it's appreciated).
**If you want to speak:**
    * **In-person:** Raise your hand when the Chair asks for speakers on the relevant item.
    * **Online:** You must register before the deadline and ensure your camera and microphone are working (required by state law).  
  
**If you agree with the staff recommendation:**
    * **Consent agenda item:** If you want to speak, it will be taken off consent. If you want it to remain on consent, you can fill out a yellow card and drop it off at the podium to record your presence in the hearing's minutes.
    * **Not on consent agenda, no speakers in opposition:** You can speak or wait to answer questions or rebut opposing speakers if you are the applicant or their representative.
    * **Special note for online participation:** You must register before the deadline and ensure your camera and microphone work. If you are the applicant, it is strongly recommended that you attend the meeting in person to respond to the Commission's questions and address opposing speakers' comments.​  

​​​  

  * How long do I have to speak?
Each person is allotted a maximum of **three minutes** to speak (sometimes less if there are many speakers). If you are coordinating with other speakers, plan your speeches to cover all your points collectively. The applicant will have an opportunity to rebut opposing speakers.​  

  * Can I provide a presentation or handouts?
Yes, however your three minutes include setup, getting to the podium, and passing out handouts.
    * **Presentations (in-person):** Email a PDF to Jorge.Mendoza@dallas.gov and Liliana.Lopez@dallas.gov and they will load it onto the presentation computer.
    * **Handouts (in-person):** Clearly write the case number on the top page, bring 20 copies, and hand them to staff for distribution when the case is read into the record.​  

  
​  

  * How can my attendance be noted in the meeting minutes?
Fill out a yellow card and drop it off in the box at the podium. You do not have to speak to be noted as in attendance.​  

  * I cannot participate in the meeting. Is there another way to make my voice heard?
Yes! Please contact the case planner and your District's Commissioner. For more information, enter the case number (e.g., Z-25-000___) in the search field on [_DallasNow_](https://aca-prod.accela.com/DALLASTX/Default.aspx) (no registration is required), look up the CPC Member List on the [CPC Resources](https://dallascityhall.com/government/Boards-and-Commissions/City-Plan-and-Zoning-Commission/Pages/resources.aspx) page, or call 214-670-4209.​  

  * How can I watch the meeting?
    * **Live viewing:** Refer to the posted agenda [HERE](https://www.google.com/search?q=https://www.dallascityhall.com/government/citysecretary/Pages/City-Council-Agendas.aspx) or select “Join Webinar" [HERE](https://dallascityhall.com/government/Boards-and-Commissions/City-Plan-and-Zoning-Commission/Pages/Meetings.aspx).
    * **Recording:** Go to the City Meetings Video Player [HERE](https://www.google.com/search?q=https://dallas.legistar.com/Calendar.aspx) and scroll to "Boards and Commissions" to find the desired recording.​
