### **Affordable Housing Policy**
On August 14, 2018, the City Council adopted an Affordable Housing Policy affirming that affordable housing is a priority in the City of Anaheim in response to the housing affordability crisis. The Policy encourages dialogue between City staff and all developers of upcoming rental and for-sale housing proposed in Anaheim to consider options and approaches for addressing the City' s affordable housing needs, as set forth in the Housing Element of the City's General Plan, and other documents that guide the City's affordable housing goals.
Recognizing that there can be financial implications associated with the delivery of affordable housing, the City Council also adopted an Affordable Housing Action Plan in conjunction with the Policy ([Click here to view the Policy and Action Plan](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/23785/RES-2018-106-Affordable-Housing-Policy---Signed-and-Replaced-Scans)). The Action Plan includes items related to the following:
  * Regulatory Relief - Reduce bureaucracy by providing relief from regulations that create barriers to the development of affordable housing.
  * Process Incentives - Develop an affordable housing incentive program that reduces costs and/or development time during the permitting process.
  * Middle Income Housing - Develop a middle-income housing program that promotes and incentivizes entry-level rental and for-sale affordable housing for individuals earning middle-class incomes.
  * Affordable Housing Ambassador Program - Develop an affordable housing ambassador program where the City would designate a staff person to promote and facilitate the development of affordable housing in Anaheim.
  * Affordable Housing Development Funding - Ensure that the City remains competitive to receive State and Federal funding, leverages the Anaheim Housing Authority' s remaining resources and assets to maximize the production of affordable housing, and encourages the State to adopt and pursue funding sources for affordable housing that can replace those lost through the elimination of redevelopment agencies.


### **Inclusionary Housing Requirements for New For-Rent Residential Projects**
On October 29, 2024, the City Council adopted the [Inclusionary Housing Ordinance No. 6593](https://records.anaheim.net/CityClerk/DocView.aspx?id=5538865) to establish an affordable housing requirement for new, for-rent residential development projects with over 35 units. This ordinance established [Chapter 17.40 - Inclusionary Housing](https://codelibrary.amlegal.com/codes/anaheim/latest/anaheim_ca/0-0-0-85552) of the Anaheim Municipal Code and outlines the requirements and alternative compliance options including, but not limited, to payment of an in-lieu fee. [Resolution No. 2024-126](https://records.anaheim.net/CityClerk/DocView.aspx?id=5575892) established an inclusionary housing in-lieu fee schedule that will be deposited into the Anaheim Local Housing Trust Fund (A-LTHF) to expand housing opportunities and address some of the City's affordable housing challenges. The Inclusionary Housing Ordinance is one solution representing a key component of the City of Anaheim's broader affordable housing development strategy. View the [2021-2029 Housing Element of the Anaheim General Plan](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/65020/) for more information on the City's goals, policies, and programs related to housing. For more information on the Inclusionary Housing Ordinance or other related initiatives, contact staff within the [Affordable Housing Development Division](https://www.anaheim.net/780/) and the [Planning Services Division](https://anaheim.net/691/Planning-Zoning). 
### **Anaheim Local Housing Trust Fund**
On August 20, 2024, the City Council adopted the Anaheim Local Housing Trust Fund [Ordinance No. 6588](https://records.anaheim.net/CityClerk/DocView.aspx?id=5378814) which established the Anaheim Local Housing Trust Fund (A-LHTF). The A-LHTF is designed to receive and invest public and private contributions, including the local in-lieu fees, state and federal housing grants, and philanthropic donations, to implement strategic housing solutions. 
On May 29, 2025, the City Council adopted the [Anaheim Local Housing Trust Guidelines and Fund Allocation Plan](https://anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/66427/). To address these needs, the A-LHTF will invest in three primary program areas:
  * **Build More Homes Initiative:** Funds the development and rehabilitation of affordable rental and for-sale housing to increase the city's long-term supply.
  * **First-Time Homeowners Program:** Offers financial support for low- and moderate-income households to purchase their first home in Anaheim.
  * **Housing Stability Program:** Provides one-time emergency assistance to prevent displacement of low-income seniors and families at risk of eviction or homelessness.


For more information on the A-LHTF or other related initiatives, contact staff within the [Affordable Housing Development Division](https://www.anaheim.net/780/).
