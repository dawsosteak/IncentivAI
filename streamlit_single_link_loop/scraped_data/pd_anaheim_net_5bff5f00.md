#  Property & Evidence 
Property and Evidence is responsible for receiving, storing, and maintaining items obtained as evidence during the course of an investigation, as well as storing found property and property turned in for safekeeping.
**When is Property & Evidence open? **
Property and Evidence is open Monday through Friday from 8 am to 5 pm, excluding weekends and holidays.
Property is released on Tuesday through Thursday between 3 pm and 5 pm, excluding weekends and holidays. An appointment must be made to pick up items. Please call 714-765-1849 to schedule an appointment.
When you arrive at the Main Station, located at 425 S Harbor Boulevard, please enter the front lobby. You do not need to wait in line. Instead, you will find a phone located at the lobby and follow the prompts for property pick up.
**How does a citizen get their property back?**
  * Release of items held as Evidence:
    * You **must** contact the investigator assigned to the case and request a release. If the investigator authorizes the release, you can contact Evidence and Property at 714-765-1849 to schedule an appointment. You must have your report number and a valid photo ID when picking up your property.
    * Evidence can only be claimed by the person named on the release; letters will **not** be accepted.
  * Release of items held for Safekeeping:
    * Property **must** be claimed within 90 days, or it will be destroyed. In order to retrieve items being held in Safekeeping, the owner must present a valid photo ID. The owner can designate another person to pick up the property for them; however, the designated person must have a signed letter authorizing them to pick up the property. The letter must include the name of the person authorized to pick up the property, description of the property, case number, and the signature of the property owner (signed and printed). In addition, the authorized person picking up the items **must** have a valid photo ID.
    * Extensions can be granted in 30-day increments by sending a letter or calling Property and Evidence requesting an extension.
  * Release of Firearms held for Safekeeping:
    * There are specific guidelines / procedures for firearms with **no exceptions**. For firearm inquiries and releases, please contact Mr. Joseph Hockenberry at 714-765-1706.
  * Release of cash / currency held for Safekeeping:
    * Please contact Ms. Maria Vega Velez at 714-765-1589 or Ms. Nicole Rapp at 714-765-1537. A letter from the owner is **not** accepted when picking up cash. Cash will only be returned to the owner, unless otherwise specified by the officer or detective assigned to the case.
  * Release of bicycles held for Safekeeping:
    * Please contact Property and Evidence at 714-765-1849 to schedule an appointment to pick up bicycles, scooters or any other motorized bikes. Keep in mind that due to the size of the items, it may be stored at an off-site location.


**How does a citizen file a claim for property they find?**
To claim property that is found, you need to file a Finders' Claim form, which you get at the Police Department Main Station, located at 425 S Harbor Boulevard. When you arrive at the main station, let the front counter Cadets know that you would like a Finder's Claim form (APD Form 360 and APD 360-A), fill it out, and give it to the Cadets. Make sure you give the Cadets your current address and phone number.
The process requires the property to be held for 90 days. After the ninety (90) days have passed, you may contact Property and Evidence at 714-765-1849 with the GO number and property report number to inquire about the property. If the property has not been claimed, a Property and Evidence Technician will guide you through the remaining steps of the process, which include running an ad in a local paper / online publication for 7 continuous days with specific information.
