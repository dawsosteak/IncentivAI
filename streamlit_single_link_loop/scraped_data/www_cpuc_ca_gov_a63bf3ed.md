#  Strategic Energy Management (SEM)
SEM was launched in 2018 ([D.16-08-019](https://docs.cpuc.ca.gov/PublishedDocs/Published/G000/M166/K232/166232537.PDF)). SEM involves holistic, long-term customer engagement**,** and creates multi-year, phased
[expanded](https://pda.energydataweb.com/#!/documents/3978/view) to non-industrial market sectors including commercial, agriculture, and multi-family, if the current SEM guidebooks are followed. The[D.23-02-002),](https://docs.cpuc.ca.gov/PublishedDocs/Published/G000/M501/K931/501931085.PDF) supports this expansion, while allowing application of the program savings parameters listed below.
In SEM, regression models and existing condition baselines are used to estimate savings from all program activities at a customer facility. SEM activities include capital projects, behavioral, retro-commissioning, operational (BRO) measures, and promotes customer knowledge of integrating demand side management (IDSM) measures.
If the prescribed program design and guidebooks are followed, eligible project savings will be based on: 
  * Net-to-Gross Ratio of 1 
  * Existing Conditions Baseline
  * 5-year Effective Useful Life (EUL)
