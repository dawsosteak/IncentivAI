#  Link Labs Policies 
The Link Labs is a public space where library patrons of all ages can create and connect. The Link Labs comprises of two rooms: the Link Makerspace and the Link Media Lab. Each is housed with advanced equipment and ample space for patrons to work on projects. 
## Who can access the labs? 
Stations in the Link Labs are available by reservation-only to all Anaheim Public Library card holders 13 & up. Parent or Legal Guardian consent is required for patrons ages 13-17. Children 12 and under must always be accompanied by a parent or guardian at the same station in the labs; children cannot use a station by themselves. The computers in this space are for Link Labs projects only; patrons needing computers for general use may utilize the Adult, Teen, and Children's computer labs as appropriate. 
## DIY: Do It Yourself 
All stations in the Link Labs are self-service after completion of the corresponding orientation. Staff may provide guidance on using equipment or consultation on projects, but staff will not complete projects or perform tasks in place of the patron. The patron has sole creative control over the project and accepts full responsibility of labor. 
### Copyrighted Materials 
Projects in the Link Labs may be worked on in the labs under the assumption that the content is owned by the patron, used with permission, or under fair use. The library reserves the right to stop a patron’s reservation if it is determined that content being worked on is in violation of U.S. copyright law or otherwise illegal. City of Anaheim is not liable for content produced by patrons using library resources. Additionally, as the library is a public space, patrons using the labs may not require staff or other patrons in the library to sign Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs). 
### Release of Liability 
Patrons using the Link Labs agree that City of Anaheim, its employees, instructors, and volunteers are released of liability from damage, loss, or injury to a patron or patron’s personal belongings. 
## Library Rules of Conduct 
In addition to Anaheim Public Library's Rules of Conduct, Computer Use, and Public Internet Use Policy, all patrons in the Link Labs are subject to lab-specific rules. These rules include, but are not limited to the following: 
  * Food and drinks are NOT allowed in labs, to ensure that equipment and media are kept clean and undamaged. Beverages in non-spillable containers are permitted.
  * Respect the privacy and space of other patrons using the labs.
  * Keep equipment, materials, and personal belongings within the designated work station.
  * Projects worked on in the lab may not include obscene or otherwise inappropriate for the library environment. Media used in a fine art or historical context may be allowed per the discretion of staff.


Patrons may refer to the full Library Rules of Conduct for more information. 
## Orientations 
Patrons reserving a station in the Link Labs for the first time must complete the Orientation to the Link Labs as well as an orientation for the corresponding equipment, regardless of skill level. Orientations may be completed online in advance. Otherwise, in-person orientations are scheduled per staff availability and may be conducted during the first 15-30 minutes of a reservation. 
### Equipment Agreement Form 
The patron making the reservation must sign an Equipment Agreement Form prior to using the labs for the first time. The reserving patron is responsible for equipment used by all guests on the reservation. Damage to equipment due to negligence or poor conduct may result in a suspension of Link Labs privileges. 
### Badging 
Patrons will earn a “badge” for each orientation they complete. These badges are recorded in a database specific to Link Labs, and completed badges will allow a patron to reserve the corresponding station. 
## Reservations 
Reservations may be made at least 24 hours in advance by contacting the Link Labs during library hours. Same-day reservations must be made in person, and subject to availability. Patrons may reserve a station for two (2) hours per reservation per day. Group reservations will be evaluated on a case-by-case basis; otherwise, reservations are one person per station. 
### Sign In 
Sign in with lab staff to begin the reservation and check out equipment. The primary patron who made the reservation must be present for the entire duration and is responsible for the equipment and conduct of other patrons on the reservation. Cancellations must be made prior to reservation time by contacting the Link Labs during lab hours. A 15-minute grace period is allowed for late arrival without notice, after which the reservation will be considered a "No-show" and cancelled. Repeated “No-shows” may result in a suspension of lab privileges until the responsible patron re-takes the orientation. 
### Equipment Kits 
Every station reservation includes all related equipment and hand tools. These kits are checked out to the library card holder making the reservation. They are to be checked back in and inspected by staff at the end of every reservation. These kits are for use in the lab spaces only. If the Makerspace or its equipment is found to be damaged or missing when a patron is finished using it, that patron may be subject to a replacement cost. 
### Sign Out 
Sign out with staff once completing the reservation. All equipment and unused materials must be returned to their original location or returned to staff prior to ending the reservation. Stations frequently left in disarray may result in a suspension of lab privileges until the responsible patron re-takes the orientation. 
## Makerspace 
The Makerspace is an open, collaborative workshop equipped with technology for fabricating physical products, including 3D printers, sewing machines, vinyl cutting machines, laser cutter and engraver, and sublimation heat presses. 
### Dress Code 
The Makerspace is a work designated area. For safety, all visitors in the lab must wear closed-toed flat shoes, long hair must be tied back, and no loose clothing or dangling jewelry may be worn. 
### Materials 
The Link Makerspace has a select amount of materials available free to use for patrons. Monthly limits may apply. Patrons may bring in their own materials that are on the staff-approved list. Other materials may be denied use in the lab unless or until staff can verify its safe handling. Please see staff complete list of approved materials. 
### Production limits 
Projects may only be worked on during allotted reservation time. The Link Makerspace does not conduct overnight prints. The Link Makerspace is also not equipped for high volume production. Staff can refer patrons to a commercial facility for larger runs. 
The Link Makerspace may not be used to produce prohibited items including, but not limited to: functioning weapons, drug paraphernalia, or items that display graphic/obscene content. 
## Media Lab 
The Media Lab is a public space for audio, video, and photo production. The lab is equipped with a recording booth, green screen, photo/video camera, teleprompter, three-point lighting set up, as well as an editing bay fitted with an Apple Mac computer and professional creative software. This room also houses the Memory Lab. 
### Recording 
The Media Lab is a DIY space. Staff will set up library equipment for reservations but will not operate equipment for patron projects. 
### Set-up 
The Media Lab provides only the equipment as listed on the reservation portal. Please seek staff approval if bringing personal equipment such as lights. Staff will not assist with patron owned equipment. 
### Editing 
Staff can provide resources and tutorials for editing but will not edit patron projects themselves. 
### Dress Code 
The Media Lab is an open, public space. Full dress is required at all times. The Media Lab does not provide changing rooms. Makeup must be applied prior to using the lab. 
### Props/Materials 
In addition to the Library Rules of Conduct, the following items are NOT allowed in the Media Lab: 
  * Messy materials such as liquids, powders, gels, or glitter
  * Prop imitations of prohibited items such as weapons, alcoholic beverages, or drug paraphernalia
  * Oversized props (larger than can fit under a desk)


Please contact staff for any questions regarding personal items in the Media Lab. 
### Resources for Press and Media professionals 
The Media Lab is available to all patrons 13 & up with an Anaheim Library Card. Regardless of media experience, all patrons must complete the required orientations and make a reservation to use the labs. 
Media representatives looking to interview library staff will be referred to the appropriate point of contact. 
## Memory Lab 
The Memory Lab is a DIY space for digitizing and archiving analog media: photographs, film, documents, VHS, audiocassettes, etc. Patrons are responsible for handling their own media. Staff is not responsible for damage to media during the digitization process. 
### Repairs/Restoration 
The Memory Lab offers DIY digitization services only. Staff will not assist with repairs of physical media, but can recommend local facilities. 
For help with digital restoration, check the calendar for upcoming classes or our online resources. 
If media brought in has hazardous damage (mold, leakage, etc.), patron must remove the items from the library and the reservation will be cancelled and not rescheduled until all items and their containers are cleaned/sanitized. 
### Saving your media 
All media digitized in the Memory Lab must be saved to a personal external drive or cloud storage account. Memory Lab computers are reset after each session, and files saved to these computers will be deleted. Anaheim Public Library is not responsible for lost or damaged media.
