#  Black History Month 
![BHM Website Banner ](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=69574)
## Explore Black history, voices, and stories in Anaheim through engaging programs, resources, and services.
# PROGRAMS
  * **Talks & Treats book club: "Beloved" by Toni Morrison at East Anaheim Branch**
    * Tuesday, Feb. 10, 12 p.m. - 1 p.m.**  
**
  * **Let's Talk Music: Harlem Renaissance Jazz at Central**Library****
    * Wednesday, Feb. 11, 4:30 p.m. - 6 p.m.
    * Join us for a conversation exploring Black History, the birth of Jazz music, and the Harlem Renaissance. We'll also play a game of "Salute the Sample".
  * **Craft and Film Appreciation at Ponderosa-Joint Use Branch**
    * Wednesday, Feb. 11, 5 p.m. - 6:30 p.m.
    * This family-friendly event highlights the history, culture, and contributions of African Americans. Through creative craft activities and age-appropriate film appreciation, participants engage in meaningful learning experiences that encourages reflection, dialogue, and cultural awareness.
  * **Mini-Jazz Series: Jacob Montano Trio at Central**Library****
    * Saturday, Feb. 14, 11 a.m. - 12 p.m.
    * Join us in celebrating Black History Month at the Central Library as we honor Black resilience and innovation through the emergence of Jazz during the Harlem Renaissance with a mini-Jazz series! If you would like to dress up in Harlem Renaissance attire please feel free. During performances, light refreshments will be provided, courtesy of Crazy Cajun Creations - Cajun Coffee with a bite.
  * **A Literary Speakeasy: Poetry Salon at Central Library**
    * Saturday, Feb. 14, 3 p.m. - 4:30 p.m.
    * Join us for an afternoon of poetry and soft-jazz in celebration of Black History Month. Our Black History Month Literary Salon is inspired by the works of bell hooks, a renowned scholar and activist who reintroduced the world to the power of living according to a love ethic. Join Anaheim's Poet Laureate, Camille Hernandez, and guest authors (Jen Cheng, Maestro DeSean, and Sakile aka Nomad the Poet) as they share their bell hooks-inspired writings on changing our realities through love.
  * **Adult Book Club "Beloved" by Toni Morrison at Euclid Branch**
    * Tuesday, Feb. 17, 5 p.m. - 6 p.m.
  * **Southern and Soul Food with Georgia's Restaurant at Haskett Branch**
    * Wednesday, Feb. 18, 5:30 p.m. - 6:30 p.m.
    * Learn the magic behind soul food through a Southern cuisine and soul food demonstration led by staff from Georgia's Restaurant.
  * **Talks & Treats Book Club: "Parable of the Sower" by Octavia Butler**
    * Thursday, Feb. 19, 12 p.m. - 1 p.m.
  * **Mini-Jazz Series: Jacob Montano Trio at Central**Library****
    * Saturday, Feb. 21, 11 a.m. - 12 p.m.
    * Join us in celebrating Black History Month at the Central Library as we honor Black resilience and innovation through the emergence of Jazz during the Harlem Renaissance with a mini-Jazz series! If you would like to dress up in Harlem Renaissance attire please feel free. During performances, light refreshments will be provided, courtesy of Crazy Cajun Creations - Cajun Coffee with a bite.
  * **Mini-Jazz Series: Luxe Wav at Central**Library****
    * Sunday, Feb. 22, 1 p.m. - 2 p.m.
    * Join us in celebrating Black History Month at the Central Library as we honor Black resilience and innovation through the emergence of Jazz during the Harlem Renaissance with a mini-Jazz series! If you would like to dress up in Harlem Renaissance attire please feel free. During performances, light refreshments will be provided, courtesy of Crazy Cajun Creations - Cajun Coffee with a bite.
  * **STEAM Explorers: Art and Quilting at Ponderosa-Joint Use Branch**
    * Saturday, Feb. 28, 11 a.m. - 12 p.m.
    * In honor of Black History Month, we will be learning about Faith Ringgold and her quilting art. Gather inspiration from her artwork and create your own mini quilt.
  * **STEAM Explorers: Art and Quilting at Haskett Branch**
    * Saturday, Feb. 28, 2 p.m. - 3 p.m.
    * Celebrate Black History Month at the Haskett Branch as we explore the art of the late Faith Ringgold. Known for her storytelling through quilting and other media, she has made a lasting mark on the art world. Gather inspiration from her artwork and create your own mini quilt.**  
**
  * **African Drum Circle with Mister Boom Boom at Haskett Branch**
    * Tuesday, Mar. 3, 5 p.m. - 6 p.m.
    * Join Mister Boom Boom for an energetic, hands-on drum circle celebrating the power and spirit of African rhythm. Centered on the djembe, this immersive experience explores West African drumming and its global influence through instruments like bongos, cajón, and more--all rooted in African tradition.
  * **African Drum Circle with Mister Boom Boom at Ponderosa-Joint Use Branch**
    * Wednesday, Mar. 4, 5:30 p.m. - 6:30 p.m.

Centered on the djembe, this immersive experience explores West African drumming and its global influence through instruments like bongos, cajón, and more--all rooted in African tradition.
