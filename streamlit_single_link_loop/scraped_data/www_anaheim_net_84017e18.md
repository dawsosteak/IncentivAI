#  Sustainable Schools Award - For Educators & Administrators 
Each year, Anaheim Public Utilities recognizes K-12 Anaheim schools for making positive environmental impacts on campus and in curriculums. Through the [Sustainable Schools Award](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/70010/sustainable-schools-flyer-2026), schools showcase their energy and water-efficiency programs, unique environmental initiatives, and environmental education programs for students and staff. Up to two winning schools are recognized each year and receive the opportunity to select one grand prize, including ENERGY STAR® laptops, digital smart boards, or support for an on-site school water or energy sustainability project. 
In addition to the Sustainable Schools Award, Anaheim Public Utilities offers educators additional resources such as facility tours, extracurricular programs, and projects to enhance the educational experience for Anaheim students.
For more information, call 714-765-4274.

## Congratulations to Past Award Recipients!
  

These schools submitted exemplary applications showcasing their sustainability efforts on campus and in their school's curriculum. 
### Katella High School
  * Transformed campus their landscape to drought tolerant with a built in bioswale to capture rainwater


### Baden Powell Elementary
  * Installed Solar Panel Chargers


### Magnolia High School 
  * Installed water cistern to relieve the campus of flooding and completed solar installations to power the campus and their 2.5 acre garden


### Sycamore Junior High School 
  * "Upcycling Challenge" student art campaign


### Cambridge Virtual Academy
  * Student-initiated project called Steamapalooza "Ecovations!" to construct a greenhouse entirely from 926 recycled water bottles


### South Junior High School 
  * Sustainability is embedded in school culture to focus on "Sustainable South"
