Zoning Changes
  

Individual property owners may request a change to zoning laws that govern their property. This page ​provides resources for zoning change reques​ts including: general zoning changes, Specific Use Permits (SUP), Planned Development (PD), and public deed restrictions.
Additionally, you can also find resources for miscellaneous zoning items such as development or landscape plans, minor amendments, and alcohol spacing variance applications.
[**The City of Dallas is updating the Dallas' Development Code.  
Learn more & get involved at Dallas Zoning Reform**](https://www.dallaszoningreform.com/)  

  

[Tax documents and plats are obtained from Dallas County Records Building at 500 Elm Street. Click here](https://www.dallascounty.org/services/records.php)

**Dallas Development Code

[Dallas Development Code of Ordinances](https://codelibrary.amlegal.com/codes/dallas/latest/overview)  
This is a link to our Development Code of Ordinances. The table of contents on the left can be used to navigate to specific sections. Sections can be shared, downloaded as pdf or Word documents, bookmarked, and/or printed.
  
[Zoning Regulations - Volume III, Chapter 51A, Article IV](https://codelibrary.amlegal.com/codes/dallas/latest/dallas_tx/0-0-0-75160)  
This is a link to the zoning regulation provisions. There are other provisions that apply in conjunction with these provisions, but Article IV is where the bulk of the provisions for zoning are located. The [Zoning Districts Standards Overview Chart](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/sustainabledevelopment/planning/DCH%20Documents/ZoningDistrictStandards.pdf) is a summary comparison for many of the provisions in various zoning districts. This [Zoning Districts](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/sustainabledevelopment/planning/Pages/zoning-districts.aspx) page can be a helpful quick reference, but there may be exceptions and updates, so always consult the [code](https://codelibrary.amlegal.com/codes/dallas/latest/overview) for updated regulations.

Here is a link to the [Signs webpage](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/sustainabledevelopment/buildinginspection/Pages/Signs.aspx) with general information on signs, permitting, and inspections.

CPC Resources & Schedule
**Current Case Schedules  
**
View [CPC Resources](https://dallascityhall.com/government/Boards-and-Commissions/City-Plan-and-Zoning-Commission/Pages/resources.aspx) for schedules and view [Current Alcohol Variance](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/pnv/DCH%20Documents/Current_Planning/PDF/2025%20AV%20calendar__PUBLIC%20VERSION_FINAL_24-1202.pdf) schedule. Current Planning accepts 10 applications for each review cycle, however this number is based on case complexity and staffing. Submitted applications will be reviewed and assigned to the appropriate review cycle depending on the number of applications received for each deadline. If there is a surplus for a deadline, the application will be assigned to the next review cycle.
The tentative City Plan Commission (CPC) date shown for the cutoff date is dependant on deadlines for additional information being met and postponement by the applicant and/or the opposition.
Please note that the cases will be assigned to a planner one month before the assigned ZRT date.

Elected and Appointed Officials
**City Plan Commission**  

Staff provides support for City Plan Commission, the Zoning Ordinance Advisory Committee, and affiliated subcommittees, and task forces.  
  
The [City Plan Commission (CPC)](https://dallascityhall.com/government/Boards-and-Commissions/City-Plan-and-Zoning-Commission/Pages/default.aspx) acts as a quasi-judicial board and advisory committee to City Council. Public meetings are generally held on a bi-weekly basis. To speak on a specific item on the agenda at the next meeting, you can the [CPC website](https://dallascityhall.com/government/Boards-and-Commissions/City-Plan-and-Zoning-Commission/Pages/Meetings.aspx). The home page contains instructions on speaker registration, public meeting schedule and attendance, member information, and more. The page also provides links to each of the subcommittee pages, including:[  
Zoning Ordinance Advisory Committee (ZOAC)](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/pnv/Pages/Code-Amendments.aspx),   
[Comprehensive Land Use Committee (CLUP)](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/pnv/Pages/comprehensive-land-use-plan-committee.aspx),   
[Urban Design Peer Review Committee (UDPRC)](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/pnv/Pages/urban-design-peer-review-panel.aspx), and [Special Provision Sign Districts Committees (SSDAC & ADSAC)](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/pnv/Pages/SPSD.aspx).
  

City Plan Commission (CPC) briefings and public hearings are held in a hybrid format via videoconference and in-person. Information about how to access the CPC meetings, how to register to speak, CPC members and more can be found on the [CPC website](https://dallascityhall.com/government/Boards-and-Commissions/City-Plan-and-Zoning-Commission/Pages/Meetings.aspx).    
To speak remotely at a CPC hearings, the speaker must register to speak by 5:00 pm on the Tuesday before the hearing. In-person speakers are not required to register.
