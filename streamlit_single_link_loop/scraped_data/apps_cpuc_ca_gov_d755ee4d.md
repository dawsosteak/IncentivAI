Woods  Apr 20, 2022 9:17 pm [Flag as Offensive or Inappropriate](javascript:;) Randall Meals  Mountain View, CA94040 To: CPUC Date: 20 April 2022 Re: Rule Making 20-09-001, Agenda ID 201416 Considering: “Conclusions of Law” 5. The Final Rule encourages recipients to prioritize investments in fiber optic infrastructure, finding that such advanced technology enables the next generation of applications solutions for all communities, can deliver superior, reliable performance, and is generally most scalable to meet future needs” In planning the deployment of last mile broadband in the state of California we would ask the commission to eliminate all preferences given to fiber optic infrastructure over any other alternatives, including wireless technologies. 1. Factually, fiber optic infrastructure does not deliver superior performance versus leading-edge wireless technologies. Advances in wireless technologies have hit a “value inversion” tipping point in cost vs. performance advantage over fiber, where leading-edge wireless technologies deployed in the last mile can readily deliver multi-gigabit performance equal to or superior to fiber at far lower deployment and operating costs than fiber. This value inversion will continue to accelerate, with high-performance and pervasive wireless alternatives for the last mile leveraging the off-the-shelf commodity components available in the billions, such as Wi-Fi 6E, and soon Wi-Fi 7. 2. Factually, fiber optic infrastructure does not deliver superior reliability versus leading-edge wireless technologies. a. Fiber optic infrastructure, whether aerial or underground, is subject to physical disruption or damage from natural or man-made causes, as is common experience with all other examples of wireline infrastructure (phonelines, cable, electricity, …). b. Ready redundancy is comparatively easy and economical with leading-edge wireless technologies, whether by physical additions or by traffic steering though alternative pathways. This includes standing up temporary wireless access points and gateways. 3. Factually, fiber optic infrastructure is not generally more scalable than leading-edge wireless technologies. For example, with the opening of the 6GHz band, with Wi-Fi 6E and Wi-Fi 7, scalability can be quickly and dynamically delivered on an ongoing basis by a wide variety of means, including binding multiple channels and using parallel transmission techniques, all as needed. Growth in bandwidth needs can be as simple as adding more access points to a location or mesh. 4. In closing the Digital Divide, time-to-deploy is paramount to delivering the attendant benefits to the underserved. Analysis after analysis has shown the criticality of eliminating the Digital Divide to education, job growth, increased economic output, improved health care, agricultural productivity, and decreasing the social gaps in our democracy. Conversely, the impact of the Digital Divide can be characterized as devastating to much of the underserved. Fortunately, the leading-edge wireless technologies can be unquestionably faster and easier to deploy and redeploy as needs change versus digging trenches or stringing aerial fiber, quickening the elimination of the Digital Divide and the reaping of immense social and economic benefits. 5. Finally, as a matter of principle, we believe that proposed last mile deployments should not be de facto constrained by predetermined technology preferences. What seems fantastic today soon seems obsolete tomorrow. We urge that promising new communications technologies not be subject to disincentives. Respectfully Submitted, Randall R. Meals, Chairman & Co-Founder Instant!
