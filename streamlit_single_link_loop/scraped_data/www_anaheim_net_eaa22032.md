# Saturday, November 15, 2025
**11:00 a.m. to 4:00 p.m.**
## Anaheim Central Library
## 500 W. Broadway, Anaheim, CA 92805
  

###   

Celebrate a vibrant world of literature at Anaheim Central Library. Indie Author Day is a literary gathering, hosting a diverse array of activities. Explore the book fair, discovering hidden literary gems along with writing groups and small press publishers. Engage with local authors in autograph sessions and lose yourself in captivating readings. Our author tent offers an intimate space for verse enthusiasts. Join writing workshops, where seasoned authors and poets share their craft. It's a day dedicated to fostering literary connections, indie authors, and embracing the magic of storytelling in all its forms.

##  ![Melissa Adylia Calasanz Anaheim Public Library BIO png Headshot](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=67159)Melissa Adylia Calasanz
### Author
Melissa Adylia Calasanz is a multi-hyphenate compassionate, curious, creative human. She is a SAF/AFTRA Dancer/Actor/VO Artist, as well as a _Pushcart Prize_ and _Best of the Net_ nominated poet. Melissa's poetry is inspired by nature's ability to cultivate conversations regarding the universal cycles of life, death, and all the spaces between; aging and sensuality; as well as intense topics such as witnessing a murder, childhood neglect, and food insecurity. She is a lover of cats, plants, dance, poetry and people - all of which, she believes, have the ability to add value to the world beyond any one person's personal or professional identity. She is currently working on literary and stage projects in association with her podcast "You Buy The Shoes I Kick You With Them." Keep up to date with Melissa's projects and links via Instagram. IG: @MelissaAdyliaPauseBreatheSmile IG: @AllTheChaptersNoOneSees

##  ![Tye Grays-author pic](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=67153)Tye Grays
### Author
Tye Grays is a survivor with a passion in helping others cope and conquer life's most difficult trials. She eagerly promotes self-empowerment, encourages the blessings of having unwavering faith, and naturally imparts the wisdom she's learned along the way with anyone in need of guidance. Born and raised in Los Angeles, California, she is a mother, daughter, cousin, aunt, Godmother, confidant, and a loyal friend. Grays got her start in the beauty industry as a highly sought after licensed cosmetologist and before the age of 20, she became a successful entrepreneur and businesswoman. You can find her leveling up and fortifying her spiritual foundation as she understand that to thrive and live a purpose driven life requires that you courageously defy fear and complacency. To connect, email her at Tyeproductions72@gmail.com
  

##  ![Jake Teran](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=67156)Jake Teran
### Author
Jacob Teran is a proud Chicano living in the San Gabriel Valley, Los Angeles. Jacob is a 2nd generation Chicano who was born in Montebello, Los Angeles, east of Los Angeles. He has published his first three short stories on Somos en Escrito, called “A Quiet Night on the Boulevard,” “Niños del Sol,” “No Soy Cholo,” and several other poems in online journals and magazines as well as physical anthologies. He is currently teaching composition to several departments in colleges that include indigenous and Chicanx literature. In addition, Jacob is an advocate for social justice, self-care, and embracing the identity of others. Jacob currently lives in the SGV where he is working on several projects based on his experiences growing up in his barrio that deal with gang lifestyle, drugs, violence, and finding one’s identity in a chaotic concrete valley that he calls home. He has just recently published his first novella, _The Calling_ , a riveting story of a young Chicano’s journey in discovering his own identity with decisions he must make on his own. The protagonist, completely focused on joining the Marine Corps after dropping out of high school and occupying a juvenile detention center, Guillermo Tierra grapples with societal obstacles to reach a goal. When external issues arise that Tierra cannot join the Marine Corps at first, he must make a decision that will ultimately alter the trajectory of his life and his calling he believes. 
  

##  ![Brenda Vaca-author pic](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=67154)Brenda Vaca
### Author
Brenda Vaca is a Xicana poet, author, and independent publisher from Unceded Tongva Territory Sejatnga, today known as South Whittier, CA. She earned her B.A. in English at U.C. Berkeley with a Minor in Creative Writing. Later, she went on to graduate school in Berkeley, earning a Master of Divinity and a Master of Arts in Biblical Languages from the Pacific School of Religion and Graduate Theological Union. She served as an ordained reverend working primarily in "Latinx" communities for 10 years until an interfaith pilgrimage to Central America in August 2015 altered the course of her life. She has enjoyed being a guest speaker & poet at local community colleges, San Francisco LitCrawl, The Cheech, Chicano Park, American Poetry Museum, Wisconsin Book Festival, among many other wonderful community spaces. Riot of Roses is her debut collection of poetry published by her indie house, Rior of Roses Publishing House. She founded Riot of Roses Publishing House in 2021 to amplify historically silenced voices and narratives, publishing to date 15 titles and counting! Writing and publishing are her joyful rebellion. Check out her links [here](https://linktr.ee/brendavaca).
