#  America 250 Poster Contest 
![USCM logo.jpg](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=70095)
Calling local artists and creatives! You're invited to take part in the America 250 City Art Poster Project, celebrating the our nation's 250th anniversary.
## About the Contest
To commemorate the historic milestone, the U.S. Conference of Mayors, in partnership with the National Endowment for the Arts, has launched the America 250 City Art Poster Project.
The project is a nationwide contest that encourages our community and others to celebrate civic and national pride while highlighting the power of artists to interpret and express community identity.
We invite artists, designers and other creatives to submit commemorative posters that reflect Anaheim’s civic pride, history and community spirit while honoring the nation’s 250th anniversary.
For more about the national America 250 City Art Poster Project, visit the [U.S. Conference of Mayors website](https://www.usmayors.org/programs/america-250-city-art-poster-project/).
## Poster Guidelines
Submitted artwork should commemorate America's 250th anniversary in a way that is inspiring and reflective of community pride.
Posters should:
  * Celebrate civic pride and community identity
  * Reflect a connection to history and optimism for the future
  * Be nonpartisan with no political messages or inflammatory language
  * Incorporate the words “Anaheim” and “America 250” in the design


## Contest Themes
Artists working in any visual medium are encouraged to participate.
## Contest Eligibility
You can take part if you:
  * Are age 13 or older
  * Identify as part of the Anaheim community — you live, work, attend school, volunteer or otherwise actively participate in community life in Anaheim
  * Consider yourself an artist or creative of any skill level, including young, inspiring artists


## Submission Requirements
America 250 City Art Poster submissions for the city of Anaheim must be:
  * Original work and not previously exhibited
  * Suitable for public display, reproduction and large-scale exhibition
  * Designed to reference America 250 and the city of Anaheim
  * Created in any two-dimensional visual art medium that meets the size and layout requirements outlined below (digital illustration, painting, mixed media, collage, diptych or triptych image, etc.)


## Size, Layout and File Specifications
### Size and Layout
  * Live area: 24 inches wide by x 32 inches high
  * With bleed: 24.5 inches wide by 32.5 inches high (0.25-inch bleed on all sides)
  * Orientation: Vertical


### File Specifications
  * Format: PDF
  * Resolution: 300 DPI at 100 percent scale
  * Color mode: CMYK (four color)


## Review and Selection
Submissions will be reviewed by city staff, who will select up to 20 entries for consideration by the Cultural and Heritage Commission. The commission will then select the top three entries, with the final selection made by the mayor’s office to represent the city in the national America 250 City Art Poster Project.
## Deadline
Submissions must be received by April 14, 2026, at 11:59 p.m.

## Legal Information
### Artificial Intelligence and Original Work Requirement
Submissions must be original works created by a human artist and may not include generative artificial intelligence, or AI, content.
This includes any tool or feature that generates new visual elements, including text-to-image, image-to-image, generative fill or expand, style transfer and automated “create an image/design” functions.
Artists may still use standard digital design software for non-generative functions such as formatting, cropping, resizing, color correction, layering, vector drawing and typography.
The city of Anaheim may request drafts or working files to confirm compliance. Entries may be disqualified if they are found to include generative AI content.
### Ownership
The final selected poster will become the property of the city of Anaheim. Copyright is not transferred through this ownership.
The city will own the physical and/or deliverable embodiment of the selected work, including the final production-ready digital files submitted for printing and display, as well as any prints produced for city display and archival purposes.
### Copyright and Intellectual Property
Each artist retains all copyrights to their original artwork submitted to the city.
By submitting an entry, the artist grants the city of Anaheim a nonexclusive license to use, reproduce, publish, display, distribute and archive images of the submitted artwork for governmental purposes. These uses may include contest administration, public exhibition, public communications, educational use and historical documentation in any media now known or later developed.
The artist represents and warrants that the submission:
  * Is the artist’s original work
  * Does not infringe on any third-party rights
  * Includes any necessary permissions or releases for identifiable persons, copyrighted material or trademarked elements included in the artwork


Submitted artwork may be signed by the original artist, and the city will credit the artist when reasonably practicable.
