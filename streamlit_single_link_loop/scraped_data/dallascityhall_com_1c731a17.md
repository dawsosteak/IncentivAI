      * Q: What is the local preference program (LPP)?  ![close accordion](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/budget/financialtransparency/PublishingImages/home-2019/Icons-Close.svg)![open accordion](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/budget/financialtransparency/PublishingImages/home-2019/Icons-Plus.svg)

The program applies to two types of procurements&#58; Requests for Bids (RFBs) and Requests for Competitively Sealed Proposals (RFCSPs).       * Q: How does the City define "principal place of business" for the LPP?  ![close accordion](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/budget/financialtransparency/PublishingImages/home-2019/Icons-Close.svg)![open accordion](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/budget/financialtransparency/PublishingImages/home-2019/Icons-Plus.svg)
<div>A company's principal place of business is (1) its headquarters, (2) the primary executive or administrative office from which operations and activities of the business are directed, controlled, and coordinated by its officers or owners, or (3) an established office, plant, store, warehouse, or other facility where the majority of the business' operations and activities are conducted and located. <br> <br>A location used solely as a message center, post office box, mail drop, or for similar services or activities that provides no substantial function to the business is not a principal place of business.</div>
      * Q: I own a local company. How close do I have to be to the lowest bid to be eligible for the award through the LPP?  ![close accordion](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/budget/financialtransparency/PublishingImages/home-2019/Icons-Close.svg)![open accordion](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/budget/financialtransparency/PublishingImages/home-2019/Icons-Plus.svg)
<div>To be eligible for the award, a bid must be within&#58; <br>• 3% of the lowest bid for a contract valued at greater than $500,000 for goods <br>• 5% of the lowest bid for a contract valued at less than $500,000 for goods or services <br>• 5% of the lowest bid for a contract valued at less than $100,000 for construction services <br> <br>Please note, awards are not guaranteed even if the bid is within the percentages outlined above. Procurement Services will present the local preference and lowest bidder options to the City Council for consideration and final award. <br> <br>If more than one bidder submits the lowest bid, the award will go to the bidder who is a Dallas resident. If all bidders (or none) are residents, the Mayor will cast lots to determine the awardee.</div>

<div>To be eligible, a company must prove that&#58; <br>• Its principal place of business is located in Dallas OR <br>• At least 100 full-time employees are Dallas residents OR <br>• At least 20% of its total full-time employees are Dallas residents OR <br>• If available, it is participating in the Workforce Development Program <br> <br>For General Service contracts, the City will award five out of 100 evaluation points if the company satisfies one of the four requirements above.

      * Q: What is the Tourism Public Improvement District assessment?  ![close accordion](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/budget/financialtransparency/PublishingImages/home-2019/Icons-Close.svg)![open accordion](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/budget/financialtransparency/PublishingImages/home-2019/Icons-Plus.svg)
<div>The City Council authorized the creation of the Tourism Public Improvement District (TPID) in 2012 under Chapter 372 of the Texas Local Government Code. It was last renewed in 2016 for a 13-year term (until September 2029). The boundaries of the TPID are non-contiguous and include 120 designated hotel properties with 100 or more rooms within the city. <br> <br>The authorized management entity for the TPID provides incentives and marketing efforts aimed at promoting Dallas as a premier convention and tourism destination. TPID hotel property owners recover the assessment from their hotel guests at a rate of 2% of the price of the room nights sold and remit this guest fee to the City each month. <br> <br>In compliance with state law, the City collects TPID assessments from hotel property owners in the same way it administers its hotel occupancy tax (HOT) under Chapter 351 of the Tax Code. The City Controller’s Office sends the assessments paid by TPID hotels to the TPID each month (after deducting an administrative fee) until the total budget for services and improvements for the 13-year renewal term is collected. <br> <br>For additional TPID information, please visit the City Controller's website.</div>

<div>A minority- or woman-owned business enterprise (M/WBE) is a small business owned and operated by a socially and economically disadvantaged person or people. This includes U.S. citizens or lawfully admitted permanent residents who are Black, Latino/a/e, Asian, Native/indigenous, and/or women. <br> <br>Owned and operated means at least 51% of the business is owned by one or more of these individuals and that these individuals control the management and daily business operations.</div>
      * Q: How do I certify my business as an M/WBE?  ![close accordion](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/budget/financialtransparency/PublishingImages/home-2019/Icons-Close.svg)![open accordion](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/budget/financialtransparency/PublishingImages/home-2019/Icons-Plus.svg)
<div>The certification process for a minority- or women-owned business enterprise (M/WBE) is designed to ensure the business is actually owned, controlled, and operated by an eligible person or people. Businesses that want to be counted toward the Business Inclusion and Development (BID) Plan participation goals must be certified as an M/WBE by an agency recognized by the City of Dallas. <br> <br>Businesses seeking certification must complete an application and provide supporting documentation to the respective certification agency. Stemmons Freeway <br>5th Floor, Suite 550 <br>Dallas, TX 75247 <br>(214) 630-0747 / Fax (214) 637-2241 <br> <br>The Women's Business Council - Southwest <br><a>http&#58;//www.wbcsouthwest.org/</a> <br>2201 N. Collins, Suite 158 <br>Arlington, TX 76011 <br>(817) 299-0566 / Fax (817) 299-0949 <br>Toll-Free&#58; (866) 451-5997</div>
      * Q: What is the local preference program (LPP)?  ![close accordion](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/budget/financialtransparency/PublishingImages/home-2019/Icons-Close.svg)![open accordion](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/budget/financialtransparency/PublishingImages/home-2019/Icons-Plus.svg)
<div>The City's local preference program values the participation of companies who have their principal place of business in Dallas or who hire Dallas residents. The program applies to two types of procurements&#58; Requests for Bids (RFBs) and Requests for Competitively Sealed Proposals (RFCSPs). It does not apply to any procurements that include federal funding.</div>
      * Q: How do I qualify for the LPP in a Request for Competitively Sealed Proposals (RFSCP) procurement?  ![close accordion](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/budget/financialtransparency/PublishingImages/home-2019/Icons-Close.svg)![open accordion](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/budget/financialtransparency/PublishingImages/home-2019/Icons-Plus.svg)
<div>To be eligible, a company must prove that&#58; <br>• Its principal place of business is located in Dallas OR <br>• At least 100 full-time employees are Dallas residents OR <br>• At least 20% of its total full-time employees are Dallas residents OR <br>• If available, it is participating in the Workforce Development Program <br> <br>For General Service contracts, the City will award five out of 100 evaluation points if the company satisfies one of the four requirements above.
      * Q: What contracts are considered General Service?  ![close accordion](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/budget/financialtransparency/PublishingImages/home-2019/Icons-Close.svg)![open accordion](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/budget/financialtransparency/PublishingImages/home-2019/Icons-Plus.svg)
General Service contracts include insurance, insurance-related services such as claims adjustment and policy administration, technical services related to the purchase of a high technology item, or other types of manual, physical, or intellectual labor performed on behalf of the City and purchased for a lawful municipal purpose. They do not include personal services, professional services, planning services, or facility construction.
