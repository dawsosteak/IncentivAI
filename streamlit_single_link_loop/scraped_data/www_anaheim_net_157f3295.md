#  Project S.A.Y. (Support Anaheim's Youth) 
Project S.A.Y. is a key initiative by the City of Anaheim aimed at addressing the growing social challenges faced by today’s youth. This dynamic program serves junior high and high school students, along with their families, by offering a blend of outreach, recreation, education, social engagement, and personalized guidance. Its mission is to equip young people with the skills to make informed decisions, adopt healthy lifestyles, and successfully transition from adolescence to adulthood.
T![Project SAY in Front of a Ribbon](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=63276)o achieve this, Project S.A.Y. employs seven Community Services Outreach Coordinators, each assigned to a designated sector within the Anaheim Union High School District (AUHSD) and Canyon High School. These coordinators work closely with school administrators, providing critical support to students dealing with behavioral challenges, academic struggles, and other personal obstacles. With expertise in youth development, conflict resolution, character building, and gang, drug, and violence prevention, the Outreach Coordinators serve as trusted mentors who help guide students toward positive choices.
By being stationed at schools and community facilities near the youth they serve, Outreach Coordinators foster meaningful relationships with students and their families. These relationships of trust extend into the home, where Project S.A.Y. staff collaborate with parents to address challenges and create a supportive environment for growth and success.

## **Teen Centers: Safe Spaces for Growth and Engagement**
Project S.A.Y. also operates six teen centers throughout Anaheim, providing a safe and supportive environment for local youth. These centers serve as hubs for mentorship, recreation, and personal development, offering activities such as tutoring, leadership training, career exploration, and recreational programs. By fostering a sense of community and belonging, the teen centers give young people a positive alternative to negative influences while helping them build valuable life skills.

## What Sets Project S.A.Y. Apart?
Project S.A.Y. offers unique, hands-on programs designed to empower and inspire youth, including:
  * **Home Assessment Visits** – Personalized support for families in their home environment.
  * **Youth Behavior Guidance & Modification** – Helping students develop positive habits and attitudes.
  * **Youth Leadership Councils** – Encouraging young people to take on leadership roles in their![Project SAY Chalk Drawing](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=63275) communities.
  * **Themed Retreats & Activity Days** – Engaging experiences that promote personal growth and teamwork.
  * **Community Service Projects** – Instilling a sense of civic responsibility and pride.
  * **Individualized Project Plans** – Tailored strategies based on the Youth Development Framework to address personal goals and challenges.


Through one-on-one and small group sessions, Outreach Specialists help youth develop essential life skills, including leadership, conflict resolution, goal setting, and decision-making. By taking ownership of their personal growth and actively planning activities of interest, participants become empowered to shape their own future—with the guidance and support of Project S.A.Y. staff.
With its outreach efforts, teen centers, and personalized support, Project S.A.Y. is more than a program—it’s a movement dedicated to guiding Anaheim’s youth toward a bright and successful future.
