## About Anaheim's Poet Laureate  
  

We are looking for our 2026-28 poet laureate! 
The poet laureate is Anaheim's literary ambassador, inspiring appreciation of poetry and the arts in the city. 
A poet laureate serves as the official poet for a city, county, state or nation. The tradition goes back centuries. In America, the precursor to our nation’s poet laureate today dates back to 1937.
Anaheim’s poet laureate must be a published poet, have strong ties to the city for at least five years, be at least 21 years old and be able to give public poetry readings.
Anaheim’s poet laureate holds free workshops at Anaheim Public Libraries, leads poetry readings at city events and heads up community gatherings that promote poetry, literature and the arts. You can learn more about Anaheim's poet laureate below.
Apply by Thursday, Dec. 18, by 5 p.m. 
Contact Art Varela at (714) 765-5250 or Avarela@anaheim.net for questions about the application process.

# Poet Laureate
![Camille logo 7-5-24](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=55918)
Camille Hernandez is Anaheim’s poet laureate and literary ambassador, working to connect our community through writing. 
A poet, essayist and teaching artist, Camille is a self-proclaimed “public library girl” who grew up in neighboring La Palma and walked to her local library every day after school to read as many new books as she could.
  

Camille attended Los Coyotes Elementary, part of the Centralia Elementary School District, and Walker Junior High School and Kennedy High School, part of the Anaheim Union High School District.
She won her first poetry competition at the age of 7 for a poem about checking out books at the library. And for her high school graduation speech, Camille delivered an epic poem told in couplet rhymes!
  

She believes everyone should have access to life-changing stories, different perspectives, writing tools to share their own stories and perspectives and pathways to sharing their writing with the public.
Camille is a narrative poet. She uses poetry to tell stories of wisdom and woes from her life experiences, family history, Black and Filipino cultures and her communities.
Her debut book, “The Hero and the Whore: Reclaiming Healing and Liberation Through the Stories of Sexual Exploitation in the Bible,” mixes both poetry and essay to open conversations about protection, healing, and trauma-informed care.
Camille’s poems are published in So to Speak, FIYAH, Braving the Body, Spoken Black Girl Magazine, Torch Literary
Calling herself a “literary doula,” Camille works to provide readers and writers with the strength to give life to the unnamed experience, create new vocabularies for the lives we pioneer and flex the great muscle of empathy.
She’s taught literary arts workshops throughout Orange and Los Angeles counties. She will use her tenure as poet laureate to expand literary arts in Anaheim through community collaborations, leading workshops, and demystifying pathways to publication for aspiring authors.
Camille attended the University of California, Santa Cruz, where she graduated with a bachelor’s in community studies. She earned a master’s in educational leadership and administration from Trident University while she worked as an admissions adviser to graduate candidates.
She was a poetry mentee for the Writer-to-Writer Mentorship Program through the Association of Writers & Writing Programs. She was an inaugural fellow of the Roots.Wounds.Words. Writer’s Retreat and is a current fellow of The Watering Hole.
Camille is working on publishing her first collection of poems and is writing a children’s book with her daughter. You can read her writings and connect with her at Camillehernandez.substack.com.
Camille lives in downtown Anaheim with her husband and three children.
# Past Poets Laureate
## Past Poet Laureate: Wendy Van Camp
  
Wendy Van Camp served as Anaheim’s poet laureate and literary ambassador from 2022 to 2024.![Van Camp](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=55674)
During her tenure, Van Camp edited two issues of the Anaheim Poetry Review, an anthology of community poetry. She also taught poetry workshops, supported the library's open mic events, coordinated the annual library event Indie Author Day twice during her term and mentored Anaheim's community of poets.
Van Camp is what's known as a speculative poet, part of a genre of poetry that draws inspiration from science fiction, technology, fantasy or mythology.
She also is a fan of Jane Austen and her writing on Britain in the 1800s. "Persuasion" is her first and favorite Austen read.
Van Camp holds a bachelor's in radio, television and film production from California State University, Long Beach. She's also a graduate of the Ad Astra Speculative Fiction Workshop.
She lives in east Anaheim with her husband Allen Van Camp, an employment law attorney. The couple has lived in Anaheim for more than 20 years.
You can learn more about Van Camp [here](https://wendyvancamp.com/).
You can see her poet laureate Instagram [here](https://www.instagram.com/anaheim_poetlaureate/) and her Facebook [here](https://www.facebook.com/anaheimpoet/).
  

## Past Poet Laureate: Grant Hier  
![Grant Hier web](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=20148)  

Grant Hier was Anaheim’s inaugural poet laureate and literary ambassador from 2018 to 2020.
During his tenure, he shared his love of poetry with Anaheim at public readings, library events, community gatherings and social media. 
“The best poetry shows us who we are,” Hier says. “It broadens our perspective. It shows us how other people think and live and how much we have in common. And that builds compassion and community.”
Hier’s favorite poem? “Song of Myself” by American great Walt Whitman.
“I love it for its all-inclusiveness and tremendous heart,” he says. “He revolutionized poetry with his form.”
See Grant's Anaheim Poet Laureate X posts [here](https://twitter.com/anaheim_poet).
See Grant's Anaheim Poet Laureate Instagram posts [here](https://www.instagram.com/anaheim_poet/).
