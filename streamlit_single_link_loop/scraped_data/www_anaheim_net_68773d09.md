#  ATID Housing Committee 
The ATID Housing Committee is a five-member board overseeing housing funding for workers in Anaheim's hospitality industry.
The committee meets quarterly and makes recommendations on workforce housing, or housing that supports those working at hotels in the Anaheim Tourism Improvement District, also known as, ATID. The committee, made up of hoteliers and a city representative, considers recommendations for funding of workforce housing programs.
Formed in 2026, the committee is part of a program to fund housing needs for hospitality workers formed by the 94 hotels that make up the ATID.
In 2025, hotels and the city of Anaheim agreed to dedicate 9 percent, or about $3 million, of ATID's yearly funding to housing needs. ATID hotels assess themselves 2 percent of hotel stays, on top of the city of Anaheim’s 15 percent hotel-stay tax, with that dedicated money going to tourism marketing, convention bookings, transportation projects, and housing needs. 
ATID workforce housing funding goes into the city’s Local Housing Trust Fund and is accounted for separately as part of an ATID Area Worker Housing Program subaccount.
