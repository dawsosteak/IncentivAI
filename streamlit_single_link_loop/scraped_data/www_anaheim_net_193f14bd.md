#  Summer VolunTEEN Program 
![VolunTEEN banner](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=67170)
# 2026 Applications are now closed.
  

The Anaheim Community Services Department provides teens, 13 - 17 years of age, the opportunity to give back to their community and earn service hours through the Summer VolunTEEN Program. Teens can volunteer at various summer programs throughout Anaheim such as:
  * [Summer Day Camp and Wilderness Survival Camp at Oak Canyon Nature Center](https://www.anaheim.net/1110/OAKS-Day-Camp)
  * [Tiny Tots Academy](https://www.anaheim.net/2287/Tiny-Tots-Academy)
  * [CampVenture](https://www.anaheim.net/2285/CampVenture)
  * [Summer Nights Under the Stars at Pearson Park Amphitheatre](https://www.anaheim.net/1432/Pearson-Park-Amphitheatre)
  * [Haskett Library](https://ca-anaheim.civicplus.com/Facilities/Facility/Details/Haskett-Branch-Library-20)
  * [Ponderosa Joint-Use Library](https://ca-anaheim.civicplus.com/Facilities/Facility/Details/Ponderosa-JointUse-Library-19)
  * [Euclid Library](https://ca-anaheim.civicplus.com/Facilities/Facility/Details/Euclid-Branch-Library-21)
  * [Canyon Hills Library](https://ca-anaheim.civicplus.com/Facilities/Facility/Details/Canyon-Hills-Branch-Library-24)
  * [East Anaheim Gymnasium](https://www.anaheim.net/966/East-Anaheim-Gymnasium)


### Eligibility
Applicants must be 13 years of age. Because of limited space, Anaheim residents and students of Anaheim schools will be given priority placement. Applications will be available beginning Monday, February 2nd. **Please note: Due to high volume of applications, no application will be accepted after the deadline, April 10th at 5pm.**

  5. Fill in contact information.

### Current TB Clearance & Fingerprinting Background Checks
The Community Services Department is required to have volunteers' TB clearance on file. All applicants are required to submit a copy of a TB clearance slip dated within the last two years (2024 - present will be accepted). Applicants are responsible for the cost of the test.
State Law AB 1663 requires all recreation program volunteers be fingerprinted for a background check and cleared by the California Department of Justice before placement. Fingerprinting is available at no-cost and must be completed by City of Anaheim staff.
### Fingerprinting Appointments & Locations
Fingerprints are scheduled by appointment only. Appointments may be made by contacting the Volunteer Coordinator at (714) 765-4994 or VolunteerOffice@anaheim.net. All appointments will take place at City Hall.
### REMINDERS
  * Fingerprinting is for **NEW** VolunTEEN Program applicants only. Returning VolunTEENS do not need to be fingerprinted.
  * Applicants **MUST** bring a current, government-issued I.D. photo card (state I.D., driver's license, US passport) to their fingerprinting appointment.


### Application Submission & Deadline
Completed applications (including TB tests) will be accepted beginning Monday, February 2nd, 2026. Applications will only be accepted online.
Site selection priority will be given to those who have completed the registration process including attendance at a VolunTEEN Orientation. Applications will be accepted from February 2nd until April 10th, or until program fills to capacity.
The last day to submit a complete application is Friday, April 10th - applications must be received by 5:00 p.m. to be considered. The program is limited; partial or incomplete applications will not be accepted.
### Assignment & Hour Confirmation Letters
Volunteer Assignment emails will be sent to VolunTEENS by May 30th. At the end of the 2026 Program, all VolunTEENS will receive a letter confirming the number of community service hours performed. Hour confirmation emails will be sent by August 30th.
### Questions & Additional Information
For questions or additional information, please contact the Volunteer Coordinator at (714) 765-4994 or volunteeroffice@anaheim.net.
