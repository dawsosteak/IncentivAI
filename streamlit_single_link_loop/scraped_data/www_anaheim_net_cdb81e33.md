#  Home Water Treatment Devices 
## Anaheim tap water exceeds all state and federal standards.
In fact, we conduct more than 44,000 tests annually to make sure it stays that way. As a result, Anaheim Public Utilities customers do not need to purchase home water treatment devices to make tap water safe to drink. 
Anaheim Public Utilities customers routinely ask questions about tap water and whether they need a residential water treatment device. Here are a few of the most frequently asked questions we receive:

## Home Water Treatment Device Need-to-Know Info
  

### Undocumented Performance Claims 
  
Broad statements such as “removes contaminants” or “provides pure water” without certified test data to support their performance claims should be a warning sign. California law prohibits such undocumented or broad claims relating to water purifiers. 
  

### Registered Water Treatment Devices  
  

Residential water treatment devices designed to reduce or remove contaminants that have federal or state minimum health standards must be registered with the California State Water Resources Control Board (SWRCB) to be approved for registration. Manufacturers must provide evidence to support each contaminant removal claim. It also means the company's literature and packaging have been reviewed and found not to contain false, misleading or exaggerated claims.
The California Residential Water Treatment Device Registration Program now has a searchable database of all currently registered devices. 
Find a list of registered devices at [SWRCB Residential Water Treatment Devices](https://www2.waterboards.ca.gov/rwtd/).
  

### Reviewing Product Information
Anaheim’s water is safe to drink, but if you choose to purchase a water treatment device, you should carefully read its product information to understand what you are buying in terms of performance. Additionally, be certain to follow the manufacturer's instructions for installation, operation, and maintenance. For example, if you improperly install or maintain a device, such as failing to change its filters when required, you can actually cause water quality problems. 
  

### A Matter of Taste
Finally, when purchasing a home water treatment device, one must consider if the price is worth the added expense to ensure peace of mind and/or achieve a desired water taste. 
Find a brief description of the most common options available on our [Types of Home Water Treatment Devices page](https://www.anaheim.net/670/Types-of-Home-Water-Treatment-Devices).
