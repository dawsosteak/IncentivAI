##  ![Francesca Lia Block](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=66057)Francesca Lia Block
### Author and Publisher
Francesca Lia Block is the award-winning and best-selling author of over thirty books of fiction, non-fiction and poetry. She edits LIT ANGELS journal on Substack and teaches creative writing in Los Angeles and online. Check out her website: [here](https://www.francescaliablock.com/).
  

  

  

  

  

##  ![darcy](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=66200)Darcy Staniforth
### Scholar and Paranormal Coordinator
Darcy Staniforth is an American Studies scholar, paranormal investigator, writer, and performer sought out for her expertise in popular culture, comedy, death, and unexplained phenomena. When not working with students both inside and outside of the classroom, she is the Paranormal Coordinator for the Kellogg House and Director of Tour Operations and a Ghost Host for Haunted Orange County. Learn more: [here](https://www.instagram.com/darcystaniforth/?hl=en).
  

![erica](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=66201)
## Erica Preus
### Special Effects Makeup Artist
Erica Preus is an Emmy award-winning special effects makeup artist. She has worked on The Santa Clauses (2022), The Orville (2019), American Horror Story (2016-2019), and Legion (2019). She has edited and assisted in the publication of five books about makeup and the entertainment industry. Learn more about her work: [here.](https://www.ericapreus.com/)
  

##  ![Susan Johnson](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=66163)Susan Johnson
### Professor of English
Susan Johnson is a Professor of English at Compton College. As a lifelong fan of monsters, the power of monsters and horror to address not only society’s fears but also to spotlight transgressive social change is a source of endless fascination. This has led her to write and speak about monsters, horror films, and books in her academic work, at conferences, and on podcasts. She has created and taught multiple college courses that explore monsters and their meaning.

### **Ghoulish Gameplay - Establishing Terror in TTRPGs**
###   

In this seminar, we will be discussing the ins and outs of fostering fear through tabletop role-playing games. Whether you’re a fan of D&D, Vampire the Masquerade, Candela Obscura, or any other TTRPG, join library technician Nicole in learning all about different types of fear, their effect on players, and how to foster a safe space in horror-centered gameplay.
###   

| 
###   
  
### 11 a.m.
|   
| 
### **Spirits Among the Stacks: Tales of Haunted Libraries with Darcy Staniforth**
  
While many of us go to the library to check out a book on hauntings, what if the library itself had its own hauntings to check out? Listen to stories of historically haunted libraries during this entertaining presentation. **  
**  
### 12 p.m.
|   
| 
### **Reader or Not, Here it Comes: How the Horror Genre Continues to Save Our Lives**
  
Hear from award-winning author, Tananarive Due, as she speaks about how Horror genre is vital to the art of literacy and our livelihoods. Followed by a conversation with Anaheim's Poet Laureate Camille Hernandez.  
### 1 p.m.
| 
### **Monsters in Storytelling with Susan Johnson**
  
The power of monsters and horror address not only society’s fears but also spotlight transgressive social change. Hear how monsters play that pivotal role in literature.   
| 
### **Book Signing with Tananarive Due**

### **Shudder and Shiver Horror Trivia**
  
Think you know all there is to know about slashers, thrillers, and heart-pounding spooky stories? Test your knowledge of all things horror against other dreadheads at 'Shudder and Shiver Horror Trivia! Fabulous Scream-Inducing Prizes for the top 3 winners! | 
### **The Lover, The Secret & The Dark House: Creating Gothic Conflict with Francesca Lia Block**
  
In this seminar we will look at the who/what/how/where and why of Gothic literature, with an emphasis on Beloved by Toni Morrison, and writers in the audience will be able to consider ways to apply what they learn to their own work through a brief exercise.**  
**

### **The Art of Spooky Spackle**
**  
** Unleash your spooky side, and join us in making ghost paintings out of spackle! In this craft, participants will learn how to make uniquely textured paintings on canvas with this unexpected medium. No art experience is needed, but beware - you may be bringing ghosts home!**

### **From Palette to Screen: Creating Movie Monsters**

** Join us for an afternoon of terrifying transformation as Emmy Award winning Special Effects Makeup Artist, Erica Preus, demonstrates the techniques used to bring your favorite monsters to life on the big screen.

##  ![Ari Loeb](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=66062)Ari Loeb
### Author, Stuntman
Ari Loeb is an ex-circus artist and choreographer (Cirque du Soleil, Pilobolus, Spiderman on Broadway). He moved to Los Angeles to pursue a career in stunts, and has since worked on over one hundred films and video games, specializing in creature horror, such as The Walking Dead, Zombieland: Double-Tap, Renfield, and the Resident Evil franchise. His debut novel, "Thunder, Az" is an award-winning piece of survival horror, set in the Arizona desert. His new novel, "The Stunted Man" won the Literary Titan Award for "Best Fiction," and the American Fiction Award for "Best Psychological Horror.” Nicolas Cage calls it "Compelling, poetic, and funny as hell!” Ari’s dark art photography, “Liosso Art” has been exhibited around the world and in various films. He is currently working on a Black Panther video game from Marvel, and he is also using motion capture technology to train robots how to be acrobats. He lives in Los Angeles with his dog and cat. Check out his website: [here](https://www.ariloeb.com/). View his art photography: [here](http://liossoart.com). IG: @ari_loeb
  

##  ![David Sandner](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=66063)David Sandner
### Author
David Sandner is a 2025 Finalist for a Shirley Jackson Award for His Unburned Heart (Raw Dog Screaming), a novella about Mary Shelley and her husband’s heart. He is a Mythopoeic Award nominated scholar who recently published The Afterlife of Frankenstein: A Century of Mad Science, Automata, and Monsters inspired by Mary Shelley, 1818-1918 (Lanternfish, 2023). His at work on The Frankenstein Singularity, a novel set in the same Frankenverse as his novella. Novel Egyptian Motherlode (2024) and novelettes Mingus Fingers (2019) and Hellhounds (2022), co-written with Jacob Weisman, explore American music and a strange magic and came out recently from Fairwood Press. New story “Night School,” also written with Weisman, is now online in Abyss & Apex magazine. His stories and poems have appeared in leading magazines Asimov's, Weird Tales, PodCastle, and others and in anthologies the HWA Poetry Showcase, The Mammoth Book of Dark Magic, Monstroddities, and others. He publishes scholarship on Mary Shelley, Philip K. Dick, horror, the critical history of the fantastic, fantasy, science fiction, and the sublime. He is a Professor of English and Creative Writing at California State University, Fullerton. Check out his website: [here](http://davidsandner.com).
  

##  ![Emilia Mondragon](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=66067)Emilia Mondragón
### Indie Author
Emilia Mondragón is an independent queer author from LA County and was raised by Mexican-immigrant parents. Over the course of her life, she has dipped her toes in many creative fields, such as film, theater, singing, acting, and dancing, but ultimately decided to pursue her lifelong dream of becoming an author. She is a lover of fantasy, horror, and romance, and writes stories with a mixture of the three. She self-published her vampire urban fantasy, DAYWALKERS, in 2024, and her adult epic fantasy, THE MOON PRINCE, was released in July 2025. Check out her website: [here](https://emiliavmondragon.squarespace.com).
  

##  ![Evan Baughfman](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=66064)Evan Baughfman
### Author & Playwright
Evan Baughfman is a Southern California playwright, author, and educator. He is a playwriting member of PlayGround-L.A., and is a company member with Force of Nature Productions. A number of Evan’s plays are published through Heuer Publishing, YouthPLAYS, Next Stage Press, and Drama Notebook. Evan has also found success writing horror fiction, his work found recently in anthologies by Graveside Press, Inkd Publishing, and No Bad Books Press. Evan’s own books include: The Emaciated Man, Vanishing of the 7th Grade, Bad for Your Teeth, Try Not to Die in a Dark Fairy Tale, and the upcoming short story collection, Mauls of the Wild. Check out his books and author profile: [here](http://amazon.com/author/evanbaughfman). IG: @Agent00evan_716
  

##  ![Evan Marlowe](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=66065)Evan Marlowe
### Author, Screenwriter, Director
Evan Marlowe is the writer/director of the all-puppet horror feature  _Abruptio_ , which the  _New York Times_ called an “exceptional sociopolitical thriller.” The film garnered 23 awards on the festival circuit before being distributed by the legendary Anchor Bay Entertainment. He has published 10 works, including the acclaimed fairy tale serial killer novel  _Pauper King_ and its sequel  _The Sallow Scourge._ He is a proud member of the Horror Writers Association. Check out his website: [here](https://estuartmarlowe.com/ "https://estuartmarlowe.com/"). IG: @[es](https://www.instagram.com/es_marlowe/)[_marlowe](https://www.instagram.com/es_marlowe/)

##  ![Nikkita Bell](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=66059)Nikkita Bell
### Author & Illustrator
From the mystical land of Los Angeles, California, Nikkita Bell is an author-illustrator who has carved out a distinctive niche where literature meets visual storytelling. Specializing in adult dark paranormal and urban fantasy with horror and romantic elements and a gothic neo-noir flair, each of Bell’s novels becomes an immersive experience where readers encounter dangerous, high-stakes, and fast-paced narratives through captivating illustrations and music-inspired themes.  
Through her groundbreaking hybrid graphic novel format, Bell welcomes you to the gothic side of storytelling filled with morally complex anti-heroines who embrace their darker natures and never apologize for it. She has built her brand on a simple but powerful premise: villainous books about villainous women doing villainous things.  
When she isn’t crafting the next femme fatale to follow to Hell and back, Nikkita enjoys orchestral music, pretending to sing opera, and snuggling with her husband and two cats, Ciri and Yennefer. Check out her website: [here](http://www.nikkitabell.com). IG and TikTok: @nikkitabell
