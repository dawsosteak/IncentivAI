#  Executive Staff 
  1. ![Charles “Chuck” Cinnamo](https://pd.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=133)
####  Charles “Chuck” Cinnamo 
Deputy Chief
Deputy Chief Charles "Chuck" Cinnamo serves as the Deputy Chief of Police for the Anaheim Police Department, bringing nearly three decades of law enforcement leadership and a strong foundation in public safety operations to the role.
A native of Southern California, Deputy Chief Cinnamo holds a Bachelor of Arts degree in Vocational Education from San Diego State University. He also earned two Associate of Science degrees from Cuyamaca College in Computer Technology and Electronics Technology.
Before entering law enforcement, Deputy Chief Cinnamo worked for the San Diego Unified School District as an Instructional Aide, teaching math to special education students. This experience shaped his approach to mentorship and leadership.
He began his law enforcement career in 1996 with the San Diego County Sheriff's Department, graduating from the Corrections Academy. Later that same year, he attended the San Diego Regional Public Safety Training Institute at Miramar College to become a Deputy Sheriff. Over his tenure with the Sheriff's Department, he served in various assignments and rose through the ranks from Corrections Deputy to Assistant Sheriff.
His early assignments included working in corrections at the Central Detention Facility and San Diego Central Jail, including jail administration and training. As a Deputy Sheriff, he served in patrol in communities such as Lemon Grove, Spring Valley, Imperial Beach, and Bonita, and later transitioned into investigative roles, including service as a School Resource Officer and area investigator.
As a Sergeant and Lieutenant, he held supervisory and management assignments in patrol, traffic, investigations, community-oriented policing, and weapons training. Upon promotion to Captain, he led operations at the Communication Center, oversaw Property and Evidence, and served as "Chief of Police" for the Vista Station. He then commanded the department's Narcotics and Gangs Division.
As Commander and later Assistant Sheriff, Deputy Chief Cinnamo provided executive oversight to major functional areas of the Sheriff's Department, including Centralized Investigations, the Regional Crime Lab, Jail Medical Services, Jail Investigations, and the Sheriff's Transportation Detail.
In 2024, he joined the Anaheim Police Department as Deputy Chief of Police, continuing his commitment to professional excellence, collaboration, and innovation in public safety.
Deputy Chief Cinnamo is a graduate of several premier law enforcement leadership programs, including the FBI National Academy (Session 253), the Police Executive Research Forum's Senior Management Institute for Police, and California POST's Supervisor School, Management Course, and Executive Development Course. He also graduated from the Drug Enforcement Administration's Drug Unit Commanders Academy.
He is an active member of the International Association of Chiefs of Police (IACP), the Police Executive Research Forum (PERF), the California Police Chiefs Association (CPCA), the California Peace Officers' Association (CPOA), and the Retired Deputy Sheriff's Association of San Diego County.
  

  2. ![Rodney Duckwitz](https://pd.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=134)
####  Rodney Duckwitz 
Captain
Captain Rodney Duckwitz was born and raised in Anaheim and is a graduate of Loara High School, Class of 1987. He began his service with the City of Anaheim in 1993 as a Code Enforcement Officer before joining the Anaheim Police Department the following year. He graduated from the Fullerton College Police Academy in 1993.
As a police officer, Captain Duckwitz served in a variety of assignments, including Patrol, Traffic, Community Policing, and the Mounted Enforcement Unit. He was promoted to sergeant in 2015, where he continued to lead in Patrol and Traffic Safety while also supervising the Mounted Enforcement Unit.
Upon his promotion to lieutenant in 2019, Captain Duckwitz took on a broad range of leadership roles. His assignments included Patrol Watch Commander, the Orange County Family Justice Center, and the Investigations Division. While in Investigations, he oversaw major sections including Homicide, Robbery and Major Assaults, Gangs, VICE, Family Crimes, Sexual Assault, Criminal Intelligence, and Forensics.
Promoted to the rank of captain in 2023, Captain Duckwitz has served as the Commanding Officer of both the Support Services Division and the Investigations Division. He currently serves as the Commanding Officer of the Operations Division, overseeing Personnel and Training, Traffic Safety, Strategic Planning, Emergency Management, the Real-Time Crime Center, and Crime Analysis. He also manages the Department's wellness and support programs, including the Peer Support Team and Chaplain Program. In addition, he holds ancillary responsibility as the Commander of the Mounted Enforcement Unit.
Captain Duckwitz is a graduate of the POST Executive Development Course and the Police Executive Research Forum's Senior Management Institute for Police (Session 93). He holds an Associate of Arts degree in Business from Golden West College, a Bachelor of Science in Occupational Studies from California State University, Long Beach, a Master of Arts from Chapman University, and a Doctor of Education from the University of Southern California. His doctoral dissertation focused on housing and resource challenges faced by Commercially Sexually Exploited Children (CSEC) and other minors who are victims of human trafficking.
He is a member of the International Association of Chiefs of Police (IACP), the California Police Chiefs Association (Cal Chiefs), the Peace Officers Research Association of California (PORAC), the California Peace Officers' Association (CPOA), and the Police Executive Research Forum (PERF).
  

  3. ![Curtis Faulkner](https://pd.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=135)
####  Curtis Faulkner 
Captain
Captain Curtis Faulkner was raised in Anaheim and is a proud graduate of Western High School, Class of 1988. He went on to earn a Bachelor of Science degree in Political Science from California State University, Fullerton, in 1994.
In 1995, Captain Faulkner began his law enforcement career with the Los Angeles Police Department. He graduated from the LAPD Academy in 1996 and served the department until 2001. During his time with LAPD, he held a variety of assignments as a police officer, including Patrol, Community Policing, Traffic, and as a Public Information Officer. As a detective, he worked in the Financial Crimes Unit and the Detective Training Unit.
Captain Faulkner joined the Anaheim Police Department in 2001, where he has served in numerous assignments throughout his career. As an officer, he worked in Patrol, Economic Crimes, and on the Primary Response Officer (PRO) Team. He was promoted to sergeant and went on to supervise Patrol, Personnel, and the PRO Team.
Upon promotion to lieutenant, Captain Faulkner held a wide range of leadership roles, including watch commander, supervisor of the PRO Team, and assignments in Personnel and Training, Internal Affairs, and as District Commander in the Central, South, and East Districts. He also oversaw investigations in the Crimes Against Persons Unit and served in the Special Operations Division.
As Captain of the Patrol Division, Captain Faulkner has been responsible for overseeing Patrol Operations, the K9 Unit, the Anaheim Detention Facility, and Air Support.
Captain Faulkner is a graduate of the Sherman Block Supervisory Leadership Institute (Class 298), and the Senior Management Institute for Police through the Police Executive Research Forum (Session 92). He maintains professional affiliations with the International Association of Chiefs of Police (IACP), the California Police Chiefs Association (Cal Chiefs), the Peace Officers Research Association of California (PORAC), the California Peace Officers' Association (CPOA), and PERF.
  

  4. ![Lorenzo Glenn](https://pd.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=136)
####  Lorenzo Glenn 
Captain
Captain Lorenzo Glenn began his law enforcement career in 1991 after graduating from Golden West College Police Academy (Class 103). He initially served with the Westminster Police Department, working in patrol until joining the Anaheim Police Department in December 1993.
As a police officer, Captain Glenn held a variety of assignments including Patrol, Gang Enforcement, Street Narcotics, Traffic, and Auto Theft investigations. He was promoted to sergeant in 2003 and went on to serve in Patrol, the Tourist Oriented Policing Detail (TOP), Internal Affairs, Administrative Services, Community Services, and K-9. He also launched the Department's Peer Support Team in 2012, reflecting his long-standing commitment to officer wellness and resiliency.
In 2015, Captain Glenn was promoted to lieutenant and served as a Watch Commander and Traffic Section Commander. He later held roles as District Commander for the East and Central Districts, and subsequently, the West District, where he also oversaw the Homeless Outreach Team (HOT) and the Psychiatric Emergency Response Team (PERT). He later managed the Anaheim Detention Facility and Air Support Unit.
Captain Glenn currently serves as the Commanding Officer of the Operations Support Division, which includes the Department's District Policing Teams, Community Engagement, Traffic, Youth Services, and Homeless and Mental Health Outreach.
A lifelong learner, Captain Glenn holds a Bachelor's Degree in Sociology from the University of Montana, where he played college football as a student-athlete. He earned a Master's Degree in Emergency Services Administration from California State University, Long Beach in 2008, and most recently, a Master's Degree in Psychology with an emphasis in Human Factors from Grand Canyon University in 2024.
Captain Glenn is a graduate of the Sherman Block Supervisory Leadership Institute (Class of 2007), the California POST Command College (Class 65), and the FBI National Academy (Session 291). He is a former President of the Public Safety Peer Support Association (2016 to 2021) and is an active member of several professional organizations, including the International Association of Chiefs of Police (IACP), the California Police Chiefs Association (Cal Chiefs), the Police Executive Research Forum (PERF), and the National Organization of Black Law Enforcement Executives (NOBLE).
  

  5. ![Eric Trapp](https://pd.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=137)
####  Eric Trapp 
Captain
Captain Eric Trapp began his law enforcement career as a Police Explorer with the Newport Beach Police Department while in high school. In 1995, he was hired by the Anaheim Police Department and graduated from Class 124 of the Orange County Sheriff's Academy.
As a police officer, Captain Trapp served in a variety of assignments, including Primary Response Officer, Field Training Officer, and as part of the Resort Policing Detail. He was later promoted to detective and was assigned to the Sexual Assault / Family Crimes Detail. He was also a member of the Department's Special Weapons and Tactics (SWAT) Team and served as an instructor for the Orange County Sheriff's Department Advanced Officer Training Program. In addition, he contributed to multiple advisory committees, including the North Orange County Regional Occupational Program.
Upon promotion to sergeant, Captain Trapp held several leadership assignments, including as the detective sergeant in the Family Crimes Detail, investigator in Internal Affairs, and the Department's Public Information Officer. He also served as the Executive Officer for the Patrol Division and developed and managed the Department's Critical Infrastructure Protection Program.
As a lieutenant, Captain Trapp served in a broad range of assignments, including Patrol Watch Commander, District Commander, Gang Suppression and Intervention Commander, Public Information Officer, Property Crimes Section Commander, Personnel and Training Commander, Youth Services Section Commander, and Resort Commander. He also led the Department's Tactical Response Group, overseeing SWAT, Snipers, Tactical Negotiators, Mounted Enforcement, and the Mobile Field Force.
As a captain, he has commanded the Operations Support Division, overseeing District Policing Teams, Mental Health and Homeless Outreach initiatives, and both the Traffic and Youth Services Sections. He currently serves as the Commanding Officer of the Investigations Division, managing all investigative and undercover units within the Department, along with the Forensic Services Detail. He continues to lead the Department's Tactical Response Group.
Captain Trapp holds a Bachelor of Science in Criminal Justice from California State University, Long Beach, and a Master of Science in Leadership and Management from the University of La Verne. He is a graduate of Session 283 of the FBI National Academy, the Police Executive Research Forum's Senior Management in Policing program, and has completed executive education coursework at the Harvard Kennedy School of Government.
Captain Trapp and his wife, who is also a sergeant with the Anaheim Police Department, reside in the City of Anaheim.
  

  6. ![Kerrstyn Vega](https://pd.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=138)
####  Kerrstyn Vega 
Police Support Services Administrator
Kerrstyn Vega began her public service career with the Anaheim Police Department in 2006 as a Records Specialist in the Records Bureau. Prior to joining the Department, she managed a retail photography studio, where she gained valuable experience in operations, customer service, and team leadership.
Over the years, Kerrstyn has advanced through a range of civilian leadership roles across multiple areas of the Department. Her assignments have included serving as a Senior Office Specialist and Staff Analyst in the Homeland Security and Emergency Management Bureau, where she supported critical preparedness and response initiatives. She later transitioned to the Budget and Finance Division as a Staff Analyst and Administrative Analyst, where she was eventually promoted to Administrative Services Manager.
In her current role as Police Support Services Manager, Kerrstyn oversees a wide portfolio of operational support functions, including the Budget and Finance Bureau (with oversight of the Payroll Unit), the Records Section (including Property and Evidence), the Communications Section, the Department's Information Technology operations, and fleet and facility management.
Kerrstyn holds a Bachelor of Science degree in Criminal Justice Administration from California State University Dominguez Hills and a Master of Science degree in Homeland Security from San Diego State University.
She has completed several notable leadership programs, including the POST Civilian Management Course, the FBI-LEEDA Command Leadership Course, the City of Anaheim Profession of Management Course, the Harvard University Performance Leadership Course, and the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department Leadership Development Course. She is also a member of the National Homeland Security Association.
