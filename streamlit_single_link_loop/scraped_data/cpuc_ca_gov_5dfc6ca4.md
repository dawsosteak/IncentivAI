ADMINISTRATIVE LAW JUDGE RULING
* * *
1. The Notice of Intent (NOI) is rejected as premature. The NOI has not demonstrated that the NOI was timely filed (Part I(B)) for the following reason(s): A prehearing conference was held on December 5, 2014. Sierra Club submitted the NOI beyond the 30 days after the prehearing conference but soon after September 22, 2015, when D.15-09 022 issued. In its motion to file the NOI, Sierra Club asserts that the Commission should grant the NOI as timely. The filer explains that D.15-09-022 expanded the scope of this proceeding and Sierra Club plans to participate on new issues described in that decision. According to the provisions of ¬ß 1804(a)(1), a procedure for filing new or revised NOIs where new issues so emerged has to be determined by the Commission. No such procedure has been determined in this proceeding. 2. The Administrative Law Judge (ALJ) provides the following guidance: D.15-09-022 provides that a revised scoping memo will be forthcoming (D.15-09-022 at 23). Rule 17.1(b) allows filing of amended NOIs within 15 days after the issuance of the scoping memo. Sierra Club may file an amended NOI within the time frame set in Rule 17.1(b) or in the revised scoping memo if it provides a different procedure for filing new or revised NOIs. IT IS RULED that: 1. The Notice of Intent is rejected as premature.
* * *
2. Sierra Club may file an amended NOI within the time frame set in Rule 17.1(b) or in the revised scoping memo, as set forth.
