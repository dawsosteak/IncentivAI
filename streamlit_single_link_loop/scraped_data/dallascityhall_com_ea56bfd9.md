Page Content
Making a plan is the second step to prepare for a disaster. When making a plan, consider everyone in your household. Start by creating an [Emergency Plan](https://knowhat2do.com/make-a-plan/emergency-plan/) that accounts for all members of your family.  You should include[ Infants & Young Children](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/officeemergencymanagement/Pages/Infants-and-Young-Children.aspx), [Seniors](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/officeemergencymanagement/Pages/Seniors.aspx), and those who may have [Access and Functional Needs](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/officeemergencymanagement/Pages/Access-and-Functional-Needs.aspx). [Pets](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/officeemergencymanagement/Pages/pets.aspx) should also be included into your plan. 
Visit the [Family Communication Plan](https://www.ready.gov/collection/family-communication-plan) to include creating a plan on how all members of your family will communicate in the event of a power or phone outage. 
Click the videos below to learn more about making a plan.
