#  Battery Storage 
Battery Storage provides Anaheim Public Utilities customers with the economic and environmental benefits while accelerating the adoption of renewable energy in your home by storing energy when the sun is out and using it when it's not. Battery Storage also allows a certain level of back-up power during a planned or unforeseen power outage.
##    
Installation/Efficiency Guidelines 
  * Must be UL Certified
  * Energy Storage system must have minimum of 5kWh capacity 
  * Eligible Energy Storage Unit installation must be hardwired and part of an existing solar PV system
  * Energy Storage Unit shall be capable of storing energy produced from electrical energy, such as, solar PV energy, wind energy or some other residential produced electricity
  * Customer agrees to be on a [Time of Use](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/25947/Developmental-Schedule-D-TOU-2-050119) rate and [MyPower Savings Program](https://www.anaheim.net/5920/myPower-Savings) for at least 12 months
  * Please see program application for additional program guidelines 


## Rebate Amount
Up to $3,000 per household   
Guidelines on how to [apply](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/70755/Battery-Storage-Rebate-Application_552026).
**Effective July 1, 2026****,**   
the maximum rebate amount will be   
up to $1,500 per household.
## Customer Owned Solar Energy
Anaheim has supported more than 3,500 customers installing solar throughout the city. 
Beginning January 1, 2021, Anaheim Public Utilities will be transitioning to Net Energy Metering (NEM) 2.0, which is designed to offer the most benefit to customers who also install an energy storage system or are able to change their daily energy use to align with the needs of the grid. 
NEM programs are designed to credit electric customers for excess energy produced by their solar panels and delivered back on the electric grid. For more information about Anaheim's NEM 2.0 and customer owned solar, please [click here](https://www.anaheim.net/636).
