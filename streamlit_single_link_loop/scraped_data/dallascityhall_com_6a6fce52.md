For more than 35 years the City of Dallas Women, Infants, and Children (WIC) Program has served Dallas County by helping pregnant women, new mothers and their young children to stay healthy.  

![Women, Infants and Children Logo](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/community-care/PublishingImages/Pages/WIC/wic-logo-rgb.jpg)
  * Important Notice: Currently limiting in person contact. Read More.
Please add description and details  



This program is funded by a grant through the Texas Department of State Health Services. The City of Dallas WIC Program currently serves more than 75,000 people each month in a total of 16 clinics located throughout Dallas County. Many of our clinics are open six days a week and they are all eager to serve.
To qualify for the City of Dallas WIC Program, you must meet income guidelines, live in Dallas County and have a nutritional need. The program is available without regard to race, color, language, sex or national origin. U.S. citizenship is not a requirement for program participation.
Our mission is to deliver world class service in order to impact the lives of Dallas families through nutrition and breastfeeding education and support.

## Frequently Asked Questions  

  * What is WIC?
WIC is a Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants and Children. It is federally funded by the United States Department of Agriculture.  

  

  * How is WIC funded?
WIC is funded by an appropriation from Congress to the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA). The USDA allocates monies to the various States and Territories (American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico and the US Virgin Islands) Health Departments. Various local WIC agencies receive funds based on the number of WIC participants they serve.  

WIC is also funded, in part, by private industry. WIC generates revenue through competitive bidding on some of the foods the WIC program provides. This is inclusive of a rebate.  

  * How long has WIC been around?
The WIC Program was started as a pilot program in Pineville, Kentucky in 1974 and was adopted as a major, nation-wide program in 1975.  

  * Where is WIC available?
WIC is available in all 50 States and in the United States territories of American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico and the US Virgin Islands.  

  * What is the connection between the City of Dallas and the WIC Program?
The City of Dallas administers the WIC Program through a grant contract with the Texas Department of State health Services. This WIC/City of Dallas partnership developed when the WIC Program was first initiated in the state of Texas in 1974.  

  * What does WIC provide?
  

    * Electronic Benefit Transfer for nutritious food (EBT card)
    * Nutrition classes
    * Individual nutrition counseling
    * Breastfeeding support
    * Community referrals
