#  Sustainability Education Center 
## About the Sustainability Education Center:
The Sustainability Education Center (SEC) is Anaheim Public Utilities’ new flagship educational demonstration center that incorporates technology, sustainability, and workforce development programs. The SEC supports Anaheim customers through hands-on learning in energy and water, helps transition the community to a lower-carbon future, and continues our efforts to support local students with career development.
Located at 888 S. Rose Place, the facility is centrally located and close to dozens of schools within several miles of the site. 
The LEED Silver building is 8,700 square feet, and includes a bioswale designed to capture rainwater, drought-tolerant landscaping with drip irrigation, EV charging stations, high-efficiency space conditioning, 17 kW of rooftop solar, and a SmartFlower solar PV system that tracks the sun during daylight hours. The facility features a Utility Learning Lab that has flexible space and seating, an exhibit hall, and an outdoor plaza.
The exhibit hall features immersive exhibits that showcase the journey of Anaheim’s water and power. Students and adults alike can explore how electricity and water are delivered to homes, learn about renewable energy resources, and better understand regional water supplies.
The construction was performed by Oltmans Construction, and the design and engineering were performed by SVA Architects. The exhibits were developed by K2 Design & Fabrication.
The Sustainability Education Center represents a major step forward in serving the community, offering a place where residents and students can connect with their local utility in a meaningful way, and builds upon the inspiration that came from parents interested in finding rewarding careers for their children right here in Anaheim.

## Learning Experiences and Exhibits at the SEC:
The SEC features highly interactive, engaging, and educational exhibits that highlight: 
  * Energy and water efficiency measures and strategies
  * Solar and other renewable technologies
  * Electric vehicle (EV) charging stations
  * Battery storage
  * Water conservation & drought-tolerant garden displays
  * STEM-based student programs
  * Job skills training in green jobs and utility-themed career opportunities
  * Demonstrations on how water and electricity get to Anaheim homes and businesses
  * Exhibits integrate LED displays that can be easily updated and allow for translation into multiple languages


## Grants:
Anaheim Public Utilities received two grants for the Sustainability Education Center. The first grant was secured through an appropriation from the office of Congressmember Lou Correa of up to $3 million. Grant funds are utilized for educational and workforce development equipment and services.
The second is a micro-grant received from the American Public Power Association. This grant supports the development of virtual tools utilized by local educators for their students, as well as public power utilities across the country.
