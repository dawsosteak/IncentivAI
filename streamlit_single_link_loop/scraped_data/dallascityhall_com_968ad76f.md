## FAQS  

[View All FAQs](https://dallascityhall.com/government/Boards-and-Commissions/Landmark-Commission/Pages/faq.aspx)
  * What is a Certificate of Appropriateness?
A Certificate of Appropriateness (CA) is a permit to allow construction, demolition, or alteration of any structure or site that is located in a [designated landmark district](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/sustainabledevelopment/historicpreservation/Pages/landmark_districts.aspx) or is designated as [a landmark site](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/sustainabledevelopment/historicpreservation/Pages/designated_landmark_structures.aspx). A Certificate of Appropriateness does not replace a building permit, which must be obtained separately [if required for your project.](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/sustainabledevelopment/buildinginspection/Pages/building_inspection_faqs.aspx#A20)  

  * What is the process for Landmark Commission application review?
Review the Landmark Commission's application review process [here](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/sustainabledevelopment/historicpreservation/Pages/Landmark-Review-Process.aspx).  

  * Who should I contact regarding the status of a Landmark Commission review
See staff listing [here](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/sustainabledevelopment/historicpreservation/Pages/Contacts.aspx).
