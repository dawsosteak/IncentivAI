#  Recruiting 
Welcome to the Anaheim Police Department recruitment page. This will be your resource for information regarding the application process, testing, and other clarifying steps.
Email a Recruiter today to get more information.
  1. [Police Officer - Lateral](https://pd.anaheim.net/160/Recruiting#tabc9bb7ea6-7fbe-4a8d-93a1-29df83713c51_0)


To be considered a Police Officer - Lateral candidate, you must meet the following qualifications:
  * Be currently employed as a Police Officer with a California law enforcement agency.
  * Successfully completed the probationary period in your current or former agency.
  * Possess a valid CA Commission on Peace Officer Standards and Training (POST) Basic Certificate or higher.
  * Possess a valid California Driver's License.

  1. [Police Officer - Trainee](https://pd.anaheim.net/160/Recruiting#tabc9bb7ea6-7fbe-4a8d-93a1-29df83713c51_1)


A Police Officer Trainee applicant must meet the following qualifications:
  * Possess a valid California Driver's License by the date of appointment.
  * A minimum of 21 years of age upon completion of the academy training.
  * Graduated from an accredited high school or successfully completed the GED (General Educational Development) test.
  * Applicants must be legally authorized to work in the United States under federal law.
  * Be physically fit to perform the essential functions, including corrected or uncorrected vision of 20 / 20 or better; physical strength and agility to perform foot pursuits and physical restraint of suspects.

  1. [Corrections Officer](https://pd.anaheim.net/160/Recruiting#tabc9bb7ea6-7fbe-4a8d-93a1-29df83713c51_2)


A Corrections Officer applicant must meet the following qualifications:
  * Experience in personal protection methods.
  * Knowledge of physical restraint techniques, detention facility laws and codes; record keeping procedures, and basic computer knowledge.
  * The ability to successfully complete State requirements of peace officer training and firearms training as per Penal Code Sections 830.55/832; successfully complete the Standards and Training for Corrections Core course, in a full stress environment; as well as successfully complete the facility training officer program.
  * The ability to effectively read and write; communicate verbally and in writing; think and speak logically; understand complex reports and training materials; demonstrate firearms proficiency; act quickly and accurately in emergency situations; deal effectively with cultural and social conflict; demonstrate leadership skills; have sound observation skills; interpersonal sensitivity; confront and solve problems.
  * Possess a valid Class C California Driver's License by date of appointment.
  * The ability to work any shift, including nights, weekends, and holidays.
  * Applicants must be legally authorized to work in the United States under federal law.
  * **Note** : Employees will be assigned to numerous training programs, including the Standards and Training for Corrections (STC) Core Course and P.C. 832 powers of arrest and firearms training. Employees must also obtain and maintain a valid Level III module certificate from a California POST-approved reserve academy by the end of the one-year probationary period. 
    * Any applicant who has not successfully completed the STC Core Course or Supplemental Core Course must pass the STC – Adult Corrections Officer entry-level selection examination. All required training programs must be successfully completed within the one-year probationary period.

  1. [Police Dispatcher I & II](https://pd.anaheim.net/160/Recruiting#tabc9bb7ea6-7fbe-4a8d-93a1-29df83713c51_3)


A Police Dispatcher I applicant must meet the following qualifications:
  * Experience performing high-volume public contact work.
  * Knowledge of English usage and spelling; record-keeping methods; principles of human relations; and basic safety precautions and procedures.
  * The ability to learn the functions of the position; act quickly in an emergency and adopt an effective course of action; work under stress, dealing with emergency situations; speak clearly and concisely; listen to and record information clearly and accurately; remember numerous details; read, understand and apply moderately difficult materials; understand and carry out oral and written instructions; operate a computer keyboard to enter, receive and manipulate associated data.
  * Type a minimum of 50 words per minute without errors.
  * Possess a valid California Driver's License by the date of appointment.
  * The ability to work any shift, including nights, weekends, and holidays.


[Complete job description for Police Dispatcher I](https://www.governmentjobs.com/careers/anaheim/classspecs/795517?keywords=police%2520officer&pagetype=classSpecificationshttps://www.governmentjobs.com/careers/anaheim/classspecs/795517?keywords=police%2520officer&pagetype=classSpecifications)
In addition to the qualifications listed, a Police Dispatcher II applicant must meet the following qualifications:
  * Journey-level experience operating communications equipment or equivalent public contact work, preferably involving emergency situations or police related services.
  * Knowledge of English usage and spelling; record-keeping methods; principles of human relations; and appropriate safety precautions and procedures.
  * The ability to act quickly in an emergency and adopt an effective course of action; work under stress, dealing with emergency situations; speak clearly and concisely; listen to and record information clearly and accurately; remember numerous details; read, understand, and apply moderately difficult materials; receive confident information and maintain confidentiality; understand and carry out oral and written instructions.
  * Type a minimum of 50 words per minute without errors.
  * Possess a valid California Driver's License by the date of appointment.
  * The ability to work any shift, including nights, weekends, and holidays.
  * Possess a valid Peace Officer Standards and Training (POST) Public Safety Dispatcher Basic Certificate or higher.

  1. [Traffic Control Assistant](https://pd.anaheim.net/160/Recruiting#tabc9bb7ea6-7fbe-4a8d-93a1-29df83713c51_4)


The Traffic Control Assistant position is a flexible part-time role that supports the Anaheim Police Department during major events, traffic collisions, power outages, and other incidents throughout the city. Traffic Control Assistants help direct vehicular and pedestrian traffic to support safe and efficient traffic flow.
A Traffic Control Assistant must meet the following qualifications:
  * Possess a valid California Driver’s License by the date of appointment.
  * Be legally authorized to work in the United States under federal law.
  * Be available to work assigned shifts, including weekdays, nights, weekends, holidays, and on-call assignments as required. Most assignments occur during evenings and weekends between 5:00 PM and midnight.
  * Be able to safely direct vehicular and pedestrian traffic during special events, traffic collisions, power outages, and other incidents throughout the city.
  * Have the ability to communicate effectively with the public, follow instructions, maintain composure during stressful situations, and work independently.
  * Be able to set up and remove traffic control equipment including cones, signs, barricades, and flares.
  * Possess strong customer service skills and the ability to prepare written reports and maintain records.

  1. [Police Cadet](https://pd.anaheim.net/160/Recruiting#tabc9bb7ea6-7fbe-4a8d-93a1-29df83713c51_5)


The Police Cadet program is a flexible part-time college work program designed to support students preparing for a career in law enforcement, with scheduling that works around school commitments whenever possible.
Cadets assist with a wide range of duties throughout the department, including report writing, fingerprinting, evidence handling, clerical support, vehicle shuttling, and customer service functions.
Police Cadet applicants must meet the following qualifications:
  * Possess a valid California Driver’s License by the date of appointment.
  * Be at least 18 years of age.
  * Have graduated from an accredited high school or successfully completed the GED (General Educational Development) test.
  * Be currently enrolled in a minimum of six (6) semester units at an accredited college or university and maintain a minimum 2.0 GPA.
  * Be legally authorized to work in the United States under federal law.
  * Be available to work a variety of shifts, including evenings, weekends, and holidays.
  * Have the ability to interact professionally with the public, complete reports, utilize computer systems, and support a variety of police department operations.
  * CPR and First Aid certification is required within 12 months of hire.
