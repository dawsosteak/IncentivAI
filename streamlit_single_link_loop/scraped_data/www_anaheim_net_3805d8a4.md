#  Residential Rates 
Did you know that the time of day that you charge your electric vehicle (EV) can impact your electricity bill? Energy used during peak demand times is the most expensive time for Anaheim Public Utilities to buy, and when most people get home from work or school and use electricity. Simple habit changes can help you reduce costs, and so can the right electric rate.   
  
So, when should you charge your EV?  
  
**The best time to charge your EV is during oﬀ-peak hours, around the time when you go to sleep because it helps reduce stress on the electric grid system.**
![EV Clock](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=49632)
**Which rate should I choose? The Standard Domestic Rate or Domestic Time-of-Use Rate?**
Most homes today are served under the Standard Domestic Rate. Since the average home uses 29 kWh in a day, the average home in Anaheim spends 21.49¢ per kWh for EV charging.
The Domestic Time-Of-Use Rate can help you lower costs if you use electricity during off-peak times of the day. If you charge your EV after 9 pm, throughout the morning, and up to 4:00 pm, the Domestic Time-Of-Use Rate can help you save money, since costs range between 12.00¢ - 16.65¢ per kWh used during this time.
By making a small change to when you charge your EV, you can save with the Domestic Time-of-Use Rate.

Currently, you are most likely on the Domestic Residential Rate, in which your use of electricity is NOT a rate factor. However, the more electricity you use past your lifeline daily allowance of 10 kWh, the more your utility bill will increase. This rate applies to the usage of your entire home, with no separate meter.
