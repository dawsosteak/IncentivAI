# ​​​Internships and Externships  

The Dallas City Attorney’s Office offers summer internship opportunities for law students, as well as a limited number of externship placements during the fall and spring semesters. All inquiries should be directed to Ayeh Powers, Managing Attorney, at attresumes@dallas.gov. We are currently accepting applications for Summer 2026 internship positions. Please use the link below for additional information. We appreciate your interest in the Dallas City Attorney’s Office internship and externship programs.​  

  

[Summer 2026 CAO Internship Information](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/city-attorney/Documents/CAO%20Summer%202026%20Internship%20Program.pdf)
  

Testimonials
  

  * ​Emori R.  

During my internship, I gained hands-on experience across multiple sections, including Economic and Community Development, Torts Litigation, and Employment Law. I conducted in-depth legal research, drafted comprehensive memos, and presented findings, which enhanced my understanding of effective legal practice, professionalism, and ethical considerations. My favorite part was working in the Employment Law Section, where I refined my legal research skills and contributed to the team's success with well-researched insights. Thank you once again for the invaluable opportunity and support throughout the internship. 
  * ​Gabrielle V.  

My internship at the Dallas City Attorney's Office was an incredibly enriching experience. The office not only allowed me to explore my interests but actively supported placing me in the sections that aligned perfectly with my passion. With numerous legal sections to explore, the office was open to letting interns decide what interested them most. The leadership was exemplary, consistently providing guidance, support, and inspiration while fostering an environment of learning and professional growth. Additionally, the attorneys I shadowed met me exactly where I was in law school, explaining everything in detail and ensuring I never felt lost. I also had the chance to work in the Community Courts Section, experiencing diversion programs that significantly benefit Dallas citizens. Overall, this internship provided a comprehensive and fulfilling experience, making it an ideal environment for any aspiring legal professional, but especially those committed to public service.
  * ​Malvika M.  

Coming from a background where I had minimal exposure to the legal world, being an intern for the Dallas City Attorney’s office was highly beneficial as I got to experience both transactional and litigation work. During my time, attorneys entrusted me with drafting language for an amended use contract and observing witness interviews for trial board proceedings. It was insightful to work alongside attorneys with decades of experience and ask them questions about their work, which all were happy to answer. However, the most exciting part about being an intern is getting a deeper perspective on how Dallas, a city I lived in for most of my life, operates and the many transactions and trials at the Attorney’s office to keep this city flourishing. 
  * ​Michael B.  

I really enjoyed my time at the City Attorneys office, I was able to grow as both a writer and an advocate. While I was assigned to the bankruptcy section, I was able to experience a broad breadth of work by speaking with attorneys outside of my section. The challenging and unique work allowed me to work outside of my comfort zone and then receive feedback. I would recommend any of my friends to work at the Dallas City Attorneys office!
  * ​Will C.  

My internship with the Dallas City Attorney’s Office was an invaluable experience. Beyond working on meaningful projects, I had the opportunity to observe fascinating city hall meetings. The guidance and support from my mentors were exceptional throughout my time there. I not only improved my research and writing skills but also gained a deeper understanding of how a major city like Dallas operates. I cannot overstate how wonderful my experience was.
