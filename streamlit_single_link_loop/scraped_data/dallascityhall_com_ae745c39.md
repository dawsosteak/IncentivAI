### Experience
Ahmad Goree currently serves as Chief of Staff to Dallas City Manager Kimberly Bizor Tolbert. In this role, he works closely with the City Manager to advance City Council priorities and promote collaboration across the Executive Leadership Team. Ahmad oversees engagement with internal and external stakeholders and provides executive leadership for a portfolio of departments, including the Office of Communication and Customer Experience/311, the Office of Government Affairs, and the Mayor and City Council Office, representing a combined budget of $18.6 million and more than 230 employees. 
  

Prior to joining the City of Dallas, Ahmad served as Assistant Director of Program Management and Communications in the Office of Field Operations at the U.S. Small Business Administration (SBA), based in the Dallas–Fort Worth District. In this national role, he advised the SBA Administrator (a Cabinet-level official), Deputy Administrator, and senior agency leaders on field operations, performance management, risk mitigation, and external partnerships. Ahmad led nationwide engagement efforts with elected officials, organizations, educational institutions, and government entities, strengthening the SBA’s brand visibility and expanding its outreach and impact. He also collaborated with SBA program offices to align messaging and enhance agency-wide communications. 
  

Ahmad has been recognized as a Dallas Business Journal and Fort Worth Business Press 40 Under 40 honoree and as the Dallas Black Chamber’s A. Maceo Smith Emerging Leader. ​  

### Education
Ahmad holds a bachelor’s degree in History from Jarvis Christian College and a Master of Liberal Arts in Political Science from Dallas Baptist University. He is currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Urban Planning and Public Policy at the University of Texas at Arlington. He is a proud member of Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity, Incorporated, and the International City Managers Association (ICMA).​
