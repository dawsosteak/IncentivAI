#  Is Your Business Ready to Go Solar? 
![solar commercial](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=42709)
Installing solar panels is a big decision for any business. Anaheim Public Utilities is here to help you go solar at your property. 
If you are planning to install solar panels at your business, please review the checklist below to ensure your system is properly permitted by the City’s Planning and Building Department and meets interconnection requirements from Anaheim Public Utilities.
**Important Note:** Early NEM 1.0 programs in California were intended to encourage private investment in solar and to increase the amount of renewable energy used in California. While early NEM 1.0 programs were successful in meeting these goals, they also had a tendency to shift the cost of maintaining the local grid to non-solar customers. Anaheim Public Utilities is a not-for-profit municipal utility and as such, is required to recover our cost of maintaining the local grid and purchasing power on behalf of all customers. NEM 1.0 customers who are grandfathered will not be placed on NEM 2.0 unless they seek to expand their solar panels, at which time, customers will be placed solely on NEM 2.0 for their entire system.
## Here are nine steps to installing solar for your Anaheim business:
  1. **Right-size your PV system by ensuring your building is as energy efficient as possible.** You can schedule your free [Comprehensive Energy Assessment](https://www.anaheim.net/940) that will help you understand your energy usage by calling 714-765-4250.
  2. **Know how much solar energy is needed.** Obtain your annual energy usage by logging on to your account [online](http://anaheim.net/2567/Manage-My-Account-Online) to check past consumption. If you don’t have online billing, then always feel free to call our customer service staff at 714-765-3300.
  3. **Understand how**[**net energy metering (NEM) 2.0**](http://www.anaheim.net/5587/NEM-20)**works.**
  4. **Decide on payment options: complete purchase, power purchase agreement, or lease.** Research how your choice may affect a future sale of your property. And don’t forget about a warranty for your entire system – not just the solar panels.
  5. **Obtain**[**contractor bids**](https://www.cslb.ca.gov/)**from reputable firms who are****properly**[**licensed**](http://anaheim.net/2659/Business-License)**and insured.** We recommend a minimum of three bids prior to signing a contract. The solar equipment being provided to you should be listed on the state’s [website](https://www.energy.ca.gov/programs-and-topics/programs/solar-equipment-lists).
  6. **Contractor submits plans to the**[**Anaheim Planning & Building**](https://www.anaheim.net/5252/Building-Division)**for a building permit.**
  7. **Construct your solar panel system.**
  8. **Obtain final building permit sign off in order to have a bi-directional and power production meter installed by Anaheim Public Utilities.**

Under NEM 2.0, excess energy produced during the day is best stored on your property instead of being sent back to the grid at the wholesale credit rates offered. With storage, the homeowner manages their production, storage, and how much energy they use from the grid.
  1. [Energy Efficiency](https://www.anaheim.net/6136/Solar-Energy-and-Net-Metering-For-Busine#tab96e4b441-d556-4aff-92b4-c9bc8879cd49_1)


[Reducing your energy use](http://anaheim.net/2532/For-Your-Home) is the easiest way to reduce your electricity bill and by doing so, you can install a smaller less costly solar system. You can schedule your free [Comprehensive Energy Assessment](https://www.anaheim.net/940) that will help you understand your energy usage by calling 714-765-4250.
  1. [Payment Options for Solar](https://www.anaheim.net/6136/Solar-Energy-and-Net-Metering-For-Busine#tab96e4b441-d556-4aff-92b4-c9bc8879cd49_2)


**Purchase System** – Business owner pays cash or with a loan.
**Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)** – Contractor will install and own the solar system and the business owner will purchase the power from the system at a negotiated rate. Be careful of the annual escalator percentage and be aware of any issues that may arise when (if) selling your property. 
**Lease** – Contractor will install and own the solar and the business owner will pay a flat monthly lease rate, just like leasing a car. Be careful of the annual escalator percentage and be aware of any issues that may arise when (if) selling the property. 
  1. [Contractor Selection](https://www.anaheim.net/6136/Solar-Energy-and-Net-Metering-For-Busine#tab96e4b441-d556-4aff-92b4-c9bc8879cd49_3)


Contractors will provide free estimates. You want to ensure the contractor is licensed and their license is currently [valid](https://www.cslb.ca.gov/). Ask your neighbors and friends for references and compare contractors and rates. It’s recommend that you obtain at least three quotes from licensed contractors prior to making a decision. Anaheim Public Utilities cannot recommend contractors as a municipal utility.
