Strengthening and improving our neighborhoods requires collective involvement. One key element in this process is fostering connections with our neighbors. Discover more in this [Know Your Neighbor](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=46050) factoid.
### Why Should You Know Your Neighbor?
Understanding your neighbors is invaluable. They could be a source of companionship or assistance in times of need. Effective communication lays the foundation for building positive relationships and facilitating discussions about mutual concerns later.
### Tools for Communicating with Neighbors
  * Phone Tree: Gather contact information through a [sign-up shee](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/25352/Contact-List-Template-PDF---revised-31117)t to establish a phone tree for swift communication during emergencies.
  * Private Facebook Group: Utilize Facebook's privacy settings to create a group where members can share messages, photos, and files under the administrator's control. This platform also facilitates instant messaging via Facebook Messenger for real-time interactions.
  * Email Distribution Group: Ideal for non-social media users, an email group enables communication and dissemination of neighborhood updates. Consider creating a neighborhood newsletter for internal circulation. 
  * [Nextdoor:](https://nextdoor.com/) Leverage Nextdoor to form a community group, ensuring neighbors stay informed about safety issues and upcoming events.


### Plan a Neighborhood Event - Start a New Tradition!
Fostering a sense of community begins with knowing your neighbors. Once you've established your neighborhood boundaries, consider organizing a neighborhood event to strengthen bonds. Here are some creative ideas from your Neighborhood Services team:
  1. [Walk your Neighborhood](https://www.anaheim.net/6277/Strengthen-Your-Neighborhood#tabde2f0552-8301-44c7-949d-5242086eaf8f_0)


Organizing regular neighborhood walks not only fosters community bonds and keeps you updated on each other's lives but also promotes healthy physical activity. Strategically planned, these walks enable neighbors to identify and report various issues such as malfunctioning streetlights, damaged sidewalks, missing street signs, or areas affected by graffiti or illegal dumping.
By utilizing the [My Anaheim Application](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/42314/My-Anaheim-App-English) on your smartphone, you can easily document these issues by taking photos and providing precise location information. This establishes a clear record of your reports, aiding in the maintenance and improvement of neighborhood livability.
Residents are encouraged to engage with their Neighborhood Services Coordinator by inviting them to join scheduled neighborhood walks. This interactive approach allows you and your neighbors to gain insights into how the city addresses identified issues, as well as opportunities to learn about local government processes and avenues for involvement.
  1. [Organize a Block Party](https://www.anaheim.net/6277/Strengthen-Your-Neighborhood#tabde2f0552-8301-44c7-949d-5242086eaf8f_1)


Hosting a block party is a fantastic way to welcome new neighbors and reconnect with familiar faces you may not have seen in a while. Add a seasonal theme to make it memorable, like a Fourth of July celebration, a summer barbecue, or a holiday potluck.
When planning your event, visit the City’s [Block Party](https://www.anaheim.net/365/Block-Partys) page for helpful tips on organizing a successful gathering. You may also want to invite your Neighborhood Services Coordinator to join—they can share valuable insights into Neighborhood Services, provide useful information, and offer resources to enhance your community’s well-being.
  1. [Host a Neighborhood Watch Meeting](https://www.anaheim.net/6277/Strengthen-Your-Neighborhood#tabde2f0552-8301-44c7-949d-5242086eaf8f_2)


Establishing a [Neighborhood Watch](https://www.anaheim.net/2601/Neighborhood-Watch) creates a valuable opportunity for neighbors to connect, receive training on recognizing and reporting suspicious activity, and implement effective crime prevention strategies. By collaborating with law enforcement, communities can work together to reduce crime in their area. Consider hosting a Neighborhood Watch meeting and inviting your local community policing team to share insights on current crime trends and statistics. Additionally, you can arrange a Crime Prevention Through Environmental Design (CPTED) assessment to further strengthen the safety and security of your neighborhood.
  1. [Organize a Neighborhood Clean-up](https://www.anaheim.net/6277/Strengthen-Your-Neighborhood#tabde2f0552-8301-44c7-949d-5242086eaf8f_3)


[Neighborhood Clean-up](https://www.anaheim.net/1289/Neighborhood-Clean-Up) campaigns offer Anaheim neighborhoods the opportunity to access 30-yard trash bins, provided **free of charge** once a year. These bins are invaluable for disposing of large items that cannot be accommodated in regular trash containers.
Collaborate with your Neighborhood Services Coordinator to select suitable locations within your neighborhood to host a bin over a designated weekend. Additionally, take advantage of our complimentary flyer creation and printing services, provided at no cost to you.
  

  1. [Train for Emergency Preparedness](https://www.anaheim.net/6277/Strengthen-Your-Neighborhood#tabde2f0552-8301-44c7-949d-5242086eaf8f_4)


As a neighborhood, it’s essential for families to be prepared for natural disasters and to know how to support one another while awaiting rescue services. Major disasters can delay emergency assistance for days or even weeks due to significant damage control efforts. For guidance on [disaster preparedness](https://www.anaheim.net/851/Disaster-Awareness-Preparedness), contact your [Fire and Rescue Department](https://www.anaheim.net/6096/Fire-Rescue) at (714) 765-4000 and request information.
  1. [Attend or Apply for a Board or Commission](https://www.anaheim.net/6277/Strengthen-Your-Neighborhood#tabde2f0552-8301-44c7-949d-5242086eaf8f_5)


Serving on a [board or commission](https://www.anaheim.net/176/Boards-Commissions) is a great way of participate in civic life. As a member, you can share your expertise while being directly involved in shaping your local community. The City of Anaheim has a number of boards and commissions that perform a valuable service by providing a means by which the City Council can obtain the advice, opinions, and recommendations of City residents and other members of the community. Your Neighborhood Services team encourages you to apply online when you are ready to step up and serve your community to better craft its future.
  1. [Attend a Civic Engagement Meeting ](https://www.anaheim.net/6277/Strengthen-Your-Neighborhood#tabde2f0552-8301-44c7-949d-5242086eaf8f_6)


Get involved in your city's government! Volunteer to attend your [Neighborhood District Community Meeting](https://www.anaheim.net/1332/District-Community-Meetings), hosted by the Neighborhood Services Section, and join City Council meetings to stay informed about decisions that impact your community. City Council meetings are held in person on Tuesdays at 5 p.m., you can view the calendar [here](https://www.anaheim.net/173/City-Council), or you can watch them live [online](https://www.anaheim.net/2142/Council-Meeting-Video).
