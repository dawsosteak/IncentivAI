#  Groundwater Treatment Program 
![LV 08232023 Drone-1-ed](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=51583)
Since 2020, Anaheim has been a leader in addressing per- and polyfluoralkyl substances (PFAS), a group of synthetic chemicals found in firefighting foams, stain and water resistant products (such as clothing, carpet, and nonstick pans), and many cosmetic products. These products have made their way into the local groundwater and are removed using treatment technologies, such as the one implemented by Anaheim. 
These treatment plants provide the community with reliable and sustainable drinking water that meets or exceeds all federal and state drinking water quality regulations. Anaheim operates 19 groundwater wells to meet approximately 80 to 85% of the demand, with the remainder being supplied by Metropolitan Water District (MWD). Orange County Water District (OCWD) manages the groundwater basin.
This program is being implemented over multiple phases. The first two phases, completed in 2024, include nine treatment plants with centralized and localized treatment systems. The third phase is currently in design and includes four additional treatment plants with similar groundwater treatment plants. The treatment plants include filtration for particulates and ion exchange resin treatment vessels to remove PFAS.
This program helps to manage long-term costs to maintain affordable bills for customers, while providing high quality drinking water for Anaheim. To help recover costs for the program, Anaheim (1) is being reimbursed by the Orange County Water District (OCWD) for applicable capital and a portion of the maintenance costs, (2) has been awarded state grants, and (3) is one of dozens of water agencies who have been involved in class action lawsuits against the manufacturers of PFAS chemicals.
If you would like more information on PFAS, please visit[ anaheim.net/pfos](https://www.anaheim.net/3302). 
### Program Benefits Include:
  * Increased Groundwater Availability
  * Improved Groundwater Quality
  * Improved Operability and Lifecycle Value of Assets
  * Improved Reliability and Efficiency


* * *
### Program Schedule:
  * Phase A Construction: Completed in 2024
  * Phase B Construction: Completed in 2024
  * Phase C Construction: Fall 2026 – Fall 2028 (anticipated timeline)
