### Commercial Promoter Registration 
#### A Commercial Promoter (Registrant) means a person engaged in the business of commercial promotion, publicizing, planning, or production of a promoted event who receives all or a percentage of revenues from the sale of alcohol, food, beverages, fees charged to vendors, or fees charged to the public for admission. **A promoter can be an individual, a business, or even a venue.**
  

#### A Commercial Promoter registration expires two years from the date of registration. Registrants may renew their registration for the next two-year- period before the expiration of the current period, but not before 30 days prior to expiration. The Eproval system will email you a reminder to renew your registration one month before it expires. 
  

## Procedure
###  1Register as a Commercial Promoter
Complete the registration form and identify yourself as an individual promoter or a venue.  

###  2Pay Registration Fee
An invoice will be emailed to you once the registration form is submitted and deemed complete.  

###  3Safety Plan
You may be asked to submit a General Safety Plan. The safety plan phase of the registration form will lead you through all of the required steps to begin the review process.  
  

###  4Registration Number Issuance
Once your registration has been reviewed, considered complete and your payment has been received, you will be emailed a copy of your promoter registration. This PDF document will include your promoter registration number.

## When should I apply?
## 5 Business Days
Complete and submit the registration form no less than 5 business days prior to doing business in the City of Dallas.
