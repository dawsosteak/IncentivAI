#  California LifeLine Program
California LifeLine provides discounted home phone and cell phone services to qualified households. 
[CA LifeLine Fact Sheet](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/-/media/cpuc-website/divisions/communications-division/documents/lifeline/fact-sheets/ca-lifeline-fact-sheet-fy-24-25.pdf)
**California LifeLine Home Broadband Pilot Update:**
The Commission recently approved Decision 25-08-050, which will provide up to $20 per month for standalone broadband and up to $30 per month for bundled broadband and voice. For current LifeLine providers who wish to participate in the pilot, we encourage you to file an Advice Letter (please refer to [D.25-08-050](https://docs.cpuc.ca.gov/PublishedDocs/Published/G000/M578/K668/578668809.PDF) for advice letter requirements). For non-LifeLine providers registered with the CPUC who wish to participate, we encourage you to file an advice letter requesting to become a LifeLine provider and include the requirements established in D.25-08-05. Staff will issue a resolution outlining a process for providers who do not have a Certificate of Public Convenience and Necessity (CPCN) or a Wireless ID Registration (WIR). 
##  Overview 
The California LifeLine Program provides discounts on home phone and cell phone services to qualified households. The California LifeLine discounts help consumers lower the cost of their phone bills. Only one discount per household is allowed (except for teletypewriter users and for Deaf and Disabled Telecommunications Program participants). Call a participating phone company and ask for the California LifeLine discounts to start the application process. Go to “Provider Search” at [www.californialifeline.com](http://www.californialifeline.com/ "www.californialifeline.com") to learn about the approved California LifeLine providers and cell phone plans.
California LifeLine was established by the Commission in compliance with the Moore Universal Telephone Service, [Public Utilities Code § 871](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displayText.xhtml?lawCode=PUC&division=1.&title=&part=1.&chapter=4.&article=8. "Public Utilities Code § 871") providing discounted basic residential (landline) telephone services to low-income households.
California LifeLine is funded by an [all-end-user surcharge](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/internet-and-phone/telecommunications-surcharges-and-user-fees/surcharge-rates) billed and collected by telecommunications carriers which, in turn, remit the surcharge monies to a financial institution as directed by the Commission or its representatives. Procedures for the administration of LifeLine are outlined in [General Order (GO) 153-A](https://docs.cpuc.ca.gov/PublishedDocs/Published/G000/M588/K780/588780806.PDF).
##  Contacts 
#### Your Phone Company
  * Contact your phone company if you want to be on the California LifeLine Program. 
  * Your phone company can also provide general information about the California LifeLine Program.


#### California LifeLine Call Center
Contact the California LifeLine Call Center for general information about the California LifeLine Program from 7 AM to 7 PM (Pacific Time) from Monday through Friday (except state holidays) at:  
  
1-866-272-0349   
  

#### California LifeLine Administrator
Contact the California LifeLine Administrator for California LifeLine Program application and renewal related questions and concerns from 7 AM to 7 PM (Pacific Time) from Monday through Friday (except state holidays) at:  
  
1-877-858-7463  
  

#### California LifeLine Complaints
For complaints related to the California LifeLine Program, or help resolving application denials (appeals) for program participation, please contact the CPUC’s Consumer Affairs Branch (CAB) at:  
  
1-800-649-7570  
  
Or Online by visiting CAB's webpage [Assistance With LifeLine Billing and Appeals](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/consumer-support/file-a-complaint/utility-complaint/assistance-with-lifeline-billing-and-appeals)  
  

#### California LifeLine Administrator Oversight
Questions about the California LifeLine Program Administrator oversight should be directed to lifeline@cpuc.ca.gov.
