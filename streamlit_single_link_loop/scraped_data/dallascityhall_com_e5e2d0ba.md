#  ​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​Program  
Love Your Block​
The Love Your Block Grant is officially OPEN! 🎉
Have an idea to beautify your neighborhood? Want to bring a community project to life? Now’s your chance! 
[Apply today](https://survey123.arcgis.com/share/81243963a5514e52b775dfa53a4f465a?portalUrl=https://gis.dallascityhall.com/enclaveportal) and help make a positive impact right where you live.
> > Don’t wait — applications are now being accepted! 💚 #2026LoveYourBlock​  
> 
>> # About Love Your Block  
> 
>> ### Love your block is a resident -led grant program that supports neighborhood revitalization projects in cities across the united states.   
> 
>> ##  From transforming vacant lots into community gardens to organizing cleanups, installing public art, and making small neighborhood improvements, the program empowers residents to improve the places they call home.
>>   
> 
>> Love Your Block is supported by the Bloomberg Center for Public Innovation at Johns Hopkins University, which works with local governments to unlock the insight, talent, and energy of residents to drive meaningful community change.
>>   
> 
>> Since launching in 2009, Love Your Block has engaged more than 50,000 community members, improved nearly 4,000 public spaces, removed 6.7 million pounds of trash, and supported projects in 50 U.S. cities across 33 states.​  
> 
>>   
> 
>> ![Responsive image](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/codecompliance/keep-Dallas-beautiful/PublishingImages/Pages/love-your-block/Screenshot%202026-01-13%20153613.png)   
>   
> 
>> ​​​​​
>> ​Application Period:​
>> 📅 February 14, 2026 – March 15, 2026  
> 
>>   
> 
>> ## Award Notifications:
>> Selected applicants will be notified by phone and email during the week of April 6, 2026.  
> 
>> Mini-grants will support projects that contribute to the revitalization of neighborhood and community spaces. Eligible projects may include, but are not limited to:  
> 
>>   * Community cleanups  
> 
>>   * Small home or exterior repairs
>>   * Neighborhood beautification projects
>>   * Public art installations  
> 
>>   * Improvements to shared community spaces  
> 
>> 

>>   
> 
>> ## Eligibility Criteria 
>>   1. Be an individual resident (18+), resident group, or community organization
>>   2. Renters are welcome; property ownership is not required (property owner permission may be required)
>>   3. Submit a project budget of**$2,000**
>>   4. Proposed projects shold benefit and improve your neighborhood 
>>   5. Complete the project by **August 31, 2026**
>>   6. Be willing to collect and document project outcomes​
>>
