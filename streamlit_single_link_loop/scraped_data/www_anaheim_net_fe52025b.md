## City of Anaheim
### [Homeless Housing, Assistance and Prevention (HHAP) Program Regionally Coordinated Homelessness Action Plan](https://ceo.ocgov.com/hhap-5)
The Homeless Housing, Assistance and Prevention (HHAP) Program provides one-time block grant funds to support regional coordination and expand or develop local capacity to address immediate homelessness challenges. HHAP funding is currently administered by the State of California Department of Housing and Community Development (HCD) and is allocated to three jurisdiction types for distribution: Continuums of Care, large cities and counties. To learn more about HHAP visit the State website [here](https://www.hcd.ca.gov/grants-and-funding/programs-active/homeless-housing-assistance-and-prevention-grant-program).
HHAP Program Round 5 (HHAP-6) is authorized by Section 13 Assembly Bill 166, which was signed into law by Governor Gavin Newsom on July 2, 2024.
HHAP-5 intends to sustain existing investments towards long-term sustainability of housing and supportive services and prioritize permanent housing solutions, with a strong focus on regional coordination. As part of the HHAP-6 application process, applicants were required to apply together as a region and create a Regionally Coordinated Homelessness Action Plan that included:
  1. Identification and description of the specific roles and responsibilities of each participating application within the region as they pertain to outreach and site coordination, siting and use of available land, development of interim and permanent housing options, coordinating, connect, and delivering services to individuals experiencing homelessness or at risk of experiencing homelessness within the region;
  2. Key actions the region intends to take to improve system performance measures;
  3. Key actions the region will take to ensure racial and gender equity in service delivery, housing placements, housing retention, and changes to procurement or other means to affirm equitable access to housing and services for groups overrepresented among residents experiencing homelessness;
  4. A description of how the CoC collaborates with cities and counties to address how people served through place-based encampment resolution projects have or will be included in prioritization for permanent housing within the Coordinated Entry System;
  5. Actions to reduce homelessness among individuals exiting institutional settings;
  6. An explanation of the utilization of local, state, and federal funding programs to end homelessness;
  7. A description of how the region is connecting individuals to wrap-around services from all eligible federal, state, and local benefit programs. HHAP-6 also required applicants to engage key stakeholders during the development of the Regionally Coordinated Homelessness Action Plan and have Memorandum of Understanding in place between the eligible jurisdictions in the region.


The Orange County region includes the County of Orange, the Orange County Continuum of Care, the City of Anaheim, the City of Santa Ana, and the City of Irvine. The Orange County region submitted an application for HHAP-6 on August 29. 2025, and has since been awarded by HCD. To view the approved Orange County Regionally Coordinated Homelessness Action Plan click [here](http://:%20https://ceo.oc.gov/sites/ceo/files/2025-09/HHAP%206%20Regional%20Application%20-Santa%20Ana%20AnaheimOrange%20County%20CoC-%20UID%2070_Footer.pdf).
View the presentation from the City of Anaheim Community Listening Session held on July 8, 2025 [here](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/65738/HHAP-6-Public-Sessions-PPT-61025).

## Community Care Response Team
In partnership with City Net, we conduct daily outreach to people experiencing homelessness. Teams are in the field from 7 a.m. to 7:30 p.m. Monday through Friday, rain or shine. CCRT teams help people get into shelter, housing, detox beds and offer other support services. 
[Learn more here](https://www.anaheim.net/5808/Community-Care-Response-Team)
## Anaheim Police Department HALO team
The Anaheim Police Department has a dedicated team of homeless assistance liaison officers who work with the CCRT and Hope Anaheim teams to connect people to services and resources. They play an important role upholding laws and ensuring public safety while working toward a goal of helping people exit homelessness and onto a better, healthier and safer path in life.

The authority also works with our federal and state partners, along with developers to build affordable housing here in Anaheim.
