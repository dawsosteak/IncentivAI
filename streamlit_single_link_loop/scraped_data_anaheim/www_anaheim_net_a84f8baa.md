#  GHAD watering tips 
## About the Santiago Landslide and creation of the Geologic Hazard Abatement District
In 1993, following intense El Nino rains that started in late 1992, a bluff in east Anaheim slid prompting the evacuation of homes and resulting in property damage, emergency remediation work and litigation.
Since then, a largely unseen system of wells, pumps, drains and meters has helped keep land stable and protect homes in the area of the 1993 landslide.
Following litigation and installation of the wells, pumps and drains, the Santiago Geologic Hazard Abatement District, GHAD for short, was formed to oversee and manage the system. The district is a state entity and independent of the city of Anaheim.
It's anticipated that in early 2024, the GHAD will run out of funding to keep the wells, pumps and drains operational. In anticipation of the pumps not working, its recommended residents limit outdoor watering.
