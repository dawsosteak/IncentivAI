The Anaheim Local Housing Trust Fund was created in August 2024 to help finance affordable communities, provide down payments for first-time homebuyers and help those at risk of eviction. 
The fund is designed to receive and invest both public and private funding to support housing in Anaheim. This may include in-leiu fees, state housing grants, federal housing grants and donations from philanthropic partners. 
At the time the ordinance was adopted, the city expected a major initial contribution from the Walt Disney Company. They committed $15 million to the fund in the summer of 2025, with an additional $15 million expected in five years.
The trust fund is meant to help expand affordable housing and support housing programs that serve Anaheim residents. To guide how the funds will be used, our City Council approved program guidelines and funding allocations in May 2025.
Click [here](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/66427/ADOPTED-Anaheim-Local-Housing-Trust-Fund-Program-Guidelines-and-Funding-Allocation-Plan?bidId=) to view the approved program guidelines and funding allocation.
The plan outlines three main areas of investment.
  * Financing affordable housing communities
  * Providing down payment assistance for first-time homebuyers
  * Supporting programs that promote housing stability for Anaheim renters
