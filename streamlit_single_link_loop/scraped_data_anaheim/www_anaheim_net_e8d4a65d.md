#  Pharmaceuticals & Water Supplies 
Pharmaceuticals can enter the environment in a variety of ways. A portion of the medicines we take are not entirely absorbed by our bodies and are excreted into wastewater. Additional pathways pharmaceuticals can be introduced to the environment include: 
  * Flushing leftover medications 
  * Landfill leachate 
  * Wastewater treatment plant discharge   
  



## How you can help!   
  

**Do not flush** expired or unwanted prescription and over-the-counter drugs down the toilet or drain. 
**Pharmaceutical take-back programs** accept unwanted household medicines. These programs offer safe and environmentally protective ways to dispose of them. 
Find locations with secure disposal drop-off locations or where you can obtain mail-back supplies: [Safe Medication Return | Medtakebackcalifornia.org](https://medtakebackcalifornia.org/?address=92805&search-radius=10000&filters%5B0%5D=Drop-off%20location&filters%5B1%5D=Mail-Back%20Supply%20Location)
  

## Groundwater Basin Management 
  

The Orange County Water District (OCWD) is responsible for managing the groundwater basin that provides most of northern and central Orange County’s drinking water, along with managing water supplies from the Santa Ana River and Groundwater Replenishment System (GWRS). 
OCWD has taken proactive measures to ensure high-quality and clean drinking water through extensive monitoring, and by participating in studies, forming work groups, and collaborating with outside agencies to address and study water quality issues, including: 
  * Conducting tracer studies to ensure there is an adequate time before recharged water reaches a municipal water supply well, to allow pharmaceutical compounds to degrade naturally as water travels through rivers, lakes and the subsurface layers of rocks and sand. 
  * Managing the wetlands behind Prado Dam, which provide additional natural degradation of substances in river water prior to it flowing into Orange County. 
  * Ensuring that the Groundwater Replenishment System, which uses purified wastewater for groundwater recharge, includes advanced water treatment techniques that remove pharmaceutical compounds from water. 
    * Technological improvements have allowed laboratories to monitor compounds in water at trace levels – as low as 1 part per trillion. For reference, a part per trillion is equivalent to a single drop in 20 Olympic-size swimming pools.
  * Working with the Santa Ana Watershed Project Authority on several matters pertaining to protection of the watershed and water quality 
  * Implementing a water quality monitoring program to track the presence of substances correlated with wastewater
