Anaheim is a city on the move, with a lot going on in every corner of our city.
The city is working on updating our Transit Master Plan, which will look at public transportation and identify the needs of our city's residents, visitors and workforce as they move in, out and around Anaheim.
We are home to more than 360,000 residents and Anaheim sees more than 25 million visitors every year. In addition, more than 30,000 people work at businesses just in The Anaheim Resort. With recent and planned development across our city, Anaheim is still growing and we expect to see even more people visiting, living and working in our city in years to come.
The growth we have seen in the past decade, coupled with what we expect to see in the future, calls for a look at the status of transit in our city. A lot has changed in Anaheim and our infrastructure, roads and systems must grow with those changes.
The current Transit Master Plan was adopted in 2007. This update, focused on public transit, will:
  * identify existing conditions, needs and challenges
  * analyze demand, key corridors and constraints
  * conduct public outreach and gather feedback from residents, businesses and organizations
  * present recommendations on improvements, priority projects and funding


Questions? Reach out to us at jalcock@anaheim.net.
