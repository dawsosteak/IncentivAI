#  About Anaheim Public Library 
  

#   

## ![](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=26168)
The City Librarian, Audrey A. Lujan welcomes you to the Anaheim Public Library! We are the City's leading information hub with a network of seven library branches serving the city of Anaheim and surrounding communities, plus a [Mobile Library](http://www.anaheim.net/928/Bookmobile) (Bookmobile), a STEAM Van, a book vending machine at [ARTIC](http://www.anaheim.net/3329/ARTIC) transportation center and the [Anaheim Heritage Center](http://www.anaheim.net/2473/Anaheim-Heritage-Center) which houses our local history collection. 
The Anaheim Public Library is the cornerstone of your neighborhood, providing resources and opportunities for all ages. Learn about the engaging programs and unique services offered to you and your family. Your Anaheim Public Library Card gives you access to our digital collection of both Audiobooks and eBooks through the [cloudLibrary](https://ebook.yourcloudlibrary.com/library/AnaheimPublicLibrary/Featured?media=all&src=lib) app. In addition, we offer a variety of [book recommendations](http://anaheim.net/3226/Reading-Recommendations), [digital learning tools](http://anaheim.net/1105/Digital-Resources), and a portal to the [Library Catalog](https://ana.ent.sirsi.net/client/en_US/virtual) where you can access books, historical photos, and track your reading list.

# Mission Statement  

  
Anaheim Public Library promotes literacy, lifelong learning, and a love of reading through traditional print materials as well as virtual services.   
  
Our mission is to promote information through programming and engage our community while providing a safe space for the diverse members of Anaheim to share, thrive, explore, create, enjoy, honor, celebrate, and engage with one another.  
  
_Approved by the Anaheim Public Library Board on April 8, 2019._

# Equity Statement
Anaheim Public Library is committed to achieving equity by making meaningful contributions and helping our community members realize their potential. 
Our library system is working to achieve equity in the neighborhoods we serve by:
  * Continuing and building upon an environment of respect, diversity, and inclusion.
  * Regularly evaluating services, programs, policies, and practices in order to eliminate barriers or structural inequity in favor of improved library access.
  * Collaborating with community leaders in order to identify current challenges and address how to best meet the needs of our community.
  * Encouraging open communication about the issues and values important to our community.
