#  Be Fire Safe 
Properties in or near a fire threat zone are at a higher risk for wildfire damage. Be energy smart and protect your home by taking advantage of rebates on fire-resistant measures, planting approved shade trees, and following any [Anaheim Fire & Rescue](https://www.anaheim.net/5629) recommendations to improve safety in wildfire zones.

## FIRE SAFETY REBATES
Anaheim Public Utilities provides rebates aimed to assist residents in saving money on fire safety upgrades.
  1. [Fire-Resistant Attic Insulation](https://www.anaheim.net/5418/Fire-Safety#tab4a432f92-c5bf-4ec8-ab9f-2eeea9be6ac6_0)


This type of attic insulation is a layer of fiber that reduces the risk of fires starting in the attic while at the same time decrease the energy demands of heating and cooling systems. The effectiveness of this insulation is evaluated by its Class Fire Rating and R-Value, which quantifies heat exchange resistance. 
**Installation/Efficiency Criteria**
  * Class 1 (A) Fire Rating 
  * (Invoice must detail Fire Spread 0-25)
  * R-30 rating or greater


**Incentive Amount**
  * $0.35 per sq. ft.
  * Rebate cap of $750


[Program guidelines and application](https://www.anaheim.net/1481)
![Attic Insulation](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=28826)  
**Helpful Resources**
  * [Insulation Guidelines](https://www.energystar.gov/sites/default/files/asset/document/Technical%20Bulletin%20Achieving%20Grade%201%20Insulation%20in%20Fire-Rated%20Roofs.pdf)
  * [ENERGY STAR® Certified Insulation](https://www.energystar.gov/saveathome/seal_insulate/certified_insulation)
  * [California Fire Marshal’s Building Materials Listing Program](https://calfire.govmotus.org/BMLSearch/Index)


  1. [Fire-Resistant Vents](https://www.anaheim.net/5418/Fire-Safety#tab4a432f92-c5bf-4ec8-ab9f-2eeea9be6ac6_1)


These types of vents are designed to block embers and fire from entering the home through the attic during extreme situations such as wildfires or local vegetation fires.
**Installation/Efficiency Criteria**
  * Installation of Fire Resistant Attic Insulation is recommended
  * Product must be approved under the [California Fire Marshal’s Building Materials Listing Program for use in Wildland Urban Interface Areas](https://osfm.fire.ca.gov/what-we-do/fire-engineering-and-investigations/building-materials-listing)


**Incentive Amount**
  * $350 rebate


[Program guidelines and application](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/25676/Home-Incentives-Application?bidId=)
![Ventsd](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=28827)
  1. [Uninterruptible power supply (ups)](https://www.anaheim.net/5418/Fire-Safety#tab4a432f92-c5bf-4ec8-ab9f-2eeea9be6ac6_2)


An uninterruptible power supply (UPS), also known as battery backup, provides emergency power to critical devices during an unforeseen power outage. An UPS provides power stored in a battery to allow for safe, orderly shutdown of connected equipment (e.g. computers). Medical device UPS systems are designed to provide an uninterrupted power source to keep your medical device running when it is supposed to such as CPAP Machines.
**Installation/Efficiency Criteria**
  * ENERGY STAR® Certified (for standard UPS only)  



**Incentive Amount**
  * $50 for standard equipment (Maximum of 2)
  * $200 for medical equipment - Must presently be receiving Medical Lifeline Rate (Maximum of 2)


[Program guidelines and application](https://www.anaheim.net/1481)
![Power Supply](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=27539)
  

  1. [Battery storage](https://www.anaheim.net/5418/Fire-Safety#tab4a432f92-c5bf-4ec8-ab9f-2eeea9be6ac6_3)


Battery Storage provides Anaheim Public Utilities customers with the economic and environmental benefits while accelerating the adoption of renewable energy in your home by storing energy when the sun is out and using it when it's not. Battery Storage also allows a certain level of back-up power during a planned or unforeseen power outage.
**Installation/Efficiency Criteria**
  * Must be UL Certified
  * Energy Storage system must have minimum of 5kWh capacity 
  * Eligible Energy Storage Unit installation must be standalone or part of a future existing solar PV system
  * Energy Storage Unit shall be capable of storing energy produced from electrical energy, such as, solar PV energy, wind energy or some other residential produced electricity
  * Customer agrees to be on a [Time of Use](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/25947/Schedule-TOU-2-?bidId=) rate and [MyPower Savings Program](https://www.anaheim.net/5920) for at least 12 months


**Incentive Amount**
  * Up to $3,000 per household 


[Program guidelines and application](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/55963/Battery-Storage-Rebate-Application_7_10_24)

## ADDITIONAL FIRE SAFETY RESOURCES
Anaheim residents can take advantage of programs designed to enhance fire safety and resilience.
  1. [Wildfire Home Assessment](https://www.anaheim.net/5418/Fire-Safety#tab53dac53d-4f32-4483-9d13-d02b14320413_0)


The Wildland Urban Interface is the zone of transition between unoccupied land and human development. 
Communities surrounded by wildlands are at varying degrees of risk from wildfires. Schedule a one-on-one consultation with an Anaheim Wildfire Mitigation Specialist to identify potential wildfire threats within your property and ways you can harden your home.
Schedule your FREE Wildfire Home Assessment
  1. [Fire-Resistant Shade Trees](https://www.anaheim.net/5418/Fire-Safety#tab53dac53d-4f32-4483-9d13-d02b14320413_1)


TreePower offers a number of FREE fire-resistant shade trees that meet fire threat zone guidelines.
![Zones](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=28816)  

  

**ZONE 1 -****within 30 feet of all structures or to****the property line:**
  * Trim trees regularly to keep branches a minimum of 10 feet from other trees
  * Remove branches that hang over your roof and keep dead branches 10 feet away from your chimney
  * Remove or prune flammable plants and shrubs near windows


**ZONE 2 -****within 30-100 ft of all structures or to****the property line:**
  * Remove vegetation and items that could catch fire from around and under decks
  * Remove fallen leaves, needles, twigs, bark, cones, and small branches
  * Create horizontal spacing between shrubs and trees
  * Eliminate opportunities for a vertical "fire ladder" by:
    * Removing branches beneath large trees for a 6-foot minimum clearance
    * Allowing a minimum clearance of 3 times height of shrub underneath a tree


[Get your FREE trees](https://www.anaheim.net/757/)
