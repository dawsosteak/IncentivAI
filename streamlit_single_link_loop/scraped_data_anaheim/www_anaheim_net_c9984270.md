#  Scam Alert 
### Anaheim Public Utilities never makes unsolicited customer calls or visits asking for immediate payment.
The most common scam tactics are to pose as utility employees by phone call, email, text message, or personal visit, and threatening to shut off water and/or power unless payment is made immediately.
If you receive this type of threat, hang up or close the door, and then call the number on your utility bill or call the Anaheim Police Department at 714-765-1900 to report suspicious activity. 
## **I AM A VICTIM OF UTILITY FRAUD – WHAT DO I DO?**
###   

  1. If someone is knocking on your door and threatening you, close the door and call 911.
  2. If you have made a payment to a scammer, report the crime to the Anaheim Police Department at 714-765-1900 or file a report in person at the Main or East Police Station front counter(s) for Financial Fraud.
  3. Call Anaheim Public Utilities at the number on your utility bill, and we will work with you on your account to avoid disconnection.


![Scam Alert](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=21197)
##  **DON’T FALL FOR THESE SCAM TACTICS!**  

  * Scammers instruct residents and businesses to purchase a prepaid card to solicit payment in order to avoid disconnection of their water and/or electric service. 
  * Utility impersonators may use what’s called “spoofing software,” which allows them to falsely display the Anaheim Public Utilities phone number on your caller ID. 
  * Customers are instructed to call the impersonator back at a telephone number different from the customer service number listed on the back of their utility bill to give them the prepaid card number. 


  

## **ANAHEIM PUBLIC UTILITIES PAYMENT PRACTICES**
##   

**Anaheim representatives will never** make unsolicited calls to customers asking for payment information over the phone, nor will our staff visit your home to solicit payment. Customers can pay their utility bills in one of the following ways:
  * **By mail** to Anaheim Public Utilities, P.O. Box 3222, Anaheim, CA 92803-3222 
  * **In-person** through the Anaheim West Tower drop box located outside of 201 S. Anaheim Blvd.
  * **Through our automated phone system** , by calling the phone number on your utility bill 
  * **Through our secure**[**online portal**](https://myapu.anaheim.net/iwr/user/login.seam) that is password-protected


  

_If customers are past-due on their bills, you may request a payment extension or assistance from a number of non-profit agencies._  
_The best way to ensure you will not be disconnected is to call a customer service representative and discuss ways to avoid service disruptions._
_For past-due bills, Anaheim Public Utilities will mail at least one past-due notice and one disconnection notice before disrupting your service._  
_As a not-for-profit agency, this practice is to ensure that the burden of unpaid bills does not fall on other customers._  
  

## **TIPS TO AVOID FRAUDULENT ACTIVITY AND KEEP YOUR FAMILY SAFE**
  

  * Be cautious about the personal information you share online or over the phone – do not give your financial information if someone is calling you for payment.
  * Make sure to shred all bills you are throwing in the trash.
  * Anaheim Public Utilities accepts cash, check, credit card (Visa, MasterCard, or Discover), money order, or payment via checking account.   
Prepaid cards are not accepted.
  * If you receive a disconnection notification by mail, call Anaheim Public Utilities to discuss ways to avoid a service disruption.  
  



## **STEPS ANAHEIM IS TAKING TO ADDRESS SCAMS**
  

  1. Toll-free numbers that have been reported by customers as fraudulent have been reported and shut down.
  2. When you call Anaheim Public Utilities to make a payment, our representatives will not take your financial information; instead, they will transfer you to an automated payment system. 
  3. Anaheim Public Utilities is working closely with the Anaheim Police Department on utility fraud and conducts investigations accordingly.
  4. Anaheim Public Utilities has a presence at more than 70 events per year and is always willing to speak with community groups to share fraud awareness information. Contact us at the phone number on your utility bill to request a representative to meet with your complex or neighborhood.
