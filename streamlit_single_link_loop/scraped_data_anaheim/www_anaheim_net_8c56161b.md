## Get started with your Anaheim Public Library 
Start here to learn how to use your Anaheim Public Library! With a Library card, you get access to everything we offer, including thousands of books, DVDs to borrow, Book Club kits, STEAM on the Go! kits, ukuleles, and much more. Unlock more by using your Library card number to use computers and printers in branches, and access dozens of online resources for research, test preparation, career guidance, language learning, and more. We also offer educational and engaging programs and services throughout the year!
There are so many ways to enjoy your Anaheim Public Library Card — view the FAQ below to get started!

  1. [Who can get a Library Card?](https://www.anaheim.net/1229/Get-Started#tab7d15ae3a-d047-4e17-b809-3262d0b0a33a_0)


If you are a permanent resident of California, you can get a free library card at any Anaheim Public Library. Visit your neighborhood branch to fill out an application and bring along a photo ID and something that shows your current address (parental information is acceptable for children). You are responsible for all items checked out on your library card.   
  
Do you want to complete the Library Card application in advance?   
Download and print the [**Adult and Teen Library Card Application**](https://anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/39591/Adult-Library-Card-Application) or the [**Youth Library Card Application**](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/27413/Youth-Library-Card-Application). Bring your completed application to your neighborhood branch to get your Anaheim Public Library Card.
## Online Services Card
We are currently accepting online library card applications for Anaheim Residents only. To apply, please complete the [**online library card form**](https://anaheim.net/FormCenter/Library-8/Online-Services-Card-Application-68) with a City of Anaheim Utility Bill for address verification.   

  

  1. [What's the difference between an Online Services Card and a Library Card?](https://www.anaheim.net/1229/Get-Started#tab7d15ae3a-d047-4e17-b809-3262d0b0a33a_1)


Online Services Cards give you access to computers, printers, eBooks and audiobooks, and free online resources like Gale Courses — an extensive collection of online courses available by using your card number. Patrons can find a complete list of Online Resources [**here**](https://anaheim.net/1105/Online-Resources). 
  

Library Cards give you access to everything above, plus the ability to borrow books, kits, and DVDs. 
  1. [How do I replace my Library Card if I lost it?](https://www.anaheim.net/1229/Get-Started#tab7d15ae3a-d047-4e17-b809-3262d0b0a33a_2)


Replacement cards for Adults and Teens are $1.00 and 50¢ for children. For your protection, please contact us if you've lost or misplaced library card so that the Library can note this on your account. 
Interested in a limited edition library card design? Replace your card anytime for a $1.00 donation and support programs and library materials while sporting a fun new design.
  1. [How do I borrow and return items?](https://www.anaheim.net/1229/Get-Started#tab7d15ae3a-d047-4e17-b809-3262d0b0a33a_3)


Your library card will allow you to borrow books and other materials at any Anaheim Public Library branch, the Mobile Library (Bookmobile), Exploration on Wheels van, or the book vending machine at ARTIC.  
  
Items can be returned at any Anaheim Public Library branch. Most of the branches have book drops available near the entrance or in the parking lot.
  1. [How long can I borrow items?](https://www.anaheim.net/1229/Get-Started#tab7d15ae3a-d047-4e17-b809-3262d0b0a33a_4)


Most materials can be borrowed for 21 days. New books or rentals may be subject to shorter loan periods.  
  
New accounts are limited to 5 items at any one time (2 of which can be rental items) for the first 30 days. After the first month, a maximum of 35 items may be checked out at one time. The quantity of high demand and rental items may be limited. Audiobooks and eBooks don't count towards this total and don't accrue fees.
  1. [How do I renew items?](https://www.anaheim.net/1229/Get-Started#tab7d15ae3a-d047-4e17-b809-3262d0b0a33a_5)


Most library materials may be renewed twice if there are no requests for the item. Rental materials such as videos and DVDs are not renewable.  
  
As a courtesy, items eligible for renewal will automatically renew on the day they're due, extending their loan periods from their original due dates. If you are signed up for Anaheim Public Library email notices, borrowers receive a pre-overdue notification sent three days before the item is due.   
  
You can renew your items 24 hours a day, seven days a week, by logging into your account or calling. You will need to have your library card number available.  
  
You may check your account and renewal status for your materials in the following ways:  

  * By calling 714-765-1775

  1. [How do I know if an item is overdue?](https://www.anaheim.net/1229/Get-Started#tab7d15ae3a-d047-4e17-b809-3262d0b0a33a_7)


Check your receipt or [](https://ana.ent.sirsi.net/client/en_US/virtual)**[account online](https://ana.ent.sirsi.net/client/en_US/virtual)** to know when your items are due back. We send overdue notifications as a courtesy. A lack of a notice does not absolve you of the responsibility for returning library materials and paying charges. The library refers delinquent accounts to a collection agency if not resolved within 60 days. If you'd like to receive email reminders before your items are due, be sure that we have your current email address listed on your account information page.
  1. [What are your fees and services?](https://www.anaheim.net/1229/Get-Started#tab7d15ae3a-d047-4e17-b809-3262d0b0a33a_8)


APL is now fine-free*. If items are returned after 30 days from the due date, patrons are charged a replacement fee. If the item returned is damaged, patrons will be charged a damage fee. Other service charges are outlined in the fines and fees schedule, including:
  * Copies/printing, 15¢ per page for B&W; 50¢ per page for color
  * *Video Game rental charges still apply
  * You may pay your fees online [**here**](https://anaheimlibrary-epay.anaheim.net/eCommerceWebModule/Home).


  1. [How much do lost or damaged materials cost?](https://www.anaheim.net/1229/Get-Started#tab7d15ae3a-d047-4e17-b809-3262d0b0a33a_9)


The charge for lost (not returned) items is the replacement cost of that item plus a non-refundable processing fee. The processing fee for most items is $5. Charges for slightly damaged library materials that can be repaired and continue to circulate are assessed according to the level of damage incurred. Items that are damaged beyond repair are charged the replacement cost of the item. After two years, unreturned materials will not be accepted, and the total cost will remain on your account.
