#  CII and HOA Landscape Design Assistance Program (Pilot) 
Anaheim Public Utilities is now offering a pilot program to assist Commercial Industrial and Institutional (CII) and Home Owners Associations (HOA) customers with the development of representational concept planting designs for their specific landscapes for areas where turf will be removed and replaced with low water using plants.
Customers will receive a free landscape design consultation and a representative landscape planting design concept from our designated consultant.
The consultant will meet with you for one hour and assist with your design needs before you begin your turf removal project.
For more details please call 714-765-4218 or submit an [application ](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/59075/Landscape-Design-Assistance-Program---Application-and-Questionnaire)to savewater@anaheim.net

# State Rules on Non-Functional Turf in Commercial and HOA Landscapes
In October 2023, the California State Legislature passed [Assembly Bill 1572](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billTextClient.xhtml?bill_id=202320240AB1572), which phases in a ban on decorative lawn grass watering in commercial, industrial, and institutional areas permanently. This includes government sites and common areas of Home-Owner’s Associations (HOAs).
The new law prohibits public agencies, restaurants, corporate campuses, industrial parks, HOAs and certain other property owners from watering “non-functional turf” using potable water. 
AB 1572 gradually phases out the use of potable water for irrigation of nonfunctional turf. The law’s effective dates are as follows:
  * **January 1, 2027** - Properties owned by the California Department of General Services and properties owned by local government agencies
  * **January 1, 2028** – Commercial, industrial, and institutional properties
  * **January 1, 2029** – Common areas of homeowners’ associations, common interest developments, and community service organizations or similar entities
  * **January 1, 2031** – Local government facilities in disadvantaged communities (contingent upon availability of State financial assistance)


The law does not impose restrictions on residential yards, cemeteries, parks, golf courses, and sports fields, and permits the use of potable water to the extent it is necessary to ensure the health of trees or other perennial non-turf plants.
The State Water Board is directed to create a form for compliance self-certification, and require owners of commercial, industrial, or institutional irrigated parcels larger than 5,000 square feet to self-certify compliance to the State Water Board beginning in 2030.
If you are unsure if your turf grass qualifies as ‘nonfunctional’ feel free to contact us at 714-765-4218.
