#  Solid Waste & Recycling 
[![click here for info on organic waste Opens in new window](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=50045)](https://anaheim.net/6048/Organic-waste)
The city of Anaheim contracts with Republic Services to provide a wide range of solid waste services that help keep our city clean.
Services include:
  * **Bulky Item Collection** - Large household items that cannot fit into containers, available for residential and commercial ([click here for more information](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/57657/Bulky-item-flyer-Oct-2024))
  * **Container Exchange** - Containers that have graffiti, are damaged or worn out can be exchanged
  * **Container Rentals** - 3 to 30-yard rental containers are available for residential cleanups and construction debris removal
  * **Extra Trash Service** - Extra weekly pickups are available for a nominal fee


For these services or for further information, contact Republic Services Customer Service Department at 714-238-2444[![phone icon](https://www.anaheim.net/474/Solid-Waste-Recycling)](https://www.anaheim.net/474/Solid-Waste-Recycling "Call: 714-238-2444"). ## **Defining the Future  
**
The State of California has set an ambitious goals for recycling, composting and source reduction of solid waste. To help reach these goals, the State has adopted Assembly Bills 341 and 1826 and SB 1383. AB 341 is a Mandatory Commercial Recycling bill, AB 1826 Mandatory Organic Recycling and SB 1383 provides education and outreach regarding organics recycling and edible food donation. ## SB 1383- Self-Hauling Recyclable and Organic Materials 
Self-hauling means transporting waste generated on-site instead of using the City’s collection service with Republic Services.
**Requirements for Self-Hauling **
Self-haulers must source separate all recyclable materials and organic waste generated on-site and transport those materials to a permitted solid waste facility, operation, activity, or property that processes or recovers recyclable materials and organic waste. 
Recyclable materials and organic waste must not be disposed of in a landfill when they can be recovered through available processing facilities.
For full requirements, please refer to Anaheim Municipal Code Section 10.10.040.
**Recordkeeping**
Self-haulers must maintain records for a minimum of five years for all recyclable materials and organic waste transported to each facility. Records must be provided to the City upon request and include:
• Delivery receipts and weight tickets from the receiving facility
• The type and quantity of material transported (in tons or cubic yards)
• The name and location of each facility receiving the material
If a facility does not have scales capable of weighing the material, the self-hauler is not required to record the weight but must maintain a record of the facility receiving the material.
