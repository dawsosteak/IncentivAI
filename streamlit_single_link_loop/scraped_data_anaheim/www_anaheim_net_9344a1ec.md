**Enforcement**
Anaheim is taking a tougher stance on illegal fireworks this year.
You'll see stepped up patrols, on the ground and in the air, confiscation of illegal fireworks, citations for lighting illegal fireworks and for property owners who knowingly allow illegal fireworks on their properties.
With fines up to $3,000, it's not worth it. We'll be using drones and helicopters, and you could end up with an unexpected costly fine in the days after the Fourth for documented violations at a home or other property.
Only Safe and Sane fireworks are allowed in Anaheim, _and only on July 4_. No fireworks of any kind are allowed in east Anaheim anytime because of high wildfire risk.
**Reporting illegal fireworks**
We know illegal fireworks are a big problem. For citations to be effective, we need to catch someone in the act or document fireworks use at a property. If you see someone in the act of actually lighting off fireworks, call Anaheim Police at (714) 765-1900 with a description, location and other specific, helpful details.
If you're just hearing fireworks, but not seeing who's doing them, use our online fireworks reporting tool below to share a general location. That will help us target our patrols in the days leading up to and on the Fourth of July weekend.
[![Report fireworks online banner  Opens in new window](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=36698)](https://survey123.arcgis.com/share/e07b840530c44759aff42083537713f3)
# Do the Fourth Right!
Support Anaheim schools and nonprofits by buying Safe and Sane fireworks — the only kind allowed in Anaheim and only on July 4 — by buying from 16 stands across the city. Proceeds go to school and community programs.
A lottery is held yearly to select the nonprofits that will operate stands. Operation includes submitting a permit, staffing the stand and providing onsite security 24 hours a day, seven days a week, from June 28 through July 4.
[![Fireworks application Opens in new window](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=70741)](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/70742/Final-Fireworks-Permit-Application-2026-fillable)
**Completed firework stand permit applications must be submitted by mail to Anaheim Fire & Rescue Community Risk Reduction Division, 201 S. Anaheim Blvd., Suite 300, Anaheim, CA 92805, postmarked no later than June 1, 2026.**
## **Other important dates:**
  * **Monday, June 1: Permit applications due**
  * **Sunday, June 28: Sales begin**
