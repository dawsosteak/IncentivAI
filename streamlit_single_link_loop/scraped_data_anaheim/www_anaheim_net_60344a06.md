## LSE Commercial Interest Allocation Points - California Independent System Operator (CAISO)
On September 30, 2024, FERC approved the CAISO’s Track 2 proposal for Phase 1 of the Interconnection Process Enhancements 2023 (IPE) initiative. The IPE proposal aims to streamline and reduce the number of projects backlogged in the interconnection queue process, all while coordinating resource procurement, interconnection, resource planning, and transmission planning.
CAISO has developed heightened requirements to complete an interconnection request that necessitates a greater level of project readiness to advance to the interconnection study process. In addition to the new readiness requirements required in FERC Order No. 2023, CAISO is implementing specific criteria to score and prioritize interconnection requests. The main criteria are commercial interest, project viability, and system need. Commercial interest evaluations will incorporate preliminary, non-binding feedback on specific projects from load-serving entities, such as Anaheim Public Utilities (APU).
APU is bound by APU Department Order No. 350 for all procurement of long-term energy-related resources by APU, including awarding of LSE Commercial Interest Points. For more information on APU ‘s selection criteria and consideration factors for awarding Commercial Interest Points please click [here](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/12740/350-Long-Term-Energy-Resources-?bidId=).
