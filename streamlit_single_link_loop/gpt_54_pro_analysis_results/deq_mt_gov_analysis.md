# Program Name:EPA Brownfields Grants (Montana DEQ Brownfields Program)

#Program URL: https://deq.mt.gov/cleanupandrec/Programs/brownfields

## Program Details
- **Concrete Rebate Amounts:**
- Brownfields Assessment Grants: up to $200,000
- Brownfields Assessment Grants (with waiver): up to $350,000 for a single site
- Brownfields Revolving Loan Fund Grants: up to $1,000,000
- Brownfields Cleanup Grants: up to $200,000 per site
- Brownfields Cleanup Grants: 20% cost share required; hardship waiver may be requested
- Brownfields Job Training Grants: up to $200,000

## Eligibility
- **Eligibility Requirements:**
- Eligible applicants include local governments
- Eligible applicants include land clearance authorities or other quasi-governmental entities operating under the supervision and control of, or as an agent of, local government
- Eligible applicants include government entities created by state legislature
- Eligible applicants include regional councils
- Eligible applicants include redevelopment agencies sanctioned by the State of Montana
- Eligible applicants include the State of Montana
- Eligible applicants include Indian tribes
- Nonprofit organizations are eligible for Cleanup Grants and Job Training Grants only
- The site must be a clear benefit to the community
- The applicant must not have contributed to the contamination
- The site must meet the definition of a brownfields site
- Petroleum-contaminated sites must have no viable responsible party
- Petroleum-contaminated sites must be assessed, investigated, or cleaned up by a person not potentially liable for the contamination
- Petroleum-contaminated sites must not be subject to a corrective action order under the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act
- Petroleum-contaminated sites must not be funded by the leaking underground storage tank trust fund (LUST Trust)
- Cleanup grant applicants must own the site by the time the award is made
- RLF subgrant applicants must own the site by the time the award is made
- Job training grant applicants must propose to serve a community that currently receives, or has received, EPA brownfields competitive grant assistance
- Sites listed or proposed for listing on the National Priorities List (Federal Superfund) are not eligible
- Sites subject to unilateral administrative orders, court orders, administrative orders on consent, or judicial consent decrees under CERCLA are not eligible
- Sites subject to the jurisdiction, custody, or control of the United States Government are not eligible

## Utility Information
- **Utility Company Name:** Montana Department of Environmental Quality (DEQ) / U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)
- **Utility Company Size:** Not specified

# Program Name:Alternative Energy Revolving Loan Program

#Program URL: https://deq.mt.gov/energy/Programs/AERLP

## Program Details
- **Concrete Rebate Amounts:**
- 2026 interest rate: 3.5% fixed (4.023% APR)
- Maximum loan amount: $40,000
- Maximum loan term: 10 years
- $0 down payment
- No application fee
- Closing costs include an origination fee of $250 or 2% of the loan amount, whichever is greater
- Other closing fees of approximately $200

## Eligibility
- **Eligibility Requirements:**
- Applicants may be individuals, small businesses, units of local government, units of the university system, or nonprofit organizations
- Small businesses must be independently owned and operated, not dominant in their field, and employ 100 or fewer individuals
- Funding must be used for construction of alternative energy systems or capital investments for energy conservation purposes when done in conjunction with an alternative energy system
- Eligible alternative energy systems include solar photovoltaic systems, solar thermal systems for water or space heating, geothermal systems including ground source heat pumps, and EPA-certified low-emission pellet stoves or wood stoves
- Eligible energy conservation measures include insulation, high-efficiency windows, and energy-efficient appliances when installed in conjunction with an alternative energy system
- The proposed project, activity, or equipment must be located in Montana
- The project must be technically appropriate for Montana’s climate
- The equipment or system must be proven reliable and commercially available
- The system must generate energy for the sole use of the owner, though net metering systems are eligible
- The project must meet all applicable certifications and standards, including local building codes and utility interconnection requirements
- The project must comply with applicable statutory and regulatory requirements protecting air, water, land, fish, wildlife, and recreational resources
- Applicants must pass technical, environmental, and financial review
- Completed projects are not eligible; the program does not allow retroactive financing

## Utility Information
- **Utility Company Name:** Montana Department of Environmental Quality (Montana Energy Office)
- **Utility Company Size:** State government agency; not specified as a utility size

## Eligibility
- **Eligibility Requirements:**
- Projects must be Eligible Mitigation Actions under the Volkswagen Environmental Mitigation Trust.
- Eligible project categories include Class 4-7 freight trucks, Class 4-8 school buses/shuttle buses/transit buses, Class 8 local freight trucks, airport ground support equipment, ferries/tugs, forklifts, railroad freight switchers, and light-duty EV charging stations.
- Light-duty EV charging stations must be available to the public or located at workplaces or multi-unit housing locations.
- Class 4-7 freight trucks must have a GVWR of 14,000-33,000 lbs. and a 1992-2009 engine model year.
- Class 4-7 freight trucks may be repowered with a new diesel or alternate-fueled engine, replaced with a new diesel or alternate-fueled vehicle, repowered with an all-electric engine, or replaced with a new all-electric vehicle; charging infrastructure may be included for all-electric projects.
- Eligible alternate fuels for Class 4-7 freight trucks include CNG, propane, and hybrid.
- Eligible buses must have a 2009 engine model year or older.
- Eligible buses may be repowered with a new diesel or alternate-fueled engine, replaced with an alternate-fueled vehicle, repowered with an all-electric engine, or replaced with a new all-electric vehicle; charging infrastructure may be included for all-electric projects.
- Eligible alternate fuels for buses include CNG, propane, and hybrid.
- Class 8 local freight trucks must have a 1992-2009 engine model year.
- Class 8 local freight trucks may be repowered with a new diesel or alternate-fueled engine, replaced with a new diesel or alternate-fueled vehicle, repowered with an all-electric engine, or replaced with a new all-electric vehicle; charging infrastructure may be included for all-electric projects.
- Eligible alternate fuels for Class 8 local freight trucks include compressed natural gas, propane, and hybrid.
- Airport ground support equipment must be Tier 0, Tier 1, Tier 2, uncertified, or certified to 3 g/bhp-hr spark ignition.
- Airport ground support equipment is limited to electrification projects only; no new diesel or alternate-fuel projects are eligible.
- Ferries/tugs must have unregulated, Tier 1, or Tier 2 marine engines.
- Ferries/tugs may be repowered with a new diesel or alternate-fueled engine or generator set, or repowered with an all-electric engine; replacement ferries/tugs are not eligible.
- Forklifts must have greater than 8,000 lb. lift capacity.
- Forklifts are limited to electrification projects only; no new diesel or alternate-fuel projects are eligible.
- Freight switchers must be pre-Tier 4 and operate at least 1,000 hours per year.
- Freight switchers may be repowered with a new diesel or alternate-fueled engine or generator set, replaced with a new diesel or alternate-fueled freight switcher certified to meet U.S. EPA standards, repowered with an all-electric engine, or replaced with an all-new electric freight switcher; infrastructure may be included for electric projects.
