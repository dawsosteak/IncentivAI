- **Concrete Rebate Amounts:**

- No-cost energy-efficient furnaces

- No-cost caulking
- No-cost low-flow showerheads

- Must meet the CARE or FERA income limits

- For ESA Multifamily Energy Savings (MFES) deed-restricted properties, owner must certify at least 65% of tenant households meet ESA income guidelines
- For ESA Multifamily Energy Savings (MFES) non-deed-restricted properties, owner must certify at least 80% of tenant households meet ESA income guidelines

# Program Name:California LifeLine Program

#Program URL: https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/consumer-support/financial-assistance-savings-and-discounts/lifeline/california-lifeline-eligibility

## Program Details
- **Concrete Rebate Amounts:**
- Up to $19.00 monthly flat rate home phone service discount
- Up to $19.00 monthly cell phone service discount
- Up to $39 service connection discount
- Up to $39 home phone service conversion discount

## Eligibility
- **Eligibility Requirements:**
- Must use the California LifeLine discount only for the household’s primary residence.
- Only one California LifeLine discounted phone service is allowed per household, except for teletypewriter (TTY) users and Deaf and Disabled Telecommunications Program participants.
- Household must choose either a discounted home phone or a discounted cell phone, but not both.
- Household cannot receive the discount from multiple phone companies.
- Proof of eligibility documentation is required with a completed and signed application.
- Program-based eligibility: applicant or another person in the household must be enrolled in one of the following: Medicaid/Medi-Cal; Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program (LIHEAP); Supplemental Security Income (SSI); Federal Public Housing Assistance or Section 8; CalFresh/Food Stamps/SNAP; Women, Infants and Children (WIC); National School Lunch Program (NSL); Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF), including CalWORKs, StanWORKs, Welfare-to-Work (WTW), and Greater Avenues for Independence (GAIN); Tribal TANF; Bureau of Indian Affairs General Assistance; Head Start Income Eligible (Tribal Only); Food Distribution Program on Indian Reservations; Federal Veterans and Survivors Pension Benefit Program.
- Income-based eligibility for household annual gross income at or below these limits effective June 1, 2025 to May 31, 2026: 1 person $24,200; 2 people $32,600; 3 people $41,100; 4 people $49,600; each additional member $8,500.
- Income-based eligibility for household annual gross income at or below these limits effective June 1, 2026 to May 31, 2027: 1 person $24,600; 2 people $33,300; 3 people $42,100; 4 people $50,800; each additional member $8,700.
- For a second home phone line discount, applicant must use a TTY or be part of the Deaf and Disabled Telecommunications Program; TTY users must have immediate and continuous access to the device and may need to provide a medical certificate.
- The person signing up may be disqualified if claimed on someone else’s income tax return as a dependent.

## Utility Information
- **Utility Company Name:** California Public Utilities Commission (CPUC)
- **Utility Company Size:** Statewide government entity

# Program Name:Electric Tariff Rule 20 Overhead to Underground Conversion Program

#Program URL: https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/electrical-energy/infrastructure/electric-reliability/undergrounding-program-description/rule-20

## Program Details
- **Concrete Rebate Amounts:**
- Overall Rule 20 funding can cover between 0% and 100% of overhead-to-underground conversion costs, depending on the sub-program.
- Rule 20A: 100% of qualifying project costs are paid by electric utility ratepayers through annually allocated work credits.
- Rule 20B: reimbursement equal to the cost of an equivalent overhead system, plus taxes if applicable; this typically ranges from 20% to 40% of total project cost.
- Rule 20C: a small credit for the salvage cost of the facilities, less depreciation, for projects that receive no broader Rule 20 funding.
- Rule 20D: 100% ratepayer-funded conversion of primary distribution facilities in SDG&E high fire threat areas.

## Eligibility
- **Eligibility Requirements:**
- Applicants work with the applicable California investor-owned utility on an overhead-to-underground conversion project.
- Rule 20A projects must be in areas used most often by the general public.
- Rule 20A projects must meet at least one public-interest criterion: eliminate an unusually heavy concentration of overhead lines; involve a street or road with a high volume of public traffic; benefit a civic or public recreation area or area of unusual scenic interest; or be listed as an arterial street or major collector under OPR guidelines.
- Rule 20A requires local government determination of general public interest after public hearings and consultation with the utility.
- Rule 20A requires the community to have sufficient accumulated work credits; communities may borrow forward five years of credits.
- Rule 20A requires the community to form a utility underground district by municipal resolution.
- Rule 20B applies to larger developments or areas that do not meet Rule 20A criteria.
- Rule 20B projects should involve both sides of a street for a minimum of 600 feet.
- Rule 20B applicants are responsible for installing conduit, substructures, and boxes, and for paying installation costs before reimbursement.
- Rule 20C applies to projects that do not qualify for higher Rule 20 funding levels, are less than 600 feet in length, and typically involve one or more property owners.
- Rule 20D is only available in SDG&E service territory.
- Rule 20D applies in SDG&E high fire threat areas where SDG&E determines undergrounding is the preferred wildfire-mitigation method.
- Rule 20D projects are selected by the local community.
- Rule 20D work credits may only be used for primary distribution conversion and do not cover secondary lines, services, or panel conversions.

## Utility Information
- **Utility Company Name:** California investor-owned electric utilities participating under CPUC Rule 20, including PG&E, SCE, SDG&E, Liberty Utilities, PacifiCorp, and Bear Valley Electric Service
- **Utility Company Size:** Investor-owned utilities (IOUs); exact size not specified

## Eligibility
- **Eligibility Requirements:**
- Applies to customers in the territories of California’s large electric investor-owned utilities: PG&E, SCE, and SDG&E.
- Customers applying for interconnection on or after April 15, 2023 take service on the Net Billing Tariff.
- Eligible for the types of renewable electrical generation facilities that previously would have used standard NEM tariffs.
- Customer-generators must take service on the required electrification TOU rate: E-ELEC (PG&E), TOU-D-PRIME (SCE), or EV-TOU-5 (SDG&E).
- For the export compensation adder, the customer must be a residential PG&E or SCE customer and apply to interconnect before the end of 2027.
- Customers required to add solar due to California building code for new construction are not eligible for the export compensation adder.
- Installation size is generally limited to the customer’s annual electric load plus up to 50% if the customer attests to need.

## Utility Information
- **Utility Company Name:** Pacific Gas and Electric Company (PG&E); Southern California Edison Company (SCE); San Diego Gas & Electric Company (SDG&E)
- **Utility Company Size:** Large electric investor-owned utilities (IOUs)
