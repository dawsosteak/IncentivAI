# Program Name: California Teleconnect Fund (CTF)

#Program URL: https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/consumer-support/financial-assistance-savings-and-discounts/california-teleconnect-fund

## Program Details
- 50% discount on eligible monthly recurring charges for approved advanced communications services, including high-speed internet access and broadband services.
- 25% discount on eligible voice services.
- When E-Rate or Rural Health Care Program support applies, the CTF discount is calculated on the remaining eligible balance after applicable federal support.

## Eligibility
- Must be an approved CTF participant with a valid Approval Letter.
- Participants approved or recertified on or after July 1, 2019 must have a valid CTF-ID for the eligible service address.
- Eligible participants include schools and school annexes, libraries and library annexes, community colleges, government-owned hospitals and health clinics, community-based organizations, healthcare community-based organizations, California Telehealth Network participants, and certain qualifying rural health care providers and nonprofit 2-1-1/Head Start CBOs.
- Community-based organizations may need proof of tax-exempt status and certification that they provide qualifying services.
- Eligible services are limited to approved communications services; mobile broadband services are eligible only if authorized in the participant’s Approval Letter.
- Applications and participation are managed through the eCAP portal.
- Participants must periodically recertify eligibility.

## Utility Information
- **Utility Company Name:** California Public Utilities Commission (CPUC)
- **Utility Company Size:** Statewide

# Program Name: Energy Savings Assistance Program (ESA)

#Program URL: https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/consumer-support/financial-assistance-savings-and-discounts/energy-savings-assistance

## Program Details
- $0 cost to eligible customers for weatherization services and eligible energy-efficiency measures.
- Up to $2,500 per home for minor home repairs needed to enable eligible measures, reduce infiltration, or mitigate hazardous conditions.
- Up to 2 qualifying refrigerator replacements at no charge.

## Eligibility
- Household must meet CARE or FERA income limits; ESA income eligibility is at or below 250% of Federal Poverty Guidelines.
- 1-person household: income up to $39,125.
- 2-person household: income up to $52,875.
- 3-person household: income up to $66,625.
- 4-person household: income up to $80,375.
- 5-person household: income up to $94,125.
- 6-person household: income up to $107,875.
- 7-person household: income up to $121,625.
- Each additional person: add $13,450.
- Customer must be served by an active utility account or meter, including master-metered service.
- Eligible housing types include single-family homes, multifamily units, mobile homes, and qualifying public housing.
- Renters can participate; property owner authorization is generally required for rental units except for basic measures where the requirement is waived unless the measure modifies the dwelling and requires grounding.
- Multifamily properties qualify if they meet ESA multifamily rules; deed-restricted properties require at least 65% of tenant households to meet ESA income guidelines, and non-deed-restricted properties require at least 80%.
- Applicants may qualify through income documentation, current participation in an approved categorical eligibility program, or self-certification where permitted by the utility/program rules.
- Previous participants may be eligible again if prior measures exceeded useful life or if eligible measures were not previously installed or offered.

## Utility Information
- **Utility Company Name:** California statewide participating utilities: Pacific Gas & Electric (PG&E), Southern California Edison (SCE), San Diego Gas & Electric (SDG&E), Southern California Gas (SoCalGas), Alpine Natural Gas, Bear Valley Electric, Liberty Utilities, PacifiCorp d/b/a Pacific Power, and Southwest Gas.
- **Utility Company Size:** Statewide program administered across Investor-Owned Utilities (IOUs) and Small Multi-Jurisdictional Utilities (SMJUs).

# Program Name: Self-Generation Incentive Program (SGIP) – Residential Solar and Storage Equity

#Program URL: https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/electrical-energy/demand-side-management/self-generation-incentive-program

## Program Details
- $1,100 per kWh of storage.
- $3,100 per kW of solar.
- 50% of the SGIP incentive may be paid upfront through the Advanced Payment Program.

## Eligibility
- Must be a low-income residential electric and/or gas customer in California.
- Incentive is for installing paired solar and energy storage technology at low-income residential properties.
- Applicant must meet at least one of the following:
  - Live in a single-family home with household income at or below 80% of Area Median Income.
  - Live in a single-family home and have already participated in or reserved incentives in the California Solar Initiative SASH or DAC-SASH programs.
  - Live in a single-family home and currently participate in and are eligible for CARE, FERA, or ESA programs.
  - Live in an apartment that is low-income housing with at least five rental units and is either located in a Disadvantaged Community or has at least 80% of residents with incomes at or below 60% of Area Median Income.
  - Live in an apartment property that has already participated in the SOMAH or MASH program.
- After reserving funds, applicant has one year to comply with all program requirements, including enrollment in a qualified Demand Response program.

## Utility Information
- **Utility Company Name:** California Public Utilities Commission (CPUC); program administrators include LADWP, PG&E, SCE, SoCalGas, and SDG&E via Center for Sustainable Energy
- **Utility Company Size:** Statewide California program

# Program Name: California LifeLine Program

#Program URL: https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/consumer-support/financial-assistance-savings-and-discounts/lifeline

## Program Details
- Up to $19.00/month discount on California LifeLine home phone or wireless phone service.
- Up to $20/month for standalone broadband under the California LifeLine Home Broadband Pilot.
- Up to $30/month for bundled broadband and voice under the California LifeLine Home Broadband Pilot.

## Eligibility
- Must be a qualified low-income California household.
- Only one discount per household is allowed, except for teletypewriter users and Deaf and Disabled Telecommunications Program participants.
- Household must choose the discount for either a home phone or a cell phone.
- Must apply through a participating California LifeLine provider/phone company.
- Income-based eligibility: household annual income at or below $24,200 for 1 person, $32,600 for 2 people, $41,100 for 3 people, and $49,600 for 4 people.
- Add $8,500 in allowable annual income for each additional household member.
- Or qualify through enrollment in an eligible assistance program, including Medicaid/Medi-Cal, LIHEAP, SSI, Federal Section 8, CalFresh / Food Stamps / SNAP, WIC, National School Lunch Program (NSLP), TANF, Tribal TANF, Bureau of Indian Affairs assistance, Head Start, Food Distribution Program on Indian Reservations, or Federal Veterans and Survivors Pension Benefit.

## Utility Information
- **Utility Company Name:** California Public Utilities Commission (CPUC)
- **Utility Company Size:** Statewide program

# Program Name: Emergency Load Reduction Program (ELRP)

#Program URL: https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/electrical-energy/electric-costs/copy-of-demand-response-dr/emergency-load-reduction-program

## Program Details
- $2/kWh for non-residential customers for every kilowatt-hour of electricity consumption voluntarily reduced during an ELRP event.

## Eligibility
- Non-residential customers served by Pacific Gas & Electric (PG&E), Southern California Edison (SCE), or San Diego Gas & Electric (SDG&E).
- Customers not currently participating in a CAISO market-integrated Demand Response program are eligible, except customers in the Base Interruptible Program.
- Non-residential customers already enrolled in CAISO market-integrated Demand Response programs may participate via their aggregator only for incremental load reduction beyond what is already committed in CAISO market dispatch.
- Customers with distributed energy resources that can generate energy and have permission to export are eligible.
- Aggregators of Virtual Power Plants are eligible.
- Aggregators of vehicle-to-grid resources that provide managed charging services (V1G) or vehicle-to-grid (V2G) at multiple locations are eligible.
- Customers must contact their utility or existing third-party demand response provider to enroll.
- Certain size, permitting, and other eligibility criteria apply depending on customer type.

## Utility Information
- **Utility Company Name:** Pacific Gas & Electric (PG&E); Southern California Edison (SCE); San Diego Gas & Electric (SDG&E)
- **Utility Company Size:** Three large investor-owned utilities (IOUs)

# Program Name: Solar on Multifamily Affordable Housing (SOMAH) Program

#Program URL: https://calsomah.org/costs-incentives

## Program Details
- $3.20 per AC watt for tenant-serving load (2019-2020) when the project uses the Federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) and does not use Low-income Housing Tax Credits (LIHTC).
- $3.04 per AC watt for tenant-serving load (2020-2021) when the project uses ITC and does not use LIHTC.
- $1.10 per AC watt for common-area load (2019-2020) when the project uses ITC and does not use LIHTC.
- $1.04 per AC watt for common-area load (2020-2021) when the project uses ITC and does not use LIHTC.
- $2.25 per AC watt for tenant-serving load (2019-2020) when the project does not use ITC and uses LIHTC.
- $2.14 per AC watt for tenant-serving load (2020-2021) when the project does not use ITC and uses LIHTC.
- $0.80 per AC watt for common-area load (2019-2020) when the project does not use ITC and uses LIHTC.
- $0.76 per AC watt for common-area load (2020-2021) when the project does not use ITC and uses LIHTC.
- $2.25 per AC watt for tenant-serving load (2019-2020) when the project uses both ITC and LIHTC.
- $2.14 per AC watt for tenant-serving load (2020-2021) when the project uses both ITC and LIHTC.
- $0.80 per AC watt for common-area load (2019-2020) when the project uses both ITC and LIHTC.
- $0.76 per AC watt for common-area load (2020-2021) when the project uses both ITC and LIHTC.

## Eligibility
- Must be a solar distributed generation project sited on existing multifamily affordable housing.
- Property must be located in the service territory of PG&E, SCE, SDG&E, Liberty Utilities, or PacifiCorp.
- System must qualify under the Expected Performance Based Buydown (EPBB) methodology.
- Incentive calculation relies on verified system characteristics including location, system size, shading, and orientation.
- Incentive level depends on the percentage split between tenant-serving load and common-area load.
- Incentive level also depends on whether the project leverages the Federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) and/or Low-income Housing Tax Credits (LIHTC).

## Utility Information
- **Utility Company Name:** Pacific Gas and Electric (PG&E), Southern California Edison (SCE), San Diego Gas & Electric (SDG&E), Liberty Utilities, and PacifiCorp
- **Utility Company Size:** Investor-owned utilities (IOUs)
