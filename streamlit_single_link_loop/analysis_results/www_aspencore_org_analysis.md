# Program Name:APCHA's Essential Repairs Program

#Program URL: http://apcha.org/399/Essential-Repair-Program

## Program Details
- **Concrete Rebate Amounts:**
- Up to $10,000

## Eligibility
- **Eligibility Requirements:**
- Qualified APCHA homeowners
- Funding is for critical repairs that enhance the health, safety, and longevity of the home
- Eligible repairs include furnace and water heater replacements
- Eligible repairs include roof repairs
- Eligible repairs include plumbing fixes
- Eligible repairs include accessibility modifications
- Eligible repairs include window repairs or replacements
- Eligible repairs include mold remediation
- Applications are accepted on a rolling basis until funds are fully allocated
- Households with income of 150% AMI or lower qualify for a free energy assessment

## Utility Information
- **Utility Company Name:** Aspen/Pitkin County Housing Authority (APCHA)
- **Utility Company Size:** Not specified

# Program Name:Eagle County Rebates

#Program URL: https://www.aspencore.org/eagle-rebates

## Program Details
- **Concrete Rebate Amounts:**
- 50% of residential energy efficiency project cost, up to $2,500
- 50% of residential fuel switching project cost, up to $7,500
- 50% of commercial and multifamily energy efficiency project cost, up to $5,000
- 50% of commercial and multifamily fuel switching project cost, up to $5,000
- Induction range rebate limited to $500
- Additional funding for qualifying Community Priority Participants: up to $15,000 for a single residential project
- Additional funding for qualifying Community Priority Participants: up to $50,000 for a commercial or multifamily building project

## Eligibility
- **Eligibility Requirements:**
- Property must be located in Eagle County and outside the town of Basalt
- Residential rebates are available for existing single-family homes or individual units in a multifamily building
- Commercial and multifamily rebates are available for existing buildings unless otherwise noted
- Multifamily buildings must have four or more units; projects must be in the common area and/or every unit of the building
- A free energy assessment is required for residential energy efficiency and residential fuel switching projects
- Rebate applications must be submitted within 90 days of project completion
- Invoices must be dated within 90 days of applying for a rebate
- Induction range rebate requires switching from a gas range
- EV charging projects are limited to Aspen Electric customers only
- Design assistance and commissioning projects require pre-approval
- Fuel switching projects must replace a fossil fuel system with an eligible heat pump or heat-recovery/heat-pump ventilation system
- New construction rebate requests require final construction design documents ("100% CD's")
- New construction fuel-switching eligibility requires all-electric individual homes without natural gas lines and using heat pump technology for space and water heating plus heat recovery ventilation
- No new construction rebates are provided if building code prohibits natural gas
- Additional Community Priority Participant funding is for qualifying local community members such as teachers, childcare workers, veterans, and government staff

## Utility Information
- **Utility Company Name:** Eagle County (administered by CORE)
- **Utility Company Size:** Not specified

#Program URL: https://www.eaglecounty.us/departments___services/resiliency/climate_action/community_programs.php

## Program Details
- **Concrete Rebate Amounts:**
- Free home energy assessments
- Free repairs
- Funding of up to $7,500 per household

## Eligibility
- **Eligibility Requirements:**
- Must be a household located in Eagle County
- Available to homeowners and renters
- Household income must be at or below 150% of area median income (AMI)
- 2025 Eagle County 150% AMI limits: 1 person $139,900; 2 people $159,850; 3 people $179,850; 4 people $199,800; 5 people $215,800; 6 people $231,800; 7 people $247,800; 8 people $263,750
- Households that heat with propane are prioritized

# Program Name:ReEnergize (Garfield County)

#Program URL: https://garfieldcleanenergy.org/reenergize/

## Program Details
- **Concrete Rebate Amounts:**
- Full funding for recommended improvements for households with income up to 80% AMI
- No cap on cost for households with income up to 80% AMI
- Rebates ranging from $2,000 to $4,000 for households with income between 80% and 150% AMI

## Eligibility
- **Eligibility Requirements:**
- Must be a household located in Garfield County
- Available to low- and moderate-income households
- Household income must be at or below 150% of area median income (AMI)
- 2025 Garfield County 80% AMI limits: 1 person $59,200; 2 people $67,600; 3 people $76,080; 4 people $84,480; 5 people $91,280; 6 people $98,000; 7 people $104,800; 8 people $111,520
- 2025 Garfield County 150% AMI limits: 1 person $111,000; 2 people $126,750; 3 people $142,650; 4 people $158,400; 5 people $171,150; 6 people $183,750; 7 people $196,500; 8 people $209,100
- Households at or below 80% AMI qualify for full funding; households between 80% and 150% AMI qualify for partial rebates

## Utility Information
- **Utility Company Name:** Garfield County
- **Utility Company Size:** Not specified
