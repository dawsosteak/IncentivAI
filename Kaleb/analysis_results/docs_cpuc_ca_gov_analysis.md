This is an order from the Public Utilities Commission of California (CPUC) regarding a rulemaking proceeding (R.14-10-003). The order denies Recurve Analytics, Inc.'s motion for party status in the proceeding.

Here's a summary of the key points:

**Background**: Recurve Analytics, Inc. filed a motion requesting party status in R.14-10-003, which is a rulemaking proceeding to create a consistent regulatory framework for the guidance, planning, and evaluation of integrated distributed energy resources.

**Recurve's Request**: Recurve stated that it has been a significant contributor to the development of metered energy consumption methods and tools since 2015 and has created open-source python code base used by multiple load-serving entities in California and beyond. Recurve requested to become a party in this proceeding to comment on policies supporting near-term decisions on the Avoided Cost Calculator (ACC) methods and assumptions.

**Denial of Party Status**: The Administrative Law Judge (ALJ) denied Recurve's motion for party status, citing Rule 1.4(b)(1) and (2) of the CPUC Rules of Practice and Procedure. These rules require that a person seeking party status fully disclose their interest in the proceeding and provide factual and legal contentions.

**Reasons for Denial**: The ALJ denied Recurve's motion because:

* Recurve did not provide any factual or legal contentions to support its request.
* Recurve did not contribute to the development of the record for the proposed decision, which was submitted on January 5, 2022.
* Granting party status to Recurve at this stage would be prejudicial to current parties who have actively participated in the proceeding.

**Encouragement to File Future Motion**: The ALJ encouraged Recurve to file a motion for party status in any future successor proceeding that may open soon, which is expected to address updates to the Avoided Cost Calculator.

# Ruling Denying Party Status in Rulemaking (R.) 14-10-003

## **Email Ruling**

As described below, this Email Ruling denies without prejudice the motions for party status filed in Rulemaking (R.) 14-10-003 by Advanced Energy Economy (AEE), California Solar Energy Industries Association (CALSEIA), 350 Bay Area, and Shell Energy North America, L.P. (Shell). As described below, the motions are not compliant with Commission Rules of Practice and Procedure, Rule 1.4(b).

## **Background**

On April 26, 2016, AEE filed a motion requesting party status in R.14-10 003. AEE states that it is a national association of businesses dedicated to transforming public policy to enable a world that runs on clean energy. Explaining that its participation in this proceeding is critical as the business voice for the advanced energy industry, AEE adds that issues in this proceeding impact its members.

On April 29, 2016, CALSEIA filed a motion for party status in R.14-10-003, stating that it is a not for profit solar industry trade association. CALSEIA explains that it is interested in participating in this proceeding because its members will be direct participants in the solicitations and tariffs that are to be developed in this proceeding.

On May 2, 2016, 350 Bay Area filed a motion for party status in R.14-10-003. 350 Bay Area states that it is a nonprofit organization working for reductions in carbon emissions in the Bay Area and requests to be a party in order to support expansion of clean distributed generation and demand-side resources and job and economic growth.

On May 5, 2016, Shell filed a motion for party status in R.14-10-003, describing itself as an energy service provider providing retail energy service through the direct access program and a potential provider of behind the meter distributed energy resources. Shell emphasizes that electric and gas utilities’ entry into the market for development, ownership and deployment of distributed energy resources may have an impact on competition in the distributed energy resources services market and may impact future efforts of Shell.

## **Denial**

Commission Rules of Practice and Procedure, Rule 1.4(b) requires that a person seeking party status by motion fully disclose the persons or entities in whose behalf the filing is made, the interest of such entities, and the factual and legal contentions that the entity intends to make. In their motions, AEE, CALSEIA, 350 Bay Area, and Shell describe who they are and what their interests are. However, none of the motions specify any factual or legal contentions; thus, failing to comply with Rule 1.4(b). It is reasonable to deny the motions for party status filed by AEE, CALSEIA, 350 Bay Area, and Shell.

## **Conclusion**

IT IS RULED that the motions for party status filed in Rulemaking (R.) 14-10-003 by Advanced Energy Economy, California Solar Energy Industries Association, 350 Bay Area, and Shell Energy North America, L.P. are denied without prejudice.

The Docket Office shall formally file this Email Ruling.

Administrative Law Judge Alternative Dispute Resolution Coordinator California Public Utilities Commission
