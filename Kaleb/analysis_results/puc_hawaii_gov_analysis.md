This is a report from the Public Utilities Commission of the State of Hawaii, dated December 31, 2024. The report discusses various issues related to the modernization of Hawaii's island electric grids and achieving reliability, resiliency, environmental, and affordability goals.

The report highlights several key challenges facing Hawaii's energy sector, including:

1. Load growth: The demand for electricity is increasing due to factors such as air conditioning, electric vehicles, and artificial intelligence.
2. Limited land resources: Hawaii's limited land resources make it difficult to generate sufficient renewable power to meet the state's largest loads.
3. Energy efficiency: The report notes that more stringent energy efficiency standards can be imposed on new residential and commercial cooling systems, and giant industrial loads should be required at the time of development to fully offset their electric use with new, non-fossil generation.

The report also discusses several strategies for making the most of Hawaii's limited land resources, including:

1. Improving assessments of designated agricultural lands to allow construction of energy projects when they are not displacing local food production.
2. Promotion of hybrid energy/agriculture projects.
3. Programs that facilitate government leases of land or rooftops to Hawaiian Electric for energy project development in exchange for electric bill discounts.
4. Designation of Renewable Energy Zones (REZs).

The report concludes by emphasizing the need for urgent and immediate action to modernize Hawaii's island electric grids and achieve reliability, resiliency, environmental, and affordability goals. It notes that transformational plans are much easier to describe than execute, and that success will depend on the willingness of traditional participants in the regulatory process to turn aspirations into achievements through:

1. Willpower: staying laser focused on measurable and timely results.
2. Flexibility: avoiding stagnation of customary practices that impede progress and swiftly implementing contingency plans when primary options fail.
3. Creativity: encouraging new approaches to problem-solving.
4. Collaboration: engaging new participants, such as government agencies, community leaders, and entrepreneurs, whose resources and abilities amplify the collective efforts.

Overall, the report highlights the need for a comprehensive and coordinated approach to modernizing Hawaii's energy sector, involving all stakeholders and leveraging innovative solutions to address the state's unique challenges.
