	* Director of Consumer Protection and Enforcement Division

4. **Instructions**: The guide includes instructions on how to complete each form, including templates for the Certificate of Service.

To file a Notice of Appeal with the CPUC, follow these steps:

1. Complete the Notice of Appeal Form, ensuring all required information is provided.
2. Attach the completed form to the Certificate of Service Form (if filing by hard copy) or transmit it electronically as a separate document.
3. Complete the Certificate of Service Form, confirming service on the required parties.
4. Sign and date both forms.
5. File the Notice of Appeal with the CPUC, either in paper form or electronically.

This text appears to be a collection of California Public Utilities Commission (CPUC) decisions related to energy efficiency and demand response policies. Here is a summary of each decision:

**D.12-05-015 (2012)**: The CPUC adopts a new 2-year transition period for energy efficiency portfolios, followed by the Rolling Portfolio process. During this transition period, utilities are directed to begin transitioning from short-lived energy savings to deeper retrofits.

**D.15-10-028 (2015)**: The CPUC adopts the Rolling Portfolio process, which uses a "bus stop" schedule for update deadlines. This allows updates to be submitted after one of these deadlines and will be picked up at the next deadline.

**D.16-08-019 (2016)**: The CPUC changes its policy for energy efficiency projects, requiring the use of existing conditions as the default baseline for determining energy efficiency savings. This decision also adopts new energy efficiency goals for IOUs' ratepayer-funded energy efficiency program portfolios.

**D.17-09-025 (2017)**: The CPUC sets annual energy efficiency and demand reduction targets that will double the state's energy efficiency savings by 2030, as required by SB 350. This decision also adopts new energy efficiency goals for IOUs' ratepayer-funded energy efficiency program portfolios.

**D.18-05-041 (2018)**: The CPUC orders staff to begin integrating demand response and energy efficiency potential studies, as well as integrating EE and DR for EE measures in which additional DR savings value could be gleaned for very little additional incremental cost.

**D.19-08-034 (2019)**: The CPUC updates the baseline for residential and non-residential lighting measures to Light Emitting Diodes (LEDs), significantly reducing savings potential relative to previous studies.

**2018 CPUC Energy Division White Paper**: This white paper recommends that the portfolio approach to implementing energy efficiency be kept, but work should focus on integrating the two processes as much as possible, and coordinating between them to avoid issues such as double counting.
