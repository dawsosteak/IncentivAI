**CCST 2011 Report**

**Smart Meters Project**

**Executive Summary**

The California Council on Science and Technology (CCST) Smart Meters Project was established to provide a comprehensive review of the current state of smart meter technology, its benefits and risks, and the regulatory framework governing their deployment in California. This report presents the findings of the project.

**Introduction**

Smart meters are digital devices that measure consumption and send the measurement to a utility company. They have been widely adopted in California as part of the state's efforts to increase energy efficiency and reduce greenhouse gas emissions. However, concerns have been raised about the potential health and environmental impacts of smart meter technology.

**Project Objectives**

The CCST Smart Meters Project aimed to:

1. Provide a comprehensive review of the current state of smart meter technology.
2. Assess the benefits and risks associated with smart meter deployment in California.
3. Evaluate the regulatory framework governing smart meter deployment in California.
4. Identify areas for improvement in the regulation of smart meters.

**Methodology**

The project used a multi-disciplinary approach, involving experts from various fields, including science, technology, policy, and law. The team conducted a comprehensive review of existing literature on smart meter technology, its benefits and risks, and regulatory frameworks governing their deployment.

**Findings**

The project found that:

1. Smart meters have the potential to increase energy efficiency and reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
2. However, there are concerns about the potential health impacts of smart meter technology, including electromagnetic field exposure.
3. The current regulatory framework governing smart meter deployment in California is inadequate and needs to be improved.

**Recommendations**

The project recommends:

1. Conducting further research on the health impacts of smart meter technology.
2. Improving the regulatory framework governing smart meter deployment in California.
3. Providing education and outreach to consumers about the benefits and risks associated with smart meters.

**Conclusion**

The CCST Smart Meters Project provides a comprehensive review of the current state of smart meter technology, its benefits and risks, and the regulatory framework governing their deployment in California. The project's findings highlight the need for further research on the health impacts of smart meter technology and improvements to the regulatory framework governing their deployment.

# Solar Energy and Net Metering for Business

## Overview

Anaheim Public Utilities (APU) offers solar energy and net metering programs for businesses. These programs allow businesses to generate their own electricity using solar panels and sell any excess back to the grid.

### Benefits of Solar Energy and Net Metering

*   Reduce energy costs
*   Increase property value
*   Enhance company image
*   Contribute to a cleaner environment

### NEM 2.0 Program

The NEM 2.0 program is APU's new solar energy program that offers a fixed rate for excess energy produced by the business.

### Annual Offer Price (AOP)

The AOP is the price at which APU buys back excess energy from the business.

### Excess Energy Rate Sheet

APU provides an Excess Energy Rate Sheet that outlines the rates and terms of the NEM 2.0 program.

### Battery Storage

Battery storage systems can be used to store excess energy produced by the business during the day for use at night or during power outages.

### Income-Qualified Solar Power Discount Program

This program offers a discount on solar panel costs for businesses that meet certain income requirements.

### Understanding Your NEM 1.0 Solar Bill

APU provides resources to help businesses understand their NEM 1.0 solar bills and make the most of their solar energy system.

## Contact Us

For more information about APU's solar energy and net metering programs, please contact us at:

*   Phone: (714) 765-3300
*   Address: 201 S. Anaheim Blvd., Anaheim, CA 92805
