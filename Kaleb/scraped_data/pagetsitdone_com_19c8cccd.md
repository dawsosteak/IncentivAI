# Success **Stories**
![Goveror Josh Shapiro and a group of Hershey company executives cut a ceremonial ribbon outdoors in front of a new manufacturing facility.](https://s7d9.scene7.com/is/image/statepa/pr_hershey_reese-2-scaled?fmt=webp)
## **Success** speaks for itself
We're not just talk; companies that do business in Pennsylvania find success here. From life sciences leaders to nationally-recognized brands, companies want the same things: a growing workforce, high quality of life, and a business climate that makes growth easier than ever. 
Don't just take our word for it—executives and employees alike have great things to say about Pennsylvania and the opportunities that being here creates. Discover why Pennsylvania is the best place for business from the perspective of major companies who have made it big here. Who knows? Maybe your business could be Pennsylvania's next big success story.
## Meet Top Pennsylvania Companies
[ Transcript (opens in a new tab)](https://pagetsitdone.com/content/dam/copa-getsitdone/en/documents/video-transcripts/transcript%20-%20b%20braun%20success%20story.html)
## B. Braun
Pennsylvania continues to deliver for global manufacturers like [B. Braun(opens in a new tab)](https://www.bbraun.com/en.html). With a $20 million investment to expand medical device manufacturing in the Lehigh Valley—creating at least 200 new jobs and supporting more than 1,700 existing positions—the company is building on decades of success in a state known for its skilled workforce, strategic location, and deep life sciences expertise.
> Here in Pennsylvania, we have the skilled workforce, high quality suppliers, and business environment that enable us to manufacture medical devices used by healthcare providers across the United States."
_— Rob Albert, CEO, B. Braun of America (Lehigh Valley)_

## Lampire Biological Laboratories
Operating out of Pennsylvania for nearly fifty years, [Lampire Biological Laboratories(opens in a new tab)](https://www.lampire.com/) exists at the intersection of Pennsylvania's life sciences and agriculture industries. By providing antibodies and other animal-based samples, the company helps advance research and testing for life-saving medical treatments, all while prioritizing the humane and ethical care of the over 7,500 animals that call its sprawling 600-acre farm home.
[ Transcript (opens in a new tab)](https://pagetsitdone.com/content/dam/copa-getsitdone/en/documents/video-transcripts/transcript%20-%20lampire%20-%20a%20pennsylvania%20success%20story.html)
> Pennsylvania is a very unique location. There are other states that are very attractive to the life science industry, but they don't have the diversification that Pennsylvania does."
_— Gregory F. Krug, President & CEO, Lampire Biological Laboratories (Bedford County)_

## Olympus
Olympus is a Japanese-headquartered global medtech company that designs and delivers diagnostic, surgical, and imaging products. In the U.S., its American operations are known as [Olympus Corporation of the Americas(opens in a new tab)](https://www.olympusamerica.com/), which relocated its North & South American headquarters from New York to Center Valley, Pennsylvania in 2006. The company has continued to grow its operations in Pennsylvania, and the Lehigh Valley region's quality of life and reasonable cost of living make it a place where employees want to stay.
> Olympus is a company that stands on strong principles and a strong history, and has been enabled to thrive because of the opportunities that Pennsylvania has offered."
_— Swarna Alcorn, Business Unit Vice President—Respiratory, Olympus (Lehigh Valley)_

## Dick's Sporting Goods
Founded in 1948 by Dick Stack with $300 from his grandmother’s cookie jar, [Dick’s Sporting Goods(opens in a new tab)](https://investors.dicks.com/about-us/default.aspx) looked at cities across the country — and chose Pittsburgh as its home base. Today, this leading retailer of sporting equipment, athletic apparel and more is still growing, recently acquiring the Foot Locker brand and continuing to experiment with innovative retail concepts. The company currently operates approximately 850 stores across the U.S., and cites its decision to operate out of Pennsylvania as a major strategic win.
[ Transcript (opens in a new tab)](https://pagetsitdone.com/content/dam/copa-getsitdone/en/documents/video-transcripts/transcript%20-%20dicks%20sporting%20goods%20video.html)
> We would encourage anyone who's looking to start a business or looking to grow their business and need a new place for a corporate headquarters... you really should look at Pennsylvania."
_— Edward Stack, Executive Chairman, Dick's Sporting Goods (Allegheny County)_
