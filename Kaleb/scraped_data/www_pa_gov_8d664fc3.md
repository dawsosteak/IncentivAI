# Everyday Lives
![](https://s7d9.scene7.com/is/image/statepa/Everyday-Lives-Cover?ts=1713366701303&dpr=off)
The foundation of Everyday Lives is pivotal in the work that the Office of Developmental Programs (ODP) does every day. 
It is the guide which is considered when policy is developed, programs are designed and considered, and initiatives are implemented. Supporting individuals and their families to achieve an everyday life, is the mission and vision of ODP. 
## Everyday Lives Publications
Everyday Lives: Values in Action serves as a guide to ODP as it develops policy and designs programs. Providers of services use these recommendations to support individuals and their families to achieve everyday lives.

MESSAGE FROM THE DEPUTY SECRETARY The Office of Developmental Programs (ODP) created this statistical report so that all stakeholders can review the number of individuals ODP serves, settings where they live, what services are provided to them, and the costs of those services. It should be noted that, in some cases in this report, information about ODP's autism services is listed separately from Intellectual Disability/Autism (ID/A) programs. This is because the Adult Autism Waiver (AAW) and Adult Community Autism Program (ACAP) are administered directly by the Bureau of Supports for Autism and Special Populations (BSASP), and not by the county systems. BSASP’s information is tracked and measured through different processes. Facts and figures are an important but limited view of the service system. They allow us to assess growth in the programs provided, types of services that are prioritized, and how we are investing in people. In addition to this report, ODP publishes an annual report on strategies and performance measures developed by ODP’s Information Sharing and Advisory Committee (ISAC), as well as various other reports that target specific topical areas or strategies (e.g., waiting list, employment, etc.). All of these are used to assess the system. Within this report, tables/graphs note the source of information, data extraction dates, and other relevant information specific to the data being presented. 4 DEPARTAMENTO DE SERVICIOS HUMANOS DE PENNSYLVANIA DEPARTAMENTO DE SERVICIOS HUMANOS DE PENNSYLVANIA En 1989, la Oficina de Programas para el Desarrollo de Pennsylvania (ODP, por sus siglas en inglés) invitó a personas con discapacidades, familias, defensores, funcionarios gubernamentales y proveedores de servicios a pensar en cómo debería ser el futuro para todas las personas con discapacidades. Pennsylvania había sido un líder nacional en la creación de servicios comunitarios como una alternativa a los servicios institucionales, pero era hora de reflexionar más detenidamente sobre cuál debería ser el propósito de los servicios comunitarios: qué tipo de experiencias y oportunidades deberían tener las personas y cuál debería ser el resultado general. Durante meses, los participantes realizaron investigaciones sobre las mejores prácticas y discutieron sus hallazgos. Es importante destacar que el grupo de planificación preguntó a las personas con discapacidad intelectual qué era importante para ellos. El resultado fue una lista de principios de Everyday Lives que ha guiado a ODP y al sistema de servicio desde que se publicó Everyday Lives en 1991. Profundamente arraigado en el concepto de autodeterminación, Everyday Lives promueve la creencia de que, con el apoyo de familiares y amigos, las personas con discapacidad pueden y deben decidir cómo vivir sus vidas. La Everyday Lives original estableció lo que era importante para las personas con discapacidades y recomendó cambios en el sistema de servicio que incrementó las oportunidades para la plena participación de la comunidad, brindó a las personas más control sobre los servicios y mejoró la rendición de cuentas del sistema para garantizar la salud, la seguridad y los resultados positivos. 5 abril de 2021 abril de 2021 En noviembre de 2014, la ODP formó el Comité Asesor y de Intercambio de Información (ISAC, por sus siglas en inglés) para discutir políticas y prácticas y hacer recomendaciones a la ODP. Como parte de un proceso de planificación para el futuro, más de 265 partes interesadas realizaron investigaciones y evaluaron las mejores prácticas para determinar los pasos más importantes que la ODP debe tomar para mejorar la prestación de servicios. 4 PENNSYLVANIA DEPARTMENT OF HUMAN SERVICES PENNSYLVANIA DEPARTMENT OF HUMAN SERVICES In 1989, the Pennsylvania Office of Developmental Programs (ODP) invited people with disabilities, families, advocates, governmental officials, and service providers to think about what the future should look like for all people with disabilities. Pennsylvania had been a national leader in creating community services as an alternative to institutional services, but it was time to think more deeply about what the purpose of community services should be — what kinds of experiences and opportunities people should have and what the overall outcome should be. For months, participants conducted research about best practices and discussed their findings. Most importantly, the planning group asked people with intellectual disabilities what was important to them. The result was a list of Everyday Lives principles that has guided ODP and the service system since Everyday Lives was published in 1991. Deeply rooted in the concept of self-determination, Everyday Lives promotes the belief that, with the support of family and friends, people with disabilities can and should decide how to live their lives. The original Everyday Lives stated what was important to people with disabilities and recommended changes to the service system that increased opportunities for full community participation, provided individuals with more control over services, and improved system accountability in assuring health, safety, and positive outcomes. 12 PENNSYLVANIA DEPARTMENT OF HUMAN SERVICES PENNSYLVANIA DEPARTMENT OF HUMAN SERVICES WHAT IS IMPORTANT TO FAMILIES Family is integral to an everyday life. We are born into families and we choose families. Families are the foundation of our early development and often our achievements as adults. If people with disabilities are to enjoy the everyday life that all citizens should enjoy, families will play a key role beginning in the earliest years by having a positive and promising vision for their child. Families can do this by knowing how to facilitate their child’s full inclusion into the school and community, by ensuring their child has all the experiences and opportunity needed to learn and grow toward independence, by having good advocacy skills, and by preparing for all of life’s transitions into adulthood. Families need support. Families need information, advocacy skills, and connections to other families. The realization of Everyday Lives is dependent on the service system successfully partnering with families to achieve the hopes and dreams of their family members. Families embrace and envision person-centered, family-supported, values-based everyday lives for their family members, regardless of changes in administrations, fiscal fluctuations, and unforeseeable influences. Families want these values adopted and embedded into ODP’s policies and practices across the service system.
