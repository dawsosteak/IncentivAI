#  Generation Interconnection 
![Electrical Plug-in with Earth globe design](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=481)
![Sun setting behind 4 wind turbines and 2 solar panels](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=482)
Developers looking to develop distributed generation facilities including microgrids within the City of Anaheim’s service territory and interconnect with Anaheim’s electric system should review and comply with [Rule 22](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/2056/Energy-Resource-Facility-Interconnection-Requirements-PDF?bidId=) of Anaheim’s Rules, Rates and Regulations and [Generation Interconnection Standards and ](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/484)[Guidelines](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/484).
Distributed generation is generally defined as generating resources whose combined gross output is less than 20 megawatts (MW). Most distributed generation includes: 
  * Combined heat and power (CHP)
  * Fuel cells
  * Micro turbines
  * Reciprocating engines
  * Small turbines
  * Solar photovoltaics


Standard Form Interconnection Agreement
Anaheim has also developed a [Standard Form Interconnection Agreement](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/487) which sets forth the terms and conditions that allow distributed generation developers to interconnect generation facilities with Anaheim’s electric system.
The Standard Form Interconnection Agreement along with [Generation interconnection Standards and Guidelines](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/484) help simplify interconnection process, establish standards and guidelines, and facilitate interconnection of distributed generation with Anaheim’s electric system safely and efficiently.
To get started, have your contractor submit plans to Anaheim Public Utilities' Electrical Engineering department for review and to determine if an Interconnection Agreement is needed. Preliminary approval of the site plan and interconnection single-line are required by Electrical Engineering before proceeding with obtaining a Building Permit. See this [link](https://www.anaheim.net/6136) for more information.
