# Department of Services for Children, Youth and their Families
Safe & healthy children,  
resilient families,  
strong communities.
![DCSYF's Suicide Prevention campaign infographic with tagline: "It's okay to…NOT feel okay. You are not alone. #NotAloneDE".](https://kids.delaware.gov/wp-content/uploads/sites/192/2025/06/foster-care-image-06-2025.png)
## Make a difference in a child’s life... Foster Parenting is Worth It
Consider becoming a foster parent! Join our monthly information sessions and learn how you can support children and families as they work through challenging times. Foster parents play an essential role in the reunification of families. DFS Foster Parent Information Sessions are held in New Castle County and Kent or Sussex Counties, typically on Tuesdays or Wednesdays from 6 PM to 9 PM.
**Register today at[de.gov/fostercare](https://de.gov/fostercare "This link opens in another tab/external link")**
![DCSYF's Suicide Prevention campaign infographic with tagline: "It's okay to…NOT feel okay. You are not alone. #NotAloneDE".](https://kids.delaware.gov/wp-content/uploads/sites/192/2024/12/not-alone-de_340px_height.png)
## It's okay to... NOT feel okay.
Reach out for help if you or a loved one is at risk of suicide. Suicide is preventable. We are ready to help support teens, parents and caregivers to provide preventative services such as counseling, resource sharing, and more.
**Text 988 for assistance or call[  
1-800-969-HELP (4357)](tel:18009694357)**
**We are here to help. #NotAloneDE**
**View[Teen Suicide Prevention Resources](https://kids.delaware.gov/prevention-and-behavioral-health-services/suicide-prevention/)**

## I am a ... 
Kids Topics Parent / Guardian Kid (12-18 years old) Foster Parent Future Foster Parent Adoptive Parent Future Adoptive Parent Health Care Provider Member of the Media Supportive Family Member Educator Law Enforcement / Probation Officer
  

  

Your devotion and commitment to your children are significant to their well-being. As caregivers, sometimes navigating through life’s changes with your family can become overwhelming.   
  
If you are seeking help with your child's special needs, or assistance with a child who has experienced trauma or struggling with mental health or addiction, DSCYF seeks to partner with you by providing respectful, supportive resources and services.   
  
Our shared goals are for restoring resiliency and strengthening your child and family.
  

[View More ](https://kids.delaware.gov/i-am-a-parent-guardian/)
![Optimism and Wellness](https://kids.delaware.gov/wp-content/uploads/sites/192/2019/05/parent-guardian.jpg)  

  

If you or a friend have experienced trauma, are struggling with a mental health or a drug - related emergency, we can help. Contact the Crisis Hotline at [1-800-969-HELP](tel:1-800-969-4357), available 24 hours a day, seven days a week. You can also reach out to the Crisis Text Line 24/7 by texting DE to the number 741741.
  

[View More ](https://kids.delaware.gov/i-am-a-kid-12-18-years-old/)
![ We Can Help!](https://kids.delaware.gov/wp-content/uploads/sites/192/2019/06/topic-kid.jpg)  

  

There's no small way to give back and impact the life of a child or youth. As foster parents, you play such an important role in a child's life. Foster parents are needed to provide short-term and safe care for youth who are experiencing difficult circumstances. 
  

[View More ](https://kids.delaware.gov/i-am-a-foster-parent/)
![You are Heroes!](https://kids.delaware.gov/wp-content/uploads/sites/192/2019/06/topic-foster-parent.jpg)  

  

Interested in taking the next step toward becoming a foster parent but don't know where to begin? Don't worry, reach out to the Division of Family Services team to see your next steps. Learn about the requirements for foster parenting and information sessions.
  

[View More ](https://kids.delaware.gov/i-am-a-future-foster-parent/)
![A Safe and Compassionate Place](https://kids.delaware.gov/wp-content/uploads/sites/192/2019/06/topic-future-foster.jpg)  

  

Adoption is a mixture of many emotions and experiences, such as joy, for both adoptive parents and children. While adoption brings resolution to young people’s need for permanency, the on-going needs for further bonding for both parents and children can bring about many challenges.   
  
Learn about available resources and aids to strengthen relationships and continue hope and resiliency for children.
  

[View More ](https://kids.delaware.gov/i-am-an-adoptive-parent/)
![Love Makes a Family](https://kids.delaware.gov/wp-content/uploads/sites/192/2019/06/topic-adoptive-parent.jpg)  

  

Many children are in need of a loving and caring family who will provide a permanent nurturing home. As an adoptive parent, you can help youth by relationally cultivating their growth and development. You can provide supportive guidance and encouragement leading to hope as they become part of your family.
  

[View More ](https://kids.delaware.gov/i-am-a-future-adoptive-parent/)
![Adopt a Child, Become a Hero](https://kids.delaware.gov/wp-content/uploads/sites/192/2019/06/topic-future-adoptive-parent.jpg)  

  

As a medical or mental health professional, you are an essential part of the coordination and continuum of care of young people with DSCYF.   
  
Get information about intervention and coordination of care with Medicaid /managed care organizations, mandatory reporting of abuse or neglect and other support resources.
  

[View More ](https://kids.delaware.gov/i-am-a-health-care-provider/)
![A Continuum of Healthcare](https://kids.delaware.gov/wp-content/uploads/sites/192/2019/06/topic-healthcare-provider.jpg)  

  

Members of the media who want to stay informed about vital updates and issues to DSCYF may view our press releases, social media channels or contact our office directly.
  

[View More ](https://kids.delaware.gov/i-am-a-member-of-the-media/)
![Members of the Media](https://kids.delaware.gov/wp-content/uploads/sites/192/2019/06/topic-media-1.jpg)  

  

Supportive family and community members who are seeking ways to engage with youth or offer assistance may find help here.
  

[View More ](https://kids.delaware.gov/i-am-a-supportive-family-member/)
![Support Your Family/ Community’s Youth](https://kids.delaware.gov/wp-content/uploads/sites/192/2019/06/topic-family-member.jpg)  

  

Aside from caregivers and family, education professionals provide support and guidance to our youth. DSCYF provides resources for teachers and education professionals to train and support them in their work.
  

[View More ](https://kids.delaware.gov/i-am-an-educator/)
![Equipping Education Professionals](https://kids.delaware.gov/wp-content/uploads/sites/192/2019/06/topic-educator.jpg)  

  

Continued collaboration with members of law enforcement, the judicial system, DSCYF’s divisions and community partners is valuable to the restoration of peace, safety and prevention of recidivism among youth. Find helpful resources here. 
  

[View More ](https://kids.delaware.gov/i-am-a-law-enforcement-probation-officer/)
