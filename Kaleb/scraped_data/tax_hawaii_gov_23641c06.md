# **Recent Updates**
  * #### Rental Collection Agreement
Persons authorized under agreements to collect rent (third party rent collectors) on behalf of owners of real property and transient accommodations located within Hawaii are required to file Form RCA-1 electronically. Form RCA-1 will be available for filing at Hawaii Tax Online starting on January 2, 2025.
Visit the [Rental Collection Agreement Information](https://tax.hawaii.gov/rca/) page for more information.
  * #### Pass-Through Entity Taxation
For taxable years beginning after December 31, 2022, partnerships and S corporations may elect to pay Hawai’i income taxes at the entity level. Eligible members of an electing pass-through entity (PTE) may claim a Hawai’i income tax credit for the pro rata share of PTE taxes paid.
Visit the [Pass-Through Entity Taxation](https://tax.hawaii.gov/pte) page for more information.

RIDESHARE/FOOD DELIVERY SERVICE DRIVER CHARGED FOR FAILURE TO FILE TAXES
* * *
News Release 2026-03
* * *
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE March 3, 2026
* * *
HONOLULU – In a case recently investigated by the Hawaiʻi Department of Taxation (DOTAX) and prosecuted by the Hawaiʻi Department of the Attorney General’s Criminal Justice Division, Troy R. Levaughn was charged with four counts for willfully failing to file General Excise Tax (GET) returns under section 231-35, Hawaii Revised Statutes (HRS).
* * *
Rideshare drivers — also referred to as transportation network company (TNC) drivers — are required to file tax returns and pay GET on their gross income or gross proceeds, including any discretionary tips they receive. The terms “Transportation Network Company” and “Transportation Network Company Driver” are defined in section 431:10C-701, HRS. Drivers for food delivery service marketplaces are treated similarly.
* * *
For purposes of GET, TNCs and “food delivery service marketplaces” are considered
* * *
> “marketplace facilitators” and their drivers are “marketplace sellers.” Detailed
information on the status of rideshare and food delivery service companies and drivers and their tax obligations can be found in DOTAX’s Tax Information Release 2019-03 (Revised) and Tax Information Release No. 2025-01.
* * *
> “Rideshare and food delivery service drivers are responsible for reporting their income
and filing required tax returns,” said Tax Director Gary Suganuma. “When these obligations are not met, the department will take steps to address the noncompliance, so we encourage these companies and drivers to educate themselves and to ensure tax compliance.”
* * *
Criminal defendants are presumed innocent unless and until proven guilty in a court of law.
* * *

TAX FILING TIPS FOR THE 2026 TAX SEASON April 20 is the Deadline to File Individual State Income Tax Returns
* * *
News Release 2026-02
* * *
**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**
January 20, 2026
* * *
HONOLULUThe Hawaiʻi Department of Taxation (DOTAX) has officially kicked off tax season and is sharing important tips to help taxpayers avoid unnecessary delays and frustration during the filing process.
* * *
**Start Preparing**
Begin gathering and organizing tax documents early—such as Form W -2 or HW-2, Wage and Tax Statement, Form 1099-G, certain government payments, including unemployment compensation, and Form 1099-K (payments for goods or services). Doing so will ensure there is enough time to obtain all necessary documents and forms to accurately file income tax returns by the Monday, April 20, 2026 deadline.
* * *
DOTAX also reminds taxpayers that most income is taxable, even if a 1099 form was not received. This includes earnings from the gig economysuch as ride-share driving, food deliveryor task services, property rentals and other freelance work, as well as income from digital assets.
* * *
**File Early**
Note: The federal Internal Revenue Service (IRS) tax season will open on Monday, January 26, 2026, and the filing deadline for federal tax returns or application for an extension to file is Wednesday, April 15, 2026.
* * *
Filing early is one of the best ways to protect taxpayer refunds from cybercriminals. If someone has been a victim of identity theft or want added security, they can enroll in DOTAX’s ID Theft Protection Program through Hawaiʻi Tax Online at https://hitax.hawaii.gov. Participants in the program will receive a notification when an income tax return is filed using their name and Social Security number and will be asked to confirm that they submitted the return.
* * *
**File Electronically**
E-filing offers several advantages over paper filing: it improves accuracy, provides confirmation that a return was submitted, and speeds up processing so refunds are received faster. Taxpayers can e-file using commercial tax preparation software or file for free directly through DOTAX using Hawaiʻi Tax Online at https://hitax.hawaii.gov. Hawaiʻi Tax Online offers two filing options: a guided N-11 return for taxpayers with simple tax situations and a non-guided return designed for more complex tax situations and experienced filers.
* * *
**Use Direct Deposit**
Using direct deposit is the quickest and safest way to receive a refund. Processing times are longer for taxpayers who choose to receive their refunds by check.
* * *
Check for Accuracy and Errors
* * *
Avoid processing delays, tax return adjustments and additional correspondence from DOTAX by making sure all Social Security numbers are correct, the appropriate filing status is selected, attaching required forms and income statements (HW-2s/W-2s, 1099-G, 1099-K), and signing the return.
* * *
**Check Your Refund Status**
Taxpayers may check the status of their income tax return filing on Hawaii Tax Online by visiting https://tax.hawaii.gov/wheres-my-refund. They will need their Social Security Number or Individual Taxpayer Identification Number and the exact refund amount claimed, as shown on their tax return.
* * *
## For More Help
DOTAX has a “Frequently Asked Questions” (FAQs) page that can be accessed at https://tax.hawaii.gov/faq/. If unable to resolve an issue after reading form instructions and searching the DOTAX website, Taxpayer Services agents are available by phone at 808-587-4242 Monday – Friday, 8:00 a.m. to 4:00 p.m.
