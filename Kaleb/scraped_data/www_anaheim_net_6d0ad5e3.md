#  Advanced Provider Response Unit (APRU) 
The Advanced Provider Response Unit (APRU) is a Mobile Integrated Health program developed by Anaheim Fire & Rescue that goes beyond the traditional 911 response. The APRU pairs a highly trained Nurse Practitioner with Anaheim Fire paramedics to follow up after critical incidents such as overdoses, behavioral health crises, and other calls where additional support may be needed after emergency crews leave the scene.
Rather than waiting for the next emergency, the APRU reaches out to residents following a crisis. The team works with individuals facing challenges such as addiction, mental health concerns, homelessness, or medical needs that make it difficult to remain stable and healthy. These situations affect not only the individual, but also families, neighborhoods, businesses, and the community as a whole.
By connecting with residents soon after an incident, the APRU helps break the cycle of repeated emergencies. The team provides basic medical care, evaluates ongoing health needs, and may offer appropriate medications to support recovery when needed. The APRU also helps remove common barriers by guiding individuals through available services, assisting with transportation needs, and helping them understand the next steps toward getting help.
A key part of the program is connecting residents with the right resources. The APRU works closely with behavioral health providers, treatment programs, housing services, and community organizations to help individuals move toward stability and improved quality of life. Every person is treated with dignity and respect, with the goal of helping them find long-term support.
The mission of the Advanced Provider Response Unit is to improve outcomes for individuals while strengthening the overall well-being of the Anaheim community. By helping vulnerable residents get the care and support they need, the program reduces repeat emergency calls, lessens the strain on hospitals and public safety resources, and contributes to safer neighborhoods.
Anaheim Fire & Rescue remains committed to innovative programs that support both public safety and community health. The APRU reflects that commitment by ensuring that care continues even after the emergency call is over.
