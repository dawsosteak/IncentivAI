# Transmission of Child Pornography To A Minor Charges in Tampa, FL
Being charged with transmission of child pornography to a minor in Tampa, Florida, is a serious legal situation with potentially life-changing consequences. This offense typically involves the alleged act of sending, sharing, or otherwise transmitting sexually explicit images, videos, or other media involving minors to another person who is also a minor. 
These charges often come with emotionally charged investigations, aggressive prosecution, and stiff penalties, both under Florida law and federal statutes. If you or someone you know is being investigated or has been charged, it is essential to retain an experienced Tampa lawyer for transmission of child pornography to a minor. At [Hanlon Law](https://www.criminalattorneytampa.net/), we provide a strong, informed, and personalized defense for those accused of sex crimes, especially those involving electronic communication and minors.
### What Is Transmission of Child Pornography to a Minor?
Under [Florida Statute § 847.0138](https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2019/847.0138), any person who knowingly transmits or distributes material that contains sexual conduct by a child to another person, especially a minor, can face felony charges. This includes digital communications over email, text messages, apps, chat rooms, or social media platforms.
On the federal level, [18 U.S. Code § 2252](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2252) and [§ 2252A](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2252A) govern the transportation, distribution, and transmission of child pornography across state lines or through means of interstate or international commerce, including the internet. When the alleged recipient is a minor, or presumed to be one by the defendant, the penalties can be substantially enhanced.
A federal conviction for transmitting child pornography to a minor may carry mandatory minimum sentences starting at 15 years, with potential for much longer terms if aggravating factors exist. These charges can also trigger lifetime registration as a sex offender, loss of civil rights, and devastating damage to your personal and professional life. 
### Tampa, Florida Sex Crimes Attorney
One of the most difficult aspects of defending against charges of transmitting child pornography to a minor is the sheer volume of digital evidence that prosecutors can use to support their case. However, just because material was sent or found on a device does not automatically prove intent or knowledge. 
There are numerous potential defenses that may apply. For instance, a device may be shared among multiple users, or may have been compromised or hacked. Content can be mislabeled, received inadvertently, or downloaded without the user’s full awareness, especially through peer-to-peer networks or malicious pop-ups. The prosecution must prove criminal intent, not just presence of illegal material. 
Furthermore, if law enforcement acted improperly during an investigation in any way, your rights may have been violated. At Hanlon Law, we take a meticulous approach to examining how the investigation was conducted and whether your constitutional protections were respected.
A major defense strategy in these cases may involve the legal doctrine of entrapment, especially if the investigation was initiated by law enforcement agents posing as minors. Entrapment occurs when an individual is persuaded or coerced by government agents to commit a crime they were not otherwise predisposed to commit. 
It’s not uncommon for law enforcement to initiate contact, escalate conversations, and persistently prompt illegal behavior during online sting operations. If you were manipulated or misled into sending explicit material under such circumstances, your attorney can present a strong entrapment defense. 
Additional defenses may include lack of intent, lack of knowledge, false allegations, or insufficient evidence to prove that you knew the recipient was a minor or that you had knowingly transmitted illegal content.
