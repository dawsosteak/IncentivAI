#  Business Assistance Program 
The Business Assistance Program offers resources and information beneficial for business owners in the process of opening a new business, remodeling or expanding an existing business.
## MONITORING & COMMUNICATION
Business owners will be assigned a Business Solutions Specialist (a one-point contact from plan submission to final inspection) who will be available to answer questions, monitor the progress of the project, and link communication between City staff and business owners.
## JUMP-START MEETINGS
Pre-development jump start meetings with Planning, Building and other City of Anaheim staff are available as needed to review your project and provide guidance on items to be considered as part of your overall scope of work.
## AGENCY COORDINATION
Specialists coordinate among City departments and outside agencies, including Business License, Anaheim Public Utilities, Gas Company, Department of Alcohol Beverage Control (ABC), Orange County Environmental Health and the Orange County Sanitation District.
## PROPERTY INFORMATION
The Planning Department offers an “[Online Property Resource](http://www.anaheim.net/625/Site-Selection)” to clients seeking available properties and property information. The “Online Property Resource” provides a database of available properties, demographic reports, land use and zoning information, and infrastructure information.
## FEE DEFERRAL
This program allows for payment of the impact fees for new construction (both commercial and residential) to be deferred until the development is ready for final inspection, a temporary or final certificate of occupancy. The purpose of this program is to reduce up-front costs and encourage new development within the City of Anaheim. 
To apply, the [Fee Deferral Application](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/11267/) and supporting documentation must be submitted to your assigned Business Solutions Specialist. Fees will be established and a fee deferral covenant will be prepared for recordation with the County Clerk Recorder's office. Once recorded, impact fees may be deferred until projects are ready to schedule final inspections or to request Temporary Certificates of Occupancy (TCO). See the [Fee Deferral Guide](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/17867/) for more information.   
  
The following links are examples of fee deferral covenants: 
  * [Commercial Fee Deferral Agreement greater than $1M](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/63443/)
  * [Commercial Fee Deferral Agreement less than $1M](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/63444/)
  * [Residential Fee Deferral Agreement](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/63445/) (Interest shall not be charged on designated residential development projects in accordance with SB 937).
