#  Stay Housed Anaheim Program 
The **Stay Housed Anaheim** program promotes housing stability for Anaheim renters by offering **one-time** emergency assistance with rent _or_ security deposit **owed** up to $3,000. Assistance with utilities owed up to $500 may only be considered with a rental assistance request.
Applicants must review the eligibility requirements and submit all required documentation below to be eligible for review.**Incomplete applications will not be considered for assistance.**
Submitting an application does not guarantee assistance. If an application is determined not eligible, an applicant may not reapply for a minimum six-month period.
**Landlord or utility provider participation is required.** If an application is approved, payments are made directly to a landlord or utility provider. 
Additional documentation or verification may be requested at any time to determine eligibility.
Applications will be accepted online on a first-come, first-served basis as funding is available.
Funding for this program is limited. This program may close at any time.
### Eligibility Requirements:
  * Anaheim renter for the past 12 consecutive months (owners, subleases and residents in unincorporated Anaheim are not eligible for this program). Anaheim tenants enrolled in a housing program or receiving housing assistance are not eligible for this program.
  * At risk of housing instability or facing a destabilizing event
  * Demonstrate ability to maintain housing beyond assistance period
  * Household income at 50 percent of Orange County area median income or lower

**Household Size** | **1** | **2** | **3** | **4** | **5** | **6** | **7** | **8**  
---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---  
**Maximum Income** | $59,250 | $67,700 | $76,150 | $84,600 | $91,400 | $98,150 | $104,950 | $111,700  
  

### Required Program Documentation:
  * Proof of residency in city of Anaheim
    * Current and complete lease agreement 
    * Anaheim Public Utilities bill
  * Documentation of:
    * Rent and/or utilities owed, such as a rent ledger, utility bill, etc.
    * Security deposit owed, such as current and complete lease agreement showing unit information and security deposit amount
  * Income documentation for all household members 18 years or older
