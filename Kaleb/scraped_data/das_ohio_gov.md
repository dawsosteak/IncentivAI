![logo for Disability Inclusion: Action, Access, Attitude. The State of Ohio is a disability inclusion state and strives to be a model employer of individuals with disabilities. State of Ohio Agencies are proud to support the disability inclusion initiatives outlined for state agencies, boards, and commissions, in accordance with Governor Mike DeWine’s Executive Order 2019-03D. If you would like to contact an ADA Coordinator to request a reasonable accommodation or for any related matter, we invite you to use this directory to locate the agency you are looking for to find the ADA Coordinator who can assist you. If you require assistance with using this directory, please contact Chelsea Wonski, Ohio Disability Inclusion Program, odi@das.ohio.gov.

#  IT-09 Digital Accessibility 
## State of Ohio Administrative Policy
**No: IT-09** - Information Technology
**Effective:** January 10, 2025
**Issued By:** Kathleen C. Madden, Director
* * *
  1. ## Purpose
This policy establishes minimum Digital Accessibility requirements for Agency Web Content and Mobile Applications that are made available to the public by state agencies, boards, and commissions. All defined terms are capitalized and a link to the glossary of definitions is found in section IV.
  2. ## Scope
This policy applies to all state Agencies, boards, and commissions under the authority of the Governor (collectively referred to as Agency or Agencies).
  3. ## Policy
Agencies must establish a Digital Accessibility strategy that applies to the design, development, implementation, and maintenance of Agency Web Content and Mobile Applications that are made available to the public. At a minimum, Agency Web Content and Mobile Applications must meet the following requirements:
    1. ### Web Content and Mobile Application Accessibility Requirements
Agencies must ensure that Web Content and Mobile Applications are compliant with the accessibility standards established by the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA in accordance with 42 USC Section 12132 (Title II of the Americans with Disabilities Act) and 28 CFR Part 35 (Nondiscrimination on the Basis of Disability; Accessibility of Web Information and Services of State and Local Government Entities) (The Rule).  
  
To assist in achieving website accessibility, Agencies must adopt the design and content management standards for Public-Facing Web Pages as defined by the DAS Innovate Ohio Platform (IOP) team and as required in Ohio Administrative Policy IT-08, “Website Standardization.”
    2. ### Outsourced Web Content and Mobile Application Development
Agencies must require that all contracted, in-kind, or other third-party Web Content or Mobile Application solutions comply with the accessibility standards established by the Final Rule and WCAG 2.1, Level AA.
    3. ### Commercial-Off-the-Shelf (COTS) Software Purchases
For any Web Content or Mobile Application COTS software purchases, including Software-as-a-Service, Agencies must require that the supplier certifies compliance with WCAG 2.1, Level AA.
    4. ### Reasonable Accommodations and Employment Web Content
When designing internal or externally facing websites that will be utilized by state employees or job applicants with disabilities, Agencies must comply with the reasonable accommodation and Web Content requirements defined in Ohio Administrative Policy HR-54, “Disability Inclusion and Accessibility for State of Ohio Employees and Applicants.”
    5. ### Exceptions
Section 35.201 of The Rule details Web Content accessibility compliance exceptions for Archived Web Content; preexisting Conventional Electronic Documents; certain content posted by third parties; or individualized, password-protected or otherwise secured Conventional Electronic Documents; and preexisting social media posts. (Refer to Section VI. Resources for a link to The Rule, which fully defines the required criteria for authorized exceptions.)
    6. ### Conforming Alternate Versions
Section 35.202 of The Rule details the criteria for when a Conforming Alternate Version of Web Content is acceptable.
    7. ### Accessibility Statements
Agencies must include an accessibility statement at a minimum on the Agency Home Page and primary entry points or provide a link to an accessibility statement (refer to Section VI. Resources for an Ohio.gov example). An accessibility statement must also be included for Mobile Applications, either on the launch screen, throughout the application in a footer, or through a dedicated section such as Help or Accessibility. The accessibility statement must include: 
      1. A statement of compliance with WCAG 2.1 Level AA, if compliance is attained.
      2. A statement describing steps taken to ensure continuing compliance if compliance is attained.
      3. A feedback mechanism for users to report accessibility issues with Web Content or Mobile Applications.
      4. A link to the state’s ADA Coordinators Directory (refer to Section VI. Resources) along with a statement that state employees and job applicants with disabilities can contact the appropriate agency ADA Coordinator to request reasonable accommodations in accordance with Ohio Administrative Policy HR-54, “Disability Inclusion and Accessibility for State of Ohio Employees and Applicants.”
    8. ### Compliance
In accordance with The Rule, Agency Web Content and Mobile Applications must be compliant by April 24, 2026.
  4. ## Definitions
All defined terms are capitalized within the policy and the online glossary of definitions can be found at [IT Policy Glossary - Department of Administrative Services](https://das.ohio.gov/wps/portal/gov/das/technology-and-strategy/it-policy-glossary "null").
