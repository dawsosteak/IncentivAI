# Federal Sexual Exploitation of Children in Tampa, FL
## Tampa lawyer for Federal Sexual Exploitation of Children
Tampa, Florida, has become a focal point in the nationwide effort to combat child sexual exploitation. Through rigorous federal enforcement and initiatives like [_Project Safe Childhood_](https://www.justice.gov/psc), the [_Middle District of Florida_](https://www.flmd.uscourts.gov/) has prosecuted more than 1,500 individuals for child exploitation offenses. These cases often involve severe federal statutes, such as [_18 U.S. Code § 2251_](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-1999-title18-section2251&num=0&edition=1999), which addresses the sexual exploitation of children. That’s why you’ll need a Tampa lawyer for federal sexual exploitation of children if you’ve been accused of this crime. 
## Federal Statutes and Penalties
Under 18 U.S. Code § 2251, individuals who employ, use, persuade, induce, entice, or coerce a minor to engage in sexually explicit conduct for the purpose of producing a visual depiction of such conduct face stringent penalties. Convictions can result in imprisonment ranging from 15 to 30 years. Enhanced sentences apply for repeat offenders, with penalties escalating to life imprisonment for those with multiple prior convictions.
## Notable Child Sexual Exploitation Cases in Tampa
Several high-profile cases in Tampa highlight the severe legal consequences of such offenses. We are briefly highlighting just some of those cases from the last few years:
  * **Andre Eugene Favreau (2016)** : Having pled guilty on January 27, 2016, Favreau received a 25-year sentence for [_online enticement of a minor and transportation and receipt of child pornography_](https://www.justice.gov/usao-mdfl/pr/tampa-man-sentenced-25-years-online-child-exploitation). His computer contained more than 140 images and 85 videos of child pornography, and he had been distributing such material online.
  * **Demetrius Reddick (2023)** : After pleading guilty on September 13, 2022, Reddick was sentenced to 10 years in federal prison for [_attempting to coerce and entice a minor into participating in sexual activity_](https://www.justice.gov/usao-mdfl/pr/tampa-man-sentenced-10-years-federal-prison-attempted-coercion-and-enticement-minor). He had engaged in online communications with an undercover agent posing as a 13-year-old girl and traveled to meet her with the intent to engage in sexual activities.
  * **Dontae Dajuan Burton (2025)** : Indicted on November 12, 2024, Burton was [_convicted of sex trafficking two minors and producing child sex abuse material_](https://www.justice.gov/usao-mdfl/pr/tampa-man-convicted-sex-trafficking-two-minors-and-production-child-sex-abuse-material). He arranged commercial sexual activities for the minors by creating and posting ads on adult escort websites, managing communications with clients, and transporting the victims to and from appointments. He faces a minimum sentence of 15 years, up to life, in federal prison.


As is clear in these examples, the sexual exploitation of children is a federal offense, which comes with severe consequences. 
## Enforcement and Prevention Efforts
The U.S. Attorney's Office for the Middle District of Florida, in collaboration with agencies like [_Homeland Security Investigations (HSI) Tampa_](https://www.ice.gov/node/65593), the FBI, and the [_Hillsborough County Sheriff's Office_](https://www.teamhcso.com/), actively investigates and prosecutes child exploitation cases day in and day out. [_HSI's Operation Predator_](https://www.ice.gov/factsheets/predator), an international initiative launched in 2003, has been instrumental in identifying and apprehending individuals involved in the sexual exploitation of children. Our legal team works closely with these agencies in getting criminals of such heinous crimes off the streets. 
## Reporting and Resources
The public plays a crucial role in combating child exploitation. Suspected cases can be reported to HSI's 24-hour tip line at 1-866-DHS-2-ICE or through the [_National Center for Missing & Exploited Children_](https://www.missingkids.org/home) at 1-800-THE-LOST. These resources are vital in the ongoing effort to protect children and bring perpetrators to justice.
