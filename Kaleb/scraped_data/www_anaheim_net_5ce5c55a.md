#  Solar Energy and Net Metering 
Installing solar panels is a big decision for a resident. Anaheim Public Utilities is here to help you go solar at your property. If you are planning to install solar panels at your residence, please ensure your system is properly permitted by the City’s Planning and Building Department and meets interconnection requirements from Anaheim Public Utilities. 
Our current [NEM 2.0](https://www.anaheim.net/5587) program is a wholesale-based rate NEM program. View our rates for excess energy [here](https://www.anaheim.net/5768). Anaheim is not going to NEM 3.0. 
**Important Note**  
Early NEM 1.0 programs in California were intended to encourage private investment in solar and to increase the amount of renewable energy used in California. While early NEM 1.0 programs were successful in meeting these goals, they also had a tendency to shift the cost of maintaining the local grid to non-solar customers. Anaheim Public Utilities is a not-for-profit municipal utility and as such, is required to recover our cost of maintaining the local grid and purchasing power on behalf of all customers. NEM 1.0 customers who are grandfathered will not be placed on NEM 2.0 unless they seek to expand their solar panels, at which time, customers will be placed solely on NEM 2.0 for their entire system.
### Here are the steps to installing solar for your Anaheim home:
  1. **Right size your PV system by ensuring your home is as energy efficient as possible.**
  2. **Know how much solar energy your home needs.**  
Obtain your annual energy usage by logging into your account online to check your past 12 months of energy needs. If you don’t have online billing, please call customer service at 714-765-3300.
  3. [**Understand how net energy metering (NEM) 2.0 works.**](https://www.anaheim.net/5587)  
Our 2.0 program is a wholesale-based program. [See the rate sheet for excess energy under NEM 2.0](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/34968/NEM-20-Excess-Energy-Rate-Sheet-?bidId=)
  4. **Decide on ways to install solar: Purchase, Lease, or Power Purchase Agreement.**  
Understand the different ways to go solar for your home.
  5. **Obtain contractor bids from at least three (3) solar contractors.**
  6. **Contractor will submit engineering plans to[Anaheim Planning & Building](https://www.anaheim.net/5252) online for review.**   
If under 10kW, the permit will be issued without review.
  7. **After building permit is issued, contractor will build the solar PV system.**
  8. ****Customer can operate the system after** Anaheim Planning & Building signs off on the building permit. **Anaheim Public Utilities does not issue the Permission to Operate.
