# Selling and Buying of Children in Tampa, Florida
Facing charges related to the selling or buying of children is among the most devastating and aggressively prosecuted criminal offenses in both Florida and federal courts. In Tampa, Florida, individuals accused of these crimes are often investigated by multiple law enforcement agencies, including the FBI, Homeland Security Investigations (HSI), and local task forces focused on human trafficking and child exploitation.
The severity of this charge stems from its association with human trafficking, child abuse, and exploitation crimes, all of which carry extreme legal and social consequences. If you are under investigation or have already been charged, securing an experienced Tampa lawyer for selling and buying of children charges is critical. At Hanlon Law, we are dedicated to defending the rights of the accused and ensuring a fair, meticulous defense.
## What Constitutes the Selling or Buying of Children?
The offense typically involves allegations that a person offered, received, solicited, or facilitated the transfer of a child, whether for illegal adoption, labor, sexual exploitation, or other unlawful purposes. Under [_18 U.S. Code § 2251A_](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2251A), the federal government prohibits the sale or purchase of a minor for use in the production of sexually explicit material. Florida law, under [_Florida Statute § 847.0145_](https://m.flsenate.gov/Statutes/847.0145), similarly criminalizes this act, particularly when it relates to exploitation or child pornography. 
These crimes are prosecuted as first-degree felonies both in Florida and at the federal level. Severe, life-altering penalties may include:
  * Mandatory minimum sentences of 30 years in prison (and potential sentences of life in prison)
  * Lifetime sex offender registration
  * Significant monetary fines
  * Permanent damage to personal and professional reputation
  * Civil asset forfeiture and loss of parental rights


Because of the perceived severity of the offense, prosecutors pursue these cases aggressively, often with the full weight of the federal government behind them.
## Complex Investigations and Overlapping Jurisdictions in Tampa, Florida
Cases involving the selling or buying of children are often built on digital evidence, sting operations, or multinational investigations. Law enforcement may use undercover agents who pose as buyers or sellers online. These operations can involve ambiguous conversations or manipulated situations that are later presented as proof of criminal intent. 
At Hanlon Law, we understand how these investigations work and how easy it is for someone to be falsely accused, misidentified, or manipulated into a situation they did not fully understand.
## Potential Legal Defenses
No matter how serious the charge, you are entitled to a defense. At Hanlon Law, we build strong, strategic defenses tailored to the unique circumstances of each case. 
Potential defenses may include:
  * Lack of criminal intent
  * Entrapment by law enforcement
  * Mistaken identity or digital misattribution
  * Unlawful searches and seizures of digital evidence
  * Coercion or manipulation in online interactions


We thoroughly review every detail of the government’s case, including how the investigation was initiated, how evidence was collected, and whether your rights were violated in the process.
## The Social and Personal Impact of the Charges
The impact of these allegations extends far beyond the courtroom. Even before trial, the accused may face media scrutiny, loss of employment, ostracization by the community, and family breakdowns. At Hanlon Law, we understand the emotional toll these cases take and work not only to defend your legal rights but also to preserve your dignity and privacy. We handle every case with care, confidentiality, and professionalism, giving you the support you need during one of the most challenging times of your life.
