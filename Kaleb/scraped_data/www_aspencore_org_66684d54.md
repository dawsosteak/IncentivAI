#  Regional Multifamily Energy Retrofit Collaborative  

##  Helping Eagle, Garfield, and Pitkin County communities upgrade multifamily buildings to be more energy-efficient. ﻿
### Living and operating a building in our unique mountain climate presents challenges.  

**We're here to help!**  

The Regional Mutlifamily Energy Retrofit Collaborative, led by CORE, is working to upgrade multifamily properties across Garfield, Eagle, and Pitkin Counties. 
**Our Goal:**  

To make multifamily properties more energy-efficient.
To develop and connect you with the right funding, expertise, and resources to make it happen.
### Thank you to everyone who filled out our questionnaire!
By completing our questionnaire, you're helping us understand how we can best support energy-efficient building upgrades in multifamily properties in our area. Your feedback will shape future resources, including funding, workforce development, and more.
### About Us
The Region﻿al Multifamily Energy Retrofit Collaborative is a group of local nonprofits and government organizations dedicated to supporting multifamily property owners, operators, and residents like you in Garfield, Eagle, and Pitkin Counties. We’re on a mission to help our communities upgrade multifamily buildings to be more energy-efficient and reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
  
The Collaborative members are:
  * [The Community Office for Resource Efficiency (CORE)](https://www.aspencore.org/savings-finder)
  * [Energy Smart Colorado](https://energysmartcolorado.org/)
  * [Holy Cross Energy](https://www.holycross.com/)
  * [Walking Mountains Science Center](https://www.walkingmountains.org/)


  

And, regional partners include stakeholders in: 
  * [Eagle County](https://www.eaglecounty.us/departments___services/public_health/index.php)
  * [Pitkin County](https://pitkincounty.com/1560/Climate-Action)
  * [Garfield County](https://www.garfield-county.com/environmental-health/)  



### What We Do  

The Regional Multifamily Energy Retrofit Collaborative organizations are working together to make big and bold moves to address climate change. Our focus is to reduce energy consumption in the built environment. 
  
Individually, we offer funding, expertise, connections, and resources for our neighbors to make energy-efficiency improvements to their properties. Contact the organization near you to find out how you can take action now:
  * [Eagle County: Walking Mountains Science Center](https://www.walkingmountains.org/)
  * [Garfield County: CLEER](https://cleanenergyeconomy.net/allies/)
  * [Pitkin, Eagle, and Garfield Counties: CORE](https://www.aspencore.org/savings-finder)


  
Collectively, we’re on a mission to scale our impact by reducing energy throughout our largest buildings, such as multifamily housing complexes and neighborhoods. Our primary aim for this initiative is to decrease greenhouse gas emissions, save energy, and improve living conditions in low- to moderate-income housing.
  
In 2023, the Department of Energy awarded us a $100,000 Energizing Rural Communities Prize. During “Phase 1”, we utilized this funding to gain a better understanding of multifamily housing needs in our communities as it relates to greenhouse gas emission reduction and are assessing the feasibility of energy-reducing retrofit projects.
  
Applications for “Phase 2” are due July 2024. The Regional Mutlifamily Energy Retrofit Collaborative is finalizing “Phase 1” of this project and is preparing its application to the Department of Energy for “Phase 2”. In “Phase 2,” we intend to identify buildings for retrofitting, conduct energy audits, secure financing, and implement retrofits like air-source pumps, improved insulation, and more.
