Notice of Meeting This is a notice of a public meeting of the Disadvantaged Communities Advisory Group (DACAG) to the California Energy Commission (CEC) and the California Public Utilities Commission (CPUC). The DACAG aims to begin the meeting promptly at the start time, and the end time is an estimate based on the agenda proposed. The meeting may end sooner or later than the posted end time depending on various factors.
* * *
The meeting will be held via Teleconference & Remote Access, with a primary physical meeting location as noted above. Any of the members of the DACAG may participate remotely. The public can participate remotely as instructed below or physically attend at the primary physical meeting location. The meeting may be recorded.
* * *
Agenda Any of the agenda items, other than welcome, public comments, and future meeting planning, may include a motion for specific action related to the topic and vote of the DACAG.
* * *
1) Welcome and roll call of the members. 2) Public Comment. General public comment period regarding items on or off the agenda related to the work done by the DACAG. 3) Opening remarks by CEC and CPUC leadership. 4) Moment of Gratitude. DACAG members and agency leadership give remarks on departing DACAG members. 5) Electric Program Investment Charge (EPIC) Program Community Engagement Plans. Pacific Gas and Electric (PG&E;), Southern California Edison (SCE), and San Diego Gas and Electric (SDG&E;) will present and seek feedback from the DACAG on community engagement plans for the ratepayer-funded EPIC program, which supports the development of new, emerging, and pre-commercial clean energy innovations in California. Community engagement plans are designed to reflect the needs of communities, including disadvantaged communities.
* * *
2
* * *
6) Proposed DACAG Comment Letter. Relating to the Electric Service Access, Rates and Disconnections priority area, DACAG members may consider and vote to approve a proposed comment letter on the CPUC’s Disconnections Rulemaking (R.18-07-005, Order Instituting Rulemaking to Consider New Approaches to Disconnections and Reconnections to Improve Energy Access and Contain Costs) which recommends to deny utility proposals to increase disconnection rate caps, and continue Arrearage Management Plans (AMP), Percentage of Income Payment Plans (PIPP), and the Community-Based Organizations Case Management (CBO) pilot programs with recommended changes. 7) Proposed DACAG Comment Letter. Relating to DACAG Priority Areas 1 & 2 (Electric Service Access, Rates, & DisconnectionsEnergy Programs; Electricity Generation, Transmission, & DistributionDistributed Energy Resources), in light of AB 1207 (2025) which ends a funding stream for the CPUC's Disadvantaged Communities – Single-Family Solar Homes (DAC-SASH) Program, DACAG members may consider and vote to approve a proposed comment letter to recommend continued funding for the CPUC's DAC-SASH which provides financial incentives for the installation of rooftop solar generating systems that assist income-qualified customers in overcoming barriers to the installation of onsite solar energy. 8) Break. The DACAG will break for 5 minutes. 9) Meeting Minutes. Possible approval of March 20, 2026, DACAG meeting minutes. 10) Priority Areas. DACAG members to provide updates and announcements regarding DACAG priority areas and associated activities. Members may discuss and revise items related to the 2026 priority areas, including the identification of subject matter experts.
