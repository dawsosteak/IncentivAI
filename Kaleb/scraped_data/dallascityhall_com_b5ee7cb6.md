### Experience
Mary Elizabeth “Liz" Cedillo-Pereira serves as Assistant City Manager for the City of Dallas, where she leads departments focused on housing, homelessness, community services, environmental sustainability, arts and culture, and the Dallas Public Library. With more than 20 years of experience in public service, federal government, and the practice of law, she is dedicated to strengthening communities and expanding opportunity for Dallas residents.
Throughout her career, Liz has focused on building partnerships that help neighborhoods thrive—whether by expanding affordable housing, supporting families and small businesses, advancing environmental resilience, or investing in cultural and community spaces. She previously served as the City's first Chief of Equity and Inclusion and founded Dallas's Office of Welcoming Communities and Immigrant Affairs to help ensure all residents can fully participate in civic and economic life.
Before joining the City of Dallas, Liz served as a senior advisor at the U.S. Department of Homeland Security as a Presidential Appointee and founded a Dallas-based immigration law firm that provided legal representation and advocacy for families across North Texas. She holds a law degree from Southern Methodist University and a bachelor's degree from the University of Pennsylvania and is currently a candidate for the International City/County Management Association's Credentialed Manager designation.
Liz is proud to call Dallas home and remains deeply committed to public service, collaboration, and working with others to create a city where every resident has the opportunity to succeed. ​
