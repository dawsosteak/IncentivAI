##### WHAT CAN I DO?
Use the resources below to be more sustainable in your home or business, and to help your neighborhood. You can help us reach our goals!
  * [HOME GR/OWN:](http://city.milwaukee.gov/sustainability/HOMEGR/OWN.htm) Milwaukee initiative to transform neighborhoods by repurposing properties to help grow healthy food systems
  * [Milwaukee Food Council:](http://www.milwaukeefoodcouncil.org/) Collaboration to build sustainable regional food system 
  * [Milwaukee Urban Gardens:](http://www.milwaukeeurbangardens.org/) Nonprofit land trust dedicated to acquiring and preserving urban land and partnering with neighborhoods to cultivate healthy gardens and communities 
  * [Community Gardens:](http://www.mkedcd.org/realestate/NeighborhoodGardens.html) Milwaukee offers seasonal garden permits for residents who would like to garden on a nearby vacant lot
  * [Growing Power:](http://www.growingpower.org/) Nonprofit helping to provide equal access to healthy, high-quality affordable food for people in all communities
  * [Walnut Way Conservation Corps:](http://www.walnutway.org/) Nonprofit that works to sustain an economically diverse and neighborly community through civic engagement, environmental stewardship and economic enterprise 
  * [Alice's Garden:](http://www.alicesgardenmilwaukee.com/) Urban community garden in Milwaukee providing support for family connections to land and food
  * [Center for Resilient Cities:](http://www.resilientcities.org/) A nonprofit that practices sustainable community development 
  * [Victory Garden Initiative:](http://www.victorygardeninitiative.org/) Organization that provides support, education and resources for urban gardeners 
  * [Hunger Taskforce:](http://www.hungertaskforce.org/) Organization that works to prevent hunger by providing food to people in need today and by promoting social policies to achieve a hunger free community tomorrow 
  * [Feeding America Eastern Wisconsin:](http://www.feedingamericawi.org/) A food bank that serves 330,000 people in eastern Wisconsin 
  * [University of Wisconsin Extension:](http://www.uwex.edu/ces/cty/milwaukee/hort/urbanag/) Programs in southeast Wisconsin on Family Living, Horticulture & Urban Agriculture, Youth Development and Community, Natural Resources & Economic Development 
  * [Vincent High School:](http://www5.milwaukee.k12.wi.us/school/selections/vincent-high-school/) MPS High School has an active urban agriculture program 
  * [Milwaukee Public Theatre:](http://www.milwaukeepublictheatre.org/assets/FoodResource.pdf) MPT offers a "Food for Thought Resource Guide" with resources for starting your own garden, eating healthier foods, and getting involved with neighborhood initiatives
