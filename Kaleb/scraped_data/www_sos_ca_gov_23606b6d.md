# California Voter's Choice Act
The California Voter’s Choice Act (VCA) modernizes elections in California by allowing counties to conduct elections under a model which provides greater flexibility and convenience for voters. 
This election model allows voters to choose how, when, and where to cast their ballot by:
  * Mailing every voter a ballot
  * Expanding in-person early voting
  * Allowing voters to cast a ballot at any vote center within their county
  * Providing secure ballot drop off locations throughout the county

## Vote by mail:
**More Days** and **More Ways**
Return your ballot by mail as soon as you receive it. Vote center: • Vote in person anywhere in the county up to 10 days before the election • Register to vote and vote same-day • Drop off your ballot Use a drop box: Return your ballot to a secure drop off location in any county up to 28 days before the election. In California, every active registered voter will automatically receive a ballot in the mail before every election. Check your voter registration status to ensure you receive your ballot.
