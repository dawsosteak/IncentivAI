**Requirements to become a Residential Green Provider:**
  1. A third party provider must hold one or more of the following current certifications by:
     * ICC: Green Building certifications for Plan review and inspections; Residential
     * USGBC: LEED AP with specialty; Residential (Refer: [www.usgbc.org](http://www.usgbc.org/) for more information)
     * Green Built Texas Verifier; Residential  

  2. Minimum 2 years documented experience in green building in one of the following:
     * Design, Construction, Management, or Inspection
  3. Attend and complete a mandatory training program by the City of Dallas.
  4. Pass Dallas Green Building Code exam administered by the City of Dallas (at the end of training).


**Requirements to become a Commercial Green Provider:**
  1. A third party provider must hold one or more of the following current certifications by:
     * ICC: Green Building certification: Commercial Green Construction Professional - GCP; (Refer:[www.iccsafe.org](http://www.iccsafe.org/) for more information)  

     * USGBC: LEED AP with specialty; Commercial (Refer: [www.usgbc.org](http://www.usgbc.org/) for more information)
  2. Minimum 2 years documented experience in green building in one of the following:
     * Design, Construction, Management, or Inspection
  3. Attend and complete a mandatory training program by City of Dallas.
  4. Pass Dallas Green Building Code exam administered by the City of Dallas (at the end of training). Listed below is the schedule for the classes. Should you have any questions, please contact Meenal Chauk via email at meenal.chauk@dallas.gov, or via phone (214) 948-4091.

100 Dallas Green Ordinance FAQ I work for a design firm/contractor/ developer/owner. Can I perform the 3rd party review for my company?  No, this is considered a conflict of interest under the Code and the interpretation of the Code.  The 3rd party provider must be independent of the design firm, contractor, developer, owner or other and has no financial, business, or personal interest in the project  It does not matter if the provider has no design or construction responsibility or is not involved with the project in its employment; they are employed by an entity that does

101 Dallas Green Ordinance FAQ I am an independent consultant and have been contracted to provide engineering or other similar services for a project. Can I perform the 3rd party review?  No, this is a conflict of interest. As a consultant, you now have direct responsibilities to the project and are no longer 3rd party to the project
