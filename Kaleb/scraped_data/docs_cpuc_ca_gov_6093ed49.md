This Email Ruling grants the motions for party status filed by the Center for Energy Efficiency and Renewable Technologies (CEERT), Enphase Energy, Inc. (Enphase), and Nest Labs Inc. (Nest) in Rulemaking (R.) 14-10-003, the rulemaking to create a framework for integrated demand-side resource programs.
* * *
R.14-10-003 KHY/lil
* * *
- 4 On May 29, 2015, an Email Ruling in R.14-10-003 was issued denying, without prejudice, motions by Enphase and Nest for party status. The Email Ruling denied party status because the motions were noncompliant with Commission Rules of Practice and Procedure, Rule 1.4(b), which states that a person seeking party status by motion shall fully disclose the persons or entities in whose behalf the filing is made, the interest of such entities, and the factual and legal contentions that the entity intends to make.
* * *
On June 1, 2015, Nest filed a new motion for party status in R.14-10-003. In its motion, Nest states that it provides customers with hardware, software and services that help reduce energy consumption, increase energy efficiency, and manage residential energy demand. Nest asserts that through this proceeding, the Commission will be able to encourage customer adoption of integrated demand side management by simultaneously reducing consumption, increasing efficiency and managing demand. Furthermore, in compliance with Rule 1.4(b), Nest contends that by providing the Commission information on Nest’s activities in energy efficiency, demand response, smart grid and innovative rate design, it can contribute meaningfully to this proceeding.
* * *
On June 1, 2015, CEERT filed a motion for party status in R.14-10-003. CEERT is a nonprofit public-benefit partnership of private sector clean energy company promoting global warming solutions and reliance on clean, renewable energy sources for California. CEERT asserts it has actively contributed to this proceeding by attending workshops but inadvertently did not request party status. CEERT contends that the commission’s approach to the integration of demand-side resources must play a significant role in reducing greenhouse gas emissions. CEERT advocates that advanced technologies in coordination with increased customer participation and reliability will lead to success in this proceeding.
* * *
On June 3, 2015, Enphase filed a new motion for party status in R.14-10-003. In its motion, Enphase states that it is a leader in the management, control and reliability of distributed energy resources, including demand-side resources. Enphase claims that it brings a systems-based approach to distributed energy, providing a platform for intelligent energy management. Enphase contends that it can meaningfully contribute to this proceeding by offering a unique and valuable perspective. Enphase explains that it can provide technical information not available from other sources including information regarding
* * *

- 5 the distribution system and demand response and its interaction with other distributed energy systems.
* * *
The June 1, 2015 motions for party status filed in R.14-10-003 by CEERT and Nest, and the June 3, 2015 motion for party status filed in R. 14-10-003 by Enphase are in compliance with Commission Rules of Practice and Procedure, Rule 1.4(b), which requires that a person seeking party status by motion shall fully disclose the persons or entities in whose behalf the filing is made, the interest of such entities, and the factual and legal contentions that the entity intends to make. CEERT, Enphase, and Nest have all disclosed their interests and explained what discussions they will bring to this proceeding. It is reasonable to grant party status to CEERT, Enphase, and Nest. Therefore, CEERT, Enphase, and Nest are hereby granted party status in R.14-10-003.
