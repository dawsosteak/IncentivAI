# ​Vaccines for Children (VFC) Program
Information for VFC providers.
​​​​​​​​​​The Vaccines for Children (VFC) Program provides vaccines to children who do not have health insurance. Children who are insured but whose insurance does not cover immunizations are also eligible to receive federally funded vaccines at public sites, including Federally Qualified Health Centers and Rural Health Clinics. For more information regarding PA’s VFC Program, contact 1-888-646-6864.
  * [Program Overview](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/health/programs/immunizations/vfc-overview)
  * [Provider Reenrollment](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/health/programs/immunizations/vaccines-for-children#reenrollment)
  * [Provider Enrollment](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/health/programs/immunizations/vaccines-for-children#enroll)  

  * [Provider Policies](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/health/programs/immunizations/vaccines-for-children#policy)  

  * [Provider Training](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/health/programs/immunizations/vaccines-for-children#training)  

  * [Resources and Forms](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/health/programs/immunizations/vfc-resources)  

  * [Vaccine Manufacturer Information](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/health/programs/immunizations/vaccines-for-children#manufacture)  



The Pennsylvania Immunization Program 2026 VFC Reenrollment period opens on Wednesday, April 1, 2026, and closes on Sunday, May 31, 2026. Only providers who enrolled in VFC before January 1, 2026, must complete the 2026 VFC Reenrollment in PIERS. Providers who do not complete Reenrollment will not be able to order vaccines beginning Monday, June 1, 2026.
To reenroll, providers must complete the 2026 VFC Reenrollment template in the [Pennsylvania Immunization Electronic Registry System (PIERS)(opens in a new tab)](https://vaccines.health.pa.gov/webiz_prod/login.aspx) located in the Clinic Tools section, under Enrollments. Detailed instructions for completing the reenrollment template can be found in the [2026 VFC Reenrollment Guide(opens in a new tab)](https://www.pa.gov/content/dam/copapwp-pagov/en/health/documents/topics/documents/programs/immunizations/2026%20VFC%20Reenrollment%20Guide.pdf).
2026 VFC Reenrollment guidance can also be found in the following resources:
  * 2026 VFC Reenrollment Webinar recording will be available on [TRAIN PA(opens in a new tab)](https://www.train.org/pa/welcome).
  * 2026 VFC Reenrollment Webinar PowerPoint [presentation(opens in a new tab)](https://www.pa.gov/content/dam/copapwp-pagov/en/health/documents/topics/documents/programs/immunizations/2026%20VFC%20Reenrollment%20Provider%20Webinar.pptx).
  * 2025 VFC Reenrollment Guide is an optional, self-paced, online e-Learning course available on [TRAIN PA(opens in a new tab)](https://www.train.org/pa/welcome).
    * Although this course references 2025 VFC Reenrollment, all the information applies to 2026 VFC Reenrollment.


To complete reenrollment, providers should be prepared with the following:
  1. Medical Director, primary and back-up coordinator, should read the updated Pennsylvania Immunization Program Provider Policies.
  2. Primary and back-up coordinators both complete the 2026 You Call the Shots Modules 10 and 16 training modules or the Storage and Handling Refresher Test and the VFC Refresher Test and save training certificates or statements of completion as  _PDFs_ (four in total) to upload to PIERS. The medical director only needs to complete the annual education if that person is also the primary or back-up coordinator.
  3. Current certificates of calibration for all DDLs, including at least one back up DDL saved as  _PDFs_ to upload to PIERS.
  4. Current patient population (Age groups: under 1 year old, 1-4, 5-18, and 19+; Insurance Statuses: Medicaid, uninsured, underinsured, American Indian or Native Alaskan, and private or commercial insurance, and therefore not VFC-eligible).


To enroll in the Pennsylvania VFC program, providers* must first review the [Provider Policies(opens in a new tab)](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/health/programs/immunizations/vaccines-for-children#policy) and then complete the Pennsylvania VFC Provider Application.
The application has three required sections:
  1. [Policy Acknowledgment(opens in a new tab)](https://arcg.is/04XWyP0)
  2. [Provider Agreement(opens in a new tab)](https://arcg.is/CnebD)
  3. [Provider Profile(opens in a new tab)](https://arcg.is/4Hj181)


Providers must submit each section in order.
  * After submitting the Policy Acknowledgment, providers will be automatically directed to the Provider Agreement.
  * After submitting the Provider Agreement, providers will be automatically directed to the Provider Profile.


All three sections must be fully completed and submitted before the enrollment application can be processed.
Due to limited funding, 317 adult vaccine funding will not automatically be approved for newly enrolled or reactivated eligible public providers. In rare cases, an exception can be considered where extreme need can be demonstrated by the eligible provider.

Bureau of Immunizations – August 2025 Pennsylvania Immunization Program Section 317 Vaccine Policy
* * *
Vaccine funded under Section 317 of Title 42 of the Public Services Code (42 U.S.C.A. § 247b) (Section 317) is funded as part of the Vaccines for Children (VFC) Program, and can be used by Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHC), Rural Health Clinics (RHC), State Health Centers (SHC), County Municipal Health Departments (CMHD), and other sites authorized by the Pennsylvania Immunization Program (Program) to vaccinate Section 317 eligible populations according to the guidelines below. All eligibility must be documented in the patient’s record and saved for three years in accordance with Program requirements. Adults ages 19 and older are eligible for Section 317 vaccines if they meet at least one of the following criteria: • Are uninsured, i.e., have no health insurance. • Are underinsured. This includes: o someone who has private health insurance but whose coverage does not include ACIP recommended vaccines; o someone whose private insurance covers only selected ACIP-recommended vaccines (317 eligible for non-covered vaccines only); o someone whose private insurance does not provide first dollar coverage (which includes copays, coinsurance, or deductibles) for the cost of ACIP- recommended vaccines; or o someone whose private insurance covers ACIP-recommended vaccines but has a fixed dollar limit (or cap) for payment. The individual is considered underinsured once the policy reaches the fixed dollar amount.
* * *
Other situations in which a person might qualify for Section 317 funded vaccines: • Individuals in correctional facilities and jails (congregate settings). • Vaccines for Hematopoietic Stem Cell Transplant Recipients: Any vaccines required for transplant recipients may be Section 317 funded, as indicated in Chapter 2 of Epidemiology and Prevention of Vaccine-Preventable Diseases. • Fully insured individuals seeking vaccines during public health response activities including: o Outbreak response o Post-exposure prophylaxis o Disaster relief efforts o Mass vaccination campaigns or exercises for public health preparedness These situations must be approved by the Program. Vaccines for Leisure Travel: Leisure travelers, including religious missionaries or civic volunteers, are not eligible for any section 317 funded vaccines. Charges and Fees for Immunization of section 317 Eligible Patients • The reimbursement rate set by the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) of $23.14 per dose of vaccine may be charged for the administration of vaccine to Pennsylvania Section 317 eligible adults.
* * *
Bureau of Immunizations – August 2025 • Providers may not exceed the CMS reimbursement rate when determining administration fees for Section 317 eligible populations. • Providers who bill for the vaccine administration fee of a non-Medicaid 317 eligible adult after the date of service may issue only a single bill to the patient within 90 days of vaccine administration. • The reimbursement rates set by contracted medical health plans may be charged for the administration of vaccine to adults with private health insurance coverage for immunizations (per dose of vaccine). • Providers may not deny administration of Section 317-supplied vaccine to an established patient because of the patient’s inability to pay the administration fee. In addition, unclaimed vaccine administration fees cannot be sent to collections. Therefore, providers must establish a process to waive unpaid vaccine administration fees. • Providers may not charge the patient or insurance for the cost of any Section 317-supplied vaccines.

**Pennsylvania Immunization Program**
Bureau of Immunizations – August 2025 Eligibility and Billing
* * *
Before administering vaccine supplied by the Pennsylvania Immunization Program (Program) to qualifying children, the provider must complete the necessary screening to determine whether the patient is eligible for Vaccines for Children (VFC) vaccine and document either ineligibility or eligibility and the reason for eligibility. Eligibility shall be documented in the patient record for each immunization visit. For VFC eligible infants, the entire birth stay is considered one immunization visit. Therefore, eligibility only needs to be determined and documented once during the birth stay. Information must be maintained and able to be retrieved for a minimum of three years. Children (birth through 18 years) are eligible for VFC vaccines if they meet at least one of the following criteria: • Are enrolled in Medicaid, i.e., children who have health insurance covered by a state Medicaid program. Please note this does NOT include CHIP. • Are uninsured, i.e., have no health insurance. • Are American Indian or Alaska Native. • Are underinsured. This includes: o a child who has private health insurance but whose coverage does not include ACIP recommended vaccines; o a child whose private insurance covers only selected ACIP-recommended vaccines (VFC eligible for non-covered vaccines only); o a child whose private insurance does not provide first dollar coverage (which includes copays, coinsurance, or deductibles) for the cost of ACIP- recommended vaccines; or o a child whose private insurance covers ACIP-recommended vaccines but has a fixed dollar limit (or cap) for payment. The child is considered underinsured once the family’s policy reaches the fixed dollar amount. o Underinsured children are eligible to receive VFC vaccines only though Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHC), Rural Health Clinics (RHC), State Health Centers (SHC), or County Municipal Health Departments (CMHD). Determining Eligibility of Newborns for VFC vaccines: • Under Pennsylvania Act 81 of 1975, any health insurance plan written in Pennsylvania must cover the newborn child of a covered parent for up to 31 days after the date of birth. Therefore, the parent’s insurance status applies to the newborn for up to 31 days after the date of birth. • If a newborn child’s covered parent is underinsured, the newborn is considered uninsured by the VFC program. Children with health insurance that covers all ACIP-recommended vaccines, and who fail to meet one of the previously mentioned criteria are not eligible through the VFC program. There are no income restrictions imposed by the VFC program, as long as the child meets all other enrollment criteria.
* * *
Bureau of Immunizations – August 2025 For children with private health insurance as primary and Medicaid as secondary, there are two options for providers.
* * *
Option 1: Administer VFC vaccines and bill Medicaid for the administration fee. In most situations, Medicaid is the “payer of last resort,” so claims must be filed with and rejected by all other insurers before Medicaid will consider payment for services. This is not true of the vaccine administration fee for Medicaid-eligible VFC children. Medicaid must pay the VFC provider the administration fee because vaccinations are a component of the Medicaid Early Periodic Screening, Diagnosis, and Treatment (EPSDT) program. However, once a claim is submitted to Medicaid, the state Medicaid agency has the option to seek reimbursement for the administration fee from the primary insurer. If the state Medicaid agency rejects a claim for the vaccine administration fee and states the claim must first be submitted to the primary insurer for payment, notify the Program.
* * *
Option 2: The provider can administer private stock vaccines and bill the primary insurance carrier for both the cost of the vaccine and the administration fee. If the primary insurer reimburses less than Medicaid for the vaccine administration fee, the provider can bill Medicaid for the balance, up to the amount Medicaid pays for the administration fee. Charges and Fees for Immunization of VFC Eligible Patients • The reimbursement rate set by the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) of $23.14 per dose of vaccine may be charged for the administration of vaccine to non-Medicaid VFC eligible children. • Providers may not exceed the CMS reimbursement rate when determining administration fees for VFC eligible populations. • Non-Medicaid VFC eligible includes Native American, Alaska Native, children with no insurance, or children who are underinsured. • Providers who bill for the vaccine administration fee of a non-Medicaid VFC eligible child after the date of service may issue only a single bill to the patient within 90 days of vaccine administration. • The reimbursement rates set by Pennsylvania Medicaid may be charged for the administration of vaccine to children enrolled in Medicaid (per dose of vaccine). • The reimbursement rates set by contracted medical health plans may be charged for the administration of vaccine to children with private health insurance coverage for immunizations (per dose of vaccine). • Providers may not deny administration of vaccine supplied by the Program to an established patient because of the child’s parent’s or guardian’s inability to pay the administration fee. In addition, unclaimed vaccine administration fees cannot be sent to collections. Therefore, providers must establish a process to waive unpaid vaccine administration fees. • Providers may not charge the patient or insurance for the cost of any BOI Program-supplied vaccines.
* * *
