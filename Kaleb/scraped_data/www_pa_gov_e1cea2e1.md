# Influenza
## **Influenza Virus Submission Procedures for Physicians and Laboratories Not Enrolled in the Influenza Sentinel Program**
The Bureau of Laboratories (BOL) will provide collections kits and prepaid shipping containers upon request from a physician or laboratory. Physicians and laboratories may use their own collection kits and mailers if they do not have BOL supplies on hand. Collect a nasal, nasopharyngeal, or throat specimen using a standard viral culturette or a sterile swab placed in viral transport medium. Fill out a Specimen Submission Form completely. Age, county of residence, sex and date of collection are required. Put the patient name/ID on the specimen. Please ensure that the identification information on the specimen tube is identical to the information filled on the form. . Wrap the specimen with absorbent material, e.g., paper towels, and place it into the main compartment of the biohazard bag (one per bag) then seal the zip-lock. Place the [Specimen Submission Form(opens in a new tab)](https://www.pa.gov/content/dam/copapwp-pagov/en/health/documents/topics/documents/laboratories/BOL%20Micro%20Specimen%20Submission%20Form.pdf) into the outer slot of the bag.
  * [Viral Testing Respiratory Swab Collection and Shipping Instructions(opens in a new tab)](https://www.pa.gov/content/dam/copapwp-pagov/en/health/documents/topics/documents/laboratories/Viral%20Testing%20Respiratory%20Swab%20Collection%20and%20Shipping%20Instructions.pdf)
  * [Highly Pathogenic Avian Influenza A(H5) Collection and Shipping Instructions(opens in a new tab)](https://www.pa.gov/content/dam/copapwp-pagov/en/health/documents/topics/documents/laboratories/Highly%20Pathogenic%20Avian%20Influenza%20A\(H5\)%20Collection%20and%20Shipping%20Instructions.pdf)


**CAUTION: Temperature is checked upon receipt. From the time of collection until receipt, specimens must be maintained at ≤8 °C for up to 72 hours or ≤0 °C if held for more than 72 hours. Failure to meet these conditions will result in immediate rejection.**
**Ship specimens to:**  
Pennsylvania Department of Health  
Bureau of Laboratories  
110 Pickering Way  
Exton, PA 19341-1310  
**Please print your return address on the label.**

PURPOSE
* * *
To describe the procedure for collecting, storing, and transporting respiratory specimens for Highly Pathogenic Avian Influenza A (H5) virus testing.
* * *
SPECIMEN COLLECTION SCENARIOS
* * *
Scenario 1 – Patients Without Conjunctival Symptoms
* * *
Collect a total of 3 swabs from 2 specimen types
* * *
1. Nasopharyngeal (NP) swab • Collect one NP swab and place it in a separate viral transport medium (VTM) / universal transport media (UTM) tube.
* * *
Nasal and Oropharyngeal (Throat) swabs • Collect one nasal swab and one oropharyngeal (throat) swab. • Place both swabs together into a single VTM/UTM tube.
* * *
Total: 3 swabs → 2 VTM/UTM tubes.
* * *
Scenario 2 – Patients With Conjunctivitis (with or without respiratory symptoms)
* * *
Collect a total of 3 swabs from 3 specimen types 1. Conjunctival swab(s) – swab each eye using a separate new swab and place both swabs together in one VTM/UTM vial.
* * *
2. Nasopharyngeal (NP) swab • Collect one NP swab and place it in a separate VTM/UTM tube.
* * *
3. Combined Nasal and Oropharyngeal (Throat) swabs • Collect one nasal swab and one oropharyngeal swab. • Place both swabs together into a third VTM tube.
* * *
Total: 4 swabs (or 5 if both eyes) → 3 VTM/UTM tubes.
* * *
Scenario 3 – If Above Specimens Cannot be Collected
* * *
If the above specimens cannot be collected, a single nasal swab or a single oropharyngeal swab is acceptable.
* * *
Total: 1 swab → 1 VTM/UTM tube.

**INTRODUCTION**
The Pennsylvania Department of Health's Bureau of Laboratories (BOL) provides testing for
* * *
influenza and other respiratory viruses using surveillance specimens from patients showing symptoms of influenza -like illness (ILI). This testing is part of the WHO -Collaborating Laboratory Influenza Surveillance Program. Results from these tests, along with further analysis conducted by the CDC on a subset of specimens, help track the types and subtypes of influenza in circulation, monitor antiviral resistance patterns, guide the selection of vaccine strains for the upcoming influenza season, and identify novel or mutated strains of influenza, including those originating from animals.
* * *

**SPECIMEN COLLECTION INSTRUCTIONS**
A. Nasopharyngeal (NP) Swab (preferred and required for the full respiratory panel) B. Oropharyngeal (OP) or Throat Swab C. Anterior Nasal/Nasal

A. Respiratory swabs are collected in 15 mL screw-capped tube with 3 mL of transport medium VTM/UTM. B. (See diagram below) • Resistance will be met, confirming contact with the nasopharynx. • Rub the swab tip several times across the mucosal surface. This will loosen and collect cellular material. Allow a time of contact of up to 30 seconds. • Withdraw the swab and place it in the viral transport media. Make sure the liquid medium covers the swab tip. Break or cut the end of the swab and screw the vial lid on tightly. 2. Oropharyngeal (OP) or Throat Swab • Using plastic-handled swab, vigorously swab both the tonsils and the posterior pharynx. (See diagram below) • Place the swab into the transport media. Make sure the liquid medium covers the swab tip. • Break or cut the end of the swab. Screw the vial lid on tightly. 3. Anterior Nasal/Nasal • Insert the entire soft end of the swab into your nostril no more than ¾ of an inch (1.5 cm). (See diagram below) • Slowly rotate the swab, gently pressing against the inside of your nostril at least 4 times for a total of 15 seconds. Get as much nasal discharge as possible on the soft end of the swab. • Using the same swab, repeat swabbing in your other nostril with the same end of the swab.
