_The lifecycle of an Advice Letter (AL) varies. At the time of their initial filing, all ALs require a 20-day public comment/protest period. During this time, no action can be taken to close/dispose of the AL. If no protests are received, an AL may be considered a Tier 1 or Tier 2 AL and it may be closed within an additional 10-20-day period. For those ALs that are complex or that receive protests or comments, a Resolution may be required for disposition. ALs that require a Resolution are considered Tier 3 ALs, and take more time and resources to process. Resolutions are placed on a Commission Agenda and must be reviewed and approved by the CPUC Commissioners. Resolutions require between 21 and 30 days for drafting, management review, and approval. Once approved, most draft Resolutions require a 20-day public review and comment period. All comments are due to the Commission at least 10 days prior to the date of the Commission Meeting at which the resolution will be considered. It is under this sort of scenario that an AL may stay open for 90 days or more before it has reached a final disposition._  
  
CPUC does not make copies of advice letters available for viewing and downloading by the public at this time. To obtain a copy of an advice letter or related document, follow the directions below for the appropriate industry:  
  

  * **Communications advice letters:** parties on the advice letter service list are served copies of the advice letters when filed. Parties not on the service list can request for a copy of an advice letter by completing a [_Document Order Form_](http://www.cpuc.ca.gov/orderadocument/). 
  

  * **Water advice letters:** Water and sewer utilities must provide advice letters submitted to the Commission to the parties listed on their service list. Parties not on the service list should first contact the utility to receive a copy. Utilities are to provide one copy of an advice letter without charge per General Order 96-B, Water Industry Rule 4. Class A utilities are required to provide a link to their open advice letters on their website. If at that point the parties still seek a copy of an open advice letter, they may contact the Water Division via email at _Water.Division@cpuc.ca.gov_. Copies of closed advice letters can be obtained by submitting a Public Records Act Form to the Commission that is located at [_https://publicrecords.cpuc.ca.gov_.](https://publicrecords.cpuc.ca.gov/)
  

  * **Energy advice letters:** The Energy Division provides links to the PG&E, SDG&E, SCE, and SoCal Gas utility advice letter websites [_here_](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/electrical-energy/energy-utility-advice-letter-and-tariff-information).   
Interested parties in need of any other advice letters may contact the Energy Division, Tariff Unit at _edtariffunit@cpuc.ca.gov_.
