As described below, this Email Ruling denies without prejudice the motions for party status filed in Rulemaking (R.) 14-10-003 by Advanced Energy Economy (AEE), California Solar Energy Industries Association (CALSEIA), 350 Bay Area, and Shell Energy North America, L.P. (Shell). As described below, the motions are not compliant with Commission Rules of Practice and Procedure, Rule 1.4(b).
* * *
On April 26, 2016, AEE filed a motion requesting party status in R.14-10 003. AEE states that it is a national association of businesses dedicated to transforming public policy to enable a world that runs on clean energy. Explaining that its participation in this proceeding is critical as the business voice for the advanced energy industry, AEE adds that issues in this proceeding impact its members.
* * *
On April 29, 2016, CALSEIA filed a motion for party status in R.14-10-003, stating that it is a not for profit solar industry trade association. CALSEIA explains that it is interested in participating in this proceeding because its members will be direct participants in the solicitations and tariffs that are to be developed in this proceeding.
* * *
On May 2, 2016, 350 Bay Area filed a motion for party status in R.14-10-003. 350 Bay Area states that it is a nonprofit organization working for reductions in
* * *
R.14-10-003 KHY/ek4
* * *
- 4 carbon emissions in the Bay Area and requests to be a party in order to support expansion of clean distributed generation and demand-side resources and job and economic growth.
* * *
On May 5, 2016, Shell filed a motion for party status in R.14-10-003, describing itself as an energy service provider providing retail energy service through the direct access program and a potential provider of behind the meter distributed energy resources. Shell emphasizes that electric and gas utilities’ entry into the market for development, ownership and deployment of distributed energy resources may have an impact on competition in the distributed energy resources services market and may impact future efforts of Shell.
* * *
Commission Rules of Practice and Procedure, Rule 1.4(b) requires that a person seeking party status by motion fully disclose the persons or entities in whose behalf the filing is made, the interest of such entities, and the factual and legal contentions that the entity intends to make. In their motions, AEE, CALSEIA,
* * *
## _350 Bay Area, and Shell describe who they are and what their interests_
are. However, none of the motions specify any factual or legal contentions; thus,
