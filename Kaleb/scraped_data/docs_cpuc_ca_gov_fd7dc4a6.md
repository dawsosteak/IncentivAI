Decision 21-02-033 February 11, 2021
* * *
BEFORE THE PUBLIC UTILITIES COMMISSION OF THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA
* * *
Order Instituting Rulemaking to Create a Consistent Regulatory Framework for the Guidance, Planning, and Evaluation of Integrated Distributed Energy Resources.
* * *
Rulemaking 14-10-003
* * *
ORDER EXTENDING STATUTORY DEADLINE
* * *
Summary This decision extends the statutory deadline in Rulemaking 14-10-003 to August 15, 2021. 1. Background Public Utilities Code (Pub. Util. Code) Section 1701.5(a) provides that the Commission shall resolve the issues raised in the scoping memo of a ratesetting proceeding, within 18 months of the date the proceeding is initiated, unless the Commission makes a written determination that the deadline cannot be met, and issues an order extending the deadline. On August 15, 2020, the Commission adopted Decision 20-08-012, which extended the statutory deadline by six months to resolve the remaining issues in Rulemaking (R.) 14-10-003. The decision explained that the extension was necessary to finalize the work being done regarding distributed energy resources alternative sourcing mechanisms. The Commission spent several months finalizing a staff proposal for two frameworks for distributed energy resources distribution deferral (Staff Proposal). On October 6, 2020, the Administrative Law Judge issued a
* * *
R.14-10-003 ALJ/KHY/sgu2 ruling introducing the Staff Proposal and directing comments and responses to questions. Parties filed responses and comments on October 30, 2020 and replies on November 10, 2020. The Administrative Law Judge issued a proposed decision on January 5, 2021 adopting pilots to test two frameworks for procuring distributed energy resources that avoid or defer utility capital investments. 2. An Extension of the Statutory Deadline is Necessary As further described below, an extension of the statutory deadline until August 15, 2021, is necessary and appropriate to allow the Commission time to consider the proposed decision and, if adopted, implement the proposed pilots. The proposed decision is currently scheduled to be considered by the Commission at the February 11, 2021 meeting. The extension will allow for any delays in the deliberative process. More importantly, an extension of the proceeding deadline will provide the Commission flexibility in implementing the proposed pilots, if adopted. The proposed decision also directs implementation of related workshops and advice letters. Extending the statutory deadline for six months will provide an open proceeding in which to oversee the activity of these related ordering paragraphs and ensure compliance. 3. Waiver of Comment Period Under Rule 14.6(c)(4) of the Commission’s Rules of Practice and Procedure, the Commission may reduce or waive the period for public review and comment of proposed decisions extending the deadline for resolving ratesetting proceedings. Accordingly, the otherwise applicable period for public review and comment is being waived. 4. Assignment of Proceeding Marybel Batjer is the assigned Commissioner and Kelly A. Hymes is the assigned Administrative Law Judge in these proceedings.
* * *
R.14-10-003 ALJ/KHY/sgu3 Findings of Fact 1. D.20-08-012 extended the statutory deadline to February 15, 2021, in order to resolve all issues in R.14-10-003. 2. The Commission spent several months finalizing a staff proposal for two frameworks for distributed energy resources distribution deferral. 3. On October 6, 2020, the Administrative Law Judge issued a ruling introducing the Staff Proposal and directing comments and responses to questions. 4. The Administrative Law Judge issued a proposed decision on January 5, 2021 adopting pilots to test two frameworks for procuring distributed energy resources that avoid or defer utility capital investments. 5. The proposed decision on the proposed pilots is currently scheduled to be considered by the Commission at the February 11, 2021 meeting. 6. An extension of the proceeding deadline will allow for any delays in the deliberative process. 7. An extension of the proceeding deadline will provide the Commission flexibility in implementing the proposed pilots, if adopted. 8. Extending the statutory deadline for six months will provide an open proceeding in which to oversee the implementation of related ordering paragraphs in the proposed decision and ensure compliance. 9. It is necessary and appropriate to extend the statutory deadline of this proceeding until August 15, 2021 to consider the proposed decision and, if adopted, implement the proposed pilots and related ordering paragraphs. Conclusion of Law Pursuant to the authority granted to the Commission under Pub. Util. Code § 1701.5(a), the statutory deadline should be extended to August 15, 2021.
* * *
R.14-10-003 ALJ/KHY/sgu4 IT IS ORDERED that the statutory deadline in this proceeding is extended until August 15, 2021. This order is effective today. Dated February 11, 2021, at San Francisco, California. MARYBEL BATJER President MARTHA GUZMAN ACEVES CLIFFORD RECHTSCHAFFEN GENEVIEVE SHIROMA Commissioners
