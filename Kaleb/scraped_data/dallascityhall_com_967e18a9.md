Minimum Requirements for Promotion
* * *
CODE OF RULES AND REGULATIONS OF THE CIVIL SERVICE BOARD –
* * *
Fire Captain: Fire-Rescue Department employees who have completed two years of service as a Fire Lieutenant, and are not on probation for any reason, shall be eligible to test for promotion to Fire Captain. Therefore, those who meet the eligibility requirement by January 20, 2026, may compete on this examination (Civil Service Rules – Rule 4, Section 4.4(a) (11)).
* * *
Application to take the Written Examination
* * *
All eligible candidates wishing to take this examination must submit a promotional application. The link to the application can be found on the City of Dallas Internal Positions Employment Opportunities webpage at: http://dallascityhall.com/departments/humanresources/employment/Pages/Internal-Positions.aspx
* * *
Application Window: • Application open: Wednesday, October 22, 2025 • Application closes: Friday, November 21, 2025, at 11:59 p.m.
* * *
Candidates who fail to submit an application by the deadline will not be permitted to take this examination. Upon successful registration through the NEOGOV system, you will receive an automated email confirmation at the address listed on your application. Additionally, on December 1, 2025, you will receive an email notification from the Civil Service Department regarding your eligibility status.
* * *

Candidate Check-in Process
* * *
• Check-in Time: Begins at 9:30 a.m. • Required Identification: A City of Dallas or government-issued photo ID is mandatory for entry into the exam room. Failure to present the required ID before entering the room will result in denial of admission to the examination. • Prohibited Items: o Electronic or communication devices (e.g., body cameras, cell phones, pagers, laptops, tablets, smartwatches, Bluetooth devices). o Personal belongings, including study materials, large purses, backpacks, water bottles, and earplugs. o Personal writing utensils (all necessary materials will be provided). If you bring a communication device, you will be asked to return it to your car, and you will still be subject to the check-in time. Once the testing process begins, possession of any communication device or other prohibited item will result in your disqualification. There are no exceptions to this rule.
* * *
Once the exam room doors are closed, NO CANDIDATES will be allowed to enter the exam room. Fire Captain Promotional Process – Part I
* * *
This promotional process comprises two parts: 1. Part I: Multiple-Choice Knowledge Exam o Focuses on knowledge areas outlined in Appendix (attached source list). o A Candidate Orientation Guide on the Multiple-Choice Test, including an accompanying video will be distributed by December 8, 2025. o Candidate questions regarding material in the orientation manual or video must be submitted in writing to civilserviceinfo@dallas.gov by 4:00 p.m. on December 18, 2025. o Candidates must pass Part I to proceed to Part II. 2. Scoring Weights o Part I (Written Exam) contributes 37% to a candidate’s overall promotional score. o Part II (Assessment Center) contributes 63% to a candidate’s overall promotional score. o These percentages are based on a 2022 job analysis for the position.
* * *
Fire Captain Promotional Process – Part II
* * *
• Assessment Center Orientation: o A Candidate Orientation Guide on Performance-Based Assessment with video link will be provided on February 23, 2026.All candidate inquiries must be submitted in writing to civilserviceinfo@dallas.gov by 4:00 p.m. on March 6, 2026. • Assessment Center Date: o Occurs the week of March 30, 2026. o Specific details, including exact dates and times, will be provided to qualified candidates once available. • Review Policy: o In accordance with Civil Service Board policies, assessment center test exercises are not subject to review.
