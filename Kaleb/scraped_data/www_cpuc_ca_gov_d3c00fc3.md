Our Mission Our mission is to empower California through access to safe, clean, and affordable utility services and infrastructure. Excellence Striving to achieve exceptional performance by consistently delivering quality work in a timely manner. Integrity Behaving in an open, honest, T rustworthy manner, both professionally and personally. Open Communication Exchanging information, thoughts and concerns in an open, timely manner. ## CPUC Job Classifications
The CPUC employs a wide range of job classifications across its divisions. You will see openings for more general classifications such as Staff Services Analysts and Associate Governmental Program Analysts as well as many others. In addition to these there are some main classifications that make up the core of the CPUC and the majority of its workforce. Public Utilities Regulatory Analyst (PURA I-V) The “PURA” series ranges from entry level to the highest level of technical advisor to the Commission. A PURA can conduct a broad range of research and analyses which varies in independence and complexity. Minimum qualifications for the PURA can be found here: PURA Classification Specifications. Utilities Engineer (UE) The “UE” is the Commission’s main engineering classification. The UE, under supervision of a Senior UE, can perform a wide variety of field and office engineering assignments which, depending on hired area, could be in connection with gas, electric, telecommunications, water, steam, sewer, pipeline, railroads, and or rail transit systems. Minimum Qualifications for the Utilities Engineer can be found here: UE Classification Specifications. Other CPUC job classifications include the Senior Utilities Engineer, Telecommunications Engineer Series, Administrative Law Judge PUC, and Consumer Affairs Representative PUC

## CPUC Career Opportunities
Consumer Protection and Enforcement Division (CPED) The Consumer Protection and Enforcement Division serves as the first line of defense for California utility customers. CPED collects and resolves consumer complaints, establishes and enforces rules and regulations for transportation carriers, and investigates allegations of utility waste, fraud, and abuse. CPED is comprised of three separate branches: the T ransportation Enforcement Branch, the T ransportation Licensing and Analysis Branch, and the Utilities Enforcement Branch. Energy Division The approximately 270 member staff of the CPUC’s Energy Division administer the policies that guide California’s nation leading efforts in energy efficiency, customer-side energy generation, renewable energy, and the fight against climate change. The Energy Division also provides technical support to the Commissioners and their offices, and the Administrative Law Judges. Executive Office The Executive Office assures implementation of the CPUC’s decisions and policies. The Executive Office works closely with the Commissioners for Administrative Law Judge Division (ALJ) The Administrative Law Judge Division includes over 70 Administrative Law Judges and 65 staff. The ALJ Division processes formal filings, facilitates alternative dispute resolutions, conducts hearings, develops an adequate administrative record, prepares timely proposals for Commission consideration, and prepares and coordinates Commission meeting agendas. Administrative Services Division The Administrative Services Division is responsible for CPUC finance, facilities, contracts, and other administrative functions. Administrative Services is also responsible for helping the CPUC achieve its environmental sustainability goals. Communications Division The Communications Division assists the CPUC in developing and implementing policies to promote competition in telecommunications markets and addressing regulatory changes required by state and federal legislation. External Affairs Division The External Affairs Division assists customers with utility complaints and provides information and assistance to the media, local governments and community organizations, the public, and other stakeholders about the CPUC’s many pioneering and innovative programs, policies, and proceedings. The External Affairs Division encompasses four offices: Strategic Communications; Public Advisor’s Office; Business and Community Outreach (BCO); and Consumer Affairs Branch (CAB). Human Resources Division The Human Resources Division covers hiring, talent management, HR, health & safety, labor, disability/leave and transactions. You will find representatives from the CPUC’s HR T eam at various university and community career fairs and events. Information Technology Services Division (ITSD) The Information T echnology Services Division enables the CPUC by delivering innovative and trusted technology services and solutions. Legal Division The Legal Division, through the General Counsel, is the attorney for the Commission. The General Counsel, working with the legal staff, represents and appears for the People of the State of California and the CPUC in all actions and proceedings involving any question under the Public Utilities Code or under any order or act of the CPUC. Office of Governmental Affairs (OGA) The Office of Governmental Affairs facilitates the CPUC’s interaction with the Legislature, provides expert policy advice to Commissioner offices, management and staff; informs and lends technical assistance to legislative staff, and engages in the legislative process to ensure that the organization’s perspective is represented in policy discussions and legislation. Office of Internal Audit Services (IAS) The Office of Internal Audit Services provides independent, objective assurance and advisory services designed to add value and improve the CPUC’s operations, accountability, and transparency. The Chief Internal Auditor reports to the Commissioners, through the Audit Committee, and under general direction of the President.

## Office of the Tribal Advisor
The Office of the T ribal Advisor is committed to incorporating tribal concerns into the development of regulations, and policies through engagement in discussion and consultation with tribes. We provide tribes with information about upcoming forums, hearings and workshops. Public Advocates Office The Public Advocates Office is an independent organization within the CPUC that advocates solely on behalf of utility ratepayers. Also known as Cal Advocates, they are the only State entity charged with helping ensure Californians are represented at the California Public Utilities Commission and in other forums. They do this mainly by recommending solutions and alternatives in utility customer best interests for decision-makers to consider when making policy and investment decisions on how to advance the state’s energy, water and communications goals. Rail Safety Division (RSD) The Rail Safety Division has safety oversight of railroads, rail transit systems, and highway/rail crossings. The RSD is responsible for enforcing state and federal laws, regulations, CPUC General Orders (GO), and directives relating to transportation by rail. Safety and Enforcement Division (SED) The Safety and Enforcement Division has safety oversight of electric and communications facilities, natural gas and propane gas systems. SED advocates for public safety through incident investigations and audits, development of proposed new safety rules, and taking enforcement actions to prevent violations of natural gas, electric, and telecommunications General Orders and statutory requirements. SED coordinates with California Office of Emergency Services (CalOES) and CALFIRE during wildfires, Public Safety Power Shutoffs (PSPS) events and other emergencies. Safety Policy Division (SPD) The Safety Policy Division provides advisory support to the CPUC on matters of utility safety policies. Safety is a high priority and SPD’s advice is used to enhance the CPUC’s policymaking capabilities. SPD’s focus includes safety related policy development, analysis, and implementation of regulations by the CPUC affecting electric, gas, telecommunications, water, and transportation utilities. Utility Audits Risk and Compliance Division (UARCD) The Utility Audits, Risk and Compliance Division encompasses two branches: Utility Audits Branch (UAB) and Risk and Compliance Branch (RCB). The UAB performs audits on regulated utilities to ensure utilities implement ratepayer funded programs in compliance with applicable laws, regulations, and CPUC directives. The RCB manages the enterprise risk system by identifying, measuring, reporting, and monitoring risk that affect CPUC’s objectives. Water Division The Water Division regulates over a 100 investor-owned water and sewer utilities under the CPUC’s jurisdiction providing water service to about 16 percent of California’s residents. It also provides advisory services of regulated utilities and water public programs, and monitors compliance with laws and CPUC directives.
