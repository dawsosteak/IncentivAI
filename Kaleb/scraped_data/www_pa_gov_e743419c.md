# ​​Clinical Research (Chapter 20)
If you are a patient interested in participating in medical marijuana research with an Academic Clinical Research Center, please send an email to RA-DHMMResearch@pa.gov. Upon receipt and review, a member of the Pennsylvania Department of Health, Bureau of Medical Marijuana's research division staff will provide you with information on studies in which you may be able to partake.
Participation in the Medical Marijuana Research Program will provide our medical community with invaluable data on the efficacy of medical marijuana as well as specific administration options to assist in recommending the best treatments for patients.
For a brief look into medical marijuana research, please see the information provided by the Academic Clinical Research Centers below.​
  * [Publications by Study(opens in a new tab)](https://www.pa.gov/content/dam/copapwp-pagov/en/health/documents/topics/documents/programs/medical-marijuana/Publications%20by%20Study.xlsx)


## Additional ACRC Information
### Drexel University College of Medicine, Philadelphia 
After receiving approval by the Pennsylvania Department of Health to conduct research on medical cannabis grown by a clinical registrant, and then signing a contract with the clinical registrant, the Chester-based [Agronomed Biologics LLC](https://www.agronomed.com/company), Drexel University has now opened a new [Medical Cannabis Research Center](https://drexel.edu/cannabis-research/) (MCRC) to begin conducting evidence-based research on effects medical cannabis has on patients with specific medical and behavioral maladies. [Read more about the MCRC](https://drexel.edu/news/archive/2021/march/new-medical-cannabis-research-center).  

### Penn State College of Medicine, Hershey 
In May of 2018, Penn State College of Medicine was one of eight universities approved by Gov. Tom Wolf as a Certified Academic Clinical Research Center. In June 2019, the Penn State College of Medicine ACRC, in a relationship with PA Options for Wellness, was one of the first three centers approved by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania. [Learn more about their ACRC](https://research.med.psu.edu/medical-marijuana/).  

### Sidney Kimmel Medical College at Thomas Jefferson University, Philadelphia 
The Sidney Kimmel College of Medicine at Thomas Jefferson University (TJU) became a certified Academic Clinical Research Center (ACRC) in 2018 in partnership with Ethos Cannabis. 
Sidney Kimmel Medical College at Thomas Jefferson University is currently enrolling patients for medical marijuana studies related to cancer and chronic pain. 
TJU is actively recruiting patients who are prescribed opioids and have been treated for cancer in the past three years for a 12-month observational study. The goal of this study is to assess the role of medical marijuana in the management of cancer-related pain conditions. The study involves completing monthly surveys via Zoom or telephone and using a smartphone application to complete brief surveys. Participants will be compensated for completed assessments. To learn more, contact the study team by calling 267-624-4232 or emailing mmjstudy@jefferson.edu.
Please fill out this [secure, online form](https://forms.monday.com/forms/b931f88bd7bf09e812080fe97c0cd23f?r=use1) to be contacted for current or future studies. For questions, please direct them via email at mmjstudy@jefferson.edu.
### University of Pittsburgh School of Medicine, Pittsburgh
Along with their new Clinical Registrant partner, FarmX LLC, the University of Pittsburgh School of Medicine continues its observational research on the effects of cannabis on acute pain, chronic pain, and inflammation in adult patients with sickle cell disease. They also completed a sophisticated retrospective analysis of the effects of cannabis on chronic pain, overall well-being, sleep, and opioid use with the UPMC Department of Anesthesiology. As future studies develop, more information will become available. ​
### Lake Erie College of Osteopathic Medicine-Erie (LECOM) 
LECOM became a certified ACRC in 2018 and has been conducting medical marijuana research with their CR: CannTech PA LLC, doing business as Ayr Wellness. [View the most recent updates regarding their current studies](https://lecom.edu/research/medical-marijuana-research-program/).
### Philadelphia College of Osteopathic Medicine, Philadelphia (PCOM)
PCOM, in collaboration with its clinical registrant partner [Organic Remedies](https://www.pcom.edu/campuses/philadelphia-campus/news-and-events/pcom-news/pcom-announces-research-partnership-with-organic-remedies.html), has developed a multi-study research program that will gather and share data and insights into the use and processing of medicinal cannabis and its impact on behavior, quality of life, cognition, chronic pain and opioid management. [Read more about their research program](https://www.pcom.edu/research/research-areas/medical-cannabis.html).  

### Geisinger Commonwealth School of Medicine, Scranton (GCSOM)
The Geisinger Commonwealth School of Medicine was authorized by the Department of Health as an Academic Clinical Research Center in September of 2021 and became operational in the summer of 2022, with their clinical registrant partner, Story of PA, LLC. With increasing numbers of residents registered to use medical marijuana and leveraging Geisinger's close community relations and aim to make better health easier for our patients, the Geisinger ACRC is conducting research to evaluate the impact of medical marijuana on patients cared for within the Geisinger Health System.   

### Lewis Katz School of Medicine at Temple University, Philadelphia 
Temple University is a certified Academic Clinical Research Center with Laurel Harvest as the clinical registrant for medical marijuana research. Together, we are working to deploy pharmaceutical principals to medical marijuana research, exploring cannabis components to identify mechanisms of action and optimizing formulation and dosing. Ongoing studies focus on the therapeutic potential of cannabis for applications in chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), post incisional pain and eosinophilic esophagitis (EOE). To accomplish this, Temple has engaged investigators from around our university, including those from the College of Science and Technology, School of Pharmacy, Lewis Katz School of Medicine, Temple University Hospital, and College of Public Health. This effort is anchored by Temple's well-established Center of Substance Abuse Research (CSAR). Most recently, Temple and Laurel Harvest have been working on clinical studies to focus on cannabis efficacy for sleep troubles arising from chronic pain, and cannabis use for neuropathic pain. Laurel Harvest Labs, LLC was acquired by Cresco Labs in 2021.
### The Perelman School of Medicine at the University of Pennsylvania (PSOM), Philadelphia

DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH Bureau of Medical Marijuana Guidance for Academic Clinical Research Centers (ACRCs) to study Serious Medical Conditions for Research (SMCR) Only Issued: October 2022 Revised: May 2025 Introduction: The Pennsylvania Department of Health's vision is to have a high quality, coordinated, optimized, and documented medical marijuana research program for Commonwealth residents with serious medical conditions (SMCs) as defined by the Medical Marijuana Act. Given that the potential of medical marijuana treatment for SMCs has not been fully explored, Pennsylvania’s one of a kind medical marijuana research program will provide the medical community with invaluable data on the efficacy of medical marijuana, as well as specific administration options and dosing guidance to assist in recommending the best treatment plans for patients. Because research was only allowed to be conducted on approved SMCs, the Medical Marijuana Advisory Board (MMAB), specifically the Medical Review Subcommittee, developed and proposed a policy and application that would allow Academic Clinical Research Centers (ACRCs) to request that a SMC be approved for research purposes only. The policy and application were approved by the MMAB at the July 28, 2022, Board meeting. The policy can be found here. Please keep in mind, if any ACRC’s IRB cancels a study due to safety concerns, the MMAB will automatically reevaluate its approval of the SMC for research and make a recommendation to the Secretary of Health. The Bureau of Medical Marijuana (Bureau) is providing this document as guidance to ACRCs regarding the potential options for implementation of the MMAB’s policy. Research Options: The Bureau developed two options by which an ACRC could responsibly conduct research on a SMC that has been approved for research purposes only. Option A can be implemented immediately. A notification will be sent to ACRCs when option B becomes available. Option A uses a caregiver and therefore SMCR patients cannot access dispensaries. Option B allows SMCR patients to obtain a Medical Marijuana Identification Card and have access to Clinical Registrant (CR) dispensaries only. Option A. Guidance for SMCR Patients Receiving Medical Marijuana from Caregiver. A caregiver with the Medical Marijuana Program is an individual who can pick up medical marijuana at a dispensary on behalf of their patient. Currently caregivers are primarily used when the patient is in an inpatient setting, they are a minor or are homebound. However, any adult, card-holding patient can also have up to 2 caregivers. For research, this process would work the same as it does now, except there would need to be designated ACRC or CR staff to serve as the caregiver. Upon application and approval as a caregiver, any research professional or CR dispensary employee can be a caregiver. The caregiver(s) would provide the medical marijuana to the SMCR patient.
* * *
The SMCR patient would have a Patient Authorization Letter (PAL) issued by the Office indicating that they may have medical marijuana due to their participation in SMCR research. A patient issued a PAL shall have all of the rights and obligations of a medical marijuana cardholder, except access to a dispensary. PALs are sent at the beginning of each month. Caregivers would need to register the patients that are going to participate in the research as “benefits from assistance (BFA)”. This means that the patient will not have access to their profile in the patient registry or have the option to receive a Medical Marijuana Identification Card. Caregivers are responsible for maintaining these patient accounts. For proper enrollment, the following steps must occur:  The participating caregiver (employed by the CR or ACRC) must first create the “Benefits from Assistance” patient profile from within their caregiver portal.  Patient gets certified by the practitioner affiliated with the ACRC.  Caregiver may purchase the patient’s medication, in accordance with stipulations on their certification. ACRCs must provide the Bureau with a list of all participating practitioners and research professionals by emailing RA-DHMMResearch@pa.gov. The Bureau will conduct periodic audits. The practitioner will not be able to modify the caregiver listed in Section 4 of the patient certification. Therefore, practitioners must notify the Bureau of caregiver changes. When verifying patient certifications, CR Medical Professionals must review Section 4 and Section 6 to ensure the assigned caregiver is picking up the patient’s products. ACRCs are required to ensure that SMCR patients acknowledge and agree that they cannot have an adult patient account for the duration of the study. ACRCs will need to provide the patients’ official legal names and driver’s license or state-issued photo ID numbers to the Bureau as well as a list of all participating caregivers by emailing RA-DHMMResearch@pa.gov. The Bureau will conduct periodic audits. Option B. Guidance for Enrolling SMCR patients in the Medical Marijuana Program The ACRC would enroll SMCR patients in the Research Professional portal and restrict access to medical marijuana with the Phase 2 functionality. The Phase 2 functionality permits an ACRC Research Professional to restrict the SMCR patient to specific research, therefore, limiting access to the associated CR dispensaries. For proper enrollment, the following steps must occur:  Patient registers  Patient gets certified by the practitioner affiliated with the ACRC  Research Professional enrolls patient in restricted study ACRCs must provide the Bureau with a list of all participating practitioners and Research Professionals by emailing RA-DHMMResearch@pa.gov. The Bureau will conduct periodic audits. Using Option B, the SMCR patient would be issued a Medical Marijuana Identification Card. Payment for the card may be covered by the ACRCs. ACRCs should contact the Bureau to determine how they can pay.
* * *
Additional Stipulations: ACRCs must ensure full compliance by CRs and their medical professionals. CRs should be aware of the consequences of dispensing outside of the specific recommendations. For both option A and option B, the medication will be placed (at point of sale) in a specific bag/container. Patients in option A will be required to store their PAL in the bag/container with the medication. Any additional documentation, such as leaflets, inserts, or pamphlets, should also be stored in the bag/container with the medication. Additionally, the contact information for the individuals overseeing the research should be stored in the bag/container with the medication. Only certifying practitioners employed by and engaged in the research with an ACRC can certify participating SMCR patients. Said certifying practitioners must adhere to the below guidelines. • To address the addition of SMCRs, the Bureau will update Section 2 of the patient certification. Each SMCR will be assigned a code. The specific code identifying the corresponding SMCR will only be provided to the practitioners and research professionals employed with the ACRC. The certifying practitioner will need to select the proper coded check box that corresponds with the SMCR. • The practitioner should certify the patient for the period of time they anticipate the SMCR research will take – not to exceed one year. Note: Patients may be recertified up to 60 days prior the initial certification expiring, if the research is continuing beyond the certification expiration. • The certifying practitioner will be required to specify in Section 6 of the certification that the patient can only be dispensed certain products as well as any specific strain, form, and amounts. • For Option A – the certifying practitioner will need to check a box in Section 3, indicating that the patient needs to have a Caregiver. Additionally, the certifying practitioner must provide the Caregiver information in Section 4. • A certifying practitioner must immediately notify the Bureau by emailing RA-DHMMResearch@pa.gov, the patient, and, if applicable for Option A, the Caregiver, of any withdrawal or cancellation of patient participation. The certification may be withdrawn or canceled for the following reasons: (1) if a patient no longer wishes to participate in the SMCR research, (2) if the certifying practitioner determines that the patient will no longer benefit from using this medicine, and (3) if the certifying practitioner determines the patient is noncompliant with the ACRC’s guidelines.

VI. SCORING METHODOLOGY The Act permits the Department to grant or deny a permit to an applicant based upon the criteria specified in section 603(a.1) of the Act: (1) The applicant will maintain effective control of and prevent diversion of medical marijuana. (2) The applicant will comply with all applicable laws of this Commonwealth. (3) The applicant is ready, willing and able to properly carry on the activity for which a permit is sought. (4) The applicant possesses the ability to obtain in an expeditious manner sufficient land, buildings and equipment to properly grow, process or dispense medical marijuana. (5) It is in the public interest to grant the permit. (6) The applicant, including the financial backer or principal, is of good moral character and has the financial fitness necessary to operate. (7) The applicant is able to implement and maintain security, tracking, recordkeeping and surveillance systems relating to the acquisition, possession, growth, manufacture, sale, delivery, transportation, distribution or the dispensing of medical marijuana as required by the Department. (8) The applicant meets the minimum acceptable scoring requirements set forth in 28 Pa. Code § 1211a.27a.
