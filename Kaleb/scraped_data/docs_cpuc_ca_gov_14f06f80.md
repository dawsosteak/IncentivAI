ADMINISTRATIVE LAW JUDGE’S RULING DENYING RECURVE MOTION FOR PARTY STATUS Pursuant to Commission Rules of Practice and Procedure, Rule 1.4, this ruling denies the motion for party status filed by Recurve Analytics, Inc. (Recurve). On April 1, 2022, Recurve filed a motion requesting party status in Rulemaking (R.) 14-10-003. Recurve states it has been a significant contributor to the development of metered energy consumption methods and tools since 2015. Recurve further states it has created open-source python code base currently used by multiple load-serving entities in California and beyond to enable pay-for-performance energy efficiency transactions, identify and track the procurement of flexible grid resources, and optimize electrification initiatives with analytical capabilities, including the emergency summer reliability Market Access deployments directed by the Commission in Decision 21-12-011. Recurve requests to become a party in this proceeding to comment on policies to support near term decisions on the Avoided Cost Calculator methods and assumptions. Rule 1.4(b)(1) and (2) require that a person seeking party status fully disclose the person or entities on whose behalf the motion is made, the interest of FILED 04/05/22 03:24 PM R1410003
* * *
R.14-10-003 ALJ/KHY/fzs
* * *
- 2 such persons in the proceeding, and the pertinent factual and legal contentions that the person intends to make. The motion filed by Recurve described who they represent and their interest in this proceeding. However, Recurve did not provide any factual or legal contentions. The motion of Recurve is not compliant with Rule 1.4(b). Furthermore, R.14-10-003 remains open solely for the purpose of the consideration of the proposed decision on the 2022 Avoided Cost Calculator update. The record for the proposed decision was submitted on January 5, 2022 and Recurve did not contribute to the development of the record. Allowing Recurve’s participation at this juncture in the consideration of the proposed decision is unfair to those parties who have actively developed and served opening and/or rebuttal testimony, participated in evidentiary hearing, and/or written and filed opening and/or reply briefs. Granting party status to Recurve at this stage of the proceeding is prejudicial to current parties. Rule 1.4(c) provides that, where circumstances warrant, the Administrative Law Judge may deny party status or limit the degree to which a party may participate in this proceeding. For the reasons described above, Recurve is denied party status in R.14-10-003. Recurve is, however, encouraged to file a motion for party status in the successor to this Rulemaking, which the Commission anticipates opening soon. Future updates of the Avoided Cost Calculator are anticipated to be addressed in that successor proceeding.
