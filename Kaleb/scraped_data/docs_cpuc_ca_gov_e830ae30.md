DECISION MODIFYING DECISION 16-06-007 AND ALLOWING A ONE-YEAR WAIVER TO UPDATE THE AVOIDED COST CALCULATOR
* * *
Summary This Decision modifies Ordering Paragraph 2 of Decision 16-06-007 allowing for a one-year waiver of the Commission’s Energy Division’s requirement to update the avoided cost calculator. 1. Background In Decision (D.) 16-06-007, the Commission adopted actions to address avoided cost calculator version control and updates. Specific to this Decision, Ordering Paragraph 2 of D.16-06-007 requires the Commission’s Energy Division (Energy Division) to draft a resolution recommending data updates and minor corrections to the avoided cost calculator, no later than May 1st of each year. In an April 12, 2017 letter (Letter) addressed to the Commission’s Executive Director and copied to the service list of Application 14-06-001 et al., the Energy Division requested a waiver of the May 1, 2017 requirement to update the avoided cost calculator. No party expressed opposition to the requested waiver.
* * *
R.14-10-003 ALJ/KHY/ek4
* * *
- 2 2. Discussion As explained below, this Decision modifies D.16-06-007, Ordering Paragraph 2, to require Energy Division to draft a Resolution recommending data updates and minor corrections to the avoided cost calculator, no later than May 1 each year, beginning May 1, 2018. In its April 12, 2017 Letter, Energy Division requested a 2017 waiver from the requirements of D.16-06-007, Ordering Paragraph 2, relying on Commission Rules of Practice and Procedure, Rule 16.6. Energy Division explained in its Letter that ongoing processing delays in the Commission’s contracts office have created a delay in obtaining technical consulting services to perform the update of the avoided cost calculator, thus creating an inability to meet the May 1, 2017 deadline to complete the update of the avoided cost calculator. Furthermore, Energy Division contends that updating the calculator may not be necessary at this time because data inputs have changed little since the last update was completed and adopted by the Commission seven months ago (i.e., August 2016). No party submitted comments or opposition to Energy Division’s Letter. This Decision agrees that it is unlikely there are significant changes to the data points since the fall 2016 update of the avoided cost calculator. The Commission finds it reasonable to waive the May 1, 2017 requirement for Energy Division to update the avoided cost calculator. Furthermore, given the ongoing processing delays in the Commission’s contracts office, it is prudent to ensure a sufficient amount of time for Energy Division to provide the next update. Hence, it is reasonable to modify Ordering Paragraph 2 of D.16-06-007 such that the update of the avoided cost calculator is not due until May 1, 2018.
* * *
R.14-10-003 ALJ/KHY/ek4
* * *
- 3 3. Comments on Proposed Decision The proposed decision in this matter was mailed to the parties in accordance with Section 311 of the Public Utilities Code and comments were allowed under Rule 14.3 of the Commission’s Rules of Practice and Procedure. Comments were filed on July 12, 2017 by Pacific Gas and Electric Company (PG&E;) expressing support for the proposed decision. PG&E; also highlights current efforts in this proceeding to consider the adoption of a Societal Cost Test and an interim greenhouse gas (GHG) adder, contending that adoption of either of these may require an earlier update of the avoided cost calculator. PG&E; requests that, if action is taken on either the Societal Cost Test or the interim GHG adder, the update of the avoided cost calculator occur prior to the May 1, 2018 date proposed in this decision, if necessary, to avoid potential double counting components. 4. Assignment of Proceeding Michael Picker is the assigned Commissioner and Kelly A. Hymes is the assigned Administrative Law Judge in this proceeding. Findings of Fact 1. The Energy Division submitted a letter to the Commission Executive Director requesting a waiver of Ordering Paragraph 2 of D.16-06-007. 2. No party submitted comments or opposition to Energy Division’s letter requesting a waiver from Order Paragraph 2 of D.16-06-007. 3. It is unlikely there are significant changes to the data points since the fall 2016 update of the avoided cost calculator. 4. It is reasonable to waive the May 1, 2017 requirement for Energy Division to update the avoided cost calculator.
* * *

Given the ongoing processing delays in the Commission’s contracts office, it is prudent to ensure a sufficient amount of time for Energy Division to provide the next update to the avoided cost calculator. 6. It is reasonable to modify Ordering Paragraph 2 of D.16-06-007 such that the update of the avoided cost calculator is not due until May 1, 2018. Conclusion of Law 1. The Commission should modify Ordering Paragraph 2 of D.16-06-007 and delay the next update of the avoided cost calculator to no later than May 1, 2018.
* * *
ORDER
* * *
IT IS ORDERED that Ordering Paragraph 2 of Decision 16-06-007 is modified to read as follows:
* * *
> “The Commission’s Energy Division, no later than May 1st each year
beginning in 2018, shall draft a resolution recommending data updates and minor corrections to the avoided cost calculator, and when appropriate, the inputs as described in this decision. Energy Division may issue a draft resolution updating the Avoided Cost Calculator for 2016 after this decision is adopted.” This order is effective today. Dated August 10, 2017, in Francisco, California.
