#  City Council 
The City Council represents residents and businesses in Anaheim. The City Council is made up of Anaheim's mayor, who is elected at large, and six City Council members elected by voters in their districts.

City of Anaheim COUNCIL POLICY
* * *
Subject: Public Posting of Calendars Policy # 1.13 for Key City Officials Adopted Date: 11/07/23 Amended: Page (1) of (3) A. PURPOSE
* * *
To promote transparency and allow for public viewing, this public posting of calendars policy establishes the requirements for the posting of key City of Anaheim officials’ city related event and meeting calendars on the City’s website. As such, the mayor, city council members, city manager, assistant city manager, and deputy city manager shall maintain a monthly city calendar and publish their calendars to the city’s website. This policy is also intended to make more accessible records that would otherwise be subject to a Public Records Act request. The policy is not intended to list every interaction that these listed public officials may have. Some public officials may voluntarily provide additional information that is not necessarily required by this policy. B. CALENDAR REPORTING
* * *
Posted calendars shall report all scheduled external city-related appointments, which include the attendance at public events in official city capacity, and calls and/or meetings with, members of the public, businesses, developers, union representatives, consultants, and lobbyists (collectively, “city-related appointments”) that are materially and significantly related to official city business, and that are not exempt from reporting.
* * *
Scheduled external city-related appointments, that are not calls and/or meetings, include public events attended in official city capacity. These public events include board and/or commission meetings, speaking engagements, ribbon cutting ceremonies, charity events, openings of a venue, or other events attended in official city capacity.
* * *
City officials are encouraged to report unscheduled calls and/or meetings of a material or significant nature relating to official city business. For each entry, calendars shall include the date and time of the city-related appointment; the name(s), title(s) and affiliated organizations(s) of the people in attendance; as well as a general statement of the topic of the meeting. If an event is sponsored by an organization, it is sufficient to identify the sponsoring organization in lieu of all in attendance. Additionally, the location of the city-related appointment is not required to be disclosed.
* * *
City of Anaheim COUNCIL POLICY
* * *
Subject: Public Posting of Calendars Policy # 1.13 for Key City Officials Adopted Date: 11/07/23 Amended: Page (2) of (3) C. CALENDAR REPORTING EXEMPTIONS
* * *
The following are exempted from reporting:
* * *
• Personal appointments (including, but not limited to, medical and other personal errands or appointments). • Campaign-related events or appointments (which must be attended at non-city time and not using city resources). • Attendance at an event or function for personal entertainment and not related to any official city business, or in capacity as a city official (for example, a concert, dinner, or other social ticketed event attended with family and/or friends, that was personally paid for). • Attendance at an event pursuant to the City of Anaheim Ticket Policy and that are disclosed on a Form 802. • Meetings, including those unscheduled, and for which no significant or material official city business is discussed (for example, a friendly encounter with a business owner where a city-related matter was very briefly discussed, and the encounter was not intended for that purpose). • Phone calls that are received, including those unscheduled, and for which no significant or material official city business is discussed (for example, a spontaneous call from a business owner in the city where a matter affecting that business was very briefly discussed and the call was not intended for that purpose). • Meetings with internal city staff only, including retained city consultants or vendors. • Information protected by attorney-client privilege. • Information about attorney work product. • Information about city staff recruitment. • Information about a personnel issue. • Information regarding recruiting and retention. • Information concerning confidential criminal investigations and security. • Information concerning or about whistleblowers. • Information about residents who reasonably fear that public disclosure of their appointment will result in retaliation or other tangible harm. • Information that is otherwise prohibited from disclosure under federal or state law.
* * *
City of Anaheim COUNCIL POLICY
* * *
Subject: Public Posting of Calendars Policy # 1.13 for Key City Officials Adopted Date: 11/07/23 Amended: Page (3) of (3) Calendars shall be posted on the city’s website no later than the tenth business day of each month and shall reflect the schedule of the previous calendar month.
