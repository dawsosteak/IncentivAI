I. INTRODUCTION Pursuant to the Administrative Law Judge’s (ALJ) Ruling, dated December 1, 2025, and the ALJ’s December 19, 2025 email ruling granting an extension of time for comments, Small Business Utility Advocates (SBUA) respectfully submits these reply comments on the Staff Proposal for Policy on Natural Gas Energy Efficiency Incentives (Staff Proposal). SBUA continues to support the Staff Proposal's objectives of transitioning California’s energy efficiency (EE) programs away from natural gas measures where viable electric alternatives (VEAs) exist. After reviewing other parties ’ opening comments, SBUA offers the following reply comments focusing on issues of particular importance to small business customers, including cost-effectiveness testing, equity considerations, data transparency, and refrigerant program implementation. II. REPLY COMMENTS A. Cost-Effectiveness Testing Several parties address the appropriate cost-effectiveness framework for determining whether a VEAs exists. SBUA’s position is aligned with The Joint Regional Energy Networks (Joint RENs): VEA determinations should rely on the Participant Cost Test (PCT) as the primary
* * *
Reply Comments on Staff Proposal || Small Business Utility Advocates 2 screening tool, supplemented by the Societal Cost Test ( SCT) for informational and policy guidance purposes.1 Relatedly, SBUA supports the Joint RENs’ recommendation that one-time electrification costs, such as service upgrades, electric panel upgrades, electrical rewiring costs, and additional permitting costs, should be excluded from the PCT calculation. 2 These infrastructure investments benefit multiple end uses and should not be attributed solely to individual measures. This approach is particularly important for small businesses, which may face significant upfront infrastructure barriers to electrificat ion. Several other parties also expressed support for the PCT over the Total Resource Cost (TRC) test to evaluate VEAs, with certain modifications, including Sierra Club and Cohen Ventures, Inc. dba Energy Solutions (Energy Solutions).
