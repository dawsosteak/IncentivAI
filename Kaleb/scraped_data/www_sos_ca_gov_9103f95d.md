# 2018 VCA Education Materials Archives
During the 2018 Election Cycle, the Secretary of State Office gather all version of the Elections Administration Plan (EAP), and all outreach and education materials created from advocates, community organizations, county elections officials and researchers. All of these documents can be found on the pages listed below. Please click on the buttons below to visit the VCA Archives pages for the 2018 EAP or 2018 educational materials.

Starting in 2018, all registered voters in Sacramento County Will receive their ballot in the mail a few weeks be fore the election. Most neighborhood Polling places will no longer be used for voting. Instead you will have 3 choices for how to vote. VOTE CENTER: You can vote in person by visiting any vote center in your county. All Vote Centers will be open for at least 4 days: from Saturday, June 2 through Tuesday, June 5. Some Vote Centers will be open for 11 days starting Saturday, May 26. At every vote center you can:  Vote in Person.  Register and vote the same day, all the way up through Election Day.  Drop off you complete Ballot.  Vote with an accessible voting machine.  Get help and voting materials in multiple languages VOTE-BY-MAIL: You can mail your ballot as soon as you receive it BALLOT BROPBOX: You can drop off your ballot at any secure Ballot Dropbox as soon as you receive it.
