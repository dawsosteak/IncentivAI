#  Electrical Service Checklist - New or Upgrade Commercial/Industrial 
## A 10-Step Guide for New & Growing Businesses
Whether you are a new customer to Anaheim's electric system, a long-time customer moving to a new location in our community or you need to add electric capacity to your existing facility to power the growing needs of your business, Anaheim Public Utilities (APU) and Anaheim's Building Division (ABD) are ready and willing to work with you to make your new service installation or upgrade go smoothly.  
  
We have put together this 10-step guide to help bring you the power you need for permanent service or an upgrade to existing service. For residential upgrades, please visit the following link to [request a meter spot](https://www.anaheim.net/formcenter/public-utilities-6/upgrading-residential-panel-request-form-52). For temporary construction power, view [this guide](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/480).
### 10-Step Guide
  1. Contact Electrical Engineering at 714-765-5156 to speak with an Electric System Designer (ESD) about your new electrical service and request a feasibility study for the proposed location. A feasibility study is a free service from APU to determine the nearest available electric point of connection for your electric panel.   
  

  2. Obtain a building permit and addresses for your new electrical upgrades from ABD. 
     * For larger projects, the ABD may require a formal plan check rather than an over-the-counter review. A finalized permit will be required before an electric service plan can be issued for construction. Please visit the following [link](https://www.anaheim.net/5252/Building-Division) or call 714-765-5153 for more information and fee requirements.
  3. Submit your electrical service drawings (site plan – CAD File 2024 version or earlier, elevation plans, electric single line diagram and load schedules) to your assigned ESD to obtain an electrical service plan. Depending on the scope of the project, plans generally take 8-12 weeks to generate, depending upon current backlog and complexity of the service requirements. Supporting civil structures (or substructures) and electrical service cables are to be installed by the customer’s contractor per plans. Electrical installation to the point of service will be performed by the Utility. View a sample [commercial service plan](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/474).
     * Projects within the Public Right-of-Way will require a Public Works Right-of-Way Construction Permit (RCP) before an electric service plan can be issued for construction. Please visit the following [link](https://www.anaheim.net/5761/Public-Works---Electronic-Plan-Review) or call 714-765-5176 for more information. 
  4. While the design process is underway, we ask that the customer submit their proposed electric service panel construction drawings (shop drawings) to Electrical Engineering for review and approval _prior_
     * Panel submittals must meet Electrical Utility Service Equipment Requirements Committee (EUSERC) standards and have the appropriate engineering stamp and signature by the manufacturer. Contact the ESD with any questions about EUSERC acceptability requirements which can be found [here](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/48198/EUSERC-Acceptability).
  5. Customer fees for electrical service are due after obtaining a service plan from Electrical Engineering. Fees include:
     * Applicable easement processing fees
     * Engineering
     * Inspection
     * Service crew labor and materials  
  
A transmittal letter will be provided identifying the associated fees. View a sample [letter of transmittal](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/477).
  6. The customer is to install the main meter panel, substructures (conduits, transformer concrete pads, etc.) and electrical service cables per the service plan and ABD codes and requirements. Prior to any back-filling, contact the Electric Utility Inspector as identified on the service plan for the necessary inspections.  
  

  7. Please note that if electrical facilities (such as a transformer or switch) are installed on private property, Electrical Engineering will require a Public Utility Easement (PUE). The customer is to provide PUE documentation subject to approval by the Department of Public Works Real Property Division, including:
     * Typed legal description of PUE and engineered drawing, to be prepared, stamped, and signed by a licensed land surveyor or qualified civil engineer (View a [sample PUE legal description](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/476))
     * Closure calculations if legal description is by metes and bounds
     * Title Report or Grant Deed
     * Proof of signing authority
     * All documents to be submitted on 8.5-inch by 11-inch paper  
  
Once documents have been submitted and verified, an easement deed will be compiled and issued by Anaheim Public Works Real Property Division for signature. View an [easement deed sample](https://www.anaheim.net/DocumentCenter/View/475).
  8. If an electrical outage is required, the customer will be required to contact Electrical Engineering 10 business days prior to service completion date for APU to coordinate outage with affected customers. If work cannot be performed during normal business hours, then overtime fees will apply.   
  

  9. Before your service can be completed, a series of releases are required by various departments of the city. Please contact the following:
     * Building Inspector at 714-765-5153 for panel release
     * Electric Meter Shop at 714-765-6858 for meter release
     * Electric Utility Inspector at 714-765-6873 for inspection release
     * Verification of fees paid, easements received, and panel drawings approved
  10. A field service crew will then be scheduled to energize the meter panel.
