- 4 Cc: ALJ_Support ID; ALJ Docket Office; ALJ Process Subject: R1410003 Email Ruling Granting Party Status to CC Partners and NRG
* * *
This Email Ruling grants the motions for party status filed in Rulemaking (R.) 14-10-003 by Community Choice Partners (CC Partners) and NRG Energy, Inc. (NRG), as described below.
* * *
On September 11, 2015, CC Partners filed a motion for party status in R.14-10-003. CC Partners state that they are a corporation dedicated to helping local governments achieve their energy policy goals. CC Partners explain that, as a provider of turnkey energy procurement solutions to these local governments, they have an interest in the development of the integrated demand side resources framework. In compliance with Commission Rules of Practice and Procedure, Rule 1.4(b), CC Partners present several factual and legal contentions including that the overreliance on the investor-owned utilities to drive the integration of demand side resources will impede the transition to a robust market-driven model that effectively utilizes those resources and providing equal access to the grid for all resource technologies and providers is essential to effective integration of distributed energy resources.
* * *
On September 17, 2015, NRG filed a motion for party status in R.14-10-003. In its motion, NRG states that it is the parent company of several entities that own, market or operate generation resources in California. NRG states that it is involved in several other Commission proceeding and it intends to file comments and bring into this proceeding its perspectives gained from its experience with deploying distributed energy resources and its vision for animating the deployment of these resources. In compliance with Commission Rules of Practice and Procedure, Rule 1.4(b), NRG contends that distributed energy resources compensation mechanisms, in addition to recognizing the value provided to the consumer, must be structured so as to allow access to the widest possible number of revenue streams, including revenue streams for providing distribution reliability services and services to the California Independent System Operator’s wholesale markets.
* * *
The motions for party status filed by CC Partners and NRG are granted. Commission Rules of Practice and Procedure, Rule 1.4(b) states that a person seeking party status by motion shall fully disclose the persons or entities in whose behalf the filing is made, the interest of such entities, and the factual and legal contentions that the entity intends to make. Both CC Partners and NRG fully complied with Rule 1.4. Hence, it is reasonable to grant the motions of both entities. CC Partners and NRG are hereby granted party status in R.14-10-003.
