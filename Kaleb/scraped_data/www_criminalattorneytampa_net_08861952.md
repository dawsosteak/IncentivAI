# Tampa Lawyer for Manufacturing Child Pornography
## Manufacture of Child Pornography in Tampa, FL
Child pornography is one of the most severe and unforgivable crimes under state and federal law. In Florida, as well as across the United States, the production, distribution, and possession of child pornography are met with harsh legal penalties due to the profound harm such offenses inflict on children and society. Manufacturing child pornography, in particular, is a criminal act with devastating legal consequences. If you are facing charges related to the creation of child pornography in Tampa, Florida, it is critical to understand the serious repercussions that come with this crime and reach out to a Tampa lawyer for manufacturing child pornography.
## The Legal Definition of Child Pornography
Under both federal and Florida state law, child pornography refers to any visual depiction, including photographs, videos, or digital images, of a minor (someone under 18 years of age) engaged in sexually explicit conduct. Manufacturing child pornography refers to the act of creating or producing such material, whether through photography, videography, or other forms of media. This includes situations where the offender films, photographs, or otherwise creates explicit material involving a child.
## Florida State Law on Child Pornography
In Florida, the creation of child pornography is explicitly prohibited under state statutes. Specifically, [_Section 827.071 of the Florida Statutes_](https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2018/827.071) criminalizes the production and distribution of child pornography. Violating this law can result in severe criminal penalties, as it is considered a form of child exploitation.
Florida law divides the offense of manufacturing child pornography into varying degrees of severity. The most serious charge is the first-degree felony of producing or possessing child pornography, which can result in the following penalties:
  * **First-Degree Felony** : Producing or possessing child pornography in Florida is a first-degree felony, punishable by up to 30 years in prison, a $10,000 fine, and mandatory registration as a sexual offender. A conviction for a first-degree felony may also result in lifelong supervision, including probation and restrictions on where the convicted individual can live, work, and interact with minors.
  * **Second-Degree Felony** : If the individual produces child pornography by using a minor in their care, custody, or control, they may face a second-degree felony charge, which could lead to up to 15 years in prison and a $10,000 fine.


Moreover, Florida law mandates that convicted offenders must undergo psychological evaluations and complete sex offender treatment programs while on probation or after their release from prison. These measures aim to prevent repeat offenses and ensure that offenders do not pose a future threat to minors.
## Federal Law on Child Pornography
Since manufacturing child pornography is a state and federal crime, the [_Federal Child Protection and Obscenity Enforcement Act_](https://www.congress.gov/bill/100th-congress/house-bill/3889) makes it illegal to produce, distribute, or possess child pornography, with even more severe penalties for individuals convicted under federal law. If convicted of manufacturing child pornography, the penalties include:
  * **Mandatory Minimum Sentences** : Under federal law, producing child pornography is a mandatory minimum 15-year sentence with the possibility of up to 30 years in prison. The specific sentence will depend on the number of offenses, the age of the child, and the circumstances surrounding the production of the pornography.
  * **Life Imprisonment** : In cases involving aggravated circumstances—such as the abuse of particularly young children or producing pornography involving multiple minors—the offender may face a life sentence in prison.
  * **Sex Offender Registration** : Like Florida law, federal law mandates that individuals convicted of manufacturing child pornography be placed on the [_national sex offender registry_](https://www.nsopw.gov/). This requirement can severely limit the offender’s ability to live a normal life after serving their sentence, as they will be subject to significant restrictions on where they can live, work, and travel.
  * **Fines and Civil Penalties** : Federal law also imposes substantial fines for individuals convicted of manufacturing child pornography, in addition to any restitution to victims or their families.


## Additional Legal Consequences
Aside from the criminal penalties outlined above, individuals convicted of manufacturing child pornography in Tampa, FL, face additional long-term consequences:
  1. **Social and Emotional Consequences** : Convicted individuals are likely to face social stigma, isolation, and rejection from family, friends, and their community. The long-term emotional toll of a conviction for such a crime can be devastating, not only for the individual but also for their loved ones.
  2. **Civil Lawsuits** : Victims of child pornography often pursue civil lawsuits against those who produce or distribute such material. In some cases, the convicted individual may be ordered to pay substantial damages to the victim and their family.
  3. **International Implications** : Because child pornography is illegal worldwide, individuals convicted under U.S. law may face restrictions on traveling abroad or even be detained in other countries that have strict anti-child exploitation laws.
