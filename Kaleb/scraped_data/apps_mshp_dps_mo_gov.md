The Missouri Director of Revenue has delegated the Missouri State Highway Patrol authority to conduct driver examinations as set out in Sections 302.173 and 302.720 of the Revised Statutes of Missouri. The Driver Examination Section of the Patrol administers the provisions of this law, and therefore administers both knowledge tests and skills tests throughout the state. The Patrol does not process licensing fees nor issue driver credentials for the state of Missouri (these are handled by the Missouri Department of Revenue).  
  
The Patrol maintains full-time driver testing services at **16** locations around the state, and utilizes an additional **119** part-time travel sites to provide examination services in every one of Missouri's 114 counties. Each of these locations allow for all forms of knowledge testing and provides non-CDL skills testing. The Patrol also performs the skills testing of CDL applicants, which consists of a pre-trip examination, basic skills (on the lot) examination, and road testing, at **10** locations around the state. There are **5** locations used as a combination of CDL Skills/Driver testing sites.   
  
**DISCLAIMER:** Information on the **155** locations is updated regularly. You are encouraged to contact the driver examination station number provided directly for any further questions. If you are still unable to locate a driver examination station, please feel free to contact the Driver and Vehicle Safety Division at 573-751-2341.

#### Missouri Sex Offender Registry Disclaimer
**BY USING THIS SITE, YOU AGREE THAT YOU HAVE READ AND UNDERSTAND THE TERMS AND CONDITIONS OF THIS DISCLAIMER**

DISCLAIMER: IT IS ILLEGAL TO USE ANY INFORMATION REGARDING A REGISTERED SEXUAL OFFENDER TO FACILITATE THE COMMISSION OF A CRIME.
The information provided on this website is a supplement to the sex offender registries maintained by the registering officials in Missouri. The provided information only pertains to individuals who have been convicted of, found guilty, of or please guilty to committing or attempting to commit sexual offenses and may not reflect the entire criminal history of a particular individual. Individuals required to register for crimes of kidnapping, felonious restraint or child abuse may not be listed on this website. The Missouri State Highway Patrol ("Patrol"), Criminal Justice Information Services Division, has not considered or assessed the specific risk or re-offense with regard to any individual prior to their inclusion on this website and has not made a determination that any individual included on the website is dangerous. Individuals included on the website are included solely by virtue of their conviction record and Missouri state law. The primary purpose of providing this information is to make the information easily available and accessible, not to warn about any specific individual.
Notably, the maps of offense may not show all individuals in a given area. This is because home, work, school, and temporary address information may not be adequate to map. While this registry is updated daily in an effort to assure that the information on the website is complete and accurate, the Patrol makes no representation, express or implied, that the information contained on the website is accurate. Lastly, the information contained in the registry can change at any time. For example, the current residence, status or other information regarding an individual may not be accurate. Further, some information is gathered from the individuals themselves who may fail to provide complete or accurate information. Additional information and verification can be obtained from the registering official of the jurisdiction where the sex offender resides.
History:  

In 1995 Missouri implemented a statewide Sex Offender Registration program in accordance with the Jacob Wetterling Crimes Against Children and Sexually Violent Offender Registration Act. Since then, there have been several other federal laws that have affected sex offender registration across the country. The Sex Offender Registration and Notification Act which is Title I of the Adam Walsh Child Protection and Safety Act of 2006 provided a comprehensive set of minimum standards for sex offender registration and notification. Sections 589.400-425 and 43.650 of the Revised Statutes of Missouri mandates that the Patrol shall maintain a sex offender database and a website on the Internet that is accessible to the public.
Individuals required to register and are viewable on this website will include an individual's registration status. Definitions of the statues are provided below for reference:
  * Compliant - the individual is registering as required.
  * Non-Compliant - the individual has not completed or updated his/her information and in not compliant with the Chief Law Enforcement Official in the county in which the offender resides.
  * Pending Registration - the individual has provided a change of address to another county within Missouri and has not registered with the Chief Law Enforcement Official in the county in which the individual plans to reside OR the individual has provided an address to the Department of Corrections prior to release and the individual has not registered with the Chief Law Enforcement Official of the county in which the individual plans to reside.
  * Absconder - the individual's whereabouts are unknown to law enforcement officials.
  * Incarcerated - the individual's last known whereabouts is a county, city, state, military, or federal correctional facility.
  * Moved Out of State - the individual is no longer living, working, or going to school in the state of Missouri.

## Driver Examination
The Missouri State Highway Patrol's Driver Examination section of the Driver and Vehicle Safety Division provides an effective program of driver testing to ensure Missouri licensed drivers have a level of competency consistent with safe driving habits and practices, as well as demonstrated skills and knowledge of Missouri laws relating to traffic safety.
Driver Examination administers the written and driving examinations to individuals qualifying for a Missouri Instruction Permit; Class F Operator License; Class E Chauffeur License; motorcycle license; and Commercial Driver Licenses (CDL).
Driver Examination is responsible for all employees who administer the tests, making sure the tests are administered fairly and professionally, and procures the examination stations for testing throughout the state of Missouri.
Examination stations are provided in each county of the state and CDL sites are located within each troop of the Highway Patrol.
While appointments are not necessary for the driving skills portion of a non-CDL license, they are necessary to take a Commercial Driver License (CDL) driving skills test.
Before arriving at an examination station, an individual should determine the necessary documentation required to test as well as the hours of operation for the testing location(s). Written and driving skills tests are given based upon personnel availability and volume of test applicants.
