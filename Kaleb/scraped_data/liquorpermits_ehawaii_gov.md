# Welcome
The County of Kaua‘i Liquor Control online liquor license and permit service provides an easy and convenient way for businesses to initiate the licensing and permitting process.
## Liquor License Original
The process of obtaining a Liquor license is highly regulated. To guide you through the process, a licensing officer is available. Start your application by providing preliminary information about your business. After receiving your information and application fee, a licensing officer will contact you to schedule an interview.
[Read more about what to expect](https://liquorpermits.ehawaii.gov/kauai/wf/help.html#what-to-expect)
[Start Your Application](https://liquorpermits.ehawaii.gov/kauai/wf/form/interview_request.html)
## Liquor License Renewal
Businesses with a current liquor license are required to renew their license by June 30th.
To renew, submit your application with:
  * a copy of the notarized application form [APPLICATION FOR RENEWAL OF LIQUOR LICENSE](https://www.kauai.gov/files/assets/public/v/1/liquor-control/documents/fy-25-renewal-application-filable.pdf)
  * a copy of your current [Hawai‘i State Tax Clearance](https://files.hawaii.gov/tax/forms/2022/a6_i.pdf) or Hawai‘i Compliance Express (HCE) [Vendor Compliance Certificate](https://vendors.ehawaii.gov/hce)
  * a copy of your current [DCCA Annual Report (optional)](https://hbe.ehawaii.gov/annuals)
  * proof of liquor liability insurance, and
  * renewal fee payment.


Renew Your License
## Direct Wine Shipper Permit
If your business plans to sell and ship wine to customers in the County of Kaua‘i, a permit is required by law.
To apply, submit a permit application with:
  * a copy of your current Hawaii State General Excise Tax license
  * a copy of your current Alcoholic Beverage License, and
  * permit fee payment.


[Start Your Application](https://liquorpermits.ehawaii.gov/kauai/wf/form/dwsp1.html)
