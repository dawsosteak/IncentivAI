# Birth, Death, and Other Vital Statistics
Welcome to the birth, death, and other vital statistics page, the premier source for vital statistics data in Pennsylvania. Use the following links to download or print various vital statistics and reports. At the bottom of this page you will find links to other websites and resources with an emphasis on vital statistics.
## _[Birth Statistics](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/health/health-statistics/birth-death-and-other-vital-statistics/birth-statistics)_
Large volume of the latest available and historical state, county and municipality data by age, race/ethnicity and various birth-related topics.
## _[Death Statistics](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/health/health-statistics/birth-death-and-other-vital-statistics/death-statistics)_
Large volume of the latest available and historical state, county and municipality data by age, race/ethnicity and various death-related topics.
## _[Pennsylvania Vital Statistics](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/health/health-statistics/birth-death-and-other-vital-statistics/vital-statistics-annual-report)_
Annual report of births, deaths, abortion, reported pregnancy and population statistics for the state, counties and major municipalities. Each report contains over 200 pages of statistics, charts and maps.
## _[County Health Profiles](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/health/health-statistics/birth-death-and-other-vital-statistics/county-health-profiles)_
A series of 67 individual data listings - one for each county. Numerous statistics are shown for various health-related subject areas.
## _[Maternal and Child Health Statistics](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/health/health-statistics/birth-death-and-other-vital-statistics/maternal-and-child-health)_
Twenty-nine reports (28 municipalities and PA) with statistics on birth data, deaths among youth, and infant deaths.
## _[Marriage and Divorce Statistics](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/health/health-statistics/birth-death-and-other-vital-statistics/marriage-and-divorce-statistics)_
Occurrence data for the state and counties.
## _[Most Popular First Names](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/health/health-statistics/birth-death-and-other-vital-statistics/birth-first-name-occurrences)_
Interactive first name lookup tool and frequency tables for the state.
##  [Pennsylvania Abortion Report](https://www.pa.gov/content/dam/copapwp-pagov/en/health/documents/topics/healthstatistics/vitalstatistics/documents/pennsylvania_annual_abortion_report_2024_final.pdf) (PDF)
Annual report displaying various abortion statistics for the state and counties.
##  [Maternal Death Report](https://www.pa.gov/content/dam/copapwp-pagov/en/health/documents/topics/healthstatistics/vitalstatistics/documents/maternal_death_report_2023.pdf) (PDF)  

Annual report displaying maternal death statistics for the state.
## Other resources
To access vital statistics for the United States, go to the [National Center for Health Statistics(opens in a new tab)](http://www.cdc.gov/nchs/nvss.htm) web site.

## 2024 ABORTION STATISTICS
**Executive Summary**
**In**
**Nea**
Pennsylvania has monitored and reported on abortion data since 1975. This report is produced pursuant to Section 3214 of the Abortion Control Act, 18 Pa. C.S. § 3214(e)(1). 2024, there were 32,946 abortions performed in Pennsylvania. Nearly half of all abortions performed in Pennsylvania were to both white (13,859) and to Black or African-American individuals (14,956) while abortions to individuals of Hispanic origin accounted 13.6 percent (4,487). More than 88 percent (28,999) of the abortions performed in Pennsylvania in 2024 were to unmarried individuals. rly all abortions in 2024 (30,599 or 92.9%) were performed in the first 14 weeks of pregnancy. The age group having the most abortions was 25-29, accounting for 9,144 (27.8 percent) of all 2024 abortions. Individuals under age 20 accounted for 8 percent of all abortions in 2024, and individuals under age 18 accounted for 2.4 percent. Comparatively, in 2023, individuals under age 20 accounted for 8.1 percent, and individuals under age 18 accounted for 2.5 percent. 92.6 percent of the abortions performed in 2024 were to Pennsylvania residents. Residents of other states, territories and other countries accounted for 2,451 abortions in 2024, a decrease from 2,926 in 2023. Of the abortions performed in 2024 in Pennsylvania, about 93 percent were performed in ten counties: Allegheny; Berks; Bucks; Dauphin; Delaware; Lehigh; Montgomery; Northampton; Philadelphia; and York. More than 34 percent of Pennsylvania residents having abortions were residents of Philadelphia County. Other counties with high number of resident abortions included: Allegheny (3, 608); Montgomery ( 1,880); Delaware (2,185); Lehigh (1,066); Bucks (842); and Dauphin (824). In 2024, there were 480 reports of complications from abortions that were submitted by physicians; 9.1 percent less than the 524 reported in 2023. More than 78 percent of the complications were related to retained products of conception, 16.3 percent to bleeding and 2.3 percent to infection. Notes: The source for the number of abortions reported in Pennsylvania in 2024 is the "Report of Induced Termination of Pregnancy," which is an individual report filed for each procedure. The source for the complications data is "Abortions: Report of Complications," a separate reporting form completed by physicians who have provided medical care to an individual because of a complication resulting from an abortion or attempted abortion. Unknowns are excluded from all calculations.

Maternal Deaths by Underlying Cause of Death PENNSYLVANIA OCCURRENCES, 2023 Questions on the reporting of maternal deaths should be addressed to the Division of Health Informatics, Pennsylvania Department of Health at ra-DHIContactUs@pa.gov or 717-782-2448. The Abortion Control Act requires that deaths occurring in Pennsylvania arising from pregnancy, childbirth or intentional abortion be reported to the Department of Health (department). The official maternal death definition that is used in the Abortion Control Act comes from the World Health Organization (WHO), which defines maternal deaths as the death of a woman while pregnant or within 42 days of termination of pregnancy, irrespective of the duration and site of the pregnancy, from any cause related to or aggravated by the pregnancy or its management but not from accidental or incidental causes. This definition is also used by the National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS). In order to be consistent with previous reports as well as other state and national reports, the number of maternal deaths listed does not include all deaths occurring to pregnant or recently pregnant women. The only cases listed in this report are those officially assigned with an underlying cause of death based on the International Classification of Diseases, 10th Revision (ICD-10) death code of A34, O00-O95, and O98-O99. This is different from various pregnancy related and pregnancy associated deaths which can look at the broader impacts of pregnancy on death and may include deaths where the individual was within one year of the conclusion of pregnancy. To be in compliance with the Abortion Control Act, the department annually compiles a statistical report based upon the data gathered in reports of maternal death. The statistical report is to be available for public inspection and copying. This is the department's annual statistical report. The following tables are based on the data submitted in reports required by the Abortion Control Act. Another source of information on maternal deaths is death certificates, also filed with the department.
