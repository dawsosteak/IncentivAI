#  Public Safety Power Shutoff 
![Overhead-Lines-Header](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=68133)
A Public Safety Power Shutoff (PSPS) is a **temporary, precautionary power shutoff** used when strong winds, low humidity, or similar conditions create a **high****risk of wildfire ignition**.
We understand the disruption a power outage can cause. We are committed to helping customers prepare and stay informed to minimize the impact.
Anaheim Public Utilities (APU) collaborates with other city departments—including Anaheim Fire & Rescue, Anaheim Police Department, Public Works Department, and City Public Information Office—for the benefit of residents in fire threat zones.
## MONITORING CONDITIONS
Anaheim Public Utilities monitors weather and fire conditions using tools outlined in its Wildfire Mitigation Plan, including monitoring for Red Flag Warning conditions, tracking elevated temperatures, and considering wind risk indicators such as the Santa Ana Wildfire Threat Index. 
When conditions show elevated wildfire risk, APU may take additional precautionary steps, such as increased field monitoring or system setting adjustments to lower the risk of ignition.
Under extreme conditions, APU may temporarily shut off specific line segments as a last resort to further reduce the risk of wildfire ignition.
## CUSTOMER NOTIFICATIONS
Customer notifications will be initiated if PSPS conditions are forecasted and/or PSPS events are implemented. 
Residents are highly encouraged to follow these steps:
  1. Sign up for [Anaheim Alert](https://www.anaheim.net/5387/Anaheim-Alert) and select their preferred methods of notifications (phone, text, and email).
  2. Follow the City of Anaheim on social media for safety updates from multiple departments.
  3. During any outage situation affecting your property, check on APU’s [Outage Map](https://www.anaheim.net/3146/Outage-Center) for estimated restoration updates.
  4. Call 714-765-3300. Please note that operators may experience a high call volume during emergency events.


Because fire-weather conditions can change, PSPS timing varies. Anaheim's first responders and APU will remain in close contact and provide updates as PSPS conditions continue. 
STAGES OF A PUBLIC SAFETY POWER SHUTOFF   
---  
1. **_Fire-weather monitoring_** to track and assess conditions.  
2. _**Customer outreach/notifications**_ for potentially affected customers.   
3. _**Temporary shutoff**_ of specific line segments when conditions meet elevated risk thresholds.   
4. **_Field patrols/inspections_** of overhead infrastructure before re-energizing.   
5.**_Restoration_** once equipment is confirmed safe and hazards have been cleared.   
## INSPECTIONS AND RESTORATION
Before restoring power, APU personnel patrol overhead lines and equipment to confirm the system is safe and PSPS conditions are no longer in effect. This includes checking for damaged equipment and vegetation clearance. Power is restored once hazards have cleared and equipment is confirmed safe.
  1. [How can I find out if I live in a high fire risk area? ](https://www.anaheim.net/6748/Public-Safety-Power-Shutoff#tabcf9d05aa-caba-4ae9-bb1c-a5cffa5032e6_0)


State law requires Cal Fire to classify land into Fire Hazard Severity Zones. The three zones are: moderate, high, and very high.
Discover what zone you are in online: [Fire Hazard Severity Zones](https://www.anaheim.net/6660/Fire-Hazard-Severity-Zones)
  1. [The wind is gone. why is the power not on?](https://www.anaheim.net/6748/Public-Safety-Power-Shutoff#tabcf9d05aa-caba-4ae9-bb1c-a5cffa5032e6_1)


After a Public Safety Power Shutoff (PSPS) or other outage during elevated fire-weather conditions, APU **patrols affected circuits before restoring power to confirm the system is safe to re-energize** , which can delay restoration.
This includes verifying that lines and structures are intact and vegetation is clear of conductors.
  1. [Why do other neighborhoods have power and I don’t? ](https://www.anaheim.net/6748/Public-Safety-Power-Shutoff#tabcf9d05aa-caba-4ae9-bb1c-a5cffa5032e6_2)


You may notice the power is on for other neighborhoods. During a PSPS, power is temporarily turned off in areas with high wildfire threats due to climate conditions. 
De-energization is targeted to specific overhead segments, meaning impacts can be very localized.
Once the conditions are no longer a public safety risk and APU has confirmed the lines can be re-energized, power will be restored.

## REDUCING WILDFIRE RISK FROM POWER LINES
  
Anaheim Public Utilities works year-round to reduce wildfire risk from power lines and equipment, especially in areas with elevated fire conditions, by strengthening infrastructure, improving situational awareness, and adjusting operations when needed.
To reduce the risk of wildfire ignition and PSPS, we continue to strengthen our wildfire mitigation efforts. This includes:
  * **Upgrading overhead equipment** to improve reliability and reduce ignition risk.
  * Placing select facilities and power lines **underground** in or near higher fire-risk areas.
  * During elevated fire conditions, increasing **system patrols** and field monitoring in targeted areas.
  * Adjusting operations on specific circuits and **disabling automatic reclosing** where appropriate to reduce ignition risk.
  * **Patrol/inspection practices** designed to identify hazards and support safe restoration before re-energizing.


In Fire Threat Zones, APU is undergrounding targeted overhead line segments where it provides the greatest risk reduction. To see the status and estimated completion dates of current projects, visit our page on [Undergrounding within Wildfire Threat Zones](https://www.anaheim.net/5915/Wildfire-Threat-Zones).
Learn how this work supports our broader wildfire safety strategy in our [Wildfire Mitigation Plan](https://www.anaheim.net/5487/Wildfire-Mitigation-Plan). The plan describes operational**decision-making tied to elevated weather and fire conditions.**

## PREPARING FOR WILDFIRE  
  

Wildfires can happen quickly, and preparing ahead of time helps **protect your home and family**. 
Anaheim Fire and Rescue offers tools to help you assess wildfire risk, create defensible space around your home, and plan for emergencies. 
Explore these resources and more at: [Preparing for Wildfire](https://www.anaheim.net/5629/Preparing-for-Wildfire).
Properties in or near fire threat zones can further reduce risk by using **rebates for fire-resistant upgrades** to [Be Fire Safe](https://www.anaheim.net/5418/Fire-Safety).

## CUSTOMERS WITH HEALTH-RELATED POWER NEEDS   
  

If someone in your home uses qualifying life-support medical equipment, Anaheim Public Utilities offers a [**Medical Lifeline Allowance**](https://www.anaheim.net/6524/Bill-Assistance) program that can help reduce the cost of electricity for eligible customers. 
The Medical Lifeline application also advises customers to consider providing and maintaining a backup power system for essential medical needs.
## BACKUP BATTERY SOLUTIONS
