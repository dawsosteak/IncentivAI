### Frequently Asked Questions list
When is a building permit needed and where can I get one?
Generally, a building permit is needed for work involving structural elements of a building. Permits are not required for certain construction work. Historic or conservation districts have special requirements for work on the exterior of properties. Permits can be obtained through _[DallasNow](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/sustainabledevelopment/Pages/DallasNow.aspx)._   

Can I get a permit at a Building Inspection District Office?
No, permits must be obtained through _[DallasNow](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/sustainabledevelopment/Pages/DallasNow.aspx)_.

What are the Fees for Fire Alarm Plans Review?
Fees are based on the number of initiating and/or signaling devices. The fees are published on the Fire Alarm Plan Review Request form [(MS Word)](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/sustainabledevelopment/buildinginspection/DCH%20documents/word/FireAlarmPlanReviewRequest.doc)(Adobe PDF) and range from $75 to $300. A Fire Alarm Permit Fee will be charged per Section 303.2.2 and associated Table B of Chapter 52 of the Dallas City Code. Please note that the permit fee is based on a percentage of the total value of work plus an additional standard fee. It is approximately equivalent to a $1,000 permit fee for every $100,000 of value of work.
[Fee Guide](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/sustainabledevelopment/buildinginspection/DCH%20documents/DSDFees%20\(1\).pdf) _( New! Effective July 1, 2025_
How do I register with the City as a Fire Sprinkler Contractor?
This can be done through [DallasNow](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/sustainabledevelopment/Pages/DallasNow.aspx). Please note: If this is your first time registering you will need to come in office for verfication.   

What are the fees for fire sprinkler permits?
Fire sprinkler permit fees vary based on the type of review, number of devices, and building size. Fees start at $75 and include plan reviews, resubmittals, and site plan evaluations. A full breakdown is available [here](https://dallascityhall.com/departments/sustainabledevelopment/buildinginspection/DCH%20documents/DSDFees%20\(1\).pdf).
