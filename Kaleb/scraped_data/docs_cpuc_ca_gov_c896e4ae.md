This Email Ruling grants the motion for party status filed by NLine Energy, Inc. (NLine) in Rulemaking (R.) 14-10-003.
* * *
On August 14, 2015, an Email Ruling in R.14-10-003 was issued denying, without prejudice, a motion for party status filed by NLine. The Email Ruling denied party status because the motion was noncompliant with Commission Rules of Practice and Procedure, Rule 1.4(b), which states that a person seeking party status by motion shall fully disclose the persons or entities in whose behalf the filing is made, the interest of such entities, and the factual and legal contentions that the entity intends to make. NLine failed to state the factual and legal contentions it intends to make in this proceeding.
* * *
On August 21, 2015, NLine filed a new motion for party status in R.14-10-003. In its motion, NLine states that it is a full-service small hydroelectric power development company which develops renewable distributed generation projects on the customer side of the meter. NLine contends that it plans to convey to the Commission its experiences working directly with customers to develop specialized projects to meet customer needs. In compliance with Rule 1.4(b), NLine states that it will present information regarding how to create effective relationships between the customer, the distributed generation provider, and the utility. Furthermore, NLine states it will also provide information on the development of appropriate tariffs and contracts as this will be paramount to increasing distributed energy resources.
* * *
The August 21, 2015 motion for party status filed in R.14-10-003 by NLine is in compliance with Rule 1.4(b). NLine disclosed its interests and explained what discussions it will bring to this proceeding. Therefore, it is reasonable to grant party status to NLine. NLine is hereby granted party status in R.14-10-003.
* * *
The Docket Office shall formally file this Email Ruling.
