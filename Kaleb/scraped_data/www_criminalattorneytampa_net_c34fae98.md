# Transmission of Child Pornography by Electronic Device in Tampa, FL
Facing charges of transmission of child pornography by electronic device is one of the most serious and life-altering legal situations a person can encounter. In Tampa, Florida, these cases are prosecuted aggressively under both federal and state law. They often involve digital evidence, undercover investigations, and mandatory prison sentences.
At [Hanlon Law](https://www.criminalattorneytampa.net/), we know how overwhelming these allegations can be and are committed to providing skilled, strategic criminal defense to protect your rights and your future. If you have been charged, it is vital to promptly retain a Tampa lawyer for transmission of child pornography by electronic device.
### Florida and Federal Laws on Electronic Transmission
Under [Florida Statute § 847.0137](https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2018/847.0137), it is a felony to electronically transmit child pornography using any device capable of internet access, including computers and smartphones. This law defines transmission broadly, including sharing, sending, posting, or uploading material that depicts a minor engaged in sexual conduct. On the federal level, [18 U.S.C. § 2252](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2252) governs similar conduct, especially if it involves interstate communications or file transfers. Federal charges often result in mandatory minimum sentences ranging from 5 to 15 years, depending on aggravating factors such as prior offenses or the age of the child.
Digital forensics plays a central role in building the government’s case. Prosecutors often rely on:
  * IP addresses
  * Device metadata and file timestamps
  * Chat logs and screen captures
  * Cloud storage and app data


While such evidence can appear definitive, it's not always reliable or accurate. Devices can be shared, hacked, or infected with malware that downloads content automatically. At Hanlon Law, we collaborate with forensic experts to analyze the evidence independently and expose weaknesses in the prosecution’s case.
### Tampa, Florida Sting Operations and Entrapment Defenses
Many cases involving electronic transmission originate from sting operations, where law enforcement officers pose as minors or other individuals in online environments. While these tactics are legal in many instances, they can lead to entrapment. Entrapment is an unlawful situation where a person is coerced or induced into committing a crime they otherwise would not have committed. If entrapment is suspected, Hanlon Law will aggressively challenge the legality of the sting and the conduct of the investigators involved.
A conviction for transmitting child pornography by electronic device can result in:
  * Lengthy prison sentences (state and federal)
  * Mandatory sex offender registration for life
  * Loss of employment and professional licenses
  * Social and reputational ruin
  * Restrictions on housing, travel, and internet use


Even an arrest without conviction can cause irreversible damage to your personal and professional life. These charges carry immense stigma, and the legal process can be harsh and unforgiving.
### Importance of Hiring a Skilled Tampa Sex Crimes Lawyer
At Hanlon Law, we understand that being accused does not equal being guilty. We take the time to thoroughly assess your case, listen to your version of events, and develop a defense strategy tailored to your situation. Common defenses may include:
  * Lack of intent or knowledge
  * Improper search and seizure
  * Technical errors or misidentification
  * Entrapment by law enforcement
