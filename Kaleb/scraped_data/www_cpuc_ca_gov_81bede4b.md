Notice of Meeting This is a notice of a public meeting of the Disadvantaged Communities Advisory Group (DACAG) to the California Energy Commission (CEC) and the California Public Utilities Commission (CPUC). The DACAG aims to begin the meeting promptly at the start time, and the end time is an estimate based on the agenda proposed. The meeting may end sooner or later than the posted end time depending on various factors.
* * *
The meeting will be held via Teleconference & Remote Access, with a primary physical meeting location as noted above. Any of the members of the DACAG may participate remotely. The public can participate remotely as instructed below or physically attend at the primary physical meeting location. The meeting may be recorded.
* * *
Agenda Any of the agenda items, other than welcome, public comments, member announcements, and future meeting planning, may include a motion for specific action related to the topic and vote of the DACAG.
* * *
1) Welcome and roll call of the members. 2) Public Comment. General public comment regarding items on or off the agenda related to the work done by the DACAG. 3) Opening remarks by CEC and CPUC leadership. 4) Meeting Minutes. Possible approval of February 20, 2026, DACAG meeting minutes. 5) Energy Disconnections and Reconnections (Disconnections) Rulemaking (R.18-07-005). As it relates to the Affordability and Disconnections DACAG priority area CPUC staff will briefly recap the presentation from the February 20, 2026, meeting and seek feedback from the DACAG including whether and how to adjust disconnection caps and recommendations related to pilot programs on Arrearage Management Plans (AMPs), Percentage of Income Payment Plans (PIPPs), and the Arrears Case Management Pilot Program currently scheduled to sunset in 2026. Staff will also seek input on ways to minimize impacts to vulnerable
* * *
2
* * *
customers given high statewide arrearages if pilots were allowed to sunset as scheduled and disconnection caps were modified, such as coordination between Investor-Owned Utilities (IOUs) and other entities to support customers at risk of disconnection. 6) Gas Research, Development, and Demonstration (Gas RD&D;) Program. As it relates to the DACAG priority areas of Building, Industrial, Gas System Decarbonization, and Clean Renewable Hydrogen; Long-term Planning for Natural Gas; and the Electric Program Investment Charge Program (EPIC) (related to the coordinated role of gas), the Gas RD&D; administrators of the CEC, Pacific Gas & Electric (PG&E;), and Southern California Gas Company (SoCalGas) will present and seek feedback from the DACAG on coordinated Gas RD&D; investment proposals to be funded by ratepayers. DACAG input will inform investment concepts amounting to approximately $50 million across their respective proposed Annual Gas RD&D; Plans to be submitted to the CPUC by June 1, 2026. 7) Break. The DACAG will break for 5 minutes. 8) Senate Bill (SB) 100 Non-Energy Impacts. As it relates to the Long-Term Planning DACAG priority area, DACAG members may consider and vote to approve a proposed comment letter on modeling under SB 100, including non-energy impacts. SB 100 sets the goal of supplying all retail electricity sold in California with renewable and zero-carbon resources by 2045. 9) Priority Areas. DACAG members to provide updates and announcements regarding DACAG priority areas and associated activities. Members may discuss and revise items related to the 2025 priority areas, and/or may vote to approve 2026 priority areas, including the identification of subject matter experts. a. Affordability and Disconnections. b. Building, Industrial, Gas System Decarbonization, and Clean Renewable Hydrogen. c. Electric Program Investment Charge Program (EPIC). d. Electricity Reliability, Resiliency, and Load Flexibility (Including Distribution and Transmission Grid Planning and Distributed Energy Resources). e. Long-term Planning (SB 100, SB 350, IEPR, Offshore Wind, Lithium Valley Vision, Long-term Natural Gas, Smart Grids and Broadband). f. Transportation Electrification and Zero Emission Transportation. g. Tribal and Community Engagement and Participation. h. Workforce Training and Development.
* * *
10) Low-Income Oversight Board (LIOB). DACAG member(s) to provide any update(s) regarding engagement with the LIOB. 11) Agency Announcements. CEC and CPUC staff to provide any agency announcements. 12) Agenda-Setting and Attendance. DACAG member(s) may propose and discuss any agenda items, dates, location, and attendance for future DACAG meetings. 13) Public Comment. General public comment regarding items on or off the agenda related to the work done by the DACAG. 14) Adjourn.

Pursuant to Senate Bill (SB) 350, Clean Energy and Pollution Reduction Act of 2015 (De León, Chapter 547, Statutes of 2015), the CEC and the CPUC have established the DACAG. The charter and appointment information can be found on the CEC website at https://www.energy.ca.gov/sb350/DCAG. The DACAG advises the two Commissions on the effectiveness of programs related to SB 350 in disadvantaged communities.
