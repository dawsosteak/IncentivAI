Opened in 1939, La Palma Park is Anaheim’s second-oldest park. As one of the largest green spaces in northern Anaheim, it has been heavily used and is in need of upgrades. 
A Master Plan was created with the community, and we are now actively seeking funding to revitalize and fully transform the west-end (approx. 10-acres) of La Palma Park to create a nature-based destination where families, youth, and senior groups can gather for recreation, exercise, celebrations, and to enjoy and experience nature within an urban environment.
Project elements include shaded and accessible picnic areas, a new nature-themed playground, exercise stations and walking paths, an open-air sports arena (for futsal, roller-hockey, etc.), a multi-purpose open space, native trees and native drought-tolerant landscaping, security lighting, restroom/storage facility, and other support amenities including benches, trash receptacles, drinking fountains, and bike racks.

## Nohl Ranch Trail
![Nohl Ranch Trail](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentId=56656)
The Nohl Ranch Trail is being designed to be a Class 1 Bicycle trail with an adjacent soft equestrian, hiking, and multiuse trail. This is a joint planning process with the Public Works Department. Public Works staff is working to adjust design for the north/south trail alignment to the east of Canyon High School and identify additional funding sources. This project may need to be installed in phases to accommodate funding sources. When complete, this trail will provide safe routes to schools as well as opportunities for residents to get to local amenities without using a car.

## OC River Walk 
  1. ![Riverwalk 4](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=63661)
  2. ![Riverwalk 2](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=63660)
  3. ![Riverwalk 3](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=63662)


  1. [ 1 Riverwalk 4 ](https://www.anaheim.net/2769/Park-Capital-Improvements#targetImage)
  2. [ 2 Riverwalk 2 ](https://www.anaheim.net/2769/Park-Capital-Improvements#targetImage)
  3. [ 3 Riverwalk 3 ](https://www.anaheim.net/2769/Park-Capital-Improvements#targetImage)


  

OC River Walk is a regional and iconic project that will transform a 2-mile stretch of the Santa Ana River trail, between Orangewood Avenue and the Anaheim Coves. Improvements will provide new recreational amenities, improved connectivity, and environmental benefits.
In 2021, the City completed a feasibility study which identified unique opportunities that would create an OC River Walk. Many of these opportunities will create increased community amenities such as parks, plazas, and water recreation; provide art and beautify the area; improve habitat; enhance and add access to the river; and infiltrate water to recharge our aquifer. Included in our plans for OC River Walk is a bridge for pedestrians and bicyclists that will connect Anaheim and Orange.
The Master Plan is nearly complete, and the city is working on Construction Documents and Environmental Studies for these enhancements.

## Center Greens Park 
  1. ![Center Greens](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=63743)
  2. ![Center Greens 3](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=63741)
  3. ![Center Greens 2](https://www.anaheim.net/2769/Park-Capital-Improvements)
  4. ![Center Greens 4](https://www.anaheim.net/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=63742)


  1. [ 1 Center Greens ](https://www.anaheim.net/2769/Park-Capital-Improvements#targetImage)
  2. [ 2 Center Greens 3 ](https://www.anaheim.net/2769/Park-Capital-Improvements#targetImage)
  3. [ 3 Center Greens 2 ](https://www.anaheim.net/2769/Park-Capital-Improvements#targetImage)
  4. [ 4 Center Greens 4 ](https://www.anaheim.net/2769/Park-Capital-Improvements#targetImage)


  

Center Greens Park debuted in downtown Anaheim in September 2024, transforming a grassy, informal space into an iconic park with a new skate park, walking paths, butterfly garden and more for the community to enjoy.
The 3.5-acre park has something for people of all ages and abilities to enjoy.
The $6.5 million in park improvements include a new 8,000-square-foot skate park replacing a former ramp on the site, a youth challenge course, tot lot, upgraded basketball courts, butterfly garden, landscaping, fitness trail and a shaded plaza. 
All of the new and upgraded features build on the first phase of enhancing Center Greens, which was a 4,300-square-foot playground built in 2022, with an assist from the Anaheim Ducks and Kaboom!
The playground, designed by kids and families in the community, includes a climbing wall, a bridge, monkey bars, a circular spider web climber, spinners, bucket swings, a companion swing, a zip line, teeter-totter game and musical panels.
For those who live, work in or visit downtown Anaheim, Center Greens will be another attraction along with the Anaheim Packing House, Center Street Promenade and the Muzeo Museum and Cultural Center.
The park improvements promote play, health and wellness of everyone in our community.

## **How We Fund Improvements**
The Capital Improvement section within the Community Services Department plays a crucial role in shaping Anaheim's parks, trails, and community facilities. We acquire land, create master plans, and manage the design and construction of new parks and trails, as well as the renovation and expansion of existing ones. Our work also includes the development of vital community buildings like gymnasiums and community centers.
Funding for these capital improvements comes primarily from fees paid by private developers constructing housing projects within Anaheim. Alternatively, developers may choose to dedicate and develop land specifically for parks and recreational facilities, contributing directly to the community's green spaces. We also actively pursue and secure various federal, state, and local grants to further support these important projects.
