## Instructor Code of Conduct
Instructors should communicate directly with the course location host and/or the training session manager to ensure that all audio-visual equipment and room set up is in place prior to the training. 1. Accommodate students that need tests read to them or when possible give them extra time to complete exams. If a student cannot complete an exam in the allotted time, PEMA staff will make arrangements with that student to allow them to test at another time. 2. Instructors must be prepared to potentially use their own laptop computer when instructing at various locations. 3. Refrain from public criticism of students, other instructors, FEMA, PEMA, or any other governmental entity and their staffs or policies. 4. Be considerate of students and treat them with respect and dignity, regardless of gender, place or origin, race, physical or mental disabilities, sexual orientation, religion, political beliefs, or economic status. 5. Be prepared for each course. 6. Start and end c ourses and all breaks at scheduled times. 7. Follow all policies and procedures established by PEMA for the conduct of all courses and sessions. 8. Be available during the course hours for student questions and if you cannot answer a question, refer students to appropriate PEMA staff or the STO. 9. Respond as promptly as possible to student requests, questions, or comments that are relevant to the course. 10. Never penalize a student for raising a legitimate complaint about the course, as long as that complaint is raised in an appropriate and civil manner. Advise the student to write the complaint on the course evaluation form so PEMA staff can take appropriate action. 11. Ensure each student completes all course related activities / exercises and participates fully in each course; failure to abide by this expectation may result in course failure. 12. Refrain from using profane, insulting, harassing, or otherwise offensive language. 13. Refrain from using multi- media, audio or video demonstration that contains inappropriate language or content. 14. Instructors are expected to dress in business casual attire (i.e. slacks, skirts and collared shirts). 15. Under no conditions are t -shirts, hats, jeans, shorts, tank tops, flip- flops or tennis shoes to be worn indoors while instructing. Instructor Application Instructions
* * *
Thank you for expressing an interest in becoming a Pennsylvania Emergency Management Agency (PEMA) Instructor.
* * *
Carefully follow all instructions and complete the application in its entirety. All requirements must be met prior to submitting the application. Application Process
* * *
1. All applications to become a PEMA Instructor will be made via the PEMA Instructor Application form. 2. All applications must be complete with required documentation attached or the application will be returned for correction. 3. Applications will be accepted at any time and must be submitted to the following PEMA Training and Exercise Division resource account: RA-EMTraining@pa.gov.
* * *
Page 1
* * *
Applicant Information:
* * *
• Provide your full name and FEMA Student Identification Number. • Provide your date of birth, age, and U.S. Citizen status. • Provide your home address and the county you reside in. • Provide your business address (if applicable). • Indicate your preferred mailing address. • Provide your home/work phone numbers and your email address. • Indicate if you require a reasonable accommodation to perform essential instructor functions and provide a brief description of the accommodation.
* * *
2 Page 2 Applicable Course(s):
* * *
• Indicate the course(s) you wish to be approved to instruct. General Requirements:
* * *
• Indicate total years of emergency management experience. • Indicate if you have any convictions of a Crime – Misdemeanor 2 or higher. • List one (1) reference and provide their current position, address, and home or mobile telephone. Education:
* * *
• Indicate if you are a high school graduate or have received a General Education Diploma (GED) or greater. List the name of your High School and graduation year. List post-secondary schools attended, the curriculum studied, and degree awarded, if applicable. Page 3
* * *
Employment History:
* * *
• List your current and previous employer to include your supervisor. Indicate your job title, the dates of employment, and a brief description of your position responsibilities. Page 4 Emergency Management Experience:
* * *
• List all emergency service and/or emergency management organizations of which you are currently active.
* * *
• List previous emergency service and/or emergency management positions held. Include the term years held (i.e. 2020-2023).
* * *
• Indicate if you have been expelled or suspended by an emergency service/ emergency management organization. If yes, list the reason for and the term of expulsion or suspension.
* * *
• If applicable, indicate if you were an emergency management instructor prior to this instructor application. If yes, list where you were an instructor and your reasons for leaving (if applicable).

Documentation and Resume:
* * *
• All documentation should verify the information provided in your application. The documentation to be included with the instructor application, listed on page 6, is the minimum required.
* * *
• Resumes provided should document Emergency Management and/or Emergency Service experience and training as it relates to the course(s) you wish to instruct. If you are seeking instructorship in a specialized area, be sure to include copies of certificates or relevant documentation for those specialty areas. Refrain from providing a comprehensive list of all trainings completed over a career period; instead concentrate on providing documentation specific to the subject matter of the courses you wish to instruct.
* * *
Acceptable Educational Methodology Training or Equivalent Requirement:
* * *
• NDEMU E/L/K 0141 Instructional Presentation and Evaluation Skills • Center For Domestic Preparedness ITC PER-266 Instructor Training Course • TEEX LET565 Basic Instructor Development • PA State Fire Academy Course Educational Methodology for Local Level Instructors (EMLL). • PA Department of Conservation and Natural Resources Instructor Trainer Course (30 hours – Sir Stanford Fleming College). • National Fire Academy Resident Course R-114 Fire Service Course Design or its predecessor Fire Service Instructional Methodology. • A formal post-secondary collegiate-level course in adult education methodology awarding at least 1 college credit. • A formal course of instruction in adult educational methodology and instructing adults of at least 30 hours duration conducted by another state’s fire training system, the military services, state/federal agency, or other organization (i.e. PSP Instructor Development, DOC TST, MOPTEC-General Instructor, WMD Instructor Training- COBRA) OR • Certification course certificate (not challenge examination or virtual delivery) as a Fire Instructor I by an entity accredited by the National Board on Fire Service Professional Qualifications (NBFSPQ) or the International Fire Service Accreditation Congress (IFSAC). OR • Certification as public-school teacher by the Pennsylvania Department of Education; secondary or post-secondary certification preferred.
