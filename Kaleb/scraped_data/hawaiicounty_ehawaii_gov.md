##  Online Reservation System
![](https://hawaiicounty.ehawaii.gov/camping/images/icon-tent.gif)
Welcome and aloha! This online service is designed to allow individuals to reserve and purchase camping and cabin permits for Hawaii County Department of Parks and Recreation.
**Note:** If you are experiencing difficulty viewing Google maps or location names in the dropdown menus, you will need to select SHOW THE NON-SECURE ITEMS in your browsers pop-up message box. If you continue to experience issues, you can use [FireFox browser (click here to download)](http://www.mozilla.com/en-US/firefox/ie.html).
Please note that you must PURCHASE and be able to produce a copy of your permit either in printed or electronic form, while camping or lodging within any park. It is not possible to purchase a permit at County parks. ALL PERMITS REQUIRE A FEE - THERE IS NO FREE CAMPING IN HAWAII COUNTY PARKS.
Camping Fees
Adult (18 years old and over) = $6.00* per adult per night. (Non-Residents $21.00* per adult per night)
Junior (13 to 17 years old) = $3.00* per junior per night. (Non-Residents $21.00* per junior per night)
Children (12 years and under) = *$1.00 per child per night. (Non-Residents $20.00 per child per night)  
  

*Ia $1.00 per adult and $1.00 per junior per day online service provider processing fee is included. The online service provider processing fee or any order handling fee is non-refundable, including natural disaster events, emergency park closures, etc. Additional refund policies may apply. **A valid State of Hawaii ID or Hawaii Driver's License is required for resident rates.**
The system can be used to reserve resources from the following: 
![Big Island Camping Permits](https://hawaiicounty.ehawaii.gov/camping/resources/agency,logo,HAW) |  Hawaii County parks are made up of 10 beach parks located on the pristine beaches of the island of Hawaii. These campgrounds offer camping sites and access to amenities such as restrooms, showers, and picnic tables. (Please see individual park information for a detailed amenity list.)

# Aloha!
Welcome to the Driver License Road Test Scheduler for the County of Hawai’i. Avoid phone calls and waiting in line by using this site to:
  * See road test openings by location and date.
  * Schedule and pay for a road test appointment.
  * Reschedule your road test at no additional cost.
  * Need more information? [View our help page.](https://hawaiicounty.ehawaii.gov/roadtest/wf/page/help.html)

[Schedule an Appointment](https://hawaiicounty.ehawaii.gov/roadtest/wf/form/app1.html) [Look up an Appointment](https://hawaiicounty.ehawaii.gov/roadtest/wf/index.html#!/search)
  
Masks are optional for the road test but highly recommended. If you are feeling sick, or have taken a COVID-19 test with a positive result, **do not** attempt to come to the Vehicle Registration & Licensing Office. We ask that you please re-schedule your road test appointment.   
  
Applicants under 18 years of age are required to provide Driver Education (DE -Driver Education and BTW-Behind the Wheel) certificates at time of appointment. We are unable to administer the in-vehicle skill test if you do not provide your driver education certificates at the time of check in. You will need to reschedule your appointment.   
  
A licensed driver, 21 years or older must accompany you and present a valid driver's license. Your licensed driver is required to stay at our office until you have completed your test. Your license driver must have a clear driving record on the National Drivers Registry and State of Hawai'i. If your licensed driver's license status is suspended in this State or any other State, your in-vehicle road test will be cancelled.   
  
You must check in **30 minutes** prior to your appointment time. You must provide the vehicle registration, safety check certificate and Hawai'i insurance card. The name on your vehicle registration must match the name on your insurance card. Check in at office location indicated on your confirmation slip.   
  
Upon successful completion of the in-vehicle road test, a temporary license will be issued. Additional fees will apply.   
  
The Hilo Driver's License office is located at 349 Kapiolani Street.   
  
The Kona Driver's License office is located at the West Hawai'i Civic Center at 74-5044 Ane Keohokalole Highway.   
  
**All road test appointment fees are non-refundable.**
Your vehicle must meet the following safety standards:
  * Brake lights, turn signals must work-no cracks or broken lenses
  * Speedometer, horn, and seat belts must work
  * No cracks in the windshield or stickers obstructing view
  * Front and rear license plates must be clean, affixed to the vehicle in factory mounts, no covers
  * Power windows and locks, door handles must work
  * Legal tinted windows
  * No warning lights or warning indicators
  * Rearview mirror must be free of any hanging material
  * Vehicle must be clean-no trash, odors, pet hairs, parasites
  * All lug nuts in place with acceptable tire tread
  * Convertible or Jeep type vehicles: canopy top must be engaged in the closed and locked position covering the passenger compartment


A rental or company car cannot be used. The vehicle must be clean (interior and exterior).
On the day of your road test, you must present the following documents:
  * Valid Hawai’i unexpired instruction permit
  * Driver with a valid license at least 21 years old
  * Current, original unexpired vehicle registration (decal must be on license plate)
  * Current, unexpired safety inspection form (decal must be on vehicle bumper)
  * Valid, unexpired Hawaiʻi State Insurance Card
  * Minors: Driver’s Ed (yellow) and Behind the Wheel (blue) certificates


Name on registration must match the name on insurance card. Photocopies, faxes, internet printouts, policies are not accepted.
Road test appointments are available at the Hilo, Kona, Waimea, Pahoa and Ka'ū offices.
