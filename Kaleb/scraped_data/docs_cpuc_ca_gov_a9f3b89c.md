Sierra Club has requested party status in Rulemaking (R.) 14-10-003. As described below, this Email Ruling finds the request reasonable and grants party status to Sierra Club.
* * *
R.14-10-003 KHY/vm2
* * *
- 3 On February 19, 2015, Sierra Club filed a motion for party status (Motion) in R.14-10-003. In its Motion, Sierra Club states that it is a non-profit environmental organization with approximately 146,000 members that live and purchase utility services in California. Sierra Club contends that its highest priority is eliminating the need for fossil fuel-fired power plants through the development of affordable renewable energy and zero carbon integration efforts. Sierra Club asserts that it has a significant interest in the Commission’s development of an integrated framework for demand side management policies. Additionally, Sierra Club highlights that it currently is engaged in several related proceedings regarding demand response, energy storage, and long term procurement planning.
* * *
As an advocate for the development of renewable energy, Sierra Club’s participation in R.14-10-003 is constructive. Similarly, its participation in related proceedings will also inform the record in the proceeding. As such, it is reasonable to grant Sierra Club party status in R.14-10-003.
* * *
The Docket Office shall formally file this Email Ruling.
