# Energy Savings for
#  Eagle County Residents  

##  We’re here to help Eagle County residents lower energy use at every stage.  

Whether you're planning energy-efficient upgrades or need assistance with the costs of home improvements, we’ve got the support you need.
[ Apply for a Rebate ](https://energysmartcolorado.formstack.com/forms/residential_rebate_application)
![CORE energy expert speaking with a local homeowner in Eagle County about building efficiency improvements and rebate opportunities.](https://lirp.cdn-website.com/06c12080/dms3rep/multi/opt/DSC00295--281-29-1920w.JPG)
Eagle County communities are committed to **reducing emissions by 25% by 2025, 50% by 2030, and 80% by 2050** , compared to 2014 levels. Reaching these goals will require the participation of every resident. Ready to make a difference? Get started on your electrification projects today.  

###  Eagle County Residential Building Rebates  

This funding breakdown is for Eagle County residents **who do not live within the town of Basalt**. If your property is within Basalt town limits, [visit our general residential rebates page](https://www.aspencore.org/residential-rebates). Rebates are available for existing single-family homes or individual units in a multifamily building. 
####  Residential Energy Efficiency Projects
  * Rebates amount to 50% of the project cost up to $2,500
  * Residents of Eagle County (outside of the town of Basalt)
  * An energy assessment is required, free of charge


[ Energy Efficiency Projects ](https://www.aspencore.org/eagle-rebates#EagleCountyResidentialEEProjects)
#### Residential Fuel Switching Projects
  * Rebates amount to 50% of the project cost up to $7,500
  * Residents of Eagle County (outside of the town of Basalt)
  * An energy assessment is required, free of charge﻿


[ Fuel Switching Projects ](https://www.aspencore.org/eagle-rebates#EagleCountyResidentialFSProjects)
###  Eagle County Commercial and Multifamily Building Rebates  

This funding breakdown is for Eagle County residents **who do not live within the town of Basalt**. If your property is within Basalt town limits, [visit our general commercial and multifamily rebates page](https://www.aspencore.org/commercial-multifamily-funding). Rebates are available for existing buildings unless otherwise noted. Multifamily buildings are defined as complexes with four or more units in the building. Multifamily projects must be in the common area of the building and/or every unit of the building. 
#### Commercial and Multifamily
#### Energy Efficiency Projects
  * Rebates amount to 50% of the project cost up to $5,000
  * Residents of Eagle County (outside of the town of Basalt)

#### Commercial and Multifamily
#### Fuel Switching Projects
  * Rebates amount to 50% of the project cost up to $5,000
  * Residents of Eagle County (outside of the town of Basalt)  



[ Fuel Switching Projects ](https://www.aspencore.org/eagle-rebates#EagleCountyCommercialFSProjects)
#### Not sure where to start?
Not a problem; we're here to help! [Contact our Energy Concierge](https://www.aspencore.org/energy-concierge)at any stage of your building project. Find answers to other frequently asked questions about rebates on our [FAQ webpage](https://www.aspencore.org/faq).
#### Is your building 50,000 square feet or larger?
The State’s [Building Performance Colorado](https://energyoffice.colorado.gov/bpc) program requires large commercial, multifamily, and public buildings to benchmark utility data and select a building performance compliance pathway. [Eagle County](https://www.eaglecounty.us/departments___services/community_development/index.php) is partnering with CORE to provide free, personalized support for select property owners and managers to meet these targets. Custom rebates may be available for your large commercial or multifamily building.
[ Work with CORE ](https://www.aspencore.org/eagle-county-building-performance)
#### Looking for more funding?
You may be eligible for even more funding! Local community members, from teachers and childcare workers to veterans and government staff, may qualify for savings of up to $15,000 on a single residential project, and up to $50,000 for a commercial or multi-family building project. Check out our [Community Priority Participants](https://www.aspencore.org/community-priority-participants) criteria or our [Savings Calculator](https://www.aspencore.org/federal-state-and-local-funding-programs) to see if you qualify for more incentives.
#### After I complete my project, how long do I have to submit my rebate form?
Your application must be submitted within 90 days of completing a project. Invoices must be dated within 90 days of applying for a rebate.
#### Ready to apply for a rebate?
Fill out the residential or commercial and multifamily rebate application and our Energy Concierge team will contact you.

##### Eligible Fuel Switching Projects
### Space Heating, Water Heating, Snowmelt, and Ventilation
  

Replacing a fossil fuel system with:
  * A heat pump for space heating, water heating, or snowmelt
  * A heat-recovery or heat pump ventilation system


Design costs and electrical capacity upgrade costs associated with a completed heat pump project will be included in the project cost used to determine the rebate.
[ FAQs ](https://www.aspencore.org/faq)
### New Construction
  

Rebates can be requested once final construction design documents ("100% CD's") are provided.
  * All-electric individual homes without natural gas lines, which use heat pump technology for space and water heating and heat recovery technology for fresh air ventilation


If the building code prohibits natural gas, then no rebates will be provided.

### Energy Spotlight﻿
#### English in Action - El Jebel
* * *
For nearly 20 years, English In Action has been operating in an aging 1,800-square-foot modular building. On May 1, 2024, thanks to the community's support, including a $40,000 grant from CORE, English In Action broke ground on a new 6,000-square-foot, brick-and-mortar, all-electric English In Action Center for Communications. 
  
They plan to open their doors in 2025 to welcome their growing network of more than 400 learners, 300 volunteers, and 10 staff into a safe, welcoming, energy-efficient community learning and activity center.
