# About CORE's Contractor Directory﻿
CORE assists property owners in Pitkin, Garfield, and Eagle Counties with low to no-cost property assessments to identify efficiency and electrification opportunities. We help owners and residents save energy, reduce costs, and lower greenhouse gas emissions.
  

Our [Contractor Directory](https://www.aspencore.org/contractors) connects qualified local contractors with property owners seeking to implement energy efficiency, electrification, and decarbonization projects.  
  

[ Contractors Apply Here ](https://forms.gle/3TVrNsoXXGzhMGTP8)
**CORE's Service Territory** : Roaring Fork Valley, Garfield County, and Eagle County.
![CORE staff member conducting a home energy assessment in Aspen, demonstrating their hands-on approach to climate solutions.](https://lirp.cdn-website.com/06c12080/dms3rep/multi/opt/Aspen+Art+Museum.1.24.23.457-c7b0c536-1920w.JPG)
###  Contractor Requirements  

  * Provide services to clients in CORE's service territory (Roaring Fork Valley, Garfield County, and/or Eagle County)  
  

  * Meet [CORE's Contractor Requirements](https://irp.cdn-website.com/06c12080/files/uploaded/CORE-s+Contractor+Requirments.pdf)
  * If currently enlisted as a preferred or approved contractor with Holy Cross Energy, Love Electric, Xcel, Collective Clean Energy Fund (CCEF), Colorado Energy Office home energy rebate programs (HEAR/HER), or registered with EPA for Audits, this will satisfy CORE's accreditation requirements.  
  

  * Complete the Request for Information/Qualifications form


  

**Important** : Listing in the Directory is not a guarantee of work. CORE reserves the right to remove companies from the Directory if complaints are received.
### Benefits of Joining CORE's Contractor Directory
**Market Visibility**
Enhanced brand recognition and exposure to property owners actively seeking contractors.
**Project Pipeline**
Access to customers assisted by CORE Project Managers on large retrofit projects.
**Incentive Knowledge**
Understanding of how to leverage CORE incentives for better project feasibility.
**Professional Development**
Access to training, education, and networking opportunities.

CORE Contractor’s Building Performance Hub (BPH) Requirements for Partnership If currently enlisted as a preferred or approved contractor with Holy Cross Energy, Love Electric, Xcel, Collective Clean Energy Fund (CCEF), Colorado Energy Office Home energy rebate program (HEAR or HER), or Registered with EPA for Audits, this will satisfy BPH accreditation requirements. Requirements: ● Provide services for energy efficiency and/or electrification to clients in BPH service territory (Roaring Fork Valley, Garfield County, and/or Eagle County including the Vail Valley) and demonstrated ability to mobilize across territory ● Complete the request for Information/ Qualifications ● Demonstrate responsiveness to clients, with fewer than two documented complaints from CORE customers in the past two years. ● Demonstrates call back corrections for warrantable and workmanship issues for CORE customers. ● Hold all required licenses and registrations in the jurisdictions where they operate. ● Be familiar with COREs standard incentives and communicate to clients what those are. Accreditation qualifications suggested for selection ● Accredited training in energy efficiency respective of trade to demonstrate competency in energy-efficient systems. a. BPI certifications b. Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable EnergyEnergy Skilled Recognition (https://bsesc.energy.gov/recognition) c. Alternate specialized energy efficiency training program certification/recognition for respective trade. (cold climate heat pump training). d. Advocates for electrification and energy efficiency
