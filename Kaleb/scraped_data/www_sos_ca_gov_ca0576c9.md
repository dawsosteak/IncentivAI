# California University and College Engage the Vote Championship
![Students Vote Project logo](https://elections.cdn.sos.ca.gov/images/castudentsvote/2025-StudentVoteProject-logo.png)![Students Vote Project logo](https://elections.cdn.sos.ca.gov/svp/etv-logo.png)
# **Engage the Vote Championship Overview**
The California University and College Engage the Vote Championship is a 10-week tournament where California higher education campuses compete to achieve the highest combined percentage of students who register to vote and submit a plan to vote. The [California Secretary of State Students Vote Project](https://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/castudentsvote) hosts the tournament during even numbered years leading up to the statewide primary election.
## **Who Can Participate?**
  * Eligible campuses include: 
    * 118 California Community Colleges (CCC)
    * 22 California State Universities (CSU)
    * 10 University of California campuses (UC)
    * 86 Independent California Colleges and Universities (ICCU)


## **How to Play**
### Engage the Vote Championship Outreach Toolkit
For a detailed overview of the competition, along with social media graphics, please view the [California University and College Engage the Vote Championship Outreach Toolkit](https://elections.cdn.sos.ca.gov/svp/engage-the-vote-outreach-toolkit.pdf). 
### Tournament Structure
The Engage the Vote Championship begins on **Monday, March 23, 2026 – Tuesday, June 2, 2026**.
  * **Qualifying Period:**_Monday, March 23, 2026 – Monday, May 18, 2026_
    * The first eight weeks of the tournament is known as the Qualifying Period. All California colleges and universities compete to maximize the percentage of students who register to vote and submit a plan to vote.
    * At the end of Qualifying Period, the top four campuses, one from each higher education system, with the highest combined percentage of college student voter registrations and submitted plans to vote are placed into the Playoff bracket.


  * **Playoffs:**_Tuesday, May 19, 2026 – Tuesday, June 2, 2026_
    * The final two weeks of the tournament are known as the Playoffs. Campuses compete for the highest percentage of submitted plans to vote.
    * The Playoffs have two elimination rounds and percentages reset at the start of every round. During each round, two campuses are paired against each other to compete in a single elimination matchup. The campus with the higher percentage of submitted plans to vote at the end of a round, advances to the Championship Round.


  * **Championship Round:** _Tuesday, May 26, 2026 – Tuesday, June 2, 2026_
    * The last week of the Playoffs is the Championship Round.
    * The final two campuses face off in the last round of the tournament. Only one campus will be crowned the winner.

### How to Participate
California colleges and universities can participate in the Engage the Vote Championship by registering students to vote and having students submit a plan to vote. See the two ways below to learn more about how your campus can participate in the competition.
#### 1. **College Student Voter Registration**
  * ##### **Online voter registration**
    * [Register to Vote (College Students)](https://registertovote.ca.gov/collegestudents)
  * ##### **Paper voter registration**
    * Paper voter registration cards (VRCs) can be requested by campuses from the California Secretary of State’s office. Please note on the [Voter Registration Card Statement of Distribution Form](https://elections.cdn.sos.ca.gov/vrdis.pdf) that you are requesting cards for college students and include the campus name.
    * Email completed Voter Registration Card Statement of Distribution Forms to vrc-vnc@sos.ca.gov and caststudentsvote@sos.ca.gov.
    * If you have any questions about requesting paper voter registration cards, please contact vrc-vnc@sos.ca.gov and copy caststudentsvote@sos.ca.gov.


#### 2. College Student Voting Plan Submission
  * ##### **Online College Student Voting Plan Tool**
    * The [College Student Voting Plan](https://collegehub.sos.ca.gov/collegestudentvotingplan) is an online form that can be used to create and submit a plan to vote for the upcoming statewide election. This form should be completed during the dates of the tournament.


## **Track Your Campus’s Progress**

Legal RequirementsImportant The following requirements m ust be complied with by all persons and/or organizations to whom registration cards are issued. Non-compliance may result in referral to the appropriate law enforcement agency. Receipt – A person who assumes responsibility for returning another person’s completed voter registration card (a completed voter registration card is known as an affidavit of registration) to the elections official, must complete the blue box section of the affidavit by signing, dating and listing their name, address and telephone number on the card itself and on the receipt portion of the affidavit. (Elections Code § 2158.) Return of Affidavits – Persons distributing voter registration cards must return all completed affidavits in their possession within three working days, excluding Saturdays, Sundays, and state holidays. Retaining completed affidavits for more than three days, or denying the voter’s right to return his or her completed affidavit is a misdemeanor. All blank cards must be returned to the elections official immediately after the 15th day preceding a statewide primary or general election, and whenever instructed to do so by the elections official. (Election Code §§ 2138, 2139, 18103.) Unsolicited Mailing – Any mailing of a voter registration card to a person who did not request one must be accompanied by a cover letter or other notice with each card, instructing the recipient to disregard the card if he or she is currently registered to vote. (Elections Code § 2158.) Note: Organizations receiving complete d affidavits of registration for delivery to the elections official must ensure that all affidavits completed in whole or in part by prospective registrants are promptly forwarded to the elections official. This includes a ffidavits containing irregularities or minimal information, i.e. a name and address. The elections officials, not the organization distributing or receiving affidavits, will determine the validity of the registration. Revised 06/18/21
