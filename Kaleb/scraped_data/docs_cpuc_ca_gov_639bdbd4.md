This Email Ruling addresses several motions for party status in Rulemaking (R.) 14-10-003. As discussed below, the motions for party status filed by Community Choice Partners (Partners), NRG Energy, Inc. (NRG), and the World Business Academy (Academy) are noncompliant with Commission Rules of Practice and Procedure and, therefore, are denied without prejudice. EnergyHub, the California Energy Efficiency Industry Council (Council), and CPower each filed compliant motions and are, therefore, granted party status in R.14-10-003.
* * *
On August 26, 2015, the Partners filed a motion for party status in R.14-10-003. The Partners stated that they are a corporation dedicated to helping local governments achieve their energy policy goals. The Partners explained that, as a provider of turnkey energy procurement solutions to these local governments, they have an interest in the development of the integrated demand side resources framework.
* * *
On September 1, 2015, NRG filed a motion for party status in R.14-10-003. NRG explained that it is the parent company of several entities that own, market or operate generation resources in California. NRG stated that it is involved in several other Commission proceeding and it intends to file comments and bring into this proceeding its perspectives gained from its experience with deploying distributed energy resources and its vision for animating the deployment of these resources.
* * *
On September 2, 2015, the Academy filed a motion for party status in R.14-10-003. The Academy stated that it is a corporation that investigates various issues associated with energy infrastructure. The Academy explains that its main purpose for intervention in this proceeding is to help the Commission find the optimal path forward to realizing California’s clean energy goals.
* * *
In the Commission Rules of Practice and Procedure, Rule 1.4(b) states that a person seeking party status by motion shall fully disclose the persons or entities in whose behalf the filing is made, the interest of such entities, and the factual and legal contentions that the entity intends to make. While the Partners, NRG

- 5 and the Academy fully explained who they are and their interest in this proceeding, none of them specifically addressed any factual or legal contentions they intend to make over the course of this proceeding. Hence, the motions for party status filed by the Partners, NRG and the Academy in R.14-10-003 are not compliant with Rule 1.4(b) and are denied without prejudice.
* * *
On September 2, 2015, EnergyHub, the Council and CPower each filed motions for party status in R.14-10-003. In its motion, EnergyHub states that it is a longtime leader in enabling rapid development of demand response and energy efficiency programs. EnergyHub explains that it plans to advocate for increased reliance on these programs. In compliance with Rule 1.4(b), EnergyHub asserts that while utility programs may be a source of customer engagement, they may not be the best or only source.
* * *
In its motion, the Council states that it is a statewide trade association of non utility companies that provide energy efficiency and demand response programs. The Council intends to demonstrate that ensuring consumers are interested in using and able to use demand side resources is essential to the success of integrated demand side resources. In compliance with Rule 1.4(b), the Council argues that the Commission should look to new energy measurement technologies, data analytics and measurement software for valuing and targeting distributed energy resources.
* * *
Lastly, CPower states that it is a leading provider of clean energy solutions focusing on reliable and cost-effective alternatives. CPower explains that while it supports the direction of the proceeding, it asserts that more specificity is needed, which it can provide. In compliance with Rule 1.4(b), CPower argues that while utility programs may be a source of customer engagement, they may not be the only source.
* * *
The September 2, 2015 motions for party status filed in R.14-10-003 by EnergyHub, the Council and Cpower are in compliance with Rule 1.4(b). All three entities disclosed their interests and explained what facts or legal contentions they will bring to this proceeding. Therefore, it is reasonable to grant party status to all three. Hence, EnergyHub, the Council and Cpower are hereby granted party status in R.14-10-003.
