## **2026 California Primary**
  * The last day to register to vote for the June 2, 2026, Primary Election is May 18, 2026.
  * All California active registered voters will receive a ballot for the June 2, 2026, Primary Election.
  * County elections officials will begin mailing ballots by May 4, 2026.
  * Secure ballot drop-off locations open on May 5, 2026.
  * Ballots can be dropped off at a drop-off location, county elections office, or returned by mail.
  * The first vote centers open for early in-person voting in all Voter’s Choice Act counties on May 23, 2026.
  * In person early voting locations throughout California will be open on Saturday, May 30, 2026.
  * June 2 is the last day to vote in-person or return a ballot by 8:00 p.m. Polls are open from 7:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m. Vote-by-mail ballots must be postmarked no later than June 2. If mailing on Elections Day, get a hand-stamped postmark from a postal employee inside a United States Post Office.

## What Do I Need to Register to Vote?
**OR**
You must provide: • A California Driver’s License or State ID number, • The last four digits of your Social Security Number (SSN) (if you do not have a CA ID) If you don’t have either, you can still register. You’ll be assigned a unique identifier to validate your registration. A ll numbers are securely verified: • CA ID numbers are matched with DMV records • SSNs are matched with Social Security Administration records • Unique identifiers are verified by election officials Who Can Register to Vote in California? To register, you must be: • A United States citizen and a California resident • 18 years or older on Election Day (or 16 or 17 years old to pre-register) • Not currently serving a state or federal prison term for a felony • Not currently found mentally incompetent to vote by a court I MPORTANT: O n the voter registration form, applicants must check “Yes” to confirm U.S. citizenship. If this is left blank, your registration will not be accepted. Elections Code § 2111 states that an affidavit of registration is proof of citizenship for voting purposes only; it cannot be used to prove citizenship for any other purpose. California Voter ID & Registration Requirements A Quick Guide to Voting Eligibility and Identification Rules For more information, visit vote.ca.gov or call our Voter Hotline at (800) 345-VOTE (8683). Updated 08/2025 Do I Need to Show ID to Vote in California? In most cases, NO. California does not generally require voters to show identification at the polls. When MIGHT You Be Asked for ID? You may need to show ID only if ALL the following apply: • You’re voting for the first time in a federal election • You registered by mail or online, AND • You did not provide your CA ID or SSN when registering You’ll be notified in advance if this applies to you. P oll workers may only request an ID if the voter list clearly indicates it’s required. What Forms of ID Are Acceptable (if required)? You can show a photo or non-photo ID, including: • California Driver’s License or State ID card • U.S. Passport • Student or Military ID • Official mail from a government agency • Paycheck or government check • Utility bill or bank statement For a complete list, see: https://www.sos.ca.gov/ elections/hava-id-standards Photo ID is not required, it’s just one of many valid options. ID does not need to show your address or be government-issued. Don’t Have ID When Voting? No problem, you can still cast a provisional ballot. Your vote will be counted after your eligibility is confirmed.

**Homelessness Fact Sheet**
**Voters Experiencing**
**Homelessness Fact Sheet**
For the June 2, 2026, Primary Election, ballots will begin being mailed to all active registered voters no later than May 4, 2026. In-person voting locations will also be available. Locations and hours will vary by county. For the latest information, visit vote.ca.gov. Here’s what you can do to ensure you can exercise your right to vote if you do not have a current residential address: I’m unhoused. Can I vote? All eligible voters have the right to vote, including a person who is experiencing homelessness. You may register to vote as long as you have a location where you can receive mail and be properly assigned to a voting precinct. Call or visit your county elections office to register to vote. You may register to vote at an address where you spend most of your time, such as a shelter. If you have a driver’s license or state identification, you can easily register at registertovote.ca.gov. Please note that you cannot use a P.O. Box or business address to register to vote, but you can use a P.O. Box or business address to receive mail. If you are unable to use an address when you register to vote, you must describe the location where you live so it is clear enough for your county elections official to find your voting precinct. You may use cross streets or a park as your address. This ensures that you can receive accurate elections materials based on where you live. If you are not registered to vote by May 18, 2026, you have the right to register and vote at any polling place, vote center, or county elections office using a process called same day voter registration.

## Vote by Mail
No stamp required! Every mailed ballot comes with a pre-paid ballot return envelope. Be sure to put it back into the official envelope. Sign and date the envelope and place in the mail, a county ballot drop box, a polling place, or vote center. Find a location near you at vote.ca.gov, or call or visit your local county elections office to see what options are available for you. Ballots returned by mail must be postmarked by Election Day. Ballots returned at a secure ballot drop box or a voting location must be dropped off by 8:00 p.m. on Election Day. Vote in Person Every voter has the option to vote in person. Every county in California will offer early in-person voting in the county elections offices starting no later than May 4, 2026. Other locations, times, and days of early voting vary by county. Find a location near you at vote.ca.gov, or call or visit your local county elections office to see what options are available for you. All voting locations will be open on Election Day from 7:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m. To find the location nearest you, visit vote.ca.gov. County or State Voter Information Guide If you don’t have your county voter information guide, most counties have an online version on their website. Voting locations will also have copies of the county information guide. The Secretary of State’s online voter information guide can be found at vote.ca.gov. Updated 02/2026 For more information, visit vote.ca.gov or call our Voter Hotline at (800) 345-VOTE (8683).
