**Emergency Release Policy**
The following will be considered emergencies warranting a power release prior to inspection:
* * *
• Vehicular damage • Storm damage • Fire damage • Meter damage
* * *
This is not an all-encompassing list and no guarantee of a release without inspection is assumed. Some services are too large or complicated for this allowance, and it will be at the discretion of the inspector.
* * *
A licensed contractor registered with the City of Dallas will be required to make the power release request, and the structure in question will have to be occupied or temporarily vacant due to the outage. The registered electrical contractor will be required to obtain an electrical permit prior to or the next working day of the repair and request the inspection.
* * *
During business hours Monday through Friday 8:00 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.release requests will be sent to the appropriate district office for processing. The same policy as above shall apply.
* * *
Outside of business hours, Monday through Friday from 4:31 P.M. until 10:00 p.m., or Saturday, Sunday and holidays from 8:00 a.m. until 10:00 p.m.releases will be made through the Electrical Chief’s office directly via the electrical contractor contacting the Chief or Assistant Chief at the following:
* * *
Releases requested outside of normal business hours via the Electrical Chiefs office will incur a $50.00 fee assessed to the permit.
* * *
An electrical release that requires an electrical inspector for a same day inspection will be obtained from the appropriate district office with a fee of $250.00. Same day fees shall be paid for each inspection request between 8:00 a.m. and 2:00 p.m. before the inspection is made.
* * *
An electrical release that requires an electrical inspector for an afterhours inspection or weekends has a fee of $125.00 per hour with a minimum charge of $300.00, and shall be paid between 8:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m. before the inspection is made.
* * *
Set-up releases request will be sent to the appropriate field inspector or senior inspector for review and processing.

## National Electrical Code articles and Ch. 56 amendments which bear special attention:
### – Servicing and maintenance of equipment.
110.26 – Requires a 2’W x 6’6”H egress path and egress doors must open at least 90 degrees. 210.8(A)(6) – All receptacles in a kitchen must be GFCI protected. 210.8(B)(7) – Fixed or stationary appliances within 6’ of a sink must have GFCI protection. 210.8(D) – Specific appliance GFCI protection. 210.52(C)(3) – Receptacles may not be installed below a countertop or worksurface. 215.18 – Surge protection is required on feeders that supply dwellings. Ch. 56 No. 13 article 220 – Calculations shall be provided when loads are added. 230.85(C) – Emergency disconnect required when replacing service equipment. 440.14 – HVAC disconnects must meet the requirements of 110.26(A). Ch. 56 No. 20 amending 695.6(A)(1) – The exception for fire pump service conductors is deleted. Ch. 56 No. 21 amending 705.8 – Qualified people for interconnected power supply systems are defined.
* * *
The following items are administrative policies of the City of Dallas’ Building Inspection Division.
* * *
1. Emergency Service Restoration. Service conductors or equipment damaged by accident or fire, or by
* * *
storm conditions such as ice or falling tree limbs, may be restored to the original safe condition without required upgrading to the minimum size or rating specified in Subsection 230.79.
* * *
2. Equipment requiring inspection while energized will require an electrician of the proper classification to be present at time of inspection to open the equipment.
