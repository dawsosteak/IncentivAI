#  Industries and Topics
We regulate utility companies and infrastructure in California. 
## [ Electrical Energy ](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/electrical-energy)
Learn more about electric utility consumer programs, regulatory services, news, and events. 
## [ Internet and Phone ](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/internet-and-phone)
We regulate and enforce policies for consumer protection in the communications industry. Learn more about regulatory services and consumer programs. 
## [ Natural Gas ](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/natural-gas)
Learn more about natural gas utility consumer programs, regulatory services, news, and events. 
## [ Rail Safety ](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/about-cpuc/divisions/rail-safety-division)
We regulate rail safety including freight, inter-city and commuter railroads, rail transit and rail crossings. 
## [ Transportation ](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/transportation)
Learn more about how we enforce safety of passenger carriers like limousines, charter buses, ferries, transportation network companies, such as Uber or Lyft through regulatory services. 
## [ Water ](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/water)
We investigate water system service quality issues, analyze, and process utility rate change requests. 
##  Key Issues and Topics
### [ Affordability ](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/electrical-energy/affordability)
Learn about Proceeding R.18-07-006, which assesses the impacts on affordability of individual CPUC proceedings and utility rate requests. 
### [ Aliso Canyon ](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/regulatory-services/safety/gas-safety-and-reliability-branch/aliso-canyon-well-failure)
Our investigation and analysis of the Aliso Canyon well failure and more information on the incident. 
### [ COVID-19 Relief ](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/news-and-updates/newsroom/covid-19)
Find information on what the CPUC and utilities under its jurisdiction are doing to support communities during these challenging times. 
### [ CPUC Tribal Office ](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/about-cpuc/divisions/office-of-the-tribal-advisor)
The CPUC is committed to incorporating Tribal concerns into the development of regulations, and policies through engagement in discussion and consultation with Tribes. 
### [ Environmental and Social Justice Action Plan ](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/news-and-updates/newsroom/environmental-and-social-justice-action-plan)
The ESJ Action Plan is a commitment to advancing CPUC decisions and programs that strive to provide everyone across the state with consumer protections and other benefits. 
### [ PG&E ](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/pge)
Get the latest information on how we are working with PG&E and protecting their consumers. 
### [ Prison Phone Calls ](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/internet-and-phone/rulemaking-considering-regulation-of-telecommunication-services-used-by-incarcerated-people)
Learn more about what we are doing to consider the regulation of telecommunication services used by incarcerated people. 
### [ Public Safety Power Shutoffs ](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/consumer-support/psps)
Learn what we are doing to minimize impacts of utility PSPS events. 
###  Low Cost Internet Plans
Find opportunities available in your neighborhood for low cost internet plans. 
### [ Broadband Infrastructure Deployment ](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/internet-and-phone/broadband-infrastructure-deployment)
Learn about the proceeding to to accelerate the deployment of and access to quality, affordable internet for all Californians. 
### [ Solar Consumer Protection Guide ](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/electrical-energy/demand-side-management/customer-generation/california-solar-consumer-protection-guide)
This guide provides important information to homeowners thinking of going solar. 
### [ Summer Reliability ](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/electrical-energy/summer-2021-reliability)
This page details efforts the CPUC is taking to ensure sufficient resources are available to meet California’s electricity demand for the summer.
