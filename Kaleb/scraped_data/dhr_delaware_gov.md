The Department of Human Resources (DHR) is dedicated to fostering a positive workplace, supporting employee relations, and offering benefits and career growth opportunities.
With nine key [divisions](https://dhr.delaware.gov/divisions.shtml), DHR builds a workforce to serve the needs of Delawareans across the state.
The website provides information on [jobs](https://statejobs.delaware.gov/careers/index.shtml), [benefits](https://dhr.delaware.gov/benefits/index.shtml), [compensation](https://dhr.delaware.gov/classcomp/index.shtml), and [professional development resources](https://dhr.delaware.gov/training/index.shtml). We invite you to explore our [career opportunities](https://www.jobapscloud.com/DE/) and take the next step in your professional journey with the State of Delaware.
**The State of Delaware is hiring!**   
Join us for the 5th Annual Career Fair on April 24, 2026, from 1:00 to 4:00 p.m.  
Del-One Conference Center at Delaware Technical Community College's Terry Campus in Dover.  
Meet representatives from multiple state agencies, explore career opportunities, and apply for open positions. Don't miss the opportunity to explore a career with the State of Delaware!  [ Event details and current openings. ](https://statejobs.delaware.gov/)

**Announcement:** At the March 9, 2026 State Employee Benefits Committee (SEBC) meeting, the Committee voted to increase the copay to $200 per 30-day supply for GLP-1 medications for weight management, such as Wegovy and Zepbound*, effective **July 1, 2026** for non-Medicare prescription plan participants. This copay will be **excluded** from prescription out-of-pocket maximums.
_* Zepbound is currently excluded from the State of Delaware’s formulary, however, coverage may be approved through the clinical exception process for eligible members. The $200 copay will apply to any current and future medications in this drug class._
  

Individuals with a **_diabetes diagnosis who are prescribed a GLP-1 medication in the diabetes class_** (such as Mounjaro, Ozempic or Trulicity) will continue to pay the $32 copay for a 30-day supply.

# Insurance Coverage Office
The Insurance Coverage Office is responsible for providing quality support for the state government's insurance needs. The office is responsible for managing the insurance program for state vehicles, buildings and property, and Workers' Compensation, as well as reviewing state facilities for safety issues and providing safety programs to state employees. This office works under the direction of the Director of Statewide Benefits within the Department of Human Resources.

# Employee and Labor Relations
Our office is dedicated to maintaining a fair and safe work environment for employees while helping management maintain an efficient and productive workplace.
#### Mission
To uphold management practices that ensure fairness and stability in the workplace. To help build an organizational culture that encourages innovative and respectful conflict resolution and collaboration between employees, their representatives, and management.
  

#### C.R.A.F.T
Our office strives to follow five key principles to ensure the most effective way to achieve the agency’s goals for State employees: consistency, resiliency, accountability, follow-through, and timeliness.

## Talent Management
* * *
#### **Mission**
The Division of Talent Management proudly serves the State of Delaware by delivering statewide expertise in workforce planning, accrued leave compliance, centralized onboarding and offboarding, Statewide New Hire Orientation, policy development, and the interpretation of Federal and State laws, Merit Rules, and employment regulations that guide our agencies.
At the heart of Talent Management is a commitment to people. We strive to deliver excellent customer service and create open, meaningful exchanges that build trust and understanding. We encourage creativity and continuous learning to help staff grow with confidence and achieve their goals. By staying current with best practices, laws, and regulations, we ensure our work is both reliable and effective.
We value professionalism, respect, honesty, and integrity in everything we do, and we aim to be a trusted partner to coworkers, agencies, and communities across the state. Most importantly, we celebrate collaboration, diversity of thought, and shared experiences—because we know that when we work together, we all thrive.

# Office of Women's Advancement and Advocacy
The Office of Women's Advancement and Advocacy (OWAA) is a key resource on the well-being of women and girls in Delaware, dedicated to promoting gender equity. It provides expert support—such as data analysis, research, and policy recommendations—to state leaders, helping shape informed decisions that impact women. OWAA also oversees two major initiatives:
  * The Delaware Commission for Women, which advises on strategic planning and programs.
  * The Delaware Women’s Hall of Fame Committee, which honors the achievements of outstanding Delaware women each year.

# Statewide Benefits
![Statewide Benefits Office logo.](https://dhr.delaware.gov/benefits/images/sbo-logo.png)Welcome to the Statewide Benefits Office (SBO) website. Our motto "Benefits Made Easy" focuses on the goal of helping our members understand their benefits by giving them the information, resources and tools they need when they need them, so they can make the most of their benefits and healthcare dollars. View the [Contact Information](https://dhr.delaware.gov/benefits/contact/index.shtml) page for the Statewide Benefits Office as well as the benefit vendors.
![Megaphone Announcment](https://dhr.delaware.gov/benefits/images/announcement-warning.png)
Prepare for **Open Enrollment** Coming May 4 – 15, 2026  [ Learn More ](https://dhr.delaware.gov/benefits/oe/index.shtml)
* * *
**Announcement:** At the March 9, 2026 State Employee Benefits Committee (SEBC) meeting, the Committee voted to increase the copay to $200 per 30-day supply for GLP-1 medications for weight management, such as Wegovy and Zepbound*, effective **July 1, 2026** for non-Medicare prescription plan participants. This copay will be **excluded** from prescription out-of-pocket maximums.
_* Zepbound is currently excluded from the State of Delaware’s formulary, however, coverage may be approved through the clinical exception process for eligible members. The $200 copay will apply to any current and future medications in this drug class._
  

Individuals with a **_diabetes diagnosis who are prescribed a GLP-1 medication in the diabetes class_** (such as Mounjaro, Ozempic or Trulicity) will continue to pay the $32 copay for a 30-day supply.

The Insurance Coverage Office is responsible for providing quality support for the state government's insurance needs. # Employee and Labor Relations
Our office is dedicated to maintaining a fair and safe work environment for employees while helping management maintain an efficient and productive workplace.
#### Mission
To uphold management practices that ensure fairness and stability in the workplace. To help build an organizational culture that encourages innovative and respectful conflict resolution and collaboration between employees, their representatives, and management.
  

#### C.R.A.F.T
Our office strives to follow five key principles to ensure the most effective way to achieve the agency’s goals for State employees: consistency, resiliency, accountability, follow-through, and timeliness.

## Talent Management
* * *
#### **Mission**
The Division of Talent Management proudly serves the State of Delaware by delivering statewide expertise in workforce planning, accrued leave compliance, centralized onboarding and offboarding, Statewide New Hire Orientation, policy development, and the interpretation of Federal and State laws, Merit Rules, and employment regulations that guide our agencies.
At the heart of Talent Management is a commitment to people. We strive to deliver excellent customer service and create open, meaningful exchanges that build trust and understanding. We encourage creativity and continuous learning to help staff grow with confidence and achieve their goals. By staying current with best practices, laws, and regulations, we ensure our work is both reliable and effective.
We value professionalism, respect, honesty, and integrity in everything we do, and we aim to be a trusted partner to coworkers, agencies, and communities across the state. Most importantly, we celebrate collaboration, diversity of thought, and shared experiences—because we know that when we work together, we all thrive.

# Office of Women's Advancement and Advocacy
The Office of Women's Advancement and Advocacy (OWAA) is a key resource on the well-being of women and girls in Delaware, dedicated to promoting gender equity. It provides expert support—such as data analysis, research, and policy recommendations—to state leaders, helping shape informed decisions that impact women. OWAA also oversees two major initiatives:
  * The Delaware Commission for Women, which advises on strategic planning and programs.
  * The Delaware Women’s Hall of Fame Committee, which honors the achievements of outstanding Delaware women each year.
