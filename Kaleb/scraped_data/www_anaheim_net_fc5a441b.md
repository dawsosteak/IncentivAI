#  Water & Energy Saving Tips 
**Electricity** is often consumed as if it were an unlimited resource, without taking into consideration how difficult it is to produce. Instead, we should be treating electricity as a valuable asset that can be lost at any moment.   
  
Here are 10 ways to be more sustainable with your energy usage.  
  

  1. Turn off lights when not in use. Install [dusk-to-dawn](https://www.anaheim.net/1468/Dusk-to-Dawn-Lighting---NEW-OPTIONS) lighting outdoors to ensure lights only stay on at night and not during the day. 
  2. Install and purchase [ENERGY STAR®](https://www.energystar.gov/) appliances and receive a[ ](http://www.anaheim.net/936/Energy-Rebates-Programs "rebate")[rebate ](http://www.anaheim.net/936/Energy-Rebates-Programs)for qualified models. 
  3. Use power strips; turn the power strips off when the equipment is not in use.
  4. Purchase a smart thermostat that automatically adjusts temperatures when you are not home. Apply for the [rebate ](https://www.anaheim.net/2579/Heating-Cooling-Systems)once installed.
  5. Rather than use your AC, purchase and install ceiling fans or whole house fans. Receive a [rebate ](https://www.anaheim.net/5241/Appliance-Fixtures)for qualified models. 
  6. Replace outdated light bulbs with newer energy efficient LEDs.
  7. If you must use the AC be sure to change filters regularly. Receive a rebate for an [AC Tune-Up](https://www.anaheim.net/3436/AC-Tune-Up).
  8. Bolster your windows. Use window shades and add window film to help insulate your home. [Receive a rebate](https://www.anaheim.net/404) for certified high performance windows and window film projects.
  9. Hang clothes to dry instead of using a dryer. Or purchase an [ENERGY STAR®](https://www.energystar.gov/) certified heat pump dryer and apply for the [rebate](http://www.anaheim.net/5241/Appliance-Fixtures). 
  10. Unplug that spare refrigerator or freezer. If it is still working, recycle it and receive a [rebate](https://www.anaheim.net/937/Refrigerator-Recycling-Program). 


  
Conserving **water** is another way to lead a more sustainable lifestyle. Being water wise helps lower consumption, and ultimately the cost of your bill. Here are some ways you can be sustainable at home:  
  

  1. Only run full loads of clothes when washing. Maximize your washers potential and utilize every droplet of water.
  2. Take short showers instead of baths and use low-flow shower heads. Request a[ Home Utility Check-Up](https://www.anaheim.net/475/Home-Utility-Check-Up) to have energy- and water- saving devices installed for free in your home.
  3. Run your dishwasher only when completely full and be sure to purchase an [ENERGY STAR®](https://www.energystar.gov/)   
model to receive a [rebate](https://www.anaheim.net/5241/Appliance-Fixtures). 
  4. Transform your lawn by removing water-reliant turf and replacing it with California-native, drought tolerant plants and trees. [Click here](https://www.anaheim.net/1062/Turf-Replacement-Program) for more information.
  5. When installing an irrigation system, look for types that are more efficient when using water - like drip irrigation and [rotating sprinkler nozzles.](http://socalwatersmart.com/files/pdfs/qualifying_list_nozzles.pdf)
  6. Using a rain barrel helps collect rainwater for lawn and garden irrigation. Receive a [rebate](http://socalwatersmart.com/en/residential/rebates/available-rebates/rain-barrels-cisterns/) for qualified models. 
  7. Fixing plumbing leaks not only reduces the amount of water wasted, it will also make a difference on your water bill. 
  8. Turn off the tap when not in use. Especially when brushing your teeth and shaving.
  9. If using a hose to irrigate, make sure to have a shut off nozzle attached to ensure no water is being wasted. Receive one for free with the [Home Utility Check-Up.](https://www.anaheim.net/475/Home-Utility-Check-Up)
  10. Update your home fixtures by upgrading to high-efficiency models. This can include clothes washers and toilets! [Rebates](http://socalwatersmart.com/en/residential/) are available for many appliances and fixture.
