# Historical Campaign Reports
The reports on this page result from a series of studies that represent data collected from 1991 through 1998. They analyze the role of money in state political campaigns. For these analyses to make sense, think of California's elections as two-year cycles. Each two-year election cycle has an 18-month Primary Election period followed by a six-month General Election period.
The studies below included "comprehensive" reports. They cover either the 18-month Primary Election or the six-month General Election. An asterisk (*) marks these reports after the report number.
Usually, reports on state candidates are separate from those on state propositions. Reports on candidates/officeholders cover specific periods of financial activity. They have fixed start and end dates. Most reports will have a "through" date, with no fixed start date.
Data from 1998 to now can be accessed on [**PowerSearch**](https://powersearch.sos.ca.gov/).
