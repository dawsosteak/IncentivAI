# Current Auctions
Attorney General's Asset Forfeiture Auction is ongoing and we have begun the process to ready and add more property for sale. **_At this time (starting on 10/13/25), new listings on the auction site are for "preview" purposes, however we can not lock out bids from being accepted and the winning bidder's bid will be accepted. Once all material related to the sale for other items is posted, the actual end date (when the auction closes for a specific item) will be posted_**. **_There will be at least a 10 day window in order to make a bid from the time the status goes from "Preview" to "Live Bidding"_**
> **_UPDATE as of 11/28/25 :_**
> **_Auction closing dates have been established. Please visit the auction site to see what the closing date is for any item you're interested in bidding on. Bidding will close at 7:00 pm on their respective days._**
We do have more property for sale and will continually be updating the site with these items including when additional information such as as repairs, pictures, videos and other important information has been gathered to give you the clearest picture we can provide of what you are bidding on. Please take special note of auction opening and closing dates as the dates may be adjusted where the situation warrants it. Check back often as new auctions and additional information specific to each item (more pictures, videos, other facts) may be added as they become available.

# Current Auctions
*Successful bidders must communicate with the custodians in advance to make arrangements for removal.
*Winning bidders must **pay** for item(s) **within 72 hours** of the date and time of auction closing.
*Each bid is a legal offer to purchase the item subject to auction. Upon the completion of the bidding process, the winning bid constitutes a legal and binding contract to purchase the item. By law, we may pursue legal action to enforce the contract and ask that the court charge you with the court expenses including our attorney fees. Alternatively, if we do not receive your payment within seventy-two (72) hours, we may choose to cancel your bid, and either re-auction the item, or offer it to the next highest bidder(s). In such case we have the right to recover the difference between your bid and the final sale price, together with court costs and State Procurement Office/Surplus attorneys' fees.
Call (808) 831-6757 to schedule **pick up within 14 working days** after payment is made.
