# Advisory Commissions

> – Governor Josh Shapiro

## Advisory Commission on African American Affairs
![](https://s7d9.scene7.com/is/image/statepa/african-american?ts=1751313080487&&fmt=webp-alpha&dpr=off)
The purpose of the Commission is to gather information regarding African American Pennsylvanians’ experiences and needs; review, evaluate, and assess programs affecting them; provide the Governor with information and recommendations regarding how best to meet their needs; and provide information about programs and services that may be relevant to African American Pennsylvanians’ needs. The Commission and its Executive Director also serve as an extension of the Governor’s office in their respective communities and are responsible for communicating the priorities and accomplishments of the Governor and his Administration. This effort seeks to spread awareness and increase access to programs and services provided by the Commonwealth that benefit every-day Pennsylvanians. 
###  Executive Director Sierra Dunn
![](https://s7d9.scene7.com/is/image/statepa/sierra%20dunn%20headshot%202025?ts=1750707875651&dpr=off)
Sierra
Hailing from North Philadelphia with her advocacy work taking root in Wilmington, Delaware, Sierra has spent over a decade advancing opportunities for underserved communities through organizing, education, and coalition building. Her work lives at the intersection of civic engagement, youth empowerment, and economic justice and always grounded in a deep belief that communities know what they need and deserve to be heard, resourced, and respected.
Her journey began shortly after college with the Police Athletic League (PAL) in Delaware, where she led grassroots advocacy efforts that secured record-breaking funding for mentorship and career-readiness programs. Through strategic partnerships with local school districts and police departments, she helped create a space where youth, community members, and law enforcement could build lasting relationships. These efforts expanded access to after-school programs and provided hundreds of young people with a safe and supportive environment to learn, grow, and thrive.
After the success of her community programs at the PAL and New Castle County Police department, she later went on to join McKinsey’s Social Initiative, founded as Generation USA where she held several positions mainly overseeing Career Advancement and Workforce Development where she managed a national team and spearheaded employer partnerships with companies like JPMorgan Chase, Capital One etc. Her leadership resulted in hundreds of job placements for learners from nontraditional backgrounds and the development a mentorship program to improve student retention and outcomes.
In 2024, Sierra took on the role of Regional Organizing Director in Eastern Delaware County for the Harris-Walz Coordinated Campaign in Delaware County, Pennsylvania. There, she led a team of organizers and volunteers to mobilize thousands of voters, drive civic participation, and increase turnout in one of the largest townships in the state. Her team’s efforts contributed to more than 121,000 voter contacts in just four days during the final stretch of the election.
Sierra’s purpose is simple but powerful: to help communities not just survive but thrive and reach their fullest potential.

###  Executive Director Razin Karu
![](https://s7d9.scene7.com/is/image/statepa/22954_Razin_Karu_NK_Horizontal?ts=1752701219625&dpr=off)
Razin Karu was born and raised in India and has been living in the US since 2013. He received his AA with honors in history from Bucks County Community College, where he also founded the Bucks Muslim Students Association, a chapter of the Bucks College Dems and also served as the student government president from 2016-2017. He was the first person of color to hold the office. He then transferred to Temple University, from where he received his BA in history and political science, with minors in global studies and economics. Razin is passionate about civic engagement, social justice, interfaith relations, and volunteering. 
He most recently serves as the Executive Director of the Muslim Society of Delaware Valley (2022-23) where he directed all programs and activities for the Muslim Youth Center of Philadelphia (MYCP) and the Islamic Cultural Center of Willow Grove (ICCWG) and lead the organization’s strategic planning. Prior to that, Razin served as the outreach coordinator at ICNA Relief PA (2018-2022) where he worked on programs focusing on a diverse set of community needs, including refugee services, hunger prevention, disaster relief, educational empowerment, and more. Razin serves as a member of the Bucks County New Americans Advisory Commission and is a very active member of the Indian community in Bensalem.

###  Executive Director Olga Negrón
![](https://s7d9.scene7.com/is/image/statepa/olga%20headshot%202025?ts=1751317603573&dpr=off)
Olga Negrón was the first elected Latino Councilwoman in the City of Bethlehem, and when elected, she was the only woman of color in City Council. In 2017, Negrón was re-elected with the highest votes citywide. She is no stranger to community organizations and to the people of Pennsylvania from decades in public service on numerous boards, commissions, and committees, including PCCD Crime Prevention & Community Revitalization Advisory Council, appointed by former Governor Edward G. Rendell; member of the Governor’s Advisory Council on Public Library, appointed by Governor Tom Wolf; and Vice-Chair of the Pennsylvania Latino Convention. Olga worked as a community organizer, executive director for several non-profit organizations, Neighborhood Coordinator for the City of Allentown and as the Legislative Assistant for a State Representative. After almost a decade working as an office manager and marketing director for HGSK law firm, Negrón joins the Shapiro administration as the executive director for GACLA. 
Olga hails from Naranjito, Puerto Rico. She moved to Bethlehem’s south side in June 1996 where she raised, as a single mother, her three daughters, Dr. Corazón Irizarry, multilingual OBGYN; Isis Irizarry, Esq. multilingual immigration attorney; and Paloma Irizarry, PA Certified Doula. Negrón is among 30 people nationwide, and the only one from Pennsylvania, whose stories are cited in a book published in 2014 that asserts the “War on Poverty” can be won. Olga is happily married to Magisterial District Judge Nicholas E. Englesson. 
Negrón is a well-known public servant and is regarded as the guardian angel of the underdog, always fighting for justice, righting wrongs, empowering women and girls, and being the steady voice for the voiceless.

## Advisory Commission on People with Disabilities
![](https://s7d9.scene7.com/is/image/statepa/gov_s%20advisory%20comission%20for%20id-a%20logo?ts=1776187466692&&fmt=png-alpha&dpr=off)
The purpose of the Commission is to gather information regarding the experiences and needs of people with disabilities in Pennsylvania; review, evaluate, and assess programs affecting them; provide the Governor with information and recommendations regarding how best to meet their needs; and provide information about programs and services that may be relevant to needs of people with disabilities in Pennsylvania. The Governor’s Advisory Commission on People with Disabilities consists of up to 30 volunteer members and is responsible for advising on and advocating for programs, policies and legislation that benefit Pennsylvanians with disabilities.

###  Executive Director Ashley Walkowiak
![](https://s7d9.scene7.com/is/image/statepa/27922_ashley_walkowiak_nk_8x10?ts=1754062075613&dpr=off)
Ashley N. Walkowiak currently serves as the Executive Director of the Governor’s Advisory Commission on Women. Ashley is sought after for her expertise in trauma-informed policy and practice which has been honed both within state government and in her community. She sits on the PA Interagency Human Trafficking Workgroup and the steering committee for PA’s statewide maternal health plan.
Prior to her current role, Ashley was Policy Director at the PA Department of Drug & Alcohol Programs, preceded by her role as Director of Policy, Intergovernmental Affairs, & Mediation at the PA Human Relations Commission where she implemented policy, as well as legislative and community-sensitive strategies, to further civil rights protections for Pennsylvania’s marginalized populations. Most folks recognize Ashley from her years spent in the PA Office of Victim Advocate where she worked to advance the rights and interests of crime victims.
Ashley is a mother of three and the wife of a combat veteran. Because of her own sexual violence survival journey, Ashley is passionate about healing and creating a safe and welcoming Pennsylvania for all citizens.
Ashley spent four years serving on the Cumberland County Commission for Women. She currently serves on the Board of Directors of the Children’s Advocacy Centers of Pennsylvania and has received various awards for her advocacy efforts, including City & State’s Forty Under 40, Central Penn Business Journal’s Women of Influence, and the YWCA Women of Excellence award.
