#  Renewable Energy Resources 
## A Brief History
  
In 1894, the City of Anaheim established the first municipal electric utility in Southern California. Within a year, Anaheim Public Utilities extended energy services to the downtown area and continued generating power until 1916. From 1916 to 1976, Anaheim purchased its power supply from Southern California Edison; however, faced with rising rates from Edison, the city explored electric generation and transmission projects. From 1976 to 2003, it received power from its ownership shares in [conventional resources](https://www.anaheim.net/5089/), including coal, natural gas, and nuclear energy. 
Beginning in 2003, Anaheim shifted towards providing customers with renewable resources. In response to Assembly Bill 32 (2006), which established statewide Greenhouse Gas (GHG) emissions reduction targets, Anaheim has been increasing its provision of renewable energy to customers while reducing non-renewable energy consumption to help achieve statewide GHG emissions reduction goals. 
Today, we provide electricity to our customers from multiple renewable energy projects across the western United States.
