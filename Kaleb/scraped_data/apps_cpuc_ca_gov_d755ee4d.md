Ziad Aunallah  Ramona, CA92065 Comment regarding Decision 25-12-003 in Rulemaking R.20-11-003 Public Comment regarding CPUC Decision 25-12-003 and cancellation of SGIP waitlisted projects I am submitting this comment regarding consumer impacts resulting from CPUC Decision 25-12-003, which sunset the ratepayer Self Generation Incentive Program budgets and cancelled waitlisted projects. My household installed a residential battery storage system in Ramona, California at a cost of approximately 39,750 dollars in reliance on the expected SGIP incentive of 15,000 dollars. After installation and placement on the waitlist, our project was cancelled when the program was sunset. At the time of installation, program guidance indicated that waitlisted projects would typically be funded as ratepayer funds became available, even if payment required a long wait. There was no indication that the entire program could be terminated while customers were already in the waitlist pipeline after installation. The expected SGIP incentive materially influenced our decision to install the battery system. Without the incentive, we would not have installed a system of this size and cost. The cancellation created significant financial harm and also prevented planned solar expansion that depended on the incentive. This outcome raises concerns regarding consumer reliance. Customers installed qualifying equipment in good faith based on the program structure, but lost expected incentives after making substantial financial commitments. This undermines confidence in state incentive programs designed to accelerate energy storage adoption. I respectfully request that the Commission consider the impacts on installed waitlisted projects and evaluate whether relief, grandfathering, or alternative consideration is appropriate for customers who relied on SGIP prior to program sunset.
