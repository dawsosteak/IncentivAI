#  Find More Financial Incentives to Increase Your Savings  

Stack federal, state, and local incentives for your next project.
Check out the programs below for funding opportunities for your building project. Feeling overwhelmed and not sure where to start? Contact our Energy Concierge team, and we'll help you find the right program for you!
[ Contact An Energy Concierge ](https://energysmartcolorado.formstack.com/forms/core_lead)
According to the [2023 Greenhouse Gas Emissions Report](https://www.aspencore.org/emissions-inventory), **building emissions decreased 1%** despite rising energy consumption. This means we have the opportunity to **make a significant impact** by electrifying and improving energy efficiency in our buildings. Thankfully, there are numerous programs at the federal, state, and county levels to help you make those improvements.

### APCHA's Essential Repairs Program
The Aspen/Pitkin County Housing Authority’s (APCHA) Essential Repairs Program provides up to $10,000 to qualified APCHA homeowners to address critical repairs that enhance the health, safety, and longevity of their homes. Eligible repairs include:
  * Furnace and water heater replacements
  * Roof repairs
  * Plumbing fixes
  * Accessibility modifications
  * Window repairs or replacements
  * Mold remediation


Applications are accepted on a rolling basis until funds are fully allocated. Households with an income of [150% AMI or lower](https://www.aspencore.org/community-priority-participants#PitkinAMI) qualify for a [free energy assessment](https://www.aspencore.org/energy-assessments).
[ APCHA's Essential Repair Program ](http://apcha.org/399/Essential-Repair-Program)

### Holy Cross Customers
Holy Cross Energy offers customers a [variety of energy efficiency and electrification upgrades](https://www.holycross.com/member-programs/energy-efficiency-and-rebates). Customers in good standing can receive rebates for:
  * Heating, cooling, and appliances
  * Insulation and air sealing
  * Controls and thermostats
  * Electric transportation and lawn care


If you are a Holy Cross Energy customer, submit your request for rebates to CORE and Holy Cross in a single application for either residential or commercial projects below.
[ Residential Rebate Application ](https://energysmartcolorado.formstack.com/forms/residential_rebate_application)

### ReEnergize Eagle County
This program helps full-time Eagle County residents get a free comprehensive energy assessment, energy coaching, and home improvements. Households with an income up to [150% AMI](https://www.aspencore.org/community-priority-participants#EagleCountyReEnergize) may receive up to $7,500 for:
  * Insulation, air sealing, duct sealing
  * LED lighting and programmable thermostats
  * Heating/Cooling system, water heater
  * Kitchen stove
  * Refrigerator
  * Spot ventilation


Applicants **MUST** submit one of the following items as income verification: Most recent Tax Return-IRS Form 1040, Wages, or Tax Statement W-2 (please remove Social Security Number from documents).
[ Learn More ](https://hub.walkingmountains.org/reenergize-eagle-county-contact-us)

### ReEnergize Garfield County
Residents of Garfield County may receive a free home energy assessment and funding for home energy improvements. Qualifying households are limited to up to [150% AMI](https://www.aspencore.org/community-priority-participants#GarfieldAMI). Measures that may be funded, subject to the home energy assessment, include:
  * Insulation & air sealing
  * Pipe & duct wrapping
  * Heating/cooling system upgrade
  * LED lighting


This is a program of [Garfield Clean Energy](https://garfieldcleanenergy.org/reenergize/) and is managed by [CLEER](http://www.cleanenergyeconomy.net/). funding is awarded on a first-come, first-served basis.   

[ Learn More ](https://garfieldcleanenergy.org/reenergize/)
### Colorado HEAR Program
HEAR (Home Electrification and Appliance Rebate) lowers the up-front cost of specific electric upgrades in existing Colorado homes. Rebates are taken as a point-of-sale discount through participating contractors. 
  

Households at or below 150% AMI may receive up to $14,000 for:
  * Heat Pumps
  * Heat Pump Water Heaters
  * Electrical Panel Upgrades
  * Insulation/Air Sealing
  * Electric Stoves


  

Currently available for single-family homes; expanding to condos, apartments, and manufactured homes in 2026. HEAR can be stacked with Colorado tax credits and local utility programs for greater savings.
  

To get started, verify your income through the [Colorado Energy Office portal](http://portal.neighborlysoftware.com/ENERGYREBATES-CO).
[ Learn More ](https://energyoffice.colorado.gov/home-energy-rebates)
