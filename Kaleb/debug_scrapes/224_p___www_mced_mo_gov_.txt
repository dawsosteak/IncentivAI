Clean Energy & Efficiency Financing
Business
Energy‑efficiency investments reward businesses with lower bills and higher profits. They enhance property value and market appeal while signaling responsibility to customers, giving owners pride in profitable, sustainable operations that endure.
Community
Community‑wide energy‑efficiency initiatives cut utility bills and reduce greenhouse emissions while creating jobs and improving grid reliability. These benefits nurture communal pride and strengthen a shared identity rooted in responsible stewardship.
Workforce
A thriving energy‑efficiency workforce delivers local jobs and economic prosperity, employing millions across the country. The work cannot be outsourced and offers a sense of purpose by helping homes and businesses save energy and money.

Key Features of Energy Funding Benefits
Important Energy Upgrades
Improvements may be made without delay
Fully Funded
100% Upfront costs are covered with no out of pocket requirements
Attaches to Property
PACE is an obligation of the property not the owner – both the improvement and the obligation transfer with ownership
Flexible Financing
The district offers a variety of finance programs for energy projects – direct funding as well as low cost conduit issuance for developers and owners are available
Capital Stacking
PACE can help move projects forward and lowering equity costs by carving out funding of energy components
Cash Flow Positive
Fixed rates eliminate interest rate risk from resets – the project remains cash positive for the life of the financing

MCED Program Offerings
Missouri Clean Energy District
MCED provides unlimited capital for energy projects across Missouri. The programs operated by the district provide access to capital for all property types and categories.
Private, nonprofit and public property owers may apply for energy project funding to improve multifamily, commercial, industrial, agricultural and municipal facilities.
Select your project type below to learn the particulars:
COMMERCIAL
NONPROFIT
PUBLIC FACILITIES
DEVELOPER
Commercial
For Commercial Property: Our capital partners offer a C-PACE program for affordable financing options for energy-efficient upgrades, helping their operation increase efficiency, comfort and savings.
Nonprofit
For Nonprofit: MCED provides C-PACE financing to help nonprofits fund energy-efficient improvements with affordable terms. In addition, MCED collaborates in seeking grant support to strengthen nonprofit operations, enabling organizations to lower costs, improve facilities, and dedicate more resources to their mission.
Rural Development Commercial Facility Target Fund:
Energy project assistance for property owners in rural Missouri communities Proposed to operate in conjunction with our USDA partner programs.
APPLY NOWPublic Facilities & Infrastructure Improvements:
Local Governments benefit from energy project financing for Public Facilities through the District.
APPLY NOWMCED partners with a range of capital providers to deliver C-PACE financing tailored to each project. Depending on project type, funding may come from specialty capital providers, local community banks, or district administration for larger or more complex transactions. This flexible structure ensures financing options that meet the unique needs of both nonprofit and commercial property owners.
Access to long term fixed rate financing of energy improvements is available for small and large projects on virtually any conceivable property type of the built environment.
Commercial property types include office buildings, industrial property, medical centers, hotels, malls, retail stores, farm, multifamily housing, warehouses and garages. Nonprofit property containing more than a five units qualifies as commercial property for borrowing purposes.
*Note: MCED provides capital sourcing for a wide range of funding needs. At the option of the borrower large commercial projects requiring funding greater than $2.0 Million may be processed through the Commercial Program and managed by the District Administrator to secure competitive offerings from qualified investors. Please contact the District Administrator to initiate project funding.
