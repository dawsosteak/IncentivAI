Don’t let the cost of energy efficiency upgrades be a barrier to making the Energy Smart choice.
Some energy efficiency improvements require little funding. For recommendations with a higher cost, cash-back rebates, on bill repayment programs, and loans may provide assistance to homeowners.
Many utility companies and Energy Smart Colorado partners offer rebates for completed energy improvements. Additionally, through EECBG funding, Energy Smart Colorado is offering weatherization and electrification rebates to qualified households.
You may qualify for EECBG rebates if
- You live in a community served by an Energy Smart Colorado Program Partner
- You meet at least one of the household eligibility pathways
- You are applying for improvements in your primary residence
Visit the EECBG Home Energy Upgrade Rebates page to learn more.

Federal Tax Credits and Rebates are NOW available in Colorado!
Colorado Energy Office launched the Home Electrification and Appliance Rebate (HEAR) program in November 2025. Funded by the Inflation Reduction Act, the HEAR program provides rebates to help single-family households lower upfront costs to install high efficiency electric appliances and make energy efficiency upgrades.
Limited to households that do not exceed 150% of the area median income, the HEAR Program is providing point-of-sale rebates (through registered contractors) for home electrification and weatherization improvements. Low- and moderate-income (LMI) households may combine electrification and air sealing and insulation projects to a maximum rebate of $14,000 – at 50% of the cost of the qualified electrification project. (Those at 80% AMI will qualify for 100% of their costs to be covered.)
A registered contractor must install the upgrades eligible and approved for the Colorado Home Energy Rebate Program. Registered program contractors will receive the rebate and subtract the rebate amount from the total project cost, providing an upfront discount to Colorado households at the time of installation. All upgrades must be ENERGY STAR certified. Eligible households (which may include property owners or renters) can save up to the following maximum amounts for qualifying upgrades made in an existing single-family home:
- Cold Climate Heat Pump systems: $8,000
- Electrical panel upgrades: $4,000
- Electric wiring: $2,500
- Heat pump water heaters: $1,750
- Insulation, air sealing, and ventilation: $1,600
- Electric stoves, ranges, ovens and induction cooktops: $840
- Heat pump clothes dryers are not an eligible measure in Colorado’s program
Contractors may apply to offer the rebates by following the steps on this page: Become a Registered Contractor.
Households may apply for eligibility through this online application.
More details on the HEAR program can be found on the Colorado Home Energy Rebate Program page. Call the Help Center at 866-336-0016 or email COenergyrebates@guidehouse.com with any questions not addressed on the FAQ page.
