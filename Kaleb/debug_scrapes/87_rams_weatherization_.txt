Weatherization
Individuals who meet Income limits may apply for the Weatherization program through the weatherization service provider in their area. Homeowners and renters may apply.
The weatherization provider will provide program services at no cost to qualified applicants.
To participate in the Weatherization program check:
Income Limits
Association of Alaska Housing Authorities
FAQ
Q. WHO DO I CONTACT TO SEE IF I QUALIFY FOR FREE WEATHERIZATION?
A. First check the income guidelines to get a general idea of your eligibility. If you think you are eligible, contact one of the weatherization providers listed for your area. If you live in a home owned by a state-designated regional housing authority, you should contact the housing authority directly.
Q. IS THE WEATHERIZATION PROGRAM AVAILABLE FOR RENTALS OR CONDOS?
A. Yes. The Weatherization program does include rentals and condos.
Q. WILL HOMES THAT HAVE BEEN WEATHERIZED IN THE PAST BE ELIGIBLE FOR THE WEATHERIZATION PROGRAM?
A: If a home was weatherized prior to May 14, 2008, it will be eligible to be re-weatherized in the Weatherization program for individuals meeting income guidelines. However, previously weatherized homes will not be as high a priority as homes that have never been weatherized.

Codes & Standards
Alaska Housing is here to provide support and resources to Alaska's housing developers, ensuring your project is energy efficient. We also offer specialized loans and grants for housing professionals.
By Alaska Statute, a home may only be financed by Alaska Housing when:
- The home meets Alaska Minimum Construction Standards as defined in Alaska Housing Construction Inspection Guidelines,
- The home meets the Building Energy Efficiency Standard (BEES), and
- The home is built by a general contractor with a residential endorsement to his or her contractor's license (exceptions may apply as in the case of an owner/builder).

Commercial Buildings
High performance energy efficient buildings are important because they have low energy costs and exceptional standards for health and safety. Alaska Housing offers financing programs, conducts research, and provides information and technical assistance for non-residential buildings to help promote a sustainable built environment in Alaska.
Tools
Alaska Housing has developed a variety of tools to further advance the built environment in Alaska and assist facility owners and managers in implementing energy efficiency projects.
- Cash Flow Calculator - Allows the user to develop a preliminary analysis of the cost effectiveness of an energy efficiency retrofit by comparing cash flows of various funding scenarios.
- REAL Benchmark Form - This spreadsheet can be used to track basic building and energy information to see trends in energy use and operating costs.
Financing
The Public Facility loan provides financing for energy efficiency improvements to buildings owned by regional educational attendance areas, the University of Alaska, the state or municipalities in the state.
