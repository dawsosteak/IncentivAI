Learn MoreTerms and Conditions
Rebate applications will only be accepted from qualifying customers within OUC’s service territory. To be eligible for a water rebate, the property must be located within OUC’s water service territory. To be eligible for an energy rebate, the property must be located within OUC’s electric service territory. To be eligible for a residential water/energy rebate, the dwelling type must be classified as single-family, condo or townhome by the property appraiser.
Terms and Conditions to Participate
- For residential and commercial energy and/or water rebates, a legible copy of the invoice and proof of purchase by the property owner and OUC account holder is required.
- If the invoice is entirely handwritten, OUC requires proof of payment of such an invoice (i.e. copy of the front/back of the check, a copy of the credit card statement, etc.).
- If providing financial statements, please make sure they clearly show the name of the account holder.
- Unrelated transaction information can be blacked out to protect your account information.
- We are unable to process rebates for OUC customers who have had work performed and paid by a third party.
- Where applicable, all invoices must reflect the appropriate license number of the contractor performing the work.
- All receipts/invoices must be dated and submitted within six (6) months of the date of purchase. For New Home or New Construction rebates, applications must be dated and submitted no more than six (6) months from the date of closing.
- All work performed must comply with all local and state laws and be installed according to the manufacturer’s specifications.
- OUC reserves the right to perform onsite verifications of the work performed before issuing a rebate.
- Applications will be processed in four (4) to six (6) weeks.
- Rebates will appear as a credit on your OUC bill.
- Rebate amounts can’t exceed the cost of the work performed.
- OUC reserves the right to add or remove any rebates without prior notification.
- OUC reserves the right to deny a rebate for reasons including, but not limited to, the following:
- If a rebate submittal is deemed incomplete, an electronic notification will be sent requesting supplemental information.
- If the requested information is not received within 15 days of notification, the rebate will be rejected.

What is OUC SunChoice?
SunChoice offers commercial customers a practical way to support clean energy in Central Florida without the need for on-site installations or large upfront investments.
In exchange for your premium, your business can offset part or all of its energy usage and receive Green-e® Energy Certified Renewable Energy Certificates (RECs), retired in your name. This positions your business as a sustainability leader while accelerating solar growth faster than we could achieve on our own.
Benefits of SunChoice
Flexible Subscription
SunChoice provides flexible, tiered subscription plans, allowing your business to effortlessly meet sustainability goals while staying within budget.
Green-e® Energy Certified RECs
Offset up to 100% of your energy consumption with locally produced renewable energy, with RECs retired annually to match your subscription level.
No Contract Required
No long-term contracts and easy quarterly billing help keep your business aligned with both sustainability and financial goals.
Corporate Responsibility
Demonstrate your company’s dedication to a sustainable future while meeting your energy needs with a flexible, scalable solution.
No Upfront Costs
SunChoice provides the flexibility your business needs to meet its sustainability goals, with no long-term contracts and simple quarterly billing.
Positive Community Impact
By joining, you’re contributing to local renewable energy projects that benefit our community, environment and contribute to a more rapid solar adoption.
Why Choose SunChoice?
Choosing SunChoice is more than an energy decision—it’s a statement of your leadership in sustainability. Subscribers recognize that adopting renewable energy technology today comes at a premium to accelerate solar expansion.
This flexible solution helps you meet sustainability goals and ensures that your company directly contributes to the rapid growth of renewable energy in Central Florida without placing the financial burden on all residents.

How SunChoice Works
Step 1
OUC solar farms generate clean renewable energy.
Step 2
The energy produced is fed into OUC’s electric grid to help offset traditional fuels.
Step 3
Subscribers support clean energy production by choosing a percentage of SunChoice Solar to offset their energy needs.
Step 4
Together, OUC and its subscribers accelerate the growth of large-scale solar projects in Central Florida, contributing to a greener and more sustainable future for our community.
Frequently Asked Questions
Interested in learning more about SunChoice? Click on any of the questions below for more details about the program.
Is there a cost to sign up?
One of the benefits of the SunChoice program is that there are no initial enrollment costs, regardless of what percentage you subscribe to.
What are the eligibility requirements?
You must be an OUC commercial electric customer to subscribe. Customers may enroll at any time but must remain enrolled for at least three (3) billing periods after enrollment. A customer may cancel thereafter with 30 days’ notice and enroll again after 6 months if the program is available.
OUC will have renewable energy credits associated with the customer’s Solar Energy subscription Green-e® certified and will retire them in the customer’s name.
What exactly am I purchasing?
You are not purchasing physical equipment, but rather offsetting a portion of solar power produced by an existing utility-scale array located at diverse locations throughout Central Florida. Ensuring the purchased amount is offset through OUC’s grid. You only pay a solar rate (in lieu of the fuel rate you would normally pay) to participate – no maintenance fees or installation costs.
How does the power I subscribe to get to my business?
The power generated by the solar farms is not directly fed into your business but rather goes straight into OUC’s electric grid to offset other fuel sources. You can claim the environmental benefits of the solar energy produced by OUC as an offset to your consumption.
How much solar energy am I allowed to use and what are the rates?
You can subscribe for all or a portion of your electric bill to be powered by solar energy. You can opt for a percentage of your electric usage in 10% increments with a tiered solar rate. The percentage of your energy not supplied by solar power will be supplemented with traditional energy options from our diverse fuel portfolio.
SunChoice Solar Energy Rates:
- First 30% subscription 1.0¢ per kWh
- Next 40% subscription 2.5¢ per kWh
- Next 30% subscription 4.5¢ per kWh
For example, if a customer uses 3000 kWh of energy per month and subscribes to 80% of SunChoice solar, below is an example of how the rate will be used:
| Tiered Rate Schedule | Monthly SunChoice Cost |
|---|---|
| 3000 at 30% = 900 kWh @ .01 | $9 |
| 3000 at 40% = Next 1200 kWh @ .025 | $30 |
| 3000 at 10% = Next 300 kWh @ .045 | $13.50 |
| 3000 at 80% = 2400 kWh | $52.50 |
What if the solar farm is damaged by weather or can’t produce at its maximum capacity?
If the solar farm were to become unable to produce energy, your home would not be affected.
What are Green-e® Energy-certified Renewable Energy Certificates?
Green-e® Energy Certified Renewable Energy Certificates (RECs) provide verified proof that one megawatt-hour (MWh) of electricity has been generated from a renewable energy source. As the leading certification for RECs in North America, Green-e® ensures that both the renewable energy generation and its sales comply with strict environmental and consumer protection standards.
By receiving Green-e® Energy Certified RECs, businesses can legitimately claim the environmental benefits of renewable energy, helping them meet sustainability goals and showcase their commitment to environmental leadership.
Since 1997, Green-e®—a program of the nonprofit Center for Resource Solutions based in San Francisco, CA—has set the highest standards for renewable energy and carbon offset products in North America.
