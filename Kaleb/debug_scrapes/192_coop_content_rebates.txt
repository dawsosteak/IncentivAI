Gunnison County Electric Association (GCEA) partners with Tri-State Generation and Transmission Association to provide our Energy Efficiency Rebate Program.
- General Information
- Rebate amounts are subject to change at any time including discontinuing offerings.
- All items/equipment must be new and installed and operated in GCEA service territory.
- Proof of purchase required via itemized receipt.
- Rebates are only available to GCEA members with active accounts.
- Rebate applications must be submitted within 90 days of installation or purchase (or certificate of occupancy for all-electric homes).
- Rebate amounts are based on the purchase price of the item (taxes and shipping are not included).
- PowerWise Pledge™ contributors qualify for higher rebates. Members may opt in by paying $1 per month retroactively for the current year and remaining enrolled for 12 months.
- All members are subject to a cap of $5,000 annually for PowerWise Pledge™ funded rebates, regardless of the number of accounts they hold.
- All members are subject to a cap of $20,000 per account for Tri-State funded rebates.
- Rebate amounts listed are only eligible for 2026.
- Please submit:
- Proof of purchase through receipt and/or invoice
- Energy Guide label showing ENERGY STAR® certification for appliances.
- Members will be mailed a check. Please allow 6 weeks for processing.
- If you did not receive your check after the six weeks, please call 970-641-3520
- We will contact you only if there is a problem with your application

When you use electricity can matter just as much as how much you use. Time-of-Use rates give you the opportunity to save by shifting some energy use to off-peak hours, when electricity costs less. Small changes can add up to big savings.
A TOU rate charges different amounts per kWh (the billing unit) during different times of the day. This is because electricity generation is more expensive during peak times of usage. Shifting your usage to off peak times can save you money and helps the electric grid by reducing the demand for electricity during the peak times and provides a monetary incentive to use more energy during off peak times.
On-peak hours: 5pm to 9pm Monday through Saturday
Off-peak hours: the rest of the time (including all day Sunday)
On-peak is defined as the hours during the day when electricity is most used and more expensive. Members on TOU rate pay more for energy used during peak hours compared to a lower rate paid during off-peak hours. View current rates here.
GCEA’s on-peak hours occur from 5 pm to 9 pm Monday through Saturday.
- Adjust your thermostat
- Consider investing in a programmable thermostat
- Program your thermostat to come on early enough to have your home warm by 5 pm
- Limit hot water use
- Consider placing a timer on your water heater that will help shift energy load
- Use high-wattage appliances like the dryer, washer, oven, and stove before or after the on-peak hours
- Use a toaster oven instead
- Use the delay on dishwasher
- Use a crockpot to cook meals
- Consider doing laundry on Sunday when there is no peak time
