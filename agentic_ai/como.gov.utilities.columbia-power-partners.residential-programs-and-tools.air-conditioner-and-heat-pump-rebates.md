# Air Conditioner and Heat Pump Rebates

As much as half of the energy used in your home can go to keeping it heated or cooled. City of Columbia Utilities offers rebates on air conditioners and heat pumps. We also offer a special reduced rate for heat pumps.

If you have any questions, please email [email@domain.com](mailto:email@domain.com) or call [573.441.5528](tel:5734415528).

## Air Conditioner and Air Source Heat Pump Rebates

If your cooling system is older than 10 years, you have frequent service problems and/or you see your utility bills going up, it is time to consider installing a new unit. Install a more efficient system and get a rebate from $800 to $1,200.

City of Columbia Utilities wants to pay you back for your increased energy efficiency. Due to the increased costs of higher SEER units, the amount of the rebate offered to you also goes up.

### Air conditioner/Heat pump rebate chart

**SEER2** | **Rebate**
--- | ---
15.2-18.1 | $800
18.2 or greater | $1,200
Geothermal units less than 5 tons | $1,200

*Check the AHRI Directory for the SEER2 rating confirmation before completing the purchase of a new unit. Sometimes the manufacturer’s SEER2 rating can be rated higher than the unit can perform.*

## Heat Pump Discounts

Heat pumps are more efficient than electric furnaces, which helps the utility save electricity. City of Columbia Utilities passes these savings to customers by offering premium heat pump rates during the winter to qualifying homes. 

Visit our [Residential Heat Pump](https://www.como.gov/utilities/utility-rates/#tab2) section in the rates directory for more detailed rate information. The discounted rate is applied October through May. Read below to see if you may be eligible.

There is also an additional Electrification rebate available when customers replace their air conditioners and gas furnace with a qualifying heat pump. For more information, click on the link below:

[Efficient Electrification](https://www.como.gov/utilities/columbia-power-partners/residential-programs-and-tools/efficient-electrification/)

### Program Parameters

- Be a City of Columbia Utilities electric customer.
- Have a working heat pump (rated 15.2 SEER2) installed.
- This rebate will only apply when an existing system is being replaced by a new unit. This rebate will not apply to new air conditioners and heat pumps that are not replacing a system in a residence. This rebate does not apply to new construction residences.
- Contact us at [573.441.5528](tel:5734415528) or [Efficiency@CoMo.gov](mailto:Efficiency@CoMo.gov).

## Participation Steps

- **Application:** Complete the appropriate rebate application. Each application includes a checklist of items you will need to receive a rebate. Rebates are offered on a first-come, first-serve basis to electric customers, as funding is available each fiscal year.
- [Air Conditioner/Heat Pump Rebate Application](https://www.como.gov/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/Residential_ACHeatPump_Form-1.pdf)
- **Paid receipt:** Submit the rebate application with a copy of the paid receipt for the system. The receipt must list the model numbers for the components listed on the application. Check the [AHRI Directory](https://www.ahridirectory.org/Search/SearchHome?ReturnUrl=%2f) for the equipment’s rating confirmation before completing the purchase of a new unit. Sometimes the manufacturer’s rating can be rated higher than the unit can actually perform.
- **Permits:** A copy of the final mechanical permit from the [City of Columbia Building/Site Development](https://www.como.gov/community-development/building-site-development/) must be submitted along with the application. These permits are usually obtained by the contractor. 
- **Sizing:** Columbia Water & Light reserves the right to deny a rebate for a unit that is oversized for a building. Either your contractor or the utility can help you with purchasing a properly sized air conditioning system. 
- **Deadlines:** The rebate application form and receipts must be submitted to Columbia Water & Light within six months of the air conditioner unit’s installation. Incomplete or incorrect applications will have 30 days to provide the correct information or risk losing their place in the rebate funding process. 
- **Verification:** Columbia Water & Light reserves the right to inspect the installed equipment and perform combustion safety testing on all applicable appliances in the home before the rebate payment is issued.
- **Funding:** If you need help financing your project, check out our [low-interest loan program](https://www.como.gov/utilities/columbia-power-partners/residential-programs-and-tools/home-performance-rebates-and-loans/) that is available in our Home Performance with Energy Star program.

Return the completed form to City of Columbia Utilities, P.O. Box 6015, Columbia, MO 65205-6015 or [573.441.5528](tel:5734415528).
