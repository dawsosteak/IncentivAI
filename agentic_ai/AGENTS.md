Electric utilities offer monetary incentives for renewable energy systems, energy-efficient
equipment, and building improvements. This project will build a
Database of State Incentives
for Renewables and Efficiency (DSIRE), a comprehensive source for policies and incentives
supporting the use of renewable energy. This project will discover such
incentives offered by electric utilities under 30,000 customers to update DSIRE. The
questions aim to better understand what these incentives are, how consumers may qualify for
them, how and where these incentives are currently promoted, and any other information that
will help us in formulating keywords/search terms to scrub the relevant webpages.

The primary source of information about these incentives and how they are
promoted will be found from the utilities themselves. Since we eventually need to
scrub the data directly from these pages, it will be beneficial to familiarize
ourselves with the language, phrasing, and formatting they use. It will also be
beneficial to see how DSIRE lists these incentives to understand the desired
format of our output

This information can then be used by both public and private
utility companies to help aid in the purchasing of renewable energy, electrification devices, energy
efficiency equipment, and building improvements. Not only is this beneficial to the utility companies, but
it is also useful for companies providing resources to the companies, and the consumers. A few design
constraints of this project is the need for an open resource to be used and apply to the diverse range of
websites and companies providing information.
