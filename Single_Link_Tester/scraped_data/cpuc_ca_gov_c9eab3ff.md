Summary This decision extends the statutory deadline in this proceeding to August 15, 2020. 1. Background Public Utilities (Pub. Util.) Code Section 1701.5(a)1 provides that ratesetting cases must be resolved within 18 months of the date the Scoping Memo is issued unless the Commission makes findings why that statutory deadline cannot be met and issues an order extending the 18-month deadline. Pursuant to Pub. Util. Code Section 1701.5(b), the Commission may also specify in a Scoping Memo a resolution date later than 18 months, if the Scoping Memo includes reasons for the necessity of a later date. - 3 2. An Extension of the Statutory Deadline is Necessary As further described below, an extension of the statutory deadline until August 15, 2020, is appropriate to finalize the work being done regarding alternative sourcing mechanisms and to determine the updates needed to the Avoided Cost Calculator. The issues in this proceeding are technical in nature and require more time than initially expected. For example, the development of alternative sourcing mechanisms has led to the creation of proposals for a distributed energy resources tariff but requires a review of the risks of over-procurement and under-procurement. The development of alternative sourcing mechanisms is on course but needs additional time to finalize the proposals. In addition, D.19-05-019 approved a process and schedule for updating the Avoided Cost Calculator, which is an essential part of the Commission’s cost-effectiveness analysis for distributed energy resources. The approved process includes a review of both major and minor updates to the Avoided Cost Calculator. However, the approved schedule estimates that a determination of the necessary updates will not occur until the spring of 2020. Thus, additional time is also needed to determine the revisions needed to the Avoided Cost Calculator. Accordingly, it is necessary to extend the statutory deadline of this proceeding until August 15, 2020 to complete proposals for alternative sourcing mechanisms and to determine the revisions needed to the Avoided Cost Calculator. 3.
