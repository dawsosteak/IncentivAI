Decision 21-08-020 August 5, 2021
* * *
BEFORE THE PUBLIC UTILITIES COMMISSION OF THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA
* * *
Order Instituting Rulemaking to Create a Consistent Regulatory Framework for the Guidance, Planning and Evaluation of Integrated Distributed Energy Resources.
* * *
Rulemaking 14-10-003
* * *
ORDER EXTENDING STATUTORY DEADLINE
* * *
Summary This decision extends the statutory deadline in Rulemaking 14-10-003 to September 15, 2021. 1. Background Public Utilities Code Section 1701.5(a)1 provides that ratesetting cases must be resolved within 18 months of the date the Scoping Memo is issued, unless the Commission makes a written determination that the deadline cannot be met and issues an order extending the deadline. On February 11, 2021, the Commission adopted Decision (D.) 21-02-033, which extended the statutory deadline by six months to oversee the implementation of pilots adopted in Rulemaking (R.) 14-10-003. The decision explained that the extension was necessary to provide the Commission flexibility in implementing the pilots.
* * *
## R.14-10-003 was initiated prior to changes made to Pub. Util. Code § 1701.5(a). These changes
only apply to cases filed after December 31, 2016.
* * *
R.14-10-003 ALJ/KHY/mln/sgu
* * *
- 2 On June 30, 2021, a proposed decision was issued closing R.14-10-003. The proposed decision explains that the Commission has largely resolved the issues it set out to accomplish through the development of the Competitive Solicitation Framework for distributed energy resources, pilots of two additional frameworks for the procurement of distributed energy resources, and updates on the cost-effectiveness analysis framework for distributed energy resources. The proposed decision notes that these actions have continuing related activities and finds the activities can be more appropriately resolved in R.21-06-007, the Order Instituting Rulemaking to Modernize the Electric Grid for a High Distributed Energy Resources Future, as well as an anticipated new rulemaking to address continuing activities related to the cost-effectiveness analysis framework, including the Avoided Cost Calculator. 2. An Extension of the Statutory Deadline is Necessary The proposed decision closing R.14-10-003 was issued on June 30, 2021 and is anticipated to be considered by the Commission on the August 5, 2021 Business Meeting. If consideration of the proposed decision is held for any reason, the next time the proposed decision could be considered is August 19, 2021, which occurs after the existing statutory deadline of August 15, 2021. Hence, an extension of the proceeding deadline will allow for any delays in the deliberative process. Accordingly, it is necessary and appropriate to extend the statutory deadline to September 15, 2021.
* * *
R.14-10-003 ALJ/KHY/mln/sgu
* * *
- 3 3. Waiver of Comment Period Under Rule 14.6(c)(4) of the Commission’s Rules of Practice and Procedure, the Commission may reduce or waive the period for public review and comment of proposed decisions extending the deadline for resolving ratesetting proceedings. Accordingly, the otherwise applicable period for public review and comment is being waived. 4. Assignment of Proceeding Darcie L. Houck is the assigned Commissioner and Kelly A. Hymes is the assigned Administrative Law Judge in this proceeding. Findings of Fact 1. D.21-02-033 extended the statutory deadline to August 15, 2021, in order to oversee the implementation of pilots adopted in R.14-10-003. 2. On June 30, 2021, the Administrative Law Judge issued a proposed decision closing this proceeding. 3. An extension of the existing proceeding deadline will allow for any delays in the deliberative process of the proposed decision closing R.14-10-003. 4. It is necessary and appropriate to extend the statutory deadline of this proceeding to September 15, 2021, to consider the proposed decision closing R.14-10-003. Conclusion of Law Pursuant to the authority granted to the Commission under Pub. Util. Code § 1701.5(a), the statutory deadline should be extended to September 15, 2021.
* * *
R.14-10-003 ALJ/KHY/mln/sgu
* * *
- 4 IT IS ORDERED that the statutory deadline in this proceeding is extended until September 15, 2021. This order is effective today. Dated August 5, 2021, at San Francisco, California.
