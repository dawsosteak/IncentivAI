PART I: PROCEDURAL ISSUES
* * *
A. Brief description of Decision: This decision 1) adopts the consensus recommendations from the Competitive Solicitation Framework Working Group (CSFWG) August 1, 2016 Report and 2) approves a regulatory incentive mechanism pilot, based upon the proposed pilot, the outcomes of the CSFWG and party comments. This creates a framework for competitive solicitations for distributed energy resources (DERs) to defer or replace utility capital investments in the grid. It also will test a new incentive mechanism in an attempt to better align utility incentives with procuring the lowest-cost distribution grid resources, even when that requires delaying or avoiding rate-based capital investments.

Intervenor’s claim of cost reasonableness: NRDC consistently advocates for policies to maximize cost-effective procurement and use of clean energy resources, ensure that the benefits of clean energy resources are properly accounted for, and that policies and goals align to enable the utilities to use clean energy as their first energy resource choice (as required by California law).
