This Email Ruling addresses the motion filed by Green Power Institute requesting party status in Rulemaking (R.) 14-10-003. As described below, the motion is not compliant with Commission Rules of Practice and Procedure Rule 1.4(b)(2) and is denied without prejudice.
* * *
Green Power Institute filed a motion, dated February 15, 2019, requesting party status in R.14-10-003. Green Power Institute stated that it is the renewable energy program of the Pacific Institute for Studies in Development, Environment and Security, a non-profit organization. Noting that it has been active in other Commission proceedings for a decade, Green Power Institute explained that its interest in the proceeding is focused on renewable energy.
* * *
Rule 1.4(b)(1) and (2) requires that a person seeking party status fully disclose the person or entities on whose behalf the motion is made, the interest of such persons in the proceeding, and the pertinent factual and legal contentions that the person intends to make. While the motion filed by Green Power Institute disclosed who it represents and its interests in this proceeding, the motion did not state the contentions Green Power Institute intends to make in this proceeding. The motion of Green Power Institute is not compliant with Rule 1.4(b)(2).
* * *
IT IS RULED that the motion of Green Power Institute for party status in Rulemaking 14-10-003 is denied without prejudice.
* * *
The Docket Office shall formally file this Email Ruling.
