1 BEFORE THE PUBLIC UTILITIES COMMISSION OF THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA Order Instituting Rulemaking to Examine the Commission's Post-2008 Energy Efficiency Policies, Programs, Evaluation, Measurement, and Verification, and Related Issues. Rulemaking 09-11-014 (Filed November 20, 2009) LATE-FILED NOTICE OF EX PARTE COMMUNICATION Pursuant to Rule 8.2, 8.3, and 8.5 of the California Public Utilities Commission’s (“Commission” or “CPUC”) Rules of Practice and Procedure, The Greenlining Institute (“Greenlining”) hereby gives notice of the following ex parte communications. Due to an oversight, this notice is filed one day out of time, and Greenlining humbly requests permission to file the notice nonetheless. Both communications were oral, and no written materials were distributed. Both were initiated by Greenlining, and took place at the CPUC. Greenlining was represented at both meetings by Stephanie Chen, Energy and Telecommunications Policy Director; Ryan Young, Legal Counsel; and Noemí Gallardo, Legal Fellow. The first communication occurred at approximately 2:30pm on December 3rd, with Colette Kersten, advisor to Commissioner Sandoval. This communication lasted approximately 20 minutes. The second communication occurred at approximately 3:00pm on December 3rd, with Marcelo Poirier, advisor to Commissioner Florio. This communication lasted approximately 30 minutes. F I L E D 12-07-12 04:59 PM
* * *
2 Both conversations centered on Greenlining’s recently-filed Petition for Modification regarding background check policies for contractors in energy efficiency programs. Greenlining recognized that action before the end of 2012, as requested in the petition, was unlikely, but urged each office to support Greenlining’s requests for 1) a stay of any changed in background check policies until the Commission has had a chance to review the issue in a transparent public forum, and 2) to initiate such a forum as soon as is reasonably possible. Greenlining expressed concern over the prospect that contractors would have to change their protocols multiple times if any utility changes its policy now, and then subsequently the Commission orders further changes. Greenlining also expressed the concern that allowing un-vetted policies to be implemented at this stage could result in current employees losing their jobs between the time proposed policies would be enacted and the time the Commission examines the issue. Greenlining reiterated its assertion that prior convictions for drug-related offenses are not related to the work being done, making their inclusion in a background check policy for energy efficiency work overbroad and inconsistent with current EEOC1 guidance. Greenlining reiterated its concern that including drug-related convictions in the background check process will create discriminatory results by disproportionately excluding workers of color, particularly African American and Latino workers, again for reasons unrelated to the work being done.
