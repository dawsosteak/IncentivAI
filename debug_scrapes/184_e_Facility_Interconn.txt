CITY OF FARMINGTON (FARMINGTON)  
AND FARMINGTON ELECTRIC UTILITY SYSTEM (FEUS) 
Third Revised Rate No. 19 
Cancelling Second Revised Rate No. 19 
 
RESIDENTIAL RENEWABLE TARIFF 
APPLICABILITY: 
Available to customers who normally receive electric service under FEUS Residential Service 
for the purchase of all or a portion of their energy requirements .  Service shall be supplied 
through one point of delivery and measured through one meter .  Not applicable for standby, 
supplemental, or resale service. 
TERRITORY: 
All territories served by the FEUS in San Juan County and Rio Arriba County. 
MONTHLY RATE: 
PHASE I (Effective July 1, 2022): 
System Infrastructure Charge ............................... $12.00 plus 
Renewable Energy Charge .................................... $0.05000 per kWh within the 100- kWh 
block of renewable energy purchased (price 
determined by market, see below) 
Wires Charge ........................................................ $0.05511 per kWh within the 100- kWh 
block of renewable energy purchased 
Energy Charge ...................................................... $0.10458 per kWh purchased in excess of 
renewable energy block allocation 
PHASE II (Effective July 1, 2023): 
System Infrastructure Charge ............................... $13.50 plus 
Renewable Energy Charge .................................... $0.05000 per kWh within the 100- kWh 
block of renewable energy purchased (price 
determined by market) 
Wires Charge ........................................................ $0.05436 per kWh within the 100- kWh 
block of renewable energy purchased 
Energy Charge ...................................................... $0.10465 per kWh purchased in excess of 
renewable energy block allocation 
PHASE III (Effective July 1, 2024): 
System Infrastructure Charge ............................... $15.00 plus 
Renewable Energy Charge .................................... $0.05000 per kWh within the 100- kWh 
block of renewable energy purchased (price 
determined by market)

Energy Charge ...................................................... $0.10500 per kWh purchased in excess of

MONTHLY MINIMUM CHARGE: 
The Monthly Minimum Charge under this tariff shall never be less than: 
PHASE I - $15.00 plus the tax adjustment. 
PHASE II - $17.00 plus the tax adjustment. 
PHASE III - $19.00 plus the tax adjustment. 
PHASE IV - $21.00 plus the tax adjustment. 
POWER COST ADJUSTMENT (PCA): 
The customer will be charged a fuel and PCA  on any energy purchased in excess of  the 
renewable energy block allocation.  The above rates include a base power supply cost and cost 
of service index of $0.04465 per kWh sold.  The above rates shall be increased or decreased 
by $0.0000001 per kWh for each $0.0000001 by which the power supply cost and cost of 
service index varies from $0.04465 per kWh sold. 
SPECIAL TAX AND ASSESSMENT ADJUSTMENT: 
Billings under this tariff may be increased by an amount equal to the sum of the taxes payable 
under the Gross Receipts and Compensating Tax Act and of all other taxes, fees, or charges 
(exclusive of payments made in lieu of ad valorem taxes) payable by Farmington/FEUS and 
levied or assessed by any governmental authority on the public utility service rendered, or on 
the right or privilege of rendering the service, or on any event incidental to the rendition of the 
service. 
TERMS OF PAYMENT: 
Bills are due and payable within fourteen (14) days of the billing date, are past due fifteen (15) 
days after bill is rendered, are delinquent twenty -five (25) days after the billing date, and 
service may be terminated for non- payment approximately thirty (30) days from the original 
38 
 
due date of the bill subject to a first delinquent notice provided fourteen (14) days prior to 
disconnection of utility service and a last notice of termination mailed five (5) business days 
prior to termination of service. If there is a returned check from the customer or the customer  
is on a payment plan, the notice is hand delivered to the customer at least two (2) days prior to 
service termination. 
CONDITIONS AND TYPE OF SERVICE: 
Service will be provided to those customers who request to receive their energy from 
Renewable Energy Resources.  “Renewable Energy” means electrical energy generated by 
means of a low or zero -emissions generation technology that has substantial long -term 
production potential and may include, without limitation, solar, wind, hydropower, 
geothermal, landfill gas, anaerobically digested waste bi omass or fuel cells that are not fossil 
fueled.  Renewable energy does not include fossil fuel or nuclear. 
The amount of power subscribed to by the customer shall be set out in an Application for 
Voluntary Renewable Energy Rate. 
Customer may purchase 100 kWh blocks of Renewable Energy up to approximately 100 
percent of monthly consumption.  Each purchase will remain in effect for a period of twelve  
(12) months as required in the Application for Voluntary Renewable Energy Rate.  In the event 
that a customer uses less energy than purchase, no credit shall be given.  Any additional costs 
incurred by FEUS in the procuring of Renewable Energy blocks will be included in the 
Renewable Energy Charge.  For energy usage in excess of renewable energy purchased the 
customer will pay the energy change and the PCA. 
TERMS AND CONDITIONS: 
A. Service under this tariff is subject to the FEUS Rules and Regulations. 
B. Service under this tariff shall be single-phase, 60 hertz, 120/240 volts.

Farmington Electric Utility System Frequently Asked Questions Related to Installing
Solar
I am considering solar for my home. What rate do I look at to understand the benefits I
might see for my investment?
Great question! We are seeing more and more customers install solar in our community. The rate
for you would be the “Residential Bidirectional Net Metering ” rate. To summarize it, you can build
a system up to 100% of your historical load. This will be based on the previous 12 months of
data of your home ’s usage. If the previous 12 months are not available, FEUS will use available
data to estimate the system size based on the size of the home, appliances and heating and
cooling sources. This is dependent on the existing infrastructure. It may be necessary to upgrade
the transformer (at your expense) , or your system may need to be sized accordingly.
What is “ net metering ” ?
Net metering is a system that utilities use to credit any renewable energy system owners for the
electricity produced by their solar panels. With net metering, you only pay for the electricity that
you use beyond what your solar panels can generate.  
Sometimes I will generate more electricity than I need for my home, other times (like at
night) I will need power from the utility.  How does the billing work?
If you used more electricity than you made in a month, your bill would be the energy you received
at the energy rate, plus the power cost adjustment. If you made more electricity than you used in
a month, FEUS will purchase the net power you sent to the utility at the avoided cost rate. Another
way to look at this is any electricity you generate is first used to offset the electricity you are
consuming at your home. Any electricity you generate that exceeds your needs at your home is
what FEUS will purchase.
Why are there restrictions on how large of a system I can build? Doesn ’t the utility need
the power?  Especially now with the power plant closing? 
There are two primary reasons:
1. T he sizing of the infrastructure near your home. In other words, oversizing your system
could have an e ffect on the lines and transformers in your neighborhood.
2. T he rate desig n allows you to bank power over- generated in paper form but not physical ly,
for when you do not produce at night. By allow ing this benefit , the utility limits your
production to match your consumption.
What will my bill look like? I already have sola r and I am confused by my bill.
Definitions:
 Residential Base - the system infrastructure charge. All accounts are charged this “base
charge ”. The amount varies depending on the rate class for the location. Currently the
Residential base is $13.50
 Residential Energy Charge - This is the charge assessed to the electricity that is sent to
you from FEUS. The current residential kWh charge is $0.10465
 Residential Energy Credit - This is the power that you produced and sent back to FEUS.
This is credited at the current residential kWh charge of $0.10465.
 Banked Consumption - This is the net difference between how much energy you sent to
FEUS and how much FEUS sent to you. If you under produce, all of your energy is
purchased back at the current residential kWh rate of $0.10465. If you overproduced, the
over production is listed on the bill as “Banked Consumption ”. That energy is bought from
you at the avoided cost rate. This rate is currently $0.035 / kWh. Our system credits back
everything you send FEUS at the full kWh rate of $0.10465, the net or “banked
consumption ” is then charged back to you at $0.06965 / kWh in order to get a net credit
of $0.035 / kWh. $0.10465 minus $0.035 = $0.06965. 
 PCA - The Power Cost Adjustment (PCA) is a mechanism utilized to recover costs
associated with fuel and Operation and Maintenance of FEUS generation facilities, as
well as power purchases, collectively referred to as power supply cost. The PCA
charge occurs when power supply costs exceed the amount contained in FEUS base
rates. Likewise, when power supply costs are less than the amount in FEUS base
rates the PCA will be negative, or a cre dit back to customers. The PCA is desig ned to
be a "pass through" mechanism to FEUS customers and is similar to the way many
other utilities recover power supply costs.

I have decided on a company to install solar and want to begin the process, can you help
me understand the steps? 
See the flowchart below.

Will I still receive an electric bill if I have solar panels?
Yes
Can FEUS help choosing a solar installer?
No, FEUS cannot be part of the process. FEUS is not associated or aligned with any solar
contractor.  FEUS  do es  not maintain a “preferred provider list ”.
Is the solar company able to sign documents on my behalf?
No. As the FEUS customer, you must sign the interconnection documents. Your signature
assures FEUS you have received all the information from the utility to make an informed decision.
Can I design my own solar system?
Yes, you can design your own system. To interconnect your system to FEUS, your design must
be reviewed and stamped by a professional engineer. Once the review and stamp is obtained,
the FEUS distribution engineer will review the design. If it meets FEUS standards, you can
proceed.  
My solar company told me that everything I produce will be sold back to FEUS. So if I
overproduce, why is there a charge on my bill?
As you produce electricity, you are first using it to offset the amount of electricity that you are
taking from FEUS. If, at the end of the month, you have produced more than you took from FEUS,
you will be credited the full kWh cost of $.10465 up to the amount of electricity that you took from
us and the overage (net) will be credited at the avoided cost rate of $0.035 / kWh. If your
overproduction is large enough that the credit covers your usage from FEUS, base charges, PCA
and tax then you will not receive a bill or you will possibly receive a credit. If the credit does not
cover those things, you will receive a bill. 
Does FEUS offer any incentives to go solar?
FEUS does not offer any specific incentives other than those that are embedded in the rate itself.
 Customers can apply for federal and state tax incentive opportunities.  
Can I just build solar on my home and disconnect from FEUS?
Of course! FEUS will need a letter from you, the FEUS customer, requesting to be disconnected
from the FEUS system.  
If I have credits on my bill from installing solar, does my credit go only to my electric bill
o r the entirety of  my bill?
If you are a customer inside the city limits of Farmington, quite likely your bill also has
water/sewer/sanitation as well. If you receive a credit from overproduction, the credit will be
applied to your total bill for the month.  
FEUS is paying me if I over generate at the “ avoided cost ” - what is this, and why is it
lower than the energy cost?
Good question. The “energy charge ” as it is defined on your bill covers some electrical
infrastructure costs. For example , not only is it the cost to buy or make electricity, but it also
includes the cost for equipment such as wires, poles, and substations you see around the area.
The avoided cost is the cost the utility pays to buy or make our own electricity, and is much lower
than the energy charge when the infrastructure is not included.
