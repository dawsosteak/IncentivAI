Residential Energy Efficiency Loans
The Home Energy Performance Loan program allows Co-op members to finance all or a portion of qualified energy efficiency measures so that upfront costs are not a barrier to long-term energy savings.
No-interest and low-interest financing are available.
No-interest and low-interest financing are available.
On Bill Financing
NHEC members with excellent payment history can finance Home Energy Performance project co-payment costs on their existing NHEC account.
- Billing will be in equal monthly payments on your electric bill
- On Bill Financing is interest free (0%)
- Terms up to 48 months are available, based on the amount of the loan
- Loan amounts up to $4,000 are available
- Minimum loan amount is $500
The balance of the loan becomes due if you move or close your NHEC account.
Third Party Financing
NHEC provides interest subsidies through participating banks and credit unions to finance Home Energy Performance project co-payments.
- Loan approval is based solely on the lending institution’s criteria–apply at the lender of your choosing with a Loan Authorization Form from NHEC
- NHEC will buy down the Loan Interest Rate to 2% through one-time payment to the lender
- Loan amounts up to $15,000 are available
- Terms up to 84 months are available, based on the amount of the loan
Before applying for financing, please be sure you already have a proposal for your Home Energy Performance project. To apply for financing with either program, please fill out the application below. Harnessing the Power of the Sun
Net Metering
The Co-op supports our members who choose to install renewable generation at their home or business. During certain hours, your photovoltaic (PV) system may generate more electricity than you can use. Through net metering members are compensated for this surplus generation that is exported to the Co-op’s distribution system.
Please reference NHEC’s Terms and Conditions Section X – Net Metering and Schedule of Rates and Fees for more information regarding our interconnection policies, rates and compensation for exports. We strongly recommend net metering members become familiar with the information contained within these documents.

Understanding Your Net Metering Bill
Net metering members will see three separate readings on their monthly bill.
For example, a Residential member would see:
N01A – The amount of electricity NHEC delivered to your home
N02A – The amount of electricity that your PV system exported to NHEC’s distribution system
PVM – This is the total amount of electricity generated by your PV system

Heat Pump Technology
Heat and Cool Your HomeHigh Efficiency Heat Pumps
Heat and cool your home up to 2-3 times more efficiently with a cold weather, high efficiency heat pump. Rather than using fuel combustion to generate new heat like other conventional HVAC systems, heat pumps use technology similar to that of your refrigerator and transfer heat from one area to another. Because they transfer heat, they are dual purpose in that they can effectively transfer heat into your home – even in New Hampshire’s frigid winters- and then out of your home- during our hot and humid summers.
Heat and Cool Your WaterHeat Pump Water Heater
Second only to heating and cooling, your water heater typically consumes the second largest amount of energy use in your home. Switch to a heat pump water heater which can be up to four times more efficient than a conventional electric water heater, with annual savings of up to $550 for a family of four.

Compare & Calculate Your Savings
For climates with moderate heating and cooling needs, heat pumps offer an energy-efficient alternative to furnaces and air conditioners. Like your refrigerator, heat pumps use electricity to move heat from a cool space to a warm space, making the cool space cooler and the warm space warmer. During the heating season, heat pumps move heat from the cool outdoors into your warm house and during the cooling season, heat pumps move heat from your cool house into the warm outdoors. Because they move heat rather than generate heat, heat pumps can provide equivalent space conditioning at as little as one quarter of the cost of operating conventional heating or cooling appliances.
